	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Turner (surname)</h1>

<p><b>Turner</b> is a common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> originating from Normandy, France, arriving in England after the Norman conquest with the earliest known records dated in the 12th century. It is the 28th-most common surname in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-surnamedb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-surnamedb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ReaneyWilson1991_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReaneyWilson1991-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Most often it derives from an occupational name applied to a maker of small objects out of wood, metal or bone, by turning on a <a href="/wiki/Lathe" title="Lathe">lathe</a> (from <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>tornier</i>, "lathe", ultimately from Latin).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In this sense it is analogous to the German surnames <a href="/wiki/Drexel_(name)" title="Drexel (name)">Drexel</a>, Drechsler, and <a href="/wiki/Dreyer" title="Dreyer">Dreyer</a>,  Polish <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Tokarz" class="extiw" title="wikt:Tokarz">Tokarz</a>, Finnish <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/sorvari" class="extiw" title="wikt:sorvari">Sorvari</a> and related to English surnames such as <a href="/wiki/Potter_(name)" title="Potter (name)">Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crocker_(name)" title="Crocker (name)">Crocker</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Smith1986_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith1986-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hanks2003_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hanks2003-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
Other occasional origins include Old French <i>tournoieur</i>, referring to someone either in charge of, or who participates in a tournament, and <i>Turnhare</i>, referring to a fast runner (one who can outrun a <a href="/wiki/Hare" title="Hare">hare</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-BrowneReaney1991_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BrowneReaney1991-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Early recordings of this surname include Ralph le Turner in the late 12th century.  The earliest recorded spelling of this family name dates from 1180 for "Warner le Turnur".<sup id="cite_ref-surnamedb_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-surnamedb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
