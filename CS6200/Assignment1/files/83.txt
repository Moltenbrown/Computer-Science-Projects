	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Williams (surname)</h1>

<p><b>Williams</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> form of the name <a href="/wiki/William_(name)" class="mw-redirect" title="William (name)">William</a> that originated in medieval <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;W_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;W-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The meaning is derived from <i>son</i> or <i>descendant of William</i>, the Northern <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> form that also gave the English name William.  Derived from an Old French given name with <a href="/wiki/Germanic_languages" title="Germanic languages">Germanic</a> elements; <i>will</i> = desire, will; and <i>helm</i> = helmet, protection.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is the second most common surname in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>; third most common in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and fourth most common in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
