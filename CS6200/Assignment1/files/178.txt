	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Willis (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Willis</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> of <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> origin. The oldest extant family of the name, the Willes family of Warwickshire, formerly of Newbold Comyn and Fenny Compton, has used the spellings 'Willis,' 'Willys,' and 'Wyllys' and appear in records from 1330.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In this case, the name derives from the name <i>de Welles</i> (under which name the family were Lincolnshire noblemen) which comes from the <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Norman</a> name <i>de Vallibus</i> (meaning 'of the valley'), which in turn was derived from the <i>Vaux</i> family tree. The Vaux family, established in England by Harold de Vaux, a close relative of <a href="/wiki/William_the_Conqueror" title="William the Conqueror">William the Conqueror</a>, appears in French records from 794. They had held power in their own right and through royal intermarriages.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are other derivations of the name, including 'son of William/ Will' (whereby the name has developed from 'Wills', and is similar to 'Wilson' and other related names);<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> because of the popularity of the name 'William' since the Norman conquest, it is clear that by no means are all people with this surname- even very distantly- related.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sources of the name notwithstanding, specific notable Willis families (aside from those in Warwickshire, aforementioned) were of: Halsnead, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> (now in <a href="/wiki/Whiston,_Merseyside" title="Whiston, Merseyside">Whiston, Merseyside</a>); <a href="/wiki/Atherfield" title="Atherfield">Atherfield</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight" title="Isle of Wight">Isle of Wight</a> (and subsequently of an estate named after it at <a href="/wiki/Woldingham" title="Woldingham">Woldingham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surrey" title="Surrey">Surrey</a>); and Monk's Barn, <a href="/wiki/Petersfield" title="Petersfield">Petersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a>. The <a href="/wiki/John_Walpole_Willis" title="John Walpole Willis">Willis</a> (later <a href="/wiki/John_William_Willis-Bund" title="John William Willis-Bund">Willis</a> <a href="/wiki/Bund_family_of_Wick_Episcopi" title="Bund family of Wick Episcopi">Bund</a>) family were of <a href="/wiki/Wick,_Worcestershire" title="Wick, Worcestershire">Wick, Worcestershire</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Cadet_branch" title="Cadet branch">cadet branch</a> of the Cambridgeshire family from which also came brothers <a href="/wiki/Sir_Thomas_Willys,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir Thomas Willys, 1st Baronet">Thomas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sir_Richard_Willis,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir Richard Willis, 1st Baronet">Richard Willis</a> (also Willys), each created a <a href="/wiki/Willys_baronets" title="Willys baronets">baronet</a>, both titles being extinct at the failure of their descendants by 1732.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Humphrey_Willis" title="Humphrey Willis">Humphrey Willis</a>, an English soldier who became <a href="/wiki/Sheriff" title="Sheriff">Sheriff</a> of <a href="/wiki/County_Donegal" title="County Donegal">County Donegal</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Fermanagh" title="County Fermanagh">County Fermanagh</a>, was of a yeomanry/ minor gentry family<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Woolavington" title="Woolavington">Woolavington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>, related on his mother's side to the <a href="/wiki/John_Popham_(judge)" title="John Popham (judge)">Pophams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Huntworth" title="Huntworth">Huntworth</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> he was the ancestor of the Willis family of <a href="/wiki/Florencecourt" title="Florencecourt">Florencecourt</a>, County Fermanagh, later also of <a href="/wiki/Monmouthshire" title="Monmouthshire">Monmouthshire</a>, of which came the physician and foreign advisor to Japan, <a href="/wiki/William_Willis_(physician)" title="William Willis (physician)">William Willis</a>, and the writer <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Armstrong_(writer)" title="Anthony Armstrong (writer)">Anthony Armstrong</a> (born George Anthony Armstrong Willis);<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> George Willis, who farmed at Florencecourt, discovered the <a href="/wiki/Florencecourt_Yew" class="mw-redirect" title="Florencecourt Yew">Florencecourt Yew</a> in 1767.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><br />
Notable persons with this surname include:
