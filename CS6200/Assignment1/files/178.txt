<p><b>Jones</b> is a surname of <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a> origins, meaning "John's son".  It is most popular in Wales and south central <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ReaneyWilson1991_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReaneyWilson1991-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-surnamedbjones_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-surnamedbjones-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jones_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jones_surname_distribution_map_in_Britain,_Ireland_and_Mann.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Jones_surname_distribution_map_in_Britain%2C_Ireland_and_Mann.png/170px-Jones_surname_distribution_map_in_Britain%2C_Ireland_and_Mann.png" decoding="async" width="170" height="319" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/bf/Jones_surname_distribution_map_in_Britain%2C_Ireland_and_Mann.png 1.5x" data-file-width="200" data-file-height="375" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jones_surname_distribution_map_in_Britain,_Ireland_and_Mann.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Distribution of Jones surname in Great Britain</div></div></div>
<p>The surname Jones first appears on record as a surname in England in 1273 with the name "Matilda Jones".<sup id="cite_ref-surnamedbjones_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-surnamedbjones-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Others put the first known record of the surname <i>Jones</i> as 1279, in <a href="/wiki/Huntingdonshire" title="Huntingdonshire">Huntingdonshire</a>, England.<sup id="cite_ref-Reaney_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reaney-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Around the time of the <a href="/wiki/Laws_in_Wales_Acts_1535_and_1542" title="Laws in Wales Acts 1535 and 1542">union of Wales with England</a>, the traditional Welsh system of patronymics was increasingly replaced by surnames. Furthermore, Christian names such as John which were common in England had become increasingly preferred to distinctively Welsh Christian names such as Marauded and Llewelyn. Thus "Mab Ioan" or "ap Sion" (and many other variations) meaning "son of John" became the surname Jones in a large number of cases, making it a very frequently used surname.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_and_21st_centuries">20th and 21st centuries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jones_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 20th and 21st centuries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jones remains the most widespread surname in Wales, borne by 5.75% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-McElduff_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McElduff-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The frequency in England is lower, at 0.75%, but still the second most popular surname, after <a href="/wiki/Smith_(surname)" title="Smith (surname)">Smith</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-McElduff_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McElduff-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The 2000 United States census provides a frequency of 0.50%, providing an overall rank of fifth most frequent with 57.7% White, 37.7% Black, 1.4% Hispanic, 0.9% Native American.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Jones was the fourth most common surname in the 1990 U.S. Census, behind only <a href="/wiki/Smith_(surname)" title="Smith (surname)">Smith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johnson" title="Johnson">Johnson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Williams_(surname)" title="Williams (surname)">Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
