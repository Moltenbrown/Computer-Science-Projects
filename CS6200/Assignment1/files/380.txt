	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Metalsmith</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Damascus_bladesmith.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Damascus_bladesmith.jpg/220px-Damascus_bladesmith.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="248" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Damascus_bladesmith.jpg/330px-Damascus_bladesmith.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Damascus_bladesmith.jpg/440px-Damascus_bladesmith.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3605" data-file-height="4067" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Damascus_bladesmith.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A bladesmith from Damascus, ca. 1900</div></div></div>
<p>A <b>metalsmith</b> or simply <b>smith</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Craft" title="Craft">craftsman</a> fashioning useful items (for example, tools, <a href="/wiki/Kitchenware" title="Kitchenware">kitchenware</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tableware" title="Tableware">tableware</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jewellery" title="Jewellery">jewellery</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Weapons" class="mw-redirect" title="Weapons">weapons</a>) out of various <a href="/wiki/Metal" title="Metal">metals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <b>Smithing</b> is one of the oldest <a href="/wiki/List_of_metalworking_occupations" title="List of metalworking occupations">metalworking occupations</a>. Shaping metal with a <a href="/wiki/Hammer" title="Hammer">hammer</a> (<a href="/wiki/Forging" title="Forging">forging</a>) is the archetypical component of smithing. Often the hammering is done while the metal is hot, having been <a href="/wiki/Heat" title="Heat">heated</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Forge" title="Forge">forge</a>. Smithing can also involve the other aspects of metalworking, such as refining metals from their ores (traditionally done by <a href="/wiki/Smelting" title="Smelting">smelting</a>), <a href="/wiki/Casting" title="Casting">casting</a> it into shapes (<a href="/wiki/Foundry" title="Foundry">founding</a>), and <a href="/wiki/File_(tool)" title="File (tool)">filing</a> to shape and size. 
</p><p>The prevalence of metalworking in the culture of recent centuries has led <i><a href="/wiki/Smith_(surname)" title="Smith (surname)">Smith</a></i> and its equivalents in various languages to be a common <a href="/wiki/Surname#Occupational_name" title="Surname">occupational surname</a> (German <a href="/wiki/Schmidt_(surname)" title="Schmidt (surname)">Schmidt</a>, Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Ferreiro" title="Ferreiro">Ferreiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferreira_(surname)" title="Ferreira (surname)">Ferreira</a>, French <a href="/wiki/Lef%C3%A8vre" title="Lefèvre">Lefèvre</a>, Spanish <a href="/wiki/Herrero" title="Herrero">Herrero</a>, Italian <a href="/wiki/Fabbri_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Fabbri (disambiguation)">Fabbri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Ferrari (disambiguation)">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferrero" title="Ferrero">Ferrero</a>, Ukrainian <a href="/wiki/Koval_(surname)" title="Koval (surname)">Koval</a> etc.). As a <a href="/wiki/Suffix" title="Suffix">suffix</a>, <i>-smith</i> <a href="/wiki/Connotation" title="Connotation">connotes</a> a meaning of a specialized craftsman—for example, <i>wordsmith</i> and <i>tunesmith</i> are nouns synonymous with writer or songwriter, respectively.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metalsmith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Pre-Industrial_Era" class="mw-redirect" title="Pre-Industrial Era">pre-industrialized</a> times, smiths held high or special social standing since they supplied the metal tools needed for farming (especially the <a href="/wiki/Plough" title="Plough">plough</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Warfare" class="mw-redirect" title="Warfare">warfare</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_smiths">Types of smiths</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metalsmith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Types of smiths">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A metalsmith is one who works with or has the knowledge and the capacity of working with "all" metals.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wessel_smedbager02.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Wessel_smedbager02.jpg/220px-Wessel_smedbager02.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="232" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3b/Wessel_smedbager02.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="232" data-file-height="245" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wessel_smedbager02.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Illustration by <a href="/wiki/Theodor_Kittelsen" title="Theodor Kittelsen">Theodor Kittelsen</a> for <a href="/wiki/Johan_Herman_Wessel" title="Johan Herman Wessel">Johan Herman Wessel</a>'s <i>The Smith and the Baker</i></div></div></div>
<p>Types of smiths include:<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>A <a href="/wiki/Blacksmith" title="Blacksmith">blacksmith</a> works with iron and steel (this is what is usually meant when referring just to "smith")<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Bladesmith" title="Bladesmith">bladesmith</a> forges <a href="/wiki/Knives" class="mw-redirect" title="Knives">knives</a>, swords, and other <a href="/wiki/Blade" title="Blade">blades</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Brownsmith" class="mw-redirect" title="Brownsmith">brownsmith</a> works with <a href="/wiki/Brass" title="Brass">brass</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Not in common use anywhere I can find. Find a more citeable source (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Coinsmith" class="mw-redirect" title="Coinsmith">coinsmith</a> works strictly with <a href="/wiki/Coins" class="mw-redirect" title="Coins">coins</a> and currency</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Coppersmith" title="Coppersmith">coppersmith</a> works with copper<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Goldsmith" title="Goldsmith">goldsmith</a> works with gold<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Gunsmith" title="Gunsmith">gunsmith</a> builds and repairs <a href="/wiki/Firearm" title="Firearm">firearms</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Locksmithing" title="Locksmithing">locksmith</a> works with <a href="/wiki/Lock_(security_device)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lock (security device)">locks</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Silversmith" title="Silversmith">silversmith</a>, or brightsmith, works with silver<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Bladesmith" title="Bladesmith">swordsmith</a> is a bladesmith who forges only <a href="/wiki/Sword" title="Sword">swords</a>.</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Tinsmith" title="Tinsmith">tinsmith</a>, tinner, or tinker works with light metal (such as tinware) and can refer to someone who deals in tinware<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A weapon-smith forges weapons like <a href="/wiki/Axe" title="Axe">axes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spear" title="Spear">spears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flail_(weapon)" title="Flail (weapon)">flails</a>, and other weapons<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Whitesmith" title="Whitesmith">whitesmith</a> works with white metal (<a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">tin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pewter" title="Pewter">pewter</a>) and can refer to someone who polishes or finishes the metal rather than forging it<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artisans_and_craftpeople">Artisans and craftpeople</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metalsmith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Artisans and craftpeople">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PunzoWorkshop76.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/PunzoWorkshop76.JPG/220px-PunzoWorkshop76.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="193" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/PunzoWorkshop76.JPG/330px-PunzoWorkshop76.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/PunzoWorkshop76.JPG/440px-PunzoWorkshop76.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2954" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PunzoWorkshop76.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Coppersmith Abdón Punzo in his workshop in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara_del_Cobre" title="Santa Clara del Cobre">Santa Clara del Cobre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lafter3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Lafter3.jpg/220px-Lafter3.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="324" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Lafter3.jpg/330px-Lafter3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Lafter3.jpg/440px-Lafter3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="480" data-file-height="706" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lafter3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>"Aeolus's Weathervane." Detail of a <a href="/wiki/Weather_vane" title="Weather vane">weather vane</a> created by using a variety of metalsmithing techniques</div></div></div>
<p>The ancient traditional tool of the smith is a <a href="/wiki/Forge" title="Forge">forge</a> or <i>smithy</i>, which is a <a href="/wiki/Furnace" title="Furnace">furnace</a> designed to allow compressed air (through a <a href="/wiki/Bellows" title="Bellows">bellows</a>) to superheat the inside, allowing for efficient melting, <a href="/wiki/Soldering" title="Soldering">soldering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Annealing_(metallurgy)" title="Annealing (metallurgy)">annealing</a> of metals. Today, this tool is still widely used by blacksmiths as it was traditionally.
</p><p>The term, <i>metalsmith</i>, often refers to artisans and craftpersons who practice their craft in many different metals, including gold, copper and silver. <a href="/wiki/Jeweler" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeweler">Jewelers</a> often refer to their craft as <i>metalsmithing</i>, and many universities offer degree programs in metalsmithing, jewelry, enameling and blacksmithing under the auspices of their fine arts programs.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Machinists">Machinists</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metalsmith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Machinists">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Machinist" title="Machinist">Machinists</a> are metalsmiths who produce high-precision parts and tools.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The most advanced of these tools, <a href="/wiki/CNC" class="mw-redirect" title="CNC">CNC</a> machines, are computer controlled and largely automated.
</p>
