<p><b>Siblings Day</b> is a holiday recognized annually in some parts of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on April 10,<sup id="cite_ref-oswego_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oswego-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and as Brothers and Sisters Day on May 31st in Europe,<sup id="cite_ref-thesun.co.uk_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thesun.co.uk-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> honoring the relationships of <a href="/wiki/Siblings" class="mw-redirect" title="Siblings">siblings</a>. Unlike <a href="/wiki/Mother%27s_Day" title="Mother&#39;s Day">Mother's Day</a> and <a href="/wiki/Father%27s_Day" title="Father&#39;s Day">Father's Day</a>, it is not federally recognized in the United States, though the Siblings Day Foundation is working to change this.<sup id="cite_ref-primary_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-primary-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Since 1998, the governors of 49 <a href="/wiki/States_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="States of the United States">states</a> have officially issued proclamations to recognize Siblings Day in their state.<sup id="cite_ref-primary2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-primary2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Siblings Days are celebrated also in the United Kingdom,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Australia,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and India.<sup id="cite_ref-Hinduism_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hinduism-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hindu</a> holiday of <a href="/wiki/Raksha_Bandhan" title="Raksha Bandhan">Raksha Bandhan</a> also celebrates the bond of brothers and sisters.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siblings_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The US holiday was conceived by Claudia Evart to honor the memory of her brother and sister who died at early ages.<sup id="cite_ref-longisland_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-longisland-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  The Siblings Day Foundation was incorporated in 1997 and achieved non-profit status in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-primary3_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-primary3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Carolyn_Maloney" title="Carolyn Maloney">Carolyn Maloney</a>, then the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">U.S. Representative</a> for <span data-sort-value="New York14&#160;!"><a href="/wiki/New_York%27s_14th_congressional_district" title="New York&#39;s 14th congressional district">New York's 14th congressional district</a></span>, officially saluted the holiday and introduced it into the official <i><a href="/wiki/Congressional_Record" title="Congressional Record">Congressional Record</a></i> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> on April 10, 1997; and in subsequent years 2001, 2005 and 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-Maloney_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maloney-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Europe, the holiday was launched in 2014 by the European Large Families Confederation (ELFAC) to celebrate siblings bonds and relationships. The May 31 feast spread in different ways in the European countries where ELFAC is present. In Portugal, <i>Dia dos Irmãos</i><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Wikipedia_and_sources_that_mirror_or_use_it" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="This claim cites another Wikipedia article. Articles need references to reliable third-party sources. (July 2019)">circular reference</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> has become very popular and the President of Republic of Portugal has greeted it publicly, in 2016 and 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><br />
</p><p>ELFAC has associate members in several European countries<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>: Austria, Cyprus, Croatia, Czech Republic, Estonia, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Portugal, Romania, Serbia and Switzerland. But adherence to the date and spirit of May 31 is open to any other European or non-European country.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebration">Celebration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siblings_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Celebration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United States, approximately 80% of people have siblings.<sup id="cite_ref-longisland_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-longisland-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The holiday is intended to be a celebration of the relationship of brothers and sisters.<sup id="cite_ref-Hinduism_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hinduism-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Examples of commemoration during this observance include giving your sibling a gift (including a surprise gift),<sup id="cite_ref-pbailey_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pbailey-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> a giftcard, and taking one out for dinner.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Nonmaterial examples of observances during this day includes giving hugs to your sibling(s), enjoying time with them, honoring their presence in your life, and greeting them on various social media platforms using childhood photos.<sup id="cite_ref-pbailey_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pbailey-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
