	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Carter (name)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Carter (name)</caption><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Language(s)</th><td>Latin, Gaelic</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Meaning</th><td>"transports goods by cart"</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Region of origin</th><td><a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a></td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Other names</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Variant form(s)</th><td><a href="/wiki/McCarter" title="McCarter">McCarter</a></td></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;border-top: #a2a9b1 1px solid;">Frequency Comparisons:<sup id="cite_ref-fb_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Carter</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Family_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Family name">family name</a>, and also may be a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>. Carter is of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> and <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> origin and is an occupational name given to one who transports goods by cart or wagon originally believed to be of <a href="/wiki/Goidelic_languages" title="Goidelic languages">Gaelic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Celtic_languages" title="Celtic languages">Celtic</a> origins and an Irish reduced form of the name <a href="/wiki/McCarter" title="McCarter">McCarter</a> with Mc meaning son of.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is known to be from the Gaelic word <i>cairt</i> meaning cart, and ultimately from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>carettarius</i>. In Gaelic, the word "cairtear", which means tourist or sojourner, is related. It is the 44th most common surname in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, 56th most common in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, 428th most common in Ireland where it is found with greatest frequency in the <a href="/wiki/Leinster" title="Leinster">Leinster</a> province at a frequency of 304, and 274th in Scotland.<sup id="cite_ref-fb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>People of <a href="/wiki/White_people" title="White people">European</a> heritage bearing this surname may trace their ancestors back to Ireland, England or Scotland. Within the past 150 years, the Carter surname has been adopted widely by <a href="/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans">African Americans</a> taken from slavemasters by former slaves upon the <a href="/wiki/Emancipation_Proclamation" title="Emancipation Proclamation">Emancipation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thirteenth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Thirteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution">13th Amendment</a>. This name is common among African Americans capable of tracing their roots back to the <a href="/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">southern United States</a> or <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> from the early 20th century onward, with some 35% of name holders of Carter in the United States being of African-American descent and it being the 22nd most common surname for Black Americans.
</p>
