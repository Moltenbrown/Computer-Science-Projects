	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Stepchild</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"Stepdaughters" redirects here. For the <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">GMA Network</a> TV series, see <a href="/wiki/The_Stepdaughters" title="The Stepdaughters">The Stepdaughters</a>.</div>
<p>A <b>stepchild</b> or, informally, <b>stepkid</b> is the offspring of one's <a href="/wiki/Spouse" title="Spouse">spouse</a>, but not one's own offspring, neither biological nor through <a href="/wiki/Adoption" title="Adoption">adoption</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Perception">Perception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepchild&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Perception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The traditional and strictest definition of a "<a href="/wiki/Stepfamily" title="Stepfamily">stepfamily</a>" is a married couple where one or both members of the couple have pre-existing children who live with them.<sup id="cite_ref-adopting1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adopting1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> More recently, the definition is often expanded to include all <a href="/wiki/Cohabiting" class="mw-redirect" title="Cohabiting">cohabiting</a> couples, whether married or not.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Some people also apply the term to non-custodial relationships, where "stepparent" can refer to the partner of a parent with whom the child does not live.<sup id="cite_ref-stepfamilies1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stepfamilies1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The term is not generally used (but can be in individual cases) to refer to the relationship with an adult child who never lived in the home with the parent's new partner.
</p><p>Children in a <a href="/wiki/Single_parent" title="Single parent">one parent family</a> often feel threatened when their parent is dating as the parent is looking for a prospective spouse. The prospective spouse can often feel threatened as the children become part of the package within the relationship. Stepfamilies can sometimes find it difficult to feel like a family as the spouse may not feel equal to the children due to the fact that a biological parent and their biological child have a stronger bond which is separate from the marriage.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
