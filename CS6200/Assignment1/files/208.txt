	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Campbell (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Campbell</caption><tbody><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center"><a href="/wiki/File:Campbell_surname_map.png" class="image"><img alt="Campbell surname map.png" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/05/Campbell_surname_map.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="286" data-file-width="200" data-file-height="286" /></a><div>Campbell <a href="/wiki/Surname_map" title="Surname map">surname map</a></div></td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Region of origin</th><td><a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a></td></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;border-top: #a2a9b1 1px solid;">Frequency Comparisons:<sup id="cite_ref-fb_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Campbell</b> is a Scottish <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>—derived from the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a> roots <i>cam</i> ("crooked") and <i>beul</i> ("mouth")—that originated as a nickname meaning "crooked mouth" or "wry mouthed."<sup id="cite_ref-Woulfe2010_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Woulfe2010-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Outside of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, Irish occurrences of the name usually derive from the surname <b><a href="/wiki/Mac_Cathmhaoil" class="mw-redirect" title="Mac Cathmhaoil">Mac Cathmhaoil</a></b>, from which also descend the surnames MacCawill, <a href="/wiki/McCaul" title="McCaul">McCaul</a>, MacCall, and Caulfield. The Irish pronunciation of <i>Cathmhaoil</i> ("battle chief") coincided with the Scottish pronunciation of <i>Campbell</i>, and the name was <a href="/wiki/Anglicised" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicised">anglicised</a> accordingly.<sup id="cite_ref-Campbell2000_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campbell2000-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Campbell is the third most common surname in Northern Ireland, fourth most common in Jamaica, seventh most common in Scotland, 12th most common in Canada, 18th most common in Australia, 41st most common in the United States and 65th most common in England.<sup id="cite_ref-fb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
