<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clan_Forbes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clan_Forbes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The name Forbes is most probably a <a href="/wiki/Surname#Typology" title="Surname">location name</a> assumed from the lands of Forbes in <a href="/wiki/Aberdeenshire" title="Aberdeenshire">Aberdeenshire</a>, in possession of this family reputedly since the time of King <a href="/wiki/William_the_Lion" title="William the Lion">William the Lion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While there are many legends surrounding the origins of this clan historians write the Forbes are descended from Kings of the Picts and a reliable tradition tells that the "Braes o’ Forbes" were once uninhabitable because of bears living in the area. Oconachar, founder of the clan, killed the bears and claimed the land as "first occupier". The present chief still holds part of the Lordship of these Forbes lands. The first person on record was Duncan Forbes who in 1271–2 received a grant of lands from <a href="/wiki/Alexander_III_of_Scotland" title="Alexander III of Scotland">Alexander III of Scotland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SPIV43_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPIV43-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Cited by <a href="/wiki/William_Forbes_Skene" title="William Forbes Skene">William Forbes Skene</a> the charter exists in the Forbes charter chest in tattered but quite legible condition.<sup id="cite_ref-HOF13_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOF13-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The next mention is a John Forbes, whose name dates from a 1306 roll containing a list of demands by English and Scottish loyalists to <a href="/wiki/Edward_I_of_England" title="Edward I of England">Edward I of England</a> for the forfeited lands of Scotsmen, the lands of John Forbes being demanded or requested by both a William Comyn and a Robert Chival.<sup id="cite_ref-SPIV43_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPIV43-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The next name may be that of his son, Christian, who received a grant of one-third of the lands of Skeith and Ardach by King <a href="/wiki/Robert_the_Bruce" title="Robert the Bruce">Robert the Bruce</a> in 1326, but doubt still remains he was a Forbes or of this family,<sup id="cite_ref-HOF13_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOF13-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> even though in the charter he is named Christian Forbes.<sup id="cite_ref-SPIV43_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPIV43-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The next name found in records is that of John Forbes <i>dominus ejusdem</i> or Lord of Forbes.<sup id="cite_ref-BP938_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BP938-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He witnessed two charters of <a href="/wiki/Thomas,_Earl_of_Mar" title="Thomas, Earl of Mar">Thomas, Earl of Mar</a> in 1358 and 1359 and in 1364 King <a href="/wiki/David_II_of_Scotland" title="David II of Scotland">David II of Scotland</a> confirmed a charter for the lands of Edinbanchory and Craiglogy by Thomas, Earl of Mar granting them to John de Forbes.<sup id="cite_ref-SPIV45_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPIV45-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He was Sheriff of Aberdeen in 1374.<sup id="cite_ref-SPIV45_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPIV45-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1378 a charter was granted to John and his wife Margaret by the Bishop of Moray for the lands of Fynrossie on the loch of Spynie. At his death before 20 August 1387 he was described as "a gude man, wise, and mychty, and manly in his time."<sup id="cite_ref-SMIV46_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SMIV46-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The son of the latter, Sir John de Forbes, Lord of Forbes, called "Sir John of the black lip"<sup id="cite_ref-CPV544_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPV544-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> was <a href="/wiki/Justiciary" class="mw-redirect" title="Justiciary">Justiciary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coroner#History" title="Coroner">Coroner</a> of Aberdeenshire.<sup id="cite_ref-SMIV46_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SMIV46-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He married Elizabeth Kennedy, daughter of Sir Gilbert Kennedy of Dunure<sup id="cite_ref-CPV544_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPV544-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and together they had four sons.<sup id="cite_ref-SCFE138_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCFE138-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> From the three younger sons sprang several cadet lines. William was the progenitor of the <a href="/wiki/Pitsligo_Castle" title="Pitsligo Castle">Pitsligo</a> line, John the ancestor of <a href="/wiki/Tolquhon_Castle" title="Tolquhon Castle">Tolquhonline</a> while the houses of Skellater and inverernan were founded by Alistair of Brux.<sup id="cite_ref-SCFE138_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCFE138-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Sir John died in 1406.<sup id="cite_ref-BP938_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BP938-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="15th_century">15th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clan_Forbes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 15th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sir John Forbes's son, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Forbes,_1st_Lord_Forbes" title="Alexander Forbes, 1st Lord Forbes">Alexander Forbes, 1st Lord Forbes</a> fought at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Harlaw" title="Battle of Harlaw">Battle of Harlaw</a> in 1411, in support of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Stewart,_Earl_of_Mar" title="Alexander Stewart, Earl of Mar">Alexander Stewart, Earl of Mar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Alexander had safe conduct from <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_of_England" title="Henry V of England">Henry V of England</a> to visit his king, <a href="/wiki/James_I_of_Scotland" title="James I of Scotland">James I of Scotland</a> at <a href="/wiki/Rouen" title="Rouen">Rouen</a> in 1421 and was allowed as his escort to bring 40 <a href="/wiki/Pikeman" class="mw-redirect" title="Pikeman">Pikeman</a> and other followers up to 100 men.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He married Elizabeth, daughter of <a href="/wiki/George_Douglas,_1st_Earl_of_Angus" title="George Douglas, 1st Earl of Angus">George Douglas, 1st Earl of Angus</a> and his wife Mary, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Robert_III_of_Scotland" title="Robert III of Scotland">Robert III of Scotland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HOF31_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOF31-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Together Alexander and Elizabeth had five children including James, the 2nd <a href="/wiki/Lord_Forbes" title="Lord Forbes">Lord Forbes</a>. Alexander Forbes was raised to the <a href="/wiki/Peerage" title="Peerage">Peerage</a> by <a href="/wiki/James_I_of_Scotland" title="James I of Scotland">James I</a> as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Forbes" title="Lord Forbes">Baron Forbes</a> between October 1444 and July 1445.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Alexander Forbes, 1st Lord Forbes died in 1448.<sup id="cite_ref-HOF31_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOF31-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>James, second Lord Forbes, married Egidia, daughter of William Keith, 1st <a href="/wiki/Earl_Marischal" title="Earl Marischal">Earl Marischal</a>, and had three sons: William, the 3rd Lord Forbes, Duncan, ancestor of the Forbeses of Corsindae and <a href="/wiki/Monymusk" title="Monymusk">Monymusk</a>, and Patrick, ancestor of the Forbeses, <a href="/wiki/Forbes_baronets" title="Forbes baronets">Baronets of Craigievar</a>, now <a href="/wiki/Lord_Sempill" title="Lord Sempill">Lord Sempill</a>, and also of the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Granard" title="Earl of Granard">Earls of Granard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alexander, fourth Lord Forbes, was in arms with his clan to revenge the murder of <a href="/wiki/James_III_of_Scotland" title="James III of Scotland">James III</a>, but after the defeat at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tillymoss&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tillymoss (page does not exist)">Tillymoss</a> he submitted to <a href="/wiki/James_IV_of_Scotland" title="James IV of Scotland">James IV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> John, the sixth Lord, succeeded his brother Arthur, the 5th Lord Forbes, in 1493. In 1536 he was charged with <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">Treason</a> and was imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Castle" title="Edinburgh Castle">Edinburgh Castle</a>, but was honourably acquitted after a long period of confinement.<sup id="cite_ref-SPIV53_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPIV53-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> John Forbes, Master of Forbes, his eldest surviving son and heir designate was arrested with his father, also on charges of Treason, and was condemned to be hanged, but due to his rank he was beheaded.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="16th_century">16th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clan_Forbes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 16th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1529, Clan Forbes was involved in a feud with the citizens of <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a>, who withheld a sort of <a href="/wiki/Blackmail" title="Blackmail">blackmail</a>, a yearly tun of wine for the fishings of the <a href="/wiki/River_Don,_Aberdeenshire" title="River Don, Aberdeenshire">Don</a>. In July 1530 Arthur Forbes of Brux and his accomplices attacked Aberdeen. The citizens took arms and drove the invaders to Greyfriars Place. The street fights lasted 24 hours. One of clan Forbes and some of the citizens were killed, a good many on both sides were wounded. Several of the inhabitants of Aberdeen, and <a href="/wiki/Commissioner" title="Commissioner">commissioners</a> were sent to the king to lodge a complaint. On 19 December the following year, the <a href="/wiki/Magistrate" title="Magistrate">magistrates</a> served letters of law-burrows against Pitsligo, Tolquhon, Corsindae, Brux, <a href="/wiki/Echt,_Aberdeenshire" title="Echt, Aberdeenshire">Echt</a>, and other gentlemen of the name of Forbes and Lord Pitsligo was obliged to find caution to the council at <a href="/wiki/Perth,_Scotland" title="Perth, Scotland">Perth</a> for his own and friends good behaviour towards the town of Aberdeen. At that time a deadly feud subsisted between Clan Forbes and <a href="/wiki/Clan_Leslie" title="Clan Leslie">Clan Leslie</a>; and it is probable that some of the Aberdeen town's people had interfered in that quarrel, which furiously raged throughout <a href="/wiki/Aberdeenshire" title="Aberdeenshire">Aberdeenshire</a>, and was attended by mutual massacres and murders.<sup id="cite_ref-ScottishClansAndTheirTartans_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ScottishClansAndTheirTartans-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Throughout the 16th century the Clan Forbes were involved in a long and bitter struggle against the <a href="/wiki/Clan_Gordon" title="Clan Gordon">Clan Gordon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In the 1520s there were murders by both sides, and one of the most prominent killed by the Forbeses was <a href="/wiki/Clan_Seton" title="Clan Seton">Seton of Meldrum</a> who was a close connection of the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Huntly" class="mw-redirect" title="Earl of Huntly">Earl of Huntly</a>, chief of Clan Gordon.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Earl of Huntly then became involved in a plot against the <i>Master of Forbes</i>, who was the son of the sixth <a href="/wiki/Lord_Forbes" title="Lord Forbes">Lord Forbes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The sixth Lord Forbes had been heavily implicated of the murder of Seton of Meldrum.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Master of Forbes was accused by the Earl of Huntly of conspiring to assassinate <a href="/wiki/James_V_of_Scotland" title="James V of Scotland">James V of Scotland</a> in 1536 by shooting at him with a cannon.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Master of Forbes was tried and executed however just days later his conviction was reversed and the Forbes family was restored to favor.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant Reformation">Protestant Reformation</a> added to the feud between the Clan Forbes and Clan Gordon in that the Gordons remained Catholic and the Forbeses became Protestant.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The traditional enemies of the Forbses such as the <a href="/wiki/Clan_Leslie" title="Clan Leslie">Clan Leslie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clan_Irvine" title="Clan Irvine">Clan Irvine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clan_Seton" title="Clan Seton">Clan Seton</a> sided with the Gordons while Protestant families such as the <a href="/wiki/Clan_Keith" title="Clan Keith">Clan Keith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clan_Fraser" title="Clan Fraser">Clan Fraser</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clan_Crichton" title="Clan Crichton">Clan Crichton</a> sided with the Clan Forbes.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Twenty Gordons were killed at a banquet held at the Forbes's <a href="/wiki/Druminnor_Castle" title="Druminnor Castle">Druminnor Castle</a> in 1571.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Later in 1571 the feud climaxed with the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tillieangus" title="Battle of Tillieangus">Battle of Tillieangus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Craibstone" title="Battle of Craibstone">Battle of Craibstone</a>, and Druminnor, then the seat of the chief of Clan Forbes was plundered.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Gordons followed this up with the massacre of twenty seven Forbeses of Towie at <a href="/wiki/Corgarff_Castle" title="Corgarff Castle">Corgarff Castle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> It took two Acts of Parliament for the clans to put down their arms.<sup id="cite_ref-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes_12-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clan.Encyclopedia.Forbes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="17th_century">17th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clan_Forbes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 17th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alexander, the 10th Lord Forbes, was a <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_general" title="Lieutenant general">Lieutenant general</a> under <a href="/wiki/Gustavus_Adolphus" class="mw-redirect" title="Gustavus Adolphus">Gustavus Adolphus</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years&#39; War">Thirty Years' War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On his return to Scotland he was given a commission and charged with suppressing uprisings in Ireland. He later retired to Germany and died on 20 April 1672 in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="18th_century">18th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clan_Forbes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 18th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Jacobite_risings" title="Jacobite risings">Jacobite risings</a> the Clan Forbes and their chief supported the British government. The Jacobites laid siege to the chief's historic seat of Culloden House in both the 1715 and 1745 risings. In September 1745, <a href="/wiki/Duncan_Forbes,_Lord_Culloden" class="mw-redirect" title="Duncan Forbes, Lord Culloden">Duncan Forbes, Lord Culloden</a>, a staunch "<a href="/wiki/Whig_(British_political_party)" class="mw-redirect" title="Whig (British political party)">whig</a>", was given a commission to raise twenty <a href="/wiki/Independent_Highland_Companies" title="Independent Highland Companies">Independent Highland Companies</a> to oppose the <a href="/wiki/Jacobite_rising_of_1745" title="Jacobite rising of 1745">Jacobite rising of 1745</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Simpson_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Simpson-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He succeeded in raising a total of eighteen Independent Highland Companies to fight against the Jacobites.<sup id="cite_ref-Simpson_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Simpson-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Branches">Branches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clan_Forbes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Branches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Forbes_baronets" title="Forbes baronets">Forbes baronets</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lord_Forbes_of_Pitsligo" title="Lord Forbes of Pitsligo">Lord Forbes of Pitsligo</a> were descended from William, second son of Sir John Forbes of that Ilk, in the time of <a href="/wiki/Robert_II_of_Scotland" title="Robert II of Scotland">Robert II</a>. Alexander, fourth Lord, was <a href="/wiki/Attainted" class="mw-redirect" title="Attainted">attainted</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Culloden" title="Battle of Culloden">battle of Culloden</a>, and living long secretly in one of his own gate lodges, died in 1762. Three families now claim the title.<sup id="cite_ref-ScottishClansAndTheirTartans_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ScottishClansAndTheirTartans-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Forbeses, <a href="/wiki/Forbes_baronets" title="Forbes baronets">Baronets of Craigievar</a>, a branch of the old House, <a href="/wiki/Craigievar_Castle" title="Craigievar Castle">Craigievar Castle</a>, sprang from Patrick Forbes of Corse, <a href="/wiki/Armour-Bearer_and_Squire_of_His_Majesty%27s_Body" title="Armour-Bearer and Squire of His Majesty&#39;s Body">armour-bearer</a> to <a href="/wiki/James_III_of_Scotland" title="James III of Scotland">James III</a>; and the Stuart-Forbesses of Pitsligo, Baronets, from Duncan of Corsindae, second son of James, second Lord Forbes. The <a href="/wiki/Edinglassie" class="mw-redirect" title="Edinglassie">Edinglassie</a> Forbeses are also a branch of the parent stock.<sup id="cite_ref-ScottishClansAndTheirTartans_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ScottishClansAndTheirTartans-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Forbeses of <a href="/wiki/Tolquhon_Castle" title="Tolquhon Castle">Tolquhon Castle</a>, a very old branch, acquired that estate in 1420, and were
progenitors of the Lairds of <a href="/wiki/Culloden,_Highland" title="Culloden, Highland">Culloden</a>. Sir Alexander Forbes of Tolquhon commanded a troop of cavalry in the Scots army at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Worcester" title="Battle of Worcester">Worcester</a>; and when the King's horse was shot, mounted him on his own, put his <a href="/wiki/Buff_coat" title="Buff coat">buff coat</a> and a bloody scarf about him, and saw him safe out of the field. The fortunes of this house were probably consumed in the fever of the <a href="/wiki/Darien_Scheme" class="mw-redirect" title="Darien Scheme">Darien Scheme</a> (like many other good old Scottish families), in which Alexander Forbes of Tolquhon appears to have embarked beyond his means, the stock he held (500) having been judicially attached.<sup id="cite_ref-ScottishClansAndTheirTartans_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ScottishClansAndTheirTartans-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sir William Forbes, eighth <a href="/wiki/Forbes_baronets" title="Forbes baronets">Baronet of Craigievar</a>, in 1884 succeeded his kinswoman as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Sempill" title="Lord Sempill">Lord Sempill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scottish_clan_chief" title="Scottish clan chief">Chief</a> of <a href="/wiki/Clan_Sempill" title="Clan Sempill">Clan Sempill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ScottishClansAndTheirTartans_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ScottishClansAndTheirTartans-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clan_chief">Clan chief</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clan_Forbes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Clan chief">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Scottish_clan_chief" title="Scottish clan chief">Clan Chief</a>: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Malcolm_Forbes,_23rd_Lord_Forbes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Malcolm Forbes, 23rd Lord Forbes (page does not exist)">Malcolm Forbes, 23rd Lord Forbes</a> and Chief of the Name and Arms of Forbes.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Castles">Castles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clan_Forbes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Castles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Castles that have been owned by the Clan Forbes have included amongst many others:
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Castle_Forbes.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Castle_Forbes.jpg/250px-Castle_Forbes.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Castle_Forbes.jpg/375px-Castle_Forbes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Castle_Forbes.jpg/500px-Castle_Forbes.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Castle_Forbes.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Castle_Forbes" title="Castle Forbes">Castle Forbes</a>, the current seat of the chief of Clan Forbes</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tolquhon_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_476318.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Tolquhon_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_476318.jpg/250px-Tolquhon_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_476318.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Tolquhon_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_476318.jpg/375px-Tolquhon_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_476318.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Tolquhon_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_476318.jpg/500px-Tolquhon_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_476318.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="426" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tolquhon_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_476318.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The ruins of Tolquhon Castle, seat of the Forbes of Tolquhon branch of the clan</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Corgarff_Castle_from_Lecht_Road.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Corgarff_Castle_from_Lecht_Road.jpg/250px-Corgarff_Castle_from_Lecht_Road.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="168" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Corgarff_Castle_from_Lecht_Road.jpg/375px-Corgarff_Castle_from_Lecht_Road.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Corgarff_Castle_from_Lecht_Road.jpg/500px-Corgarff_Castle_from_Lecht_Road.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3900" data-file-height="2613" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Corgarff_Castle_from_Lecht_Road.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Corgarff Castle</div></div></div>
<ul><li>Castlehill at Druminnor, one and a half miles north-east of <a href="/wiki/Rhynie,_Aberdeenshire" title="Rhynie, Aberdeenshire">Rhynie, Aberdeenshire</a>, was the site of the first stronghold on the lands, which the Forbeses held from 1271 to 1440.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> They then moved to Druminnor Castle.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Druminnor_Castle" title="Druminnor Castle">Druminnor Castle</a>, one mile south-east of Rhynie, Aberdeenshire, consists of a keep and tower that has been much altered and was first built by the Forbeses in the fifteenth century.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It was modified in the sixteenth century when it was made into an L-plan and there have also been later alterations.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The Forbeses feuded with the Leslies, Setons and the powerful Clan Gordon, and twenty Gordons were killed at a banquet held at Druminnor Castle in 1571.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In 1770 Druminnor Castle was sold and the Forbes family moved to Castle Forbes.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Castle_Forbes" title="Castle Forbes">Castle Forbes</a>, a few miles north east of <a href="/wiki/Alford,_Aberdeenshire" title="Alford, Aberdeenshire">Alford, Aberdeenshire</a>, was once a tower house but was replaced with a large castellated mansion of 1815.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Craigievar_Castle" title="Craigievar Castle">Craigievar Castle</a>, four and a half miles south-west of Alford, Aberdeenshire, was built by the Forbeses.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It is an L-plan tower house of seven storeys.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The castle was actually started by the Mortimer family but they ran out of money and it was completed by the Forbeses.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The castle was taken over by the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Scotland" title="National Trust for Scotland">National Trust for Scotland</a> in 1963 and is now open to the public.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tolquhon_Castle" title="Tolquhon Castle">Tolquhon Castle</a>, four miles east of <a href="/wiki/Oldmeldrum" title="Oldmeldrum">Oldmeldrum</a>, Aberdeenshire, is a ruinous keep and courtyard castle.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The original castle was built by the Prestons of Craigmillar, but it passed by marriage to the Forbeses in 1420 and they completed the castle.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Sir Alexander Forbes, sixth laird of the castle, was killed at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Pinkie_Cleugh" title="Battle of Pinkie Cleugh">Battle of Pinkie Cleugh</a> in 1547.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The present castle was built by William Forbes, the seventh laird.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The tenth Forbes laird of Tolquhon saved <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">Charles II of England</a>'s life at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Worcester" title="Battle of Worcester">Battle of Worcester</a> in 1651.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In 1716 the Forbeses sold the castle to the Farquhars because they had debts as a result of being involved in the <a href="/wiki/Darien_scheme" title="Darien scheme">Darien scheme</a>, but the eleventh Forbes laird had to be forcibly removed from the castle by a detachment of soldiers in 1718.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Corgarff_Castle" title="Corgarff Castle">Corgarff Castle</a> is in a remote spot ten miles north-west of <a href="/wiki/Ballater" title="Ballater">Ballater</a>, Aberdeenshire, and is a tall tower house.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It is four storeys high and dates from the sixteenth century.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It is the site of one of the most infamous events of the bitter feud between the Clan Forbes and Clan Gordon: <a href="/wiki/Adam_Gordon_of_Auchindoun" title="Adam Gordon of Auchindoun">Adam Gordon of Auchindoun</a> and a force ravaged through the Forbes's lands and besieged the castle that was held by twenty-six women, children and servants, the men being away.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Margaret Campbell, wife of Forbes of Towie, refused to surrender the castle and Gordon of Auchindoun lost patience when she shot one of his men in the knee.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Gordon then torched the building which went up in flames, killing everyone inside.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Culloden House at <a href="/wiki/Culloden,_Highland" title="Culloden, Highland">Culloden, Highland</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Inverness" title="Inverness">Inverness</a> mostly dates from the eighteenth century but stands on the site of a strong tower house which was formerly held by the Mackintoshes and Edmonstones, but was sold to the Forbeses in 1626.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It was the seat of <a href="/wiki/Duncan_Forbes,_Lord_Culloden" class="mw-redirect" title="Duncan Forbes, Lord Culloden">Duncan Forbes, Lord Culloden</a> who supported the British-Hanoverian Government during the <a href="/wiki/Jacobite_rising_of_1745" title="Jacobite rising of 1745">Jacobite rising of 1745</a> and was present at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Culloden" title="Battle of Culloden">Battle of Culloden</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He later protested against the terrible treatment of Jacobite prisoners after the battle.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> After the battle many wounded Jacobites had been brought to the house, but those who were not shot had their heads bashed in with musket butts.<sup id="cite_ref-Coventry_22-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coventry-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
