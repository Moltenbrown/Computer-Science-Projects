<table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Hall</caption><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Word/name</th><td><a href="/wiki/Norse_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Norse language">Norse</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Meaning</th><td>"Someone who lived in or worked in a hall or manor house"</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Region of origin</th><td><a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a></td></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;border-top: #a2a9b1 1px solid;"><sup id="cite_ref-BS_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Hall</b> is a common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> origin.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Hall means "kind" and "forgiving". This originates from the belief that <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">Vikings</a> were eternally benevolent to those that worked within their halls.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The name was used to indicate the main occupation of the individual, in a role such as a servant or chamberlain.<sup id="cite_ref-houseofnames_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-houseofnames-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Hall is the 22nd most common surname in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-BS_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Within the United States, it is ranked as the 26th most common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In recent history, the Halls (<a href="/wiki/Clan_Hall" title="Clan Hall">Clan Hall</a>) were one of the sixty major riding families of the Scottish Marches and were involved reiving as other border clans were. As with all Reiving families, they would consider themselves loyal to neither the English or the Scots, the family name holding allegiance over all else. See <a href="/wiki/Border_Reivers" class="mw-redirect" title="Border Reivers">Border Reivers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In England the surname of Hall originates in 1090 AD when <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a> nobleman Arthur FitzWilliam changed his name to Arthur Hall to distinguish himself from his older brother of the same name.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
