<p>In some provinces of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <b>Family Day</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Jour de la famille</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Canada" title="Public holidays in Canada">statutory holiday</a> occurring on the third Monday in February. In the provinces of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> (BC), <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a> it is observed as Family Day. In three other provinces, the same day is a statutory holiday but celebrated for different reasons: <b>Louis Riel Day</b> in <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a>, <b>Nova Scotia Heritage Day</b> in <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, and <b>Islander Day</b> in <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a>. Two-thirds of Canadians live in a province that observes a February statutory holiday. In the United States, <a href="/wiki/Washington%27s_Birthday" title="Washington&#39;s Birthday">Presidents Day</a> (also Washington's Birthday or other names) is also celebrated on the third Monday in February. Some provinces have changed the observance day of their holiday to match the other provinces and/or the American holiday.
</p><p>The third Monday in February is a regular working day in <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Newfoundland and Labrador</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Canada" title="Northern Canada">the territories</a>. As Family Day is not a federal statutory holiday, federal employees in all provinces (such as public servants and postal workers) work on this day. In <a href="/wiki/Yukon" title="Yukon">Yukon</a>, one Friday in February is deemed <b>Yukon Heritage Day</b>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_Day">Family Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alberta">Alberta</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Alberta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The holiday was first celebrated in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-canlii2007_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-canlii2007-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> was the only province to have a statutory holiday in February until Saskatchewan began observing it in 2007.
</p><p>The holiday was proclaimed by <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Governor_of_Alberta" title="Lieutenant Governor of Alberta">Lieutenant Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Helen_Hunley" title="Helen Hunley">Helen Hunley</a>, on the advice of her <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_Alberta" title="Premier of Alberta">premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Don_Getty" title="Don Getty">Don Getty</a>. Premier Getty said it was important for Albertans to spend time with their families, and that this holiday would emphasize the importance of family values. The date was chosen to coincide with <a href="/wiki/Washington%27s_Birthday" title="Washington&#39;s Birthday">Washington's Birthday</a>, in order to avoid disrupting trade with the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Getty faced considerable criticism at the time; many employers felt an additional statutory holiday was an unnecessary financial burden. In response, <a href="/wiki/Heritage_Day_(Alberta)" class="mw-redirect" title="Heritage Day (Alberta)">Heritage Day</a> was downgraded to a <a href="/wiki/Civic_holiday" class="mw-redirect" title="Civic holiday">civic holiday</a>, meaning employers would not be required to observe it. Under Alberta law, the employer may choose to observe Heritage Day as a general holiday, under which rules applying to general holiday pay will be used.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some Getty critics had suggested the creation of Family Day was linked to the arrest and conviction of one of Getty's sons on cocaine-related charges. Getty himself, however, has said over the years that the two events weren't related.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Saskatchewan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2006, <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_Saskatchewan" title="Premier of Saskatchewan">Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lorne_Calvert" title="Lorne Calvert">Lorne Calvert</a>, proposed the holiday for the province, starting in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-nupge_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nupge-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The bill for the Labour Standards Amendment Act, 2006, was introduced in the legislature on November 1, 2006, and received <a href="/wiki/Royal_Assent" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Assent">Royal Assent</a> on December 6.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The act officially declares Family Day on the third Monday of each February;<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> the first Family Day in Saskatchewan was February 19, 2007.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ontario">Ontario</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ontario">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> <a href="/wiki/2007_Ontario_general_election" title="2007 Ontario general election">provincial election in 2007</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dalton_McGuinty" title="Dalton McGuinty">Dalton McGuinty</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Liberal_Party" title="Ontario Liberal Party">Liberal Party</a>, promised that, if re-elected <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_Ontario" title="Premier of Ontario">premier</a>, he would establish a provincial holiday in February. On October 12, 2007, the provincial government established Family Day on the third Monday in February, to be first observed on February 18, 2008. Its creation raised Ontario's number of statutory holidays to nine per year.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> However, this holiday does not necessarily add to the number of holidays Ontarians receive because employers can substitute any non-statutory holidays that employees may already be receiving in lieu of this day. Many employers have substituted the popular Civic Holiday, which falls on the first Monday in August. Although the Civic Holiday is enjoyed by millions every year, it is not public (statutory), and workers may have to choose one holiday or the other, based on their contract, union negotiations, service requirements, etc.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Columbia">British Columbia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: British Columbia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Private_member%27s_bill" title="Private member&#39;s bill">private member's bill</a> to establish Family Day on the third Monday in February was introduced in the <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> Legislature by Liberal MLA <a href="/wiki/Bob_Chisholm" title="Bob Chisholm">Bob Chisholm</a> in 1994 but failed to pass.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Although there were renewed calls to introduce Family Day in BC between 2007 and 2011, it was opposed by the British Columbia Chamber of Commerce and the <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Campbell_(Canadian_politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Gordon Campbell (Canadian politician)">Campbell</a> government.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 10, 2011, while running for the leadership of the BC Liberal Party, <a href="/wiki/Christy_Clark" title="Christy Clark">Christy Clark</a> proposed establishing a Family Day holiday on the third Monday of February. Clark subsequently became premier; the Speech from the Throne, delivered on October 3, 2011, said that BC would observe its first Family Day on February 18, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, a two-week consultation process was held in order to determine if British Columbians preferred the holiday to fall on the second or third Monday in February.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On May 28, 2012, it was announced that Family Day would be observed on the second Monday in February each year, starting February 11, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> As this does not coincide with Presidents Day, it also provides two consecutive long weekends for tourism, particularly at BC's many ski resorts.
</p><p>On February 9, 2018, the British Columbia provincial government announced that Family Day would be moved to the 3rd Monday in February in 2019, to align their holiday with the rest of those provinces who observe it on that Monday.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Brunswick">New Brunswick</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: New Brunswick">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 5, 2010, while <a href="/wiki/2010_New_Brunswick_general_election" title="2010 New Brunswick general election">campaigning for re-election</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> Premier <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Graham" title="Shawn Graham">Shawn Graham</a> promised to establish Family Day in his province if his <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick_Liberal_Association" title="New Brunswick Liberal Association">Liberal Party</a> was returned to government,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Graham did not win re-election.
</p><p>The Liberals won <a href="/wiki/2014_New_Brunswick_general_election" title="2014 New Brunswick general election">re-election in September 2014</a>, and in February 2016 the Liberal Government started studying the implementation of Family Day.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On April 26, 2017, Premier <a href="/wiki/Brian_Gallant" title="Brian Gallant">Brian Gallant</a> announced that New Brunswick would become the newest province to observe Family Day, beginning on February 19, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_names">Other names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Other names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Louis_Riel_Day_.28Manitoba.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Louis_Riel_Day_(Manitoba)">Louis Riel Day (Manitoba)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Louis Riel Day (Manitoba)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2007, it was reported that the <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a> government was considering a February holiday. Legislation proclaiming the third Monday in February as Louis Riel Day was passed by Manitoba's Legislative Assembly on April 17, 2007, and first celebrated February 18, 2008. The day is known as Louis Riel Day, a name suggested by Manitoba school students, in honour of <a href="/wiki/Louis_Riel" title="Louis Riel">Louis Riel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9tis_people_(Canada)" class="mw-redirect" title="Métis people (Canada)">Métis</a> leader who led the fight to maintain aboriginal rights.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Islander_Day_.28Prince_Edward_Island.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Islander_Day_(Prince_Edward_Island)">Islander Day (Prince Edward Island)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Islander Day (Prince Edward Island)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The provincial government of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a> introduced Islander Day in 2009, due to the rising trend of a holiday in February. It was first held on the second Monday of February in 2009, rather than the third Monday, as in other provinces. This incongruity effected much controversy, as businesses suffered as a result of being out of sync with their partners in other provinces, as well as the United States, which celebrates Presidents Day on the third Monday of February. In April 2009, Provincial Attorney General <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Greenan" title="Gerard Greenan">Gerard Greenan</a> moved the holiday to the third Monday in February.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nova_Scotia_Heritage_Day">Nova Scotia Heritage Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Nova Scotia Heritage Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia_Liberal_Party" title="Nova Scotia Liberal Party">Nova Scotia Liberal Party</a> was elected in <a href="/wiki/2013_Nova_Scotia_general_election" title="2013 Nova Scotia general election">2013</a>, its leader <a href="/wiki/Stephen_McNeil" title="Stephen McNeil">Stephen McNeil</a> said he planned to create a February statutory holiday in <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In December 2013 the government introduced a bill to create a holiday on the third Monday in February, starting in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The permanent name for the holiday, Nova Scotia Heritage Day, was announced on June 26, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Each year it will honour a different person, the first was <a href="/wiki/Viola_Desmond" title="Viola Desmond">Viola Desmond</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The first 12, which covers 2015 – 2026, were chosen by a three-member government appointed panel from suggestions offered by Nova Scotian school children.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Other days will recognize <a href="/wiki/Mi%27kmaq" class="mw-redirect" title="Mi&#39;kmaq">Mi'kmaq</a> heritage, <a href="/wiki/Africville" title="Africville">Africville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Howe" title="Joseph Howe">Joseph Howe</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Francis_Arab&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Edward Francis Arab (page does not exist)">Edward Francis Arab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nora_Bernard" title="Nora Bernard">Nora Bernard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Best" title="Carrie Best">Carrie Best</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_William_Comeau" title="Joseph William Comeau">J. Willie Comeau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand-Pr%C3%A9_National_Historic_Site" title="Grand-Pré National Historic Site">Grand-Pré National Historic Site</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Hall_(VC)" title="William Hall (VC)">William Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rita_Joe" title="Rita Joe">Rita Joe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maud_Lewis" title="Maud Lewis">Maud Lewis</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mona_Louise_Parsons" title="Mona Louise Parsons">Mona Louise Parsons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Yukon_Heritage_Day">Yukon Heritage Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Yukon Heritage Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Yukon" title="Yukon">Yukon</a>, Yukon Heritage Day is a holiday observed on the Friday before the last Sunday of February.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Although the government and many businesses are closed on this day, it is not a statutory holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> This date is disputed by some.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">Elsewhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day_(Canada)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Governments in the remaining jurisdictions without February holidays have come under some pressure to harmonize. Ontario's enactment of Family Day has meant the Canadian financial sector, including the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Stock_Exchange" title="Toronto Stock Exchange">Toronto Stock Exchange</a>, largely shuts down on this date. In 2008, <a href="/wiki/New_Democratic_Party_of_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="New Democratic Party of Canada">federal NDP</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Jack_Layton" title="Jack Layton">Jack Layton</a> proposed that it be made a federal holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Not being a federal holiday, federally regulated workplaces (such as the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Post" title="Canada Post">post office</a>) work on Family Day regardless of the day's status in the respective provinces.
</p>
