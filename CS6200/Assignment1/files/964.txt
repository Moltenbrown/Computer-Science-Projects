<p><b>Johnson</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> of <a href="/wiki/English_name" title="English name">English</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> , <a href="/wiki/Scottish_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish name">Scottish</a> origin.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The name itself is a <a href="/wiki/Patronym" class="mw-redirect" title="Patronym">patronym</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> <i><a href="/wiki/John_(first_name)" class="mw-redirect" title="John (first name)">John</a></i>, literally meaning "son of John". The name <i>John</i> derives from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Johannes" title="Johannes">Johannes</a></i>, which is derived through <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <span lang="grc" title="Ancient Greek language text">Ἰωάννης</span> <i>Iōannēs</i> from <a href="/wiki/Hebrew" class="mw-redirect" title="Hebrew">Hebrew</a> <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886049626">.mw-parser-output .script-hebrew,.mw-parser-output .script-Hebr{font-size:1.15em;font-family:"Ezra SIL","Ezra SIL SR","Keter Aram Tsova","Taamey Ashkenaz","Taamey David CLM","Taamey Frank CLM","Frank Ruehl CLM","Keter YG","Shofar","David CLM","Hadasim CLM","Simple CLM","Nachlieli","SBL BibLit","SBL Hebrew",Cardo,Alef,"Noto Serif Hebrew","Noto Sans Hebrew","David Libre",David,"Times New Roman",Gisha,Arial,FreeSerif,FreeSans}</style><span class="script-hebrew" style="font-size: 115%;" dir="rtl">יוחנן</span> <i><a href="/wiki/Yohanan" title="Yohanan">Yohanan</a></i>, meaning "Yahweh has favoured". The name has been extremely popular in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> since the <a href="/wiki/Christian_era" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian era">Christian era</a> as a result of it being given to <a href="/wiki/St_John_the_Baptist" class="mw-redirect" title="St John the Baptist">St John the Baptist</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_John_the_Evangelist" class="mw-redirect" title="St John the Evangelist">St John the Evangelist</a> and nearly one thousand other <a href="/wiki/Christian_saints" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian saints">Christian saints</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Johnson is the <sup id="cite_ref-ANR_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ANR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> second most common in the United States<sup id="cite_ref-ANR_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ANR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and 154th most common in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-ANR_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ANR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As a Scottish family name, Johnson is occasionally a variation of <i>Johnston</i>, a habitational name.
</p>
