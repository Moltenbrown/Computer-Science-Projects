<p><b>Nîmes</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span><span title="/iː/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">iː</span><span title="&#39;m&#39; in &#39;my&#39;">m</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">NEEM</span></i></a>, <small>French:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[nim]</a></span>&#32;<span class="nowrap" style="font-size:85%">(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fr-N%C3%AEmes.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/89/Fr-N%C3%AEmes.ogg" class="internal" title="Fr-Nîmes.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</span>; <a href="/wiki/Proven%C3%A7al_dialect" title="Provençal dialect">Provençal</a>: <i lang="oc">Nimes</i> <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Occitan" title="Help:IPA/Occitan">[ˈnimes]</a></span>) is a city in the <a href="/wiki/Occitanie" title="Occitanie">Occitanie</a> <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_France" title="Regions of France">region</a> of southern France. It is the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Gard" title="Gard">Gard</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a>. Nîmes is located between the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9vennes" title="Cévennes">Cévennes</a> mountains. The estimated population of Nîmes is 151,001 (2016).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dubbed the most Roman city outside Italy,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Nîmes has a rich history dating back to the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> when the city was a regional capital, and home to 50,000–60,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-Sear1983_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sear1983-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RingWatson2013_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RingWatson2013-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MobileReference2007_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MobileReference2007-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Several famous monuments are in Nîmes, such as the <a href="/wiki/Arena_of_N%C3%AEmes" title="Arena of Nîmes">Arena of Nîmes</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Maison_Carr%C3%A9e" title="Maison Carrée">Maison Carrée</a>. Because of this, Nîmes is often referred to as the French Rome.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_N%C3%AEmes" title="Timeline of Nîmes">Timeline of Nîmes</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes.jpg/220px-2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="116" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes.jpg/330px-2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes.jpg/440px-2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2900" data-file-height="1525" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Arena_of_N%C3%AEmes" title="Arena of Nîmes">Arena of Nîmes</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The city derives its name from that of a spring in the Roman village. The contemporary coat of arms of the city of Nîmes includes a crocodile chained to a palm tree with the inscription <small>COL NEM</small>, for <i>Colonia Nemausus</i>, meaning the "colony" or "settlement" of <a href="/wiki/Nemausus" title="Nemausus">Nemausus</a>, the local Celtic god of the <a href="/wiki/Volcae_Arecomici" class="mw-redirect" title="Volcae Arecomici">Volcae Arecomici</a>. Veterans of the Roman legions who had served Julius Caesar in his Nile campaigns, at the end of fifteen years of soldiering, were given plots of land to cultivate on the plain of Nîmes.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The city was located on the <a href="/wiki/Via_Domitia" title="Via Domitia">Via Domitia</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Roman_road" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman road">Roman road</a> constructed in 118 BC which connected <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> with <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.
</p><p>Its name appears in inscriptions in <a href="/wiki/Gaulish" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaulish">Gaulish</a> as <i>dede matrebo Namausikabo</i> = "he has given to the mothers of Nîmes" and "<i>toutios Namausatis</i>" = "citizen of Nîmes".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prehistory">Prehistory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Prehistory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The site on which the built-up area of Nîmes has become established in the course of centuries is part of the edge of the alluvial plain of the Vistrenque River which butts up against low hills: to the northeast, Mont Duplan; to the southwest, Montaury; to the west, Mt. Cavalier and the knoll of Canteduc.
</p>
<h3><span id="4000.E2.80.932000_BC"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="4000–2000_BC">4000–2000 BC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 4000–2000 BC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Neolithic site of Serre Paradis reveals the presence of semi-nomadic cultivators in the period 4000 to 3500 BC on the future site of Nîmes. The population of the site increased during the thousand-year period of the Bronze Age.
The <a href="/wiki/Menhir" title="Menhir">menhir</a> of Courbessac (or La Poudrière) stands in a field, near the airstrip. This limestone monolith of over two metres in height dates to about 2500 BC, and must be considered the oldest monument of Nîmes.
</p>
<h3><span id="1800.E2.80.93600_BC"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1800–600_BC">1800–600 BC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1800–600 BC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Bronze Age has left traces of villages that were made out of huts and branches
</p>
<h3><span id="600.E2.80.9349_BC"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="600–49_BC">600–49 BC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 600–49 BC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Warrior of Grezan is considered to be the most ancient indigenous sculpture in southern Gaul.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The hill named Mt. Cavalier was the site of the early <a href="/wiki/Oppidum" title="Oppidum">oppidum</a>, which gave birth to the city. During the third and 2nd centuries BC a surrounding wall was built, closed at the summit by a dry-stone tower, which was later incorporated into the masonry of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tour_Magne&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tour Magne (page does not exist)">Tour Magne</a>. The Wars of Gaul and the fall of <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">Marseille</a> (49 BC) allowed Nîmes to regain its autonomy under Rome.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_period">Roman period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Roman period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Nemausus" title="Nemausus">Nemausus</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pont_du_Gard_BLS.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Pont_du_Gard_BLS.jpg/220px-Pont_du_Gard_BLS.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="85" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Pont_du_Gard_BLS.jpg/330px-Pont_du_Gard_BLS.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Pont_du_Gard_BLS.jpg/440px-Pont_du_Gard_BLS.jpg 2x" data-file-width="12648" data-file-height="4882" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pont_du_Gard_BLS.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pont du Gard from the south bank</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes-Interior.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes-Interior.JPG/220px-2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes-Interior.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="116" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes-Interior.JPG/330px-2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes-Interior.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes-Interior.JPG/440px-2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes-Interior.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2900" data-file-height="1525" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2015-Arena-of-N%C3%AEmes-Interior.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Arena Interior.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2015-Maison-Carr%C3%A9e-N%C3%AEmes.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/2015-Maison-Carr%C3%A9e-N%C3%AEmes.JPG/220px-2015-Maison-Carr%C3%A9e-N%C3%AEmes.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="116" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/2015-Maison-Carr%C3%A9e-N%C3%AEmes.JPG/330px-2015-Maison-Carr%C3%A9e-N%C3%AEmes.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/2015-Maison-Carr%C3%A9e-N%C3%AEmes.JPG/440px-2015-Maison-Carr%C3%A9e-N%C3%AEmes.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2900" data-file-height="1525" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2015-Maison-Carr%C3%A9e-N%C3%AEmes.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Maison Carrée.</div></div></div>
<p>Nîmes became a Roman colony sometime before 28 BC, as witnessed by the earliest coins, which bear the abbreviation <small>NEM. COL</small>, "Colony of Nemausus".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Some years later a sanctuary and other constructions connected with the fountain were raised on the site. Nîmes was already under Roman influence, though it was Augustus who made the city the capital of <a href="/wiki/Narbonne" title="Narbonne">Narbonne</a> province, and gave it all its glory. It was also known as the birthplace of the family of <a href="/wiki/Roman_Emperor" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Emperor">Roman Emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Antoninus_Pius" title="Antoninus Pius">Antoninus Pius</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Arena_de_Nimes.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Nîmes arena is popularly used for concerts and also bull related activities" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Arena_de_Nimes.jpg/220px-Arena_de_Nimes.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="123" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Arena_de_Nimes.jpg/330px-Arena_de_Nimes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Arena_de_Nimes.jpg/440px-Arena_de_Nimes.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="672" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Arena_de_Nimes.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The arena is popularly used as a venue for concerts and also bull related activities</div></div></div>
<p>The city had an estimated population of 60,000 in the time of <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Augustus</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Augustus gave the town a ring of ramparts six kilometres (3.7 miles) long, reinforced by fourteen towers; two gates remain today: the Porta Augusta and the Porte de France. An <a href="/wiki/Roman_aqueduct" title="Roman aqueduct">aqueduct</a> was built to bring water from the hills to the north. Where this crossed the <a href="/wiki/River_Gard" class="mw-redirect" title="River Gard">River Gard</a> between <a href="/wiki/Uzes" class="mw-redirect" title="Uzes">Uzes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Remoulins" title="Remoulins">Remoulins</a>, the spectacular <a href="/wiki/Pont_du_Gard" title="Pont du Gard">Pont du Gard</a> was built. This is 20 kilometres (12 miles) north east of the city.  Also, the <a href="/wiki/Maison_Carr%C3%A9e" title="Maison Carrée">Maison Carrée</a> is one of the best preserved temples to be found anywhere in the territory of the former Roman Empire; it later inspired the design of the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_State_Capitol" title="Virginia State Capitol">Virginia State Capitol</a> at Richmond.  Nothing remains of some other monuments, the existence of which is known from inscriptions or architectural fragments found in the course of excavations. It is known that the town had a civil basilica, a curia, a gymnasium and perhaps a circus. <a href="/wiki/Arena_of_N%C3%AEmes" title="Arena of Nîmes">The amphitheatre</a> dates from the end of the 2nd century AD and was one of the largest amphitheatres in the Empire.
</p>

<p>Emperor Constantine endowed the city with baths. It became the seat of the Diocesan Vicar, the chief administrative officer of southern Gaul.
</p><p>The town was prosperous until the end of the 3rd century – during the 4th and 5th centuries, the nearby town of <a href="/wiki/Arles" title="Arles">Arles</a> enjoyed more prosperity. In the early 5th century the Praetorian Prefecture was moved from <a href="/wiki/Trier" title="Trier">Trier</a> in northeast Gaul to Arles. The <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a> finally captured the city from the Romans in 473 AD.
</p>
<h3><span id="4th.E2.80.9313th_centuries"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="4th–13th_centuries">4th–13th centuries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 4th–13th centuries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nimes_1569.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Nimes_1569.jpg/220px-Nimes_1569.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="160" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Nimes_1569.jpg/330px-Nimes_1569.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Nimes_1569.jpg/440px-Nimes_1569.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6735" data-file-height="4885" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nimes_1569.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Nemausus, Nismes Civitas Narbonensis</i> surrounded by its walls, after Sebastian Minister (1569), 1572.</div></div></div>
<p>After the Roman period, in the days of invasion and decadence, the Christian Church, already established in Gaul since the 1st century AD, appeared to be the last refuge of classical civilization – it was remarkably organized and directed by a series of Gallo-Roman aristocrats. However, when the Visigoths were accepted into the Roman Empire, Nîmes was included in their territory (472), even after the Frankish victory at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Vouill%C3%A9" title="Battle of Vouillé">Battle of Vouillé</a> (507). The urban landscape went through transformation with the Goths, but much of the heritage of the Roman era remained largely intact.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Temple_de_Diane_N%C3%AEmes.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Temple_de_Diane_N%C3%AEmes.JPG/220px-Temple_de_Diane_N%C3%AEmes.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Temple_de_Diane_N%C3%AEmes.JPG/330px-Temple_de_Diane_N%C3%AEmes.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Temple_de_Diane_N%C3%AEmes.JPG/440px-Temple_de_Diane_N%C3%AEmes.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="960" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Temple_de_Diane_N%C3%AEmes.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Temple of Diana</div></div></div><p>By 725, the Muslim <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyads</a> had conquered the whole Visigothic territory of <a href="/wiki/Septimania" title="Septimania">Septimania</a> including Nîmes. In 736-737, Charles Martel and his brother led an expedition to Septimania and Provence, and largely destroyed the city (in the hands of Umayyads allied with the local Gallo-Roman and Gothic nobility), including the amphitheatre, thereafter heading back north. The Muslim government came to an end in 752, when <a href="/wiki/Pepin_the_Short" title="Pepin the Short">Pepin the Short</a> captured the city. In 754, an uprising took place against the Carolingian king, but was put down, and count Radulf, a Frank, appointed as master of the city. After the events connected with the war, Nîmes was now only a shadow of the opulent <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> city it had once been. The local authorities installed themselves in the remains of <a href="/wiki/Arena_of_N%C3%AEmes" title="Arena of Nîmes">the amphitheatre</a>.
Carolingian rule brought relative peace, but feudal times in the 12th century brought local troubles, which lasted until the days of <a href="/wiki/Louis_IX_of_France" title="Louis IX of France">St. Louis</a>. During that period Nîmes was jointly administered by a lay power resident in the old amphitheatre, where lived the Viguier and the Knights of the Arena, and the religious power based in the Bishop's palace complex, around the cathedral, its chapter and the Bishop's house; meanwhile the city was represented by four Consuls, who sat in the Maison Carrée.</p><div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hubert_Robert_-_Ruins_at_Nimes_-_WGA19596.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Hubert_Robert_-_Ruins_at_Nimes_-_WGA19596.jpg/220px-Hubert_Robert_-_Ruins_at_Nimes_-_WGA19596.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Hubert_Robert_-_Ruins_at_Nimes_-_WGA19596.jpg/330px-Hubert_Robert_-_Ruins_at_Nimes_-_WGA19596.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Hubert_Robert_-_Ruins_at_Nimes_-_WGA19596.jpg/440px-Hubert_Robert_-_Ruins_at_Nimes_-_WGA19596.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1175" data-file-height="770" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hubert_Robert_-_Ruins_at_Nimes_-_WGA19596.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ruins at Nîmes, painting by <a href="/wiki/Hubert_Robert" title="Hubert Robert">Hubert Robert</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Despite incessant feudal squabbling, Nîmes saw some progress both in commerce and industry as well as in stock-breeding and associated activities.
</p><p>After the last effort by <a href="/wiki/Raymond_VII_of_Toulouse" class="mw-redirect" title="Raymond VII of Toulouse">Raymond VII of Toulouse</a>, St. Louis managed to establish royal power in the region which became <a href="/wiki/Languedoc" title="Languedoc">Languedoc</a>. Nîmes thus finally came into the hands of the King of France.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Period_of_invasions">Period of invasions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Period of invasions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:N%C3%AEmes_La_porte_Auguste.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/N%C3%AEmes_La_porte_Auguste.png/220px-N%C3%AEmes_La_porte_Auguste.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/N%C3%AEmes_La_porte_Auguste.png/330px-N%C3%AEmes_La_porte_Auguste.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/N%C3%AEmes_La_porte_Auguste.png/440px-N%C3%AEmes_La_porte_Auguste.png 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:N%C3%AEmes_La_porte_Auguste.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Augustan Gate</div></div></div>
<p>During the 14th and 15th centuries the Rhone Valley underwent an uninterrupted series of invasions which ruined the economy and caused famine. Customs were forgotten, religious troubles developed (<i>see <a href="/wiki/French_Wars_of_Religion" title="French Wars of Religion">French Wars of Religion</a></i>) and epidemics, all of which affected the city. Nîmes, which was one of the Protestant strongholds, felt the full force of repression and fratricidal confrontations (including the <i><a href="/wiki/Michelade" title="Michelade">Michelade</a></i> massacre) which continued until the middle of the 17th century, adding to the misery of periodic outbreaks of plague.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="17th_century_to_the_French_Revolution">17th century to the French Revolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 17th century to the French Revolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the middle of the 17th century Nîmes experienced a period of prosperity. Population growth caused the town to expand, and slum housing to be replaced. To this period also belong the reconstruction of Notre-Dame-Saint-Castor, the Bishop's palace and numerous mansions (hôtels). This renaissance strengthened the manufacturing and industrial potential of the city, the population rising from 21,000 to 50,000 inhabitants.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Les_Quais_de_la_Fontaine.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Les_Quais_de_la_Fontaine.jpg/220px-Les_Quais_de_la_Fontaine.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Les_Quais_de_la_Fontaine.jpg/330px-Les_Quais_de_la_Fontaine.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Les_Quais_de_la_Fontaine.jpg/440px-Les_Quais_de_la_Fontaine.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2272" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Les_Quais_de_la_Fontaine.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Les Quais de la Fontaine, the embankments of the spring that provided water for the city, the first civic gardens of France, were laid out in 1738–55.</div></div></div>
<p>In this same period the Fountain gardens, the <i>Quais de la Fontaine</i>, were laid out, the areas surrounding the Maison Carrée and <a href="/wiki/Arena_of_N%C3%AEmes" title="Arena of Nîmes">the Amphitheatre</a> were cleared of encroachments, whilst the entire population benefited from the atmosphere of prosperity.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_the_French_Revolution_to_the_present">From the French Revolution to the present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: From the French Revolution to the present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following a European economic crisis that hit Nîmes with full force, the <a href="/wiki/French_revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="French revolution">Revolutionary period</a> awoke the slumbering demons of political and religious antagonism. The <a href="/wiki/Second_White_Terror" title="Second White Terror">White Terror</a> added to natural calamities and economic recession, produced murder, pillage and arson until 1815. Order was however restored in the course of the century, and Nîmes became the metropolis of Bas-Languedoc, diversifying its industry into new kinds of activity. At the same time the surrounding countryside adapted to market needs and shared in the general increase of wealth.
</p><p>During the Second World War, the Maquis resistance fighters <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.midi-france.info/1016_ww2.htm">Jean Robert and Vinicio Faïta were executed</a> at Nîmes on 22 April 1943. The Nîmes marshalling yards were <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20090324043355/http://www.zplace2b.com/464th/sortie.htm">bombed by American bombers</a> in 1944.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/2nd_Foreign_Infantry_Regiment" title="2nd Foreign Infantry Regiment">2º Régiment Étranger d'Infanterie (2ºREI)</a>, the main motorised infantry regiment of the French Foreign Legion, has been garrisoned in Nîmes since November 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nîmes is one of the warmest cities in France. The city has a <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a>: <i>Csa</i>), being wetter than a typical Mediterranean climate, especially for its moderately rainy summers. Its slightly inland, southerly location results in hot air over the city during summer months, temperatures above 34&#160;ºC are common in July and August, whereas winters are cool but not cold. Night temps under 0&#160;ºC are common from December to February, while snowfall occurs every year.
</p>

<p>Several important remains of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> can still be seen in and around Nîmes:
</p>
<ul><li>The elliptical <a href="/wiki/Arena_of_N%C3%AEmes" title="Arena of Nîmes">Roman amphitheatre</a>, of the 1st or 2nd century AD, is the best-preserved Roman arena in France. It was filled with medieval housing, when its walls served as <a href="/wiki/Defensive_wall" title="Defensive wall">ramparts</a>, but they were cleared under <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon</a>. It is still used as a <a href="/wiki/Bull_fighting" class="mw-redirect" title="Bull fighting">bull fighting</a> and concert arena.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Maison_Carr%C3%A9e" title="Maison Carrée">Maison Carrée</a> (<i>Square House</i>), a small <a href="/wiki/Roman_temple" title="Roman temple">Roman temple</a> dedicated to sons of <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Vipsanius_Agrippa" title="Marcus Vipsanius Agrippa">Agrippa</a> was built c. 19 BC. It is one of the best-preserved Roman temples anywhere. Visitors can watch a short film about the history of Nîmes inside.</li>
<li>The 18th-century <i>Jardins de la Fontaine</i> (Gardens of the Fountain) built around the Roman <a href="/wiki/Thermae" title="Thermae">thermae</a> ruins.</li>
<li>The nearby <a href="/wiki/Pont_du_Gard" title="Pont du Gard">Pont du Gard</a>, also built by Agrippa, is a well-preserved <a href="/wiki/Aqueduct_(bridge)" title="Aqueduct (bridge)">aqueduct</a> that used to carry water across the small <a href="/wiki/Gardon" title="Gardon">Gardon</a> river valley.</li>
<li>The nearby Mont Cavalier is crowned by the <i>Tour Magne</i> ("Great Tower"), a ruined Roman tower.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>Later monuments include:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/N%C3%AEmes_Cathedral" title="Nîmes Cathedral">The cathedral</a> (dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Saint_Castor_of_Apt" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Castor of Apt">Saint Castor of Apt</a>, a native of the city), occupying, it is believed, the site of the temple of Augustus, is partly <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> and partly <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a> in style.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9e_des_Beaux-Arts_de_N%C3%AEmes" title="Musée des Beaux-Arts de Nîmes">Musée des Beaux-Arts de Nîmes</a></li></ul>
<p>There is modern architecture at Nîmes too: <a href="/wiki/Norman_Foster,_Baron_Foster_of_Thames_Bank" title="Norman Foster, Baron Foster of Thames Bank">Norman Foster</a> conceived the  <i>Carré d'art</i> (1986), a museum of modern art and <i>mediatheque</i>, and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Nouvel" title="Jean Nouvel">Jean Nouvel</a> designed the Nemausus, a <a href="/wiki/Post-modern" class="mw-redirect" title="Post-modern">post-modern</a> residential ensemble.
</p><p>Tree-shaded boulevards trace the foundations of its former city walls.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy_and_infrastructure">Economy and infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Economy and infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nîmes is historically known for its textiles. <a href="/wiki/Denim" title="Denim">Denim</a>, the fabric of blue jeans, derives its name from this city (<i><a href="/wiki/Serge_(fabric)" title="Serge (fabric)">Serge</a> de Nîmes)</i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1810 to 1822, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Gergonne" class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph Gergonne">Joseph Gergonne</a> published a <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a> specializing in <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> from Nîmes called <i><a href="/wiki/Annales_de_Gergonne" title="Annales de Gergonne">Annales de Gergonne</a></i>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">asteroid</a> <a href="/wiki/51_Nemausa" title="51 Nemausa">51 Nemausa</a> was named after Nîmes, where it was discovered in 1858.
</p><p>Two times per year, Nîmes hosts one of the main French <a href="/wiki/Bullfighting" title="Bullfighting">bullfighting</a> events, <a href="/wiki/Feria_de_N%C3%AEmes" title="Feria de Nîmes">Feria de Nîmes</a> (festival), and several hundreds of thousands gather in the streets.
</p><p>In 2005 <a href="/wiki/Rammstein" title="Rammstein">Rammstein</a> filmed their #1 live Album <a href="/wiki/V%C3%B6lkerball" title="Völkerball">Völkerball</a> in Nîmes, and are returning in 2017.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>'s live DVD <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ais_Pour_une_Nuit" title="Français Pour une Nuit">Français Pour une Nuit</a> (English: French for One Night) was recorded in Nîmes, France, in the <a href="/wiki/Arena_of_N%C3%AEmes" title="Arena of Nîmes">Arena of Nîmes</a> on July 7, 2009, during the World Magnetic Tour.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/N%C3%AEmes-Al%C3%A8s-Camargue-C%C3%A9vennes_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Nîmes-Alès-Camargue-Cévennes Airport">Nîmes-Alès-Camargue-Cévennes Airport</a> serves the city. The <a href="/wiki/Gare_de_N%C3%AEmes" title="Gare de Nîmes">Gare de Nîmes</a> is the central railway station, offering connections to Paris (high-speed rail), Marseille, Montpellier, Narbonne, Toulouse, Perpignan, <a href="/wiki/Figueras" class="mw-redirect" title="Figueras">Figueras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> in Spain and several regional destinations. The motorway <a href="/wiki/A9_autoroute" title="A9 autoroute">A9</a> connects Nîmes with Orange, Montpellier, Narbonne, and Perpignan, the <a href="/wiki/A54_autoroute" title="A54 autoroute">A54</a> with Arles and Salon-de-Provence.
</p><p>Work is almost complete on the construction of a high-speed <a href="/wiki/TGV" title="TGV">TGV</a> line, <a href="/wiki/Contournement_N%C3%AEmes_%E2%80%93_Montpellier" title="Contournement Nîmes – Montpellier">Contournement Nîmes – Montpellier</a> bypassing Nîmes and <a href="/wiki/Montpellier" title="Montpellier">Montpellier</a> with the <a href="/wiki/LGV_M%C3%A9diterran%C3%A9e" title="LGV Méditerranée">LGV Méditerranée</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The new line is scheduled to open to passenger service on 15 December 2019 together with a new TGV station at NIMES-PONT DU GARD,(a potentially confusing name as it located some 12km from the city itself and 20km from the Pont du Gard. <a class="external autonumber" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gare_de_N%C3%AEmes_-_Pont_du_Gard">[1]</a>
</p><p>A new station is also to be opened at the same time on the existing route between Nìmes and Avignon, thus providing connections between the new line and local rail service. 
</p><p>Nîmes bus station is adjacent to the city centre railway station. Buses connect the city with nearby towns and villages not served by rail. <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://www.laregion.fr/transports-gard-regulier">https://www.laregion.fr/transports-gard-regulier</a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%AEmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> club <a href="/wiki/N%C3%AEmes_Olympique" title="Nîmes Olympique">Nîmes Olympique</a> who has recently achieved promotion to <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> is based in Nîmes.
World Archery Indoor World Cup takes place in Nimes each ear in mid January
The local rugby union team is <a href="/wiki/RC_N%C3%AEmes" title="RC Nîmes">RC Nîmes</a>.
</p><p>There is a professional volleyball team located here.
</p><p>Olympic swimming champion <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Agnel" title="Yannick Agnel">Yannick Agnel</a> was born in Nîmes.
</p><p>The city hosted the opening stages of the <a href="/wiki/2017_Vuelta_a_Espa%C3%B1a" title="2017 Vuelta a España">2017 Vuelta a España</a> cycling race.
</p>
