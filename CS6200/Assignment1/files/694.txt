	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Capoeira</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Apelido&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Apelido">Apelido</a>)</span></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Afro-Brazilian martial art that combines elements of dance kicks and music</div>
<p><b>Capoeira</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">[kapuˈejɾɐ] <small>or</small> [kaˈpwɐjɾɐ]</a></span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Afro-Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="Afro-Brazilian">Afro-Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Martial_art" class="mw-redirect" title="Martial art">martial art</a> that combines elements of <a href="/wiki/Dance" title="Dance">dance</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Roots_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roots-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Acrobatics" title="Acrobatics">acrobatics</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It was developed by enslaved Africans in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a><sup id="cite_ref-Palmares_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Palmares-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> at the beginning of the 16th century.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It is known for its acrobatic and complex maneuvers, often involving hands on the ground and inverted kicks. Emphasizes flowing movements rather than fixed stances, the <i>ginga</i>, a rocking step, is usually the focal point of the technique. The most widely accepted origin of the word <i>capoeira</i> comes from the <a href="/wiki/Tupi_language" title="Tupi language">Tupi</a> words <i>ka'a</i> ("forest") <i>paũ</i> ("round"),<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> referring to the areas of low vegetation in the Brazilian interior where fugitive slaves would hide. A practitioner of the art is called a <b>capoeirista</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">[kapuejˈɾistɐ]</a></span>).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 26 November 2014, capoeira was granted a special protected status as <a href="/wiki/Intangible_cultural_heritage" title="Intangible cultural heritage">intangible cultural heritage</a> by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fighting technique originates primarily from <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>. Since the 16th century, the Portuguese bought, sold, traded, and transported African peoples. The majority of enslaved Africans were transported from the area currently known as <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>. Brazil, with its vast territory, received almost 40% of these African people via the Atlanic slave trade. The early history of capoeira is recorded by historians such as Dr. Desch-Obi. Originally, the ancestor tradition originated from Angola and was called N'golo/Engolo; a type of ritual dance that used several elements of kicking, headbutting, slap boxing, walking on one's hands, deception, evasion etc. The purpose was also religious as it both provided a link to the afterlife (which was the opposite of the living world) and enabled a person to channel their ancestors into their dance. For example, during the dance, a person might become possessed by an ancestor in the past who was talented at N'golo. This could be applied to a martial setting in both combat and warfare which was called N'singa/ensinga; the difference to N'golo being that it included weapon use and grappling. During the Atlantic slave trade, this tradition transferred around the Americas; Brazil (capoeira), the Caribbean (Damnye) and the United States (knocking and kicking). A large number of Angolans were sent to Madagascar during the slave trade and it still survives today as <a href="/wiki/Moraingy" title="Moraingy">Moringue</a>/ Moring.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CapoeiraEarle.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/CapoeiraEarle.JPG/220px-CapoeiraEarle.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/CapoeiraEarle.JPG/330px-CapoeiraEarle.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/CapoeiraEarle.JPG/440px-CapoeiraEarle.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="796" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CapoeiraEarle.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Painting of fighting in Brazil c. 1824 by <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Earle" title="Augustus Earle">Augustus Earle</a></div></div></div>
<p>In the 16th century, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> had claimed one of the largest territories of the <a href="/wiki/Colonial_empires" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonial empires">colonial empires</a>, but lacked people to colonize it, especially workers. In the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> colony, the Portuguese, like many European colonists, chose to use <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> to build their economy.
</p><p>In its first century, the main economic activity in the colony was the production and processing of <a href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar cane">sugar cane</a>. Portuguese colonists created large sugarcane farms called "<a href="/wiki/Engenho" title="Engenho">engenhos</a>", literally "engines" (of economic activity), which depended on the labor of slaves. Slaves, living in inhumane conditions, were forced to work hard and often suffered physical punishment for small misbehaviors.<sup id="cite_ref-inicio_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inicio-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although slaves often outnumbered colonists, rebellions were rare because of the lack of weapons, harsh colonial law, disagreement between slaves coming from different African cultures, and lack of knowledge about the new land and its surroundings.
</p><p>Capoeira originated within as a product of the Angolan tradition of "<a href="/wiki/Engolo" title="Engolo">Engolo</a>" but became applied as a method of survival that was known to slaves. It was a tool with which an escaped slave, completely unequipped, could survive in the hostile, unknown land and face the hunt of the <i>capitães-do-mato</i>, the armed and mounted colonial agents who were charged with finding and capturing escapees.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>As Brazil became more urbanised in the 17th and 18th century, the nature of capoeira stayed largely the same, however compared to the United States, the nature of slavery differed. Since many slaves worked in the cities and were most of the time outside the master's supervision, they would be tasked with finding work to do (in the form of any manual labour) and in return they would pay the master any money they made. It is here where capoeira was common as it created opportunities for slaves to practice during and after work. Though tolerated until the 1800s, this quickly became criminalised after due to its association with being African, as well as a threat to the current ruling regime.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quilombos">Quilombos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Quilombos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ant%C3%B4nio_Parreiras_-_Zumbi.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Ant%C3%B4nio_Parreiras_-_Zumbi.jpg/220px-Ant%C3%B4nio_Parreiras_-_Zumbi.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="296" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Ant%C3%B4nio_Parreiras_-_Zumbi.jpg/330px-Ant%C3%B4nio_Parreiras_-_Zumbi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Ant%C3%B4nio_Parreiras_-_Zumbi.jpg/440px-Ant%C3%B4nio_Parreiras_-_Zumbi.jpg 2x" data-file-width="713" data-file-height="960" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ant%C3%B4nio_Parreiras_-_Zumbi.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Zumbi" title="Zumbi">Zumbi</a> (1927) by <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Parreiras" title="Antônio Parreiras">Antônio Parreiras</a></div></div></div>
<p>Soon several groups of enslaved persons who liberated themselves gathered and established settlements, known as <a href="/wiki/Quilombo" title="Quilombo">quilombos</a>,  in far and hard to reach places. Some quilombos would soon increase in size, attracting more fugitive slaves, Brazilian natives and even Europeans escaping the law or Christian extremism. Some quilombos would grow to an enormous size, becoming a real independent multi-ethnic state.<sup id="cite_ref-mocambos_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mocambos-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Everyday life in a quilombo offered freedom and the opportunity to revive traditional cultures away from colonial oppression.<sup id="cite_ref-mocambos_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mocambos-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In this kind of multi-ethnic community, constantly threatened by Portuguese colonial troops, capoeira evolved from a survival tool to a martial art focused on war.
</p><p>The biggest quilombo, the <a href="/wiki/Palmares_(quilombo)" title="Palmares (quilombo)">Quilombo dos Palmares</a>, consisted of many villages which lasted more than a century, resisting at least 24 small attacks and 18 colonial invasions. Portuguese soldiers sometimes said that it took more than one <a href="/wiki/Dragoon" title="Dragoon">dragoon</a> to capture a quilombo warrior since they would defend themselves with a <i>strangely moving fighting technique</i>. The provincial governor declared "it is harder to defeat a quilombo than the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> invaders."<sup id="cite_ref-mocambos_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mocambos-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Urbanization">Urbanization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Urbanization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1808, the prince and future king <a href="/wiki/John_VI_of_Portugal" title="John VI of Portugal">Dom João VI</a>, along with the Portuguese court, escaped to Brazil from the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">invasion of Portugal</a> by <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>'s troops. Formerly exploited only for its natural resources and commodity crops, the colony finally began to develop as a nation.<sup id="cite_ref-gomes_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gomes-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The Portuguese monopoly effectively came to an end when Brazilian ports opened for trade with friendly foreign nations.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Those cities grew in importance and Brazilians got permission to manufacture common products once required to be imported from Portugal, such as glass.<sup id="cite_ref-gomes_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gomes-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Registries of capoeira practices existed since the 18th century in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvador,_Bahia" title="Salvador, Bahia">Salvador</a> and <a href="/wiki/Recife" title="Recife">Recife</a>. Due to city growth, more slaves were brought to cities and the increase in <a href="/wiki/Social_relation" title="Social relation">social life</a> in the cities made capoeira more prominent and allowed it to be taught and practiced among more people. Because capoeira was often used against the colonial guard, in Rio the colonial government tried to suppress it and established severe physical punishments to its practice such as hunting down practitioners and killing them openly.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ample data from police records from the 1800s shows that many slaves and free colored people were detained for practicing capoeira:
</p>
<blockquote><p>"From 288 slaves that entered the Calabouço jail during the years 1857 and 1858, 80 (31%) were arrested for capoeira, and only 28 (10.7%) for running away.  Out of 4,303 arrests in Rio police jail in 1862, 404 detainees—nearly 10%—had been arrested for capoeira."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="End_of_slavery_and_prohibition_of_capoeira">End of slavery and prohibition of capoeira</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: End of slavery and prohibition of capoeira">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lei_%C3%81urea_(Golden_Law).tif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Lei_%C3%81urea_%28Golden_Law%29.tif/lossy-page1-220px-Lei_%C3%81urea_%28Golden_Law%29.tif.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="289" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Lei_%C3%81urea_%28Golden_Law%29.tif/lossy-page1-330px-Lei_%C3%81urea_%28Golden_Law%29.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Lei_%C3%81urea_%28Golden_Law%29.tif/lossy-page1-440px-Lei_%C3%81urea_%28Golden_Law%29.tif.jpg 2x" data-file-width="8499" data-file-height="11161" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lei_%C3%81urea_(Golden_Law).tif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Golden Law, 1888.</div></div></div>
<p>By the end of the 19th <a href="/wiki/Century" title="Century">century</a>, slavery was on the verge of departing the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Brazil" title="Empire of Brazil">Brazilian Empire</a>. Reasons included growing quilombo militia raids in plantations that still used slaves, the refusal of the Brazilian army to deal with escapees and the growth of Brazilian abolitionist movements. The Empire tried to soften the problems with laws to restrict slavery, but finally Brazil would recognize the end of the institution on May 13, 1888, with a law called <i>Lei Áurea</i> (Golden Law), sanctioned by imperial parliament and signed by <a href="/wiki/Isabel,_Princess_Imperial_of_Brazil" title="Isabel, Princess Imperial of Brazil">Princess Isabel</a>.
</p><p>However, free former slaves now felt abandoned. Most of them had nowhere to live, no jobs and were despised by Brazilian society, which usually viewed them as lazy workers.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cardoso_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cardoso-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Also, new immigration from Europe and Asia left most former slaves with no employment.<sup id="cite_ref-cardoso_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cardoso-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-favela_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-favela-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Soon capoeiristas started to use their skills in unconventional ways. Criminals and war lords used capoeiristas as body guards and hitmen. Groups of capoeiristas, known as <i>maltas</i>, raided Rio de Janeiro. The two main <i>maltas</i> were the <i>Nagoas</i>, composed of <a href="/wiki/African_slaves" class="mw-redirect" title="African slaves">Africans</a>, and the <i>Guaiamuns</i>, composed of <a href="/wiki/Black_Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Brazilian">native blacks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mulatto" title="Mulatto">people of mixed race,</a> poor whites, and <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Brazilians" title="Portuguese Brazilians">Portuguese immigrants</a>. The <i>Nagoas</i> and <i>Guaiamuns</i> were used respectively, as a hitforce by the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(Brazil)" title="Conservative Party (Brazil)">Conservative</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(Brazil)" title="Liberal Party (Brazil)">Liberal party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In 1890, the recently proclaimed Brazilian Republic decreed the prohibition of capoeira in the whole country.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Social conditions were chaotic in the Brazilian capital, and police reports identified capoeira as an advantage in fighting.<sup id="cite_ref-favela_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-favela-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the prohibition, any citizen caught practicing capoeira, in a fight or for any other reason, would be arrested, tortured and often mutilated by the police.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Cultural practices, such as the <i>roda de capoeira</i>, were conducted in remote places with sentries to warn of approaching police.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Luta_Regional_Baiana">Luta Regional Baiana</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Luta Regional Baiana">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By the 1920s, capoeira repression had declined. <a href="/wiki/Manuel_dos_Reis_Machado" title="Manuel dos Reis Machado">Mestre Bimba</a> from <a href="/wiki/Salvador,_Bahia" title="Salvador, Bahia">Salvador</a>, a strong fighter in both legal and illegal fights, met with his future student, Cisnando Lima. Both thought capoeira was losing its martial roots due to the use of its playful side to entertain tourists. Bimba began developing the first systematic training method for capoeira, and in 1932 founded the first capoeira school.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Advised by Cisnando, Bimba called his style <i>Luta Regional Baiana</i> ("regional fight from <a href="/wiki/Bahia" title="Bahia">Bahia</a>"), because capoeira was still illegal in name.<sup id="cite_ref-bimba_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bimba-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> At the time, capoeira was also known as "capoeiragem", with a practitioner being known as a "capoeira", as reported in local newspapers. Gradually, the art dropped the term to be known as "capoeira" with a practitioner being called a "capoeirista".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1937, Bimba founded the school <i>Centro de Cultura Física e Luta Regional</i>, with permission from Salvador's Secretary of Education (<i>Secretaria da Educação, Saúde e Assistência de Salvador</i>). His work was very well received, and he taught capoeira to the cultural elite of the city.<sup id="cite_ref-bimba_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bimba-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> By 1940, capoeira finally lost its criminal connotation and was legalized.
</p><p>Bimba's Regional style overshadowed traditional capoeiristas, who were still distrusted by society. This began to change in 1941 with the founding of <i>Centro Esportivo de Capoeira Angola</i> (CECA) by <a href="/wiki/Vicente_Ferreira_Pastinha" title="Vicente Ferreira Pastinha">Vicente Ferreira Pastinha</a>. Located in the Salvador neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Pelourinho" class="mw-redirect" title="Pelourinho">Pelourinho</a>, this school attracted many traditional capoeiristas. With CECA's prominence, the traditional style came to be called <i>Capoeira Angola</i>. The name derived from <i>brincar de angola</i> ("playing Angola"), a term used in the 19th century in some places. But it was also adopted by other masters, including some who did not follow Pastinha's style.<sup id="cite_ref-noronha_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-noronha-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Though there was some degree of tolerance, capoeira from the beginning of the 20th century began to become a more sanitised form of dance with less Martial application. This was due to regions mentioned above but also due to the military coup in the 1930s to 1945, as well as the Military regime from 1964-85. In both cases, capoeira was still seen by authorities as a dangerous pastime which was punishable; however during the Military Regime it was tolerated as an activity for University students (which by this time is the form of capoeira that is recognised today).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Today">Today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Capoeira is an active exporter of <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazilian culture">Brazilian culture</a> all over the world. In the 1970s, capoeira <i>mestres</i> began to emigrate and teach it in other countries. Present in many countries on every continent, every year capoeira attracts thousands of foreign students and tourists to Brazil. Foreign capoeiristas work hard to learn Portuguese to better understand and become part of the art. Renowned capoeira <i>mestres</i> often teach abroad and establish their own schools. Capoeira presentations, normally theatrical, acrobatic and with little martiality, are common sights around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014 the Capoeira Circle was added to <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO's</a> <a href="/wiki/Representative_List_of_the_Intangible_Cultural_Heritage_of_Humanity" class="mw-redirect" title="Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity">Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity</a>, the convention recognised that the "capoeira circle is a place where knowledge and skills are learned by observation and imitation" and that it "promotes social integration and the memory of resistance to historical oppression".<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Techniques">Techniques</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Techniques">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_capoeira_techniques" title="List of capoeira techniques">List of capoeira techniques</a></div>
<p>Capoeira is a fast and versatile <a href="/wiki/Martial_art" class="mw-redirect" title="Martial art">martial art</a> that is historically focused on fighting outnumbered or in technological disadvantage. The style emphasizes using the lower body to kick, sweep and take down and the upper body to assist those movements and occasionally attack as well. It features a series of complex positions and body postures that are meant to get chained in an uninterrupted flow, in order to strike, dodge and move without breaking motion, conferring the style with a characteristic unpredictability and versatility.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ginga_de_dos.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Ginga_de_dos.gif/220px-Ginga_de_dos.gif" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Ginga_de_dos.gif/330px-Ginga_de_dos.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9a/Ginga_de_dos.gif 2x" data-file-width="360" data-file-height="240" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ginga_de_dos.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Simple animation depicting part of the <i>ginga</i></div></div></div>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Ginga_(capoeira)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ginga (capoeira)">ginga</a></i> (literally: rocking back and forth; to swing) is the fundamental movement in capoeira, important both for attack and defense purposes. It has two main objectives. One is to keep the capoeirista in a state of constant motion, preventing him or her from being a still and easy target. The other, using also fakes and feints, is to mislead, fool, trick the opponent, leaving them open for an attack or a counter-attack.
</p><p>The attacks in the capoeira should be done when opportunity arises, and though they can be preceded by feints or pokes, they must be precise and decisive, like a direct kick to the head, face or a vital body part, or a strong takedown. Most capoeira attacks are made with the legs, like direct or swirling kicks, rasteiras (leg sweeps), <a href="/wiki/List_of_capoeira_techniques#Tesoura" title="List of capoeira techniques">tesouras</a> or knee strikes. Elbow strikes, punches and other forms of takedowns complete the main list. The head strike is a very important counter-attack move.
</p><p>The defense is based on the principle of non-resistance, meaning avoiding an attack using evasive moves instead of blocking it. Avoids are called <i>esquivas</i>, which depend on the direction of the attack and intention of the defender, and can be done standing or with a hand leaning on the floor. A block should only be made when the <i>esquiva</i> is completely non-viable. This fighting strategy allows quick and unpredictable counterattacks, the ability to focus on more than one adversary and to face empty-handed an armed adversary.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Auangole.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Auangole.gif/220px-Auangole.gif" decoding="async" width="220" height="137" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f7/Auangole.gif 1.5x" data-file-width="280" data-file-height="174" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Auangole.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A capoeira movement (Aú Fechado) (click for animation)</div></div></div>
<p>A series of rolls and acrobatics (like the <a href="/wiki/Cartwheel_(Capoeira)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cartwheel (Capoeira)">cartwheels</a> called <a href="/wiki/A%C3%BA" class="mw-redirect" title="Aú">aú</a> or the transitional position called negativa) allows the capoeirista to quickly overcome a takedown or a loss of balance, and to position themselves around the aggressor in order to lay up for an attack. It is this combination of attacks, defense and mobility that gives capoeira its perceived "fluidity" and choreography-like style.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Weapons">Weapons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Weapons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Through most of its history in Brazil, capoeira commonly featured weapons and weapon training, given its <a href="/wiki/Street_fighting" title="Street fighting">street fighting</a> nature. Capoeiristas usually carried knives and bladed weapons with them, and the <a href="/wiki/Berimbau" title="Berimbau">berimbau</a> could be used to conceal those inside, or even to turn itself into a weapon by attaching a blade to its tip.<sup id="cite_ref-Luan_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Luan-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The knife or razor was used in street <i>rodas</i> and/or against openly hostile opponents, and would be drawn quickly to stab or slash. Other hiding places for the weapons included <a href="/wiki/Hat" title="Hat">hats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Umbrella" title="Umbrella">umbrellas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Luan_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Luan-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mestre Bimba included in his teachings a <i>curso de especialização</i> or "specialization course", in which the pupils would be taught defenses against knives and guns, as well as the usage of knife, straight razor, scythe, club, <i>chanfolo</i> (double-edged dagger), <i>facão</i> (<a href="/wiki/Machete" title="Machete">machete</a>) and <i>tira-teima</i> (<a href="/wiki/Cane_sword" class="mw-redirect" title="Cane sword">cane sword</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-Roots_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roots-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Upon graduating, pupils were given a red scarf which marked their specialty. This course was scarcely used, and was ceased after some time. A more common custom practised by Bimba and his students, however, was furtively handing a weapon to a player before a <i>jogo</i> in order for them to use it to attack their opponent on Bimba's sign, with the other player's duty being to disarm them.<sup id="cite_ref-Roots_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roots-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This weapon training is almost completely absent in current capoeira teachings, but some groups still practice the use of razors for ceremonial usage in the <i>rodas</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="As_a_game">As a game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: As a game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Capoeira_Dance.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Capoeira_Dance.jpg/220px-Capoeira_Dance.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Capoeira_Dance.jpg/330px-Capoeira_Dance.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Capoeira_Dance.jpg/440px-Capoeira_Dance.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1120" data-file-height="840" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Capoeira_Dance.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Capoeiristas outside</div></div></div>
<p>Playing capoeira is both a game and a method of practicing the application of capoeira movements in simulated combat. It can be played anywhere, but it's usually done in a <i>roda</i>. During the game most capoeira moves are used, but capoeiristas usually avoid using punches or elbow strikes unless it's a very aggressive game.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The game usually does not focus on knocking down or destroying the opponent, rather it emphasizes skill. Capoeiristas often prefer to rely on a takedown like a <i>rasteira</i>, then allowing the opponent to recover and get back into the game. It is also very common to slow down a kick inches before hitting the target, so a capoeirista can enforce superiority without the need of injuring the opponent. If an opponent clearly cannot dodge an attack, there is no reason to complete it. However, between two high-skilled capoeiristas, the game can get much more aggressive and dangerous. Capoeiristas tend to avoid showing this kind of game in presentations or to the general public.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roda">Roda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Roda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Roda_de_capoeira1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Roda_de_capoeira1.jpg/220px-Roda_de_capoeira1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="134" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Roda_de_capoeira1.jpg/330px-Roda_de_capoeira1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Roda_de_capoeira1.jpg/440px-Roda_de_capoeira1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="427" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Roda_de_capoeira1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Capoeiristas in a <i>roda</i> (<a href="/wiki/Porto_Alegre" title="Porto Alegre">Porto Alegre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>)</div></div></div>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Roda_(formation)" title="Roda (formation)">roda</a></i> (pronounced <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">[ˈʁodɐ]</a></span>) is a circle formed by capoeiristas and capoeira musical instruments, where every participant sings the typical songs and claps their hands following the music. Two <i>capoeiristas</i> enter the <i>roda</i> and play the game according to the style required by the musical rhythm. The game finishes when one of the musicians holding a <a href="/wiki/Berimbau" title="Berimbau">berimbau</a> determine it, when one of the <i>capoeiristas</i> decide to leave or call the end of the game or when another capoeirista interrupts the game to start playing, either with one of the current players or with another <i>capoeirista</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In a <i>roda</i> every cultural aspect of capoeira is present, not only the martial side. Aerial acrobatics are common in a presentation <i>roda</i>, while not seen as often in a more serious one. Takedowns, on the other hand, are common in a serious <i>roda</i> but rarely seen in presentations.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Batizado">Batizado</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Batizado">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The batizado (lit. baptism) is a ceremonial <i>roda</i> where new students will get recognized as capoeiristas and earn their first graduation. Also more experienced students may go up in rank, depending on their skills and capoeira culture. In Mestre Bimba's Capoeira Regional, batizado was the first time a new student would play capoeira following the sound of the berimbau.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Students enter the <i>roda</i> against a high-ranked capoeirista (such as a teacher or master) and normally the game ends with the student being taken down. In some cases the more experienced capoeirista can judge the takedown unnecessary. Following the batizado the new graduation, generally in the form of a cord, is given.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Apelido">Apelido</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Apelido">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Traditionally, the batizado is the moment when the new practitioner gets or formalizes his or her <i>apelido</i> (nickname). This tradition was created back when capoeira practice was considered a crime. To avoid having problems with the law, capoeiristas would present themselves in the capoeira community only by their nicknames. So if a capoeirista was captured by the police, he would be unable to identify his fellow capoeiristas, even when tortured.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p><i>Apelidos</i> can come from many different things, such as a physical characteristic (like being tall or big), a habit (like smiling or drinking too much), place of birth, a particular skill, an animal, or trivial things.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Even though apelidos or these nicknames are not necessary any more, the tradition is still very alive not only in capoeira but in many aspects of Brazilian culture.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chamada">Chamada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Chamada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Chamada</i> means 'call' and can happen at any time during a <i>roda</i> where the rhythm <i>angola</i> is being played. It happens when one player, usually the more advanced one, calls his or her opponent to a dance-like ritual. The opponent then approaches the caller and meets him or her to walk side by side. After it both resume normal play.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While it may seem like a break time or a dance, the <i>chamada</i> is actually both a trap and a test, as the caller is just watching to see if the opponent will let his guard down so she can perform a takedown or a strike. It is a critical situation, because both players are vulnerable due to the close proximity and potential for a surprise attack. It's also a tool for experienced practitioners and masters of the art to test a student's awareness and demonstrate when the student left herself open to attack.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The use of the <i>chamada</i> can result in a highly developed sense of awareness and helps practitioners learn the subtleties of anticipating another person's hidden intentions. The <i>chamada</i> can be very simple, consisting solely of the basic elements, or the ritual can be quite elaborate including a competitive dialogue of trickery, or even theatric embellishments.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Volta_ao_mundo">Volta ao mundo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Volta ao mundo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Volta ao mundo</b> means <i>around the world</i>.
</p><p>The <i>volta ao mundo</i> takes place after an exchange of movements has reached a conclusion, or after there has been a disruption in the harmony of the game. In either of these situations, one player will begin walking around the perimeter of the circle counter-clockwise, and the other player will join the <i>volta ao mundo</i> in the opposite part of the roda, before returning to the normal game.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Malandragem_and_mandinga">Malandragem and mandinga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Malandragem and mandinga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Malandragem" title="Malandragem">Malandragem</a></i> is a word that comes from <i>malandro</i>, which means a person who possesses cunning as well as <i>malícia</i> (malice). This, however, is misleading as the meaning of <i>malicia</i> in capoeira is the capacity to understand someone's intentions. In Brazil, men who used street smarts to make a living were called <i>malandros</i>.
</p><p>In capoeira, <i>malandragem</i> is the ability to quickly understand an opponent's aggressive intentions, and during a fight or a game, fool, trick and deceive him.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Similarly capoeiristas use the concept of <i>mandinga</i>. Mandinga can be translated "magic" or "spell", but in capoeira a <i>mandingueiro</i> is a clever fighter, able to trick the opponent. Mandinga is a tricky and strategic quality of the game, and even a certain esthetic, where the game is expressive and at times theatrical, particularly in the Angola style. The roots of the term <i>mandingueiro</i> would be a person who had the magic ability to avoid harm due to protection from the <a href="/wiki/Orisha" title="Orisha">Orixás</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alternately Mandinga is a way of saying Mandinka (as in the Mandinka Nation) who are known as "musical hunters".  Which directly ties into the term "vadiação". Vadiação is the musical wanderer (with flute in hand), traveler, vagabond.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Capoeira_music" title="Capoeira music">Capoeira music</a></div>
<p>Music is integral to capoeira. It sets the tempo and style of game that is to be played within the <i>roda</i>. Typically the music is formed by <a href="/wiki/Musical_instruments" class="mw-redirect" title="Musical instruments">instruments</a> and <a href="/wiki/Song" title="Song">singing</a>. Rhythms (<a href="/wiki/Capoeira_toques" title="Capoeira toques">toques</a>), controlled by a typical instrument called <a href="/wiki/Berimbau" title="Berimbau">berimbau</a>, differ from very slow to very fast, depending on the style of the <i>roda</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Instruments">Instruments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Instruments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Capoeira-three-berimbau-one-pandeiro.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Capoeira-three-berimbau-one-pandeiro.jpg/220px-Capoeira-three-berimbau-one-pandeiro.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Capoeira-three-berimbau-one-pandeiro.jpg/330px-Capoeira-three-berimbau-one-pandeiro.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Capoeira-three-berimbau-one-pandeiro.jpg/440px-Capoeira-three-berimbau-one-pandeiro.jpg 2x" data-file-width="565" data-file-height="429" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Capoeira-three-berimbau-one-pandeiro.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A capoeira bateria showing three <a href="/wiki/Berimbau" title="Berimbau">berimbaus</a> a reco- reco and a <a href="/wiki/Pandeiro" title="Pandeiro">pandeiro</a></div></div></div>
<p>Capoeira instruments are disposed in a row called <a href="/wiki/Bateria" title="Bateria">bateria</a>. It is traditionally formed by three <a href="/wiki/Berimbau" title="Berimbau">berimbaus</a>, two <a href="/wiki/Pandeiro" title="Pandeiro">pandeiros</a>, three <a href="/wiki/Atabaque" title="Atabaque">atabaques</a>, one <a href="/wiki/Agog%C3%B4" title="Agogô">agogô</a> and one <a href="/wiki/Ganz%C3%A1" title="Ganzá">ganzá</a>, but this format may vary depending on the capoeira group's traditions or the <i>roda</i> style.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The berimbau is the leading instrument, determining the tempo and style of the music and game played. Two low pitch berimbaus (called <i>berra-boi</i> and <i>médio</i>) form the base and a high pitch berimbau (called <i>viola</i>) makes variations and improvisations. The other instruments must follow the berimbau's rhythm, free to vary and improvise a little, depending upon the capoeira group's musical style.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>As the capoeiristas change their playing style significantly following the <a href="/wiki/Capoeira_toques" title="Capoeira toques">toque</a> of the berimbau, which sets the game's speed, style and aggressiveness, it is truly the music that drives a capoeira game.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Songs">Songs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Songs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many of the songs are sung in a <a href="/wiki/Call_and_response_(music)" title="Call and response (music)">call and response</a> format while others are in the form of a narrative. Capoeiristas sing about a wide variety of subjects. Some songs are about history or stories of famous capoeiristas. Other songs attempt to inspire players to play better. Some songs are about what is going on within the roda. Sometimes the songs are about life or love lost. Others have lighthearted and playful lyrics.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>There are four basic kinds of songs in capoeira, the <i>Ladaínha</i>, <i>Chula</i>, <i>Corrido</i> and <i>Quadra</i>. The <b>Ladaínha</b> is a narrative solo sung only at the beginning of a roda, often by a <i>mestre</i> (master) or most respected capoeirista present. The solo is followed by a <i>louvação</i>, a call and response pattern that usually thanks God and one's master, among other things. Each call is usually repeated word-for-word by the responders. The <b>Chula</b> is a song where the singer part is much bigger than the chorus response, usually eight singer verses for one chorus response, but the proportion may vary. The <b>Corrido</b> is a song where the singer part and the chorus response are equal, normally two verses by two responses. Finally, the <b>Quadra</b> is a song where the same verse is repeated four times, either three singer verses followed by one chorus response, or one verse and one response.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Capoeira songs can talk about virtually anything, being it about a historical fact, a famous capoeirista, trivial life facts, hidden messages for players, anything. Improvisation is very important also, while singing a song the main singer can change the music's lyrics, telling something that's happening in or outside the roda.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<p>Determining styles in capoeira is difficult, since there was never a unity in the original capoeira, or a teaching method before the decade of 1920. However, a division between two styles and a sub-style is widely accepted.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Capoeira_Angola">Capoeira Angola</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Capoeira Angola">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira_de_Angola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Capoeira de Angola (page does not exist)">Capoeira de Angola</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://pt.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capoeira_de_Angola" class="extiw" title="pt:Capoeira de Angola">pt</a>&#93;</span> refers to every capoeira that keeps the traditions held before the creation of the Regional style.
</p><p>Existing in many parts of Brazil since colonial times, most notably in the cities of <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvador,_Bahia" title="Salvador, Bahia">Salvador</a> and <a href="/wiki/Recife" title="Recife">Recife</a>, it's impossible to tell where and when Capoeira Angola began taking its present form. The name <i>Angola</i> starts as early as the beginning of slavery in Brazil, when Africans, taken to <a href="/wiki/Luanda" title="Luanda">Luanda</a> to be shipped to the Americas, were called in Brazil "black people from Angola", regardless of their nationality. In some places of Brazil people would refer to capoeira as "playing Angola" and, according to Mestre Noronha, the capoeira school <i>Centro de Capoeira Angola Conceição da Praia</i>, created in Bahia, already used the name <i>Capoeira Angola</i> illegally in the beginning of the 1920 decade.<sup id="cite_ref-noronha_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-noronha-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The name <i>Angola</i> was finally immortalized by <a href="/wiki/Pastinha" class="mw-redirect" title="Pastinha">Mestre Pastinha</a> at February 23, 1941, when he opened the <i>Centro Esportivo de capoeira Angola</i> (CECA). Pastinha preferred the ludic aspects of the game rather than the martial side, and was much respected by recognized capoeira masters. Soon many other masters would adopt the name <i>Angola</i>, even those who would not follow Pastinha's style.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The ideal of <i>Capoeira Angola</i> is to maintain capoeira as close to its roots as possible. Characterized by being strategic, with sneaking movements executed standing or near the floor depending on the situation to face, it values the traditions of <i>malícia</i>, <i>malandragem</i> and unpredictability of the original capoeira.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Typical music <i>bateria</i> formation in a <i>roda</i> of <i>Capoeira Angola</i> is three <i>berimbaus</i>, two <i>pandeiros</i>, one <i>atabaque</i>, one <i>agogô</i> and one <i>ganzuá</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Capoeira_Regional">Capoeira Regional</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Capoeira Regional">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Capoeira Regional began to take form in the 1920 decade, when <a href="/wiki/Mestre_Bimba" class="mw-redirect" title="Mestre Bimba">Mestre Bimba</a> met his future student, José Cisnando Lima. Both believed that capoeira was losing its martial side and concluded there was a need to restructure it. Bimba created his <i>sequências de ensino</i> (teaching combinations) and created capoeira's first teaching method. Advised by Cisnando, Bimba decided to call his style <i>Luta Regional Baiana</i>, as capoeira was still illegal at that time.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The base of Capoeira Regional is the original capoeira without many of the aspects that were impractical in a real fight, with less subterfuge and more objectivity. Training was mainly focused on attack, dodging and counter-attack, giving high importance to precision and discipline. Bimba also added a few moves from other arts, notably the <i>batuque</i>, an old street fight game invented by his father.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Use of jumps or aerial acrobacies was kept to a minimum, since one of its foundations was always keeping at least one hand or foot firmly attached to the ground. Mestre Bimba often said, "<i>o chão é amigo do capoeirista</i>" (the floor is a friend to the capoeirista).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p><i>Capoeira Regional</i> also introduced the first ranking method in capoeira. <i>Regional</i> had three levels: <i>calouro</i> (freshman), <i>formado</i> (graduated) and <i>formado especializado</i> (specialist). When a student completed a course, a special celebration ceremony was had resulting with a silk scarf being tied around the capoeirista's neck.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The traditions of <i>roda</i> and capoeira game were kept, being used to put into use what was learned during training. The disposition of musical instruments, however, was changed, being made by a single berimbau and two pandeiros.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The <i>Luta Regional Baiana</i> soon became popular, finally changing capoeira's bad image. Mestre Bimba made many presentations of his new style, but the best known was the one made at 1953 to Brazilian president <a href="/wiki/Get%C3%BAlio_Vargas" title="Getúlio Vargas">Getúlio Vargas</a>, where the president would say: "<i>A Capoeira é o único esporte verdadeiramente nacional</i>" (Capoeira is the only truly national sport).<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Capoeira_Contempor.C3.A2nea"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Capoeira_Contemporânea">Capoeira Contemporânea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Capoeira Contemporânea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 1970s a mixed style began to take form, with practitioners taking the aspects they considered more important from both Regional and Angola. Notably more acrobatic, this sub-style is seen by some as the natural evolution of capoeira, by others as adulteration or even misinterpretation of capoeira.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Nowadays the label Contemporânea applies to any capoeira group who don't follow Regional or Angola styles, even the ones who mix capoeira with other martial arts. Some notable groups whose style cannot be described as either Angola or Regional but rather "a style of their own", include Senzala de Santos, Cordao de Ouro and Abada. In the case of Cordao de Ouro, the style may be described as "Miudinho", a low and fast-paced game, while in Senzala de Santos the style may described simply as "Senzala de Santos", an elegant, playful combination of Angola and Regional. Capoeira Abada may be described as a more aggressive, less dance-influenced style of capoeira.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ranks">Ranks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Ranks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">March 2018</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Because of its origin, capoeira never had unity or a general agreement. Ranking or graduating system follows the same path, as there never existed a ranking system accepted by most of the masters. That means graduation style varies depending on the group's traditions.
</p><p>The most common modern system uses colored ropes, called <i>corda</i> or <i>cordão</i>, tied around the waist. Some masters use different systems, or even no system at all.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are many entities (leagues, federations and association) that have tried to unify the graduation system. The most usual<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> is the system of the <i>Confederação Brasileira de Capoeira</i> (Brazilian Capoeira Confederation), which adopts ropes using the colors of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_flag" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazilian flag">Brazilian flag</a>, green, yellow, blue and white.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Even though it is widely used with many small variations, many big and influential groups still use different systems, in example, Porto da Barra Group that uses belts that tell the Brazilian slavery history. Even the <i>Confederação Brasileira de Capoeira</i> is not widely accepted as the capoeira's main representative.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In a substantial number of groups (mainly of the Angola school) there is no visible ranking system. There can still be several ranks: student, treinel, professor, contra-mestre and mestre, but often no cordas (belts).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Related_activities">Related activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Related activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Even though those activities are strongly associated with capoeira, they have different meanings and origins.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Samba_de_roda">Samba de roda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Samba de roda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Samba_de_roda" class="mw-redirect" title="Samba de roda">Samba de roda</a></div>
<p>Performed by many capoeira groups, <a href="/wiki/Samba_de_roda" class="mw-redirect" title="Samba de roda">samba de roda</a> is a traditional Afro-Brazilian dance and musical form that has been associated with capoeira for many decades. The orchestra is composed by <i>pandeiro</i>, <i>atabaque</i>, <i>berimbau-viola</i> (high pitch berimbau), <a href="/wiki/Chocalho" title="Chocalho">chocalho</a>, accompanied by singing and clapping. <i>Samba de roda</i> is considered one of the primitive forms of modern Samba.
</p>
<h3><span id="Maculel.C3.AA"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Maculelê">Maculelê</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Maculelê">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Maculel%C3%AA_(dance)" title="Maculelê (dance)">Maculelê (dance)</a></div>
<p>Originally the <i>Maculelê</i> is believed to have been an <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_in_Brazil" title="Indigenous peoples in Brazil">indigenous</a> armed fighting style, using two sticks or a machete. Nowadays it's a folkloric dance practiced with heavy afro-Brazilian percussion. Many capoeira groups include <i>Maculelê</i> in their presentations.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Puxada_de_rede">Puxada de rede</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Puxada de rede">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Puxada_de_rede" class="mw-redirect" title="Puxada de rede">Puxada de rede</a></div>
<p><i>Puxada de Rede</i> is a Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_mythology" title="Brazilian mythology">folkloric</a> theatrical play, seen in many capoeira performances. It is based on a traditional Brazilian legend involving the loss of a fisherman in a seafaring accident.
</p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Capoeira_in_popular_culture" title="Capoeira in popular culture">Capoeira in popular culture</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_development">Sports development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Sports development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Capoeira is currently being used as a tool in sports development (the use of sport to create positive social change) to promote psychosocial wellbeing in various youth projects around the world. Capoeira4Refugees is a UK-based NGO working with youth in conflict zones in the Middle East. Capoeira for Peace is a project based in the Democratic Republic of Congo. The Nukanti Foundation works with street children in Colombia.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="MMA">MMA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capoeira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: MMA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Many Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial arts</a> fighters have a capoeira background, either training often or having tried it before.  Some of them include <a href="/wiki/Anderson_Silva" title="Anderson Silva">Anderson Silva</a>, who is a yellow belt, trained in capoeira at a young age, then again when he was a <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Fighting_Championship" title="Ultimate Fighting Championship">UFC</a> fighter;
<a href="/wiki/Thiago_Santos_(middleweight_fighter)" class="mw-redirect" title="Thiago Santos (middleweight fighter)">Thiago Santos</a>, an active UFC middleweight contender who trained in capoeira for 8 years; Former UFC Heavyweight Champion Junior dos Santos, who trained in capoeira as a child and incorporates its kicking techniques and movement into his stand up; Marcus "Lelo" Aurelio, who is famous for knocking a fighter out with a Meia Lua De Compasso kick, and UFC veterans <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Aldo" title="José Aldo">José Aldo</a> and Andre Gusmão also use capoeira as their base.
</p>
