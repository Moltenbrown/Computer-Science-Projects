<p><b>Taylor</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> used in the British Isles of <a href="/wiki/French_name" title="French name">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> origin which originated as a <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Norman</a> <a href="/wiki/Occupational_surname" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupational surname">occupational surname</a> (meaning <i><a href="/wiki/Tailor" title="Tailor">tailor</a></i>) in France<sup id="cite_ref-SUR_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SUR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/tailleur" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:tailleur">tailleur</a></i> ("cutter"),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which is in turn derived from the <a href="/wiki/Late_Latin" title="Late Latin">Late Latin</a> <i>taliator</i>, from <i>taliare</i> ("to cut").<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The first historical evidence of the surname dates to the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Somerset" class="mw-redirect" title="County of Somerset">County of Somerset</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_West_England" title="South West England">South West England</a> in 1182.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> "Taylor" is the fourth-most common surname in United Kingdom,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> fifth-most common in England, the 11th-most common in Scotland and the 22nd-most common in Wales.<sup id="cite_ref-SUR_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SUR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It is also common in other <a href="/wiki/English-speaking_countries" class="mw-redirect" title="English-speaking countries">English-speaking countries</a> (especially <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, where it was the tenth most frequently encountered surname in the 2000 US Census),<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> but has a low incidence in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, where it is mostly concentrated in <a href="/wiki/Ulster" title="Ulster">the North</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SUR_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SUR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is often the <a href="/wiki/Anglicisation_of_names" title="Anglicisation of names">anglicized form</a> of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_most_common_surnames_in_Germany" title="List of the most common surnames in Germany">German surname</a> <a href="/wiki/Schneider_(surname)" title="Schneider (surname)">Schneider</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
