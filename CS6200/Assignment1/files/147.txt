	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Andersen</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Andersen</b> (<small>Danish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Danish" title="Help:IPA/Danish">[ˈanɐsn̩]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a>-<a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> meaning "son of Anders" (itself derived from the Greek name "<a href="/wiki/Andreas" title="Andreas">Ανδρέας/Andreas</a>", cf. <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Andrew" title="Andrew">Andrew</a>). It is the fifth most common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, shared by about 3.2% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>From the same root&#160;– the given name <i>Andreas</i>&#160;– derives the <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Andresen" title="Andresen">Andreasen</a></b> <small>Danish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Danish" title="Help:IPA/Danish">[ænˈtʁɛːˀæsn̩]</a></span> (cognate <b><a href="/wiki/Andreassen" title="Andreassen">Andreassen</a></b>) and <b><a href="/wiki/Andresen" title="Andresen">Andresen</a></b> <small>Danish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Danish" title="Help:IPA/Danish">[ænˈtʁɛːˀsn̩]</a></span>.
</p><p>The numbers of bearers of the surnames Andersen, Andreasen, Andreassen and Andresen in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> (2009):
</p>
