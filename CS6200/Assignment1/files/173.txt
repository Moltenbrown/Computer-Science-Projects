<p><b>Thompson</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Patronymic_surname" title="Patronymic surname">patronymic surname</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> origin, with a variety of spellings, meaning "son of Thom".<sup id="cite_ref-quinn2000_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quinn2000-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> An alternative origin may be geographical, arising from the placename <a href="/wiki/Thompson,_Norfolk" title="Thompson, Norfolk">Thompson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rkh2011_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rkh2011-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  During the <a href="/wiki/Plantations_of_Ireland" title="Plantations of Ireland">Plantation period</a>, settlers carried the name to Ireland.  There has been a third alternative claim made that Thom(p)son originated as the English translation of <a href="/wiki/Clan_MacTavish" title="Clan MacTavish">MacTavish</a>, which is the Anglicised version of the <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaelic languages">Gaelic</a> name of MacTamhais.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Thompson is the 14th most common surname in the United Kingdom. According to the 2010 United States Census, Thompson was the 23rd most frequently reported surname, accounting for 0.23% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
