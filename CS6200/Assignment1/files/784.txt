	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Hispanophone</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Term used to refer to Spanish-language speakers</div>
<p><i><b>Hispanophone</b></i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;i&#93;</a></sup> and <i><b>Hispanosphere</b></i> are terms used to refer to <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>-language speakers and the Spanish-speaking world, respectively. The terms derive from the <a href="/wiki/Latin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin language">Latin</a> political name of the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hispania" title="Hispania">Hispania</a>. In addition to the general definition of Hispanophone, some groups in the Hispanic world make a distinction between Castilian-speaking<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;ii&#93;</a></sup> and Spanish-speaking, with the former term denoting the speakers of the Spanish language—<a href="/wiki/Names_given_to_the_Spanish_language" title="Names given to the Spanish language">also known as Castilian</a>—and the latter the speakers of the Spanish or Hispanic languages (i.e. the <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Spain" title="Languages of Spain">languages of Spain</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Hispanic_America" class="mw-redirect" title="Languages of Hispanic America">languages of the Hispanic nations</a>).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_distribution">Geographic distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geographic distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="center"><div class="thumb tnone"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:752px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Countries_with_Spanish_as_an_official_language.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Countries_with_Spanish_as_an_official_language.svg/750px-Countries_with_Spanish_as_an_official_language.svg.png" decoding="async" width="750" height="331" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Countries_with_Spanish_as_an_official_language.svg/1125px-Countries_with_Spanish_as_an_official_language.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Countries_with_Spanish_as_an_official_language.svg/1500px-Countries_with_Spanish_as_an_official_language.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="940" data-file-height="415" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Countries_with_Spanish_as_an_official_language.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color:red; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Countries where Spanish is the sole official national language.</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #000080; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Countries and regions where the Spanish language is co-official.</div></div></div></div></div>
<p>Hispanophones are estimated at between 480<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and 577 million (including second language speakers)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Es2009_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Es2009-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> globally, making Spanish the <a href="/wiki/List_of_languages_by_number_of_native_speakers" title="List of languages by number of native speakers">second most spoken language</a> in terms of native speakers. Around 360 million live in <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_America" title="Hispanic America">Hispanic America</a> and 45 million in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> (70 million in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>). There are more than 34 million <a href="/wiki/Spanish_in_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish in the United States">Spanish speakers in the United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2007_survey_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2007_survey-8">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> There are also smaller Hispanophone groups in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> as well as in many other places around the world, particularly other countries of <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, where it is one of 24 official languages, and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.
</p><p>In a cultural, rather than merely linguistic sense, the notion of "Hispanophone" goes further than the above definition. The <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic culture">Hispanic culture</a> is the legacy of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a>, and so the term can refer to people whose cultural background is primarily associated with Spain, regardless of <a href="/wiki/Ethnicity" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnicity">ethnic</a> or geographical differences. The whole sense of identity of the Hispanic population and the Hispanophones is sometimes referred by the term <i><a href="/wiki/Hispanidad" title="Hispanidad">Hispanidad</a></i> (Hispanicity).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Countries">Countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Hispanicity" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanicity">Hispanicity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a></div>
<p>During the Spanish period between 1492 and 1898, many people from Spain <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">migrated to the new lands they had conquered</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Spaniards" title="Spaniards">Spaniards</a> took with them their language and culture,
and integrated within the society they had settled, creating a large empire that stretched all over the world and producing several multiracial populations. Their influences are found in the following continents and countries that were originally colonized by the Spaniards.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Spain" title="Languages of Spain">Languages of Spain</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Spain_languages-en.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Spain_languages-en.svg/300px-Spain_languages-en.svg.png" decoding="async" width="300" height="212" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Spain_languages-en.svg/450px-Spain_languages-en.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Spain_languages-en.svg/600px-Spain_languages-en.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1488" data-file-height="1052" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Spain_languages-en.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The languages of Spain</div></div></div>
<p>The modern-day people that live in the region of ancient <a href="/wiki/Hispania" title="Hispania">Hispania</a> are the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_people" title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">Andorran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gibraltarian" class="mw-redirect" title="Gibraltarian">Gibraltarian</a> people. <a href="/wiki/History_of_Spain" title="History of Spain">Historically</a>, the modern country of Spain was formed by the accretion of several independent <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian</a> kingdoms through dynastic inheritance, conquest and the will of the local elites. These kingdoms had their own nationalistic loyalties and political borders.
</p><p>Today, there is no single Castilian–Spanish identity for the whole country. Spain is a <i>de facto</i> <a href="/wiki/Plurinationalism" title="Plurinationalism">plurinational state</a>. Many Spanish citizens feel no conflict in recognising their multiple ethnic identities at the same time. Spain is a culturally heterogeneous country, home to a wide range of cultures, each one with its own customs and <a href="/wiki/Tradition" title="Tradition">traditions</a>. Some such cultures have <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Spain" title="Languages of Spain">their own language</a>. Since the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Transition_to_democracy_in_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Transition to democracy in Spain">transition to democracy in Spain</a> and the creation of the Spanish <i><a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous communities">autonomous communities</a></i>, after <a href="/wiki/Francoist_Spain" title="Francoist Spain">Francoist Spain</a>, there have been many movements towards more autonomy (<a href="/wiki/Decentralisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Decentralisation">delegation of powers</a>) in certain territories of the country, some with the aim of achieving full independence and others with the goal of improving the system of devolution and the state of the autonomies (or self-government entities) .
</p><p>The existence of multiple distinct cultures in Spain allows an analogy to be drawn to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. Using the term Spanish for someone of Spanish descent would then be expected to be equivalent to using <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">Briton</a> to describe someone descending from some part of the United Kingdom. Cultures within the United Kingdom, such as <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Welsh_people" title="Welsh people">Welsh</a>, would then correspond in this analogy to cultures within Spain such as <a href="/wiki/Castilian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Castilian people">Castilian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalan_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Catalan people">Catalan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galician_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Galician people">Galician</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basque_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Basque people">Basque</a> among others. In contrast with Spain, because of centuries of gradual and mutual consolidation across the Iberian Peninsula, such distinctions tend to be blurred. It is a subtle, yet important, distinction.
</p><p>In Spain, as in the United Kingdom, the economically dominant territories—<a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castile</a> and <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>—spread their language for mutual communication. However, the political dominance in the United Kingdom tends to be sharper compared to Spain, where most of <a href="/wiki/History_of_Spain#Kingdom_of_Spain" title="History of Spain">medieval realms</a> do not correspond with the actual boundaries of the autonomous communities, and the crown was unified into a sole <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_Spain" title="Monarchy of Spain">monarch</a>. For instance, Spanish people in modern times never refer to King Felipe VI of Spain as "the King of Castile," whereas the British sovereign, Queen Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom, is sometimes referred to colloquially as "the Queen of England."
</p>

<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_and_demography">Origins and demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Origins and demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>U.S. Hispanics are citizens of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> whose ancestry or national origin is of any of the nations composing the Hispanosphere. A Hispanic person's status is independent from whether or not he or she speaks the Spanish language, for not all Hispanic Americans speak Spanish. A Hispanic person may be of any race (White, Amerindian, mixed, Black, Asian or Pacific Islander). As of 2013<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> Hispanics accounted for 17.1% of the population, around 53.2 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> This was an increase of 29% since 2004, when Hispanics were 14.1% of the population (around 41.3 million people). The Hispanic growth rate over the July 1, 2003 to July 1, 2004 period was 3.6% — higher than any other ancestral group in the United States — and more than three times the rate of the nation's total population (at 1.0%). The projected Hispanic population of the United States for July 1, 2050, is 105.6 million people. According to this projection, Hispanics will constitute 25% of the nation's total population by the year 2050.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Historically, a continuous Hispanic presence in the territory of the United States has existed since the 16th century, earlier than any other group after the <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Amerindians</a>. Spaniards pioneered the present-day United States. The first confirmed European landing on the continent was that of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n" title="Juan Ponce de León">Juan Ponce de León</a>, who landed in 1513 on the shore he christened <i><a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">La Florida</a></i>. Within three decades of Ponce de León's landing, the Spanish became the first Europeans to reach the <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Mountains" title="Appalachian Mountains">Appalachian Mountains</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">Mississippi River</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Canyon" title="Grand Canyon">Grand Canyon</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">Great Plains</a>. Spanish ships sailed along the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast</a>, penetrating to present-day <a href="/wiki/Bangor,_Maine" title="Bangor, Maine">Bangor, Maine</a>, and up the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="West Coast of the United States">Pacific Coast</a> as far as <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>. From 1528 to 1536, four castaways from a Spanish expedition, including a black <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moor</a>, journeyed all the way from Florida to the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_California" title="Gulf of California">Gulf of California</a>, 267 years before the <a href="/wiki/Lewis_and_Clark_Expedition" title="Lewis and Clark Expedition">Lewis and Clark Expedition</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 1540 <a href="/wiki/Hernando_de_Soto_(explorer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hernando de Soto (explorer)">Hernando de Soto</a> undertook an extensive exploration of the present United States. In the same year <a href="/wiki/Francisco_V%C3%A1squez_de_Coronado" class="mw-redirect" title="Francisco Vásquez de Coronado">Francisco Vásquez de Coronado</a> led 2,000 Spaniards and Mexican Indians across today's <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>–<a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> border and traveled as far as central <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a>, close to the exact geographic center of what is now the continental United States. Other Spanish explorers of the United States make up a long list that includes, among others, <a href="/wiki/Lucas_V%C3%A1squez_de_Ayll%C3%B3n" class="mw-redirect" title="Lucas Vásquez de Ayllón">Lucas Vásquez de Ayllón</a>, <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A1nfilo_de_Narv%C3%A1ez" title="Pánfilo de Narváez">Pánfilo de Narváez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebasti%C3%A1n_Vizca%C3%ADno" title="Sebastián Vizcaíno">Sebastián Vizcaíno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Cabrillo" title="Juan Rodríguez Cabrillo">Juan Rodríguez Cabrillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gaspar_de_Portol%C3%A0" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaspar de Portolà">Gaspar de Portolà</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Men%C3%A9ndez_de_Avil%C3%A9s" title="Pedro Menéndez de Avilés">Pedro Menéndez de Avilés</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvar_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_Cabeza_de_Vaca" title="Álvar Núñez Cabeza de Vaca">Álvar Núñez Cabeza de Vaca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trist%C3%A1n_de_Luna_y_Arellano" title="Tristán de Luna y Arellano">Tristán de Luna y Arellano</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_O%C3%B1ate" title="Juan de Oñate">Juan de Oñate</a>. In all, Spaniards probed half of today's lower 48 states before the first English colonization attempt at <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Island" title="Roanoke Island">Roanoke Island</a> in 1585.
</p><p>The Spanish created the first permanent European settlement in the continental United States, at <a href="/wiki/St._Augustine,_Florida" title="St. Augustine, Florida">St. Augustine, Florida</a>, in 1565. <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe,_New_Mexico" title="Santa Fe, New Mexico">Santa Fe, New Mexico</a> also predates <a href="/wiki/Jamestown,_Virginia" title="Jamestown, Virginia">Jamestown, Virginia</a> (founded in 1607) and <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Colony" title="Plymouth Colony">Plymouth Colony</a> (of <i><a href="/wiki/Mayflower" title="Mayflower">Mayflower</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Pilgrims" class="mw-redirect" title="Pilgrims">Pilgrims</a> fame, founded in 1620). Later came Spanish settlements in <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">San Antonio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tucson" class="mw-redirect" title="Tucson">Tucson</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, to name just a few. The Spanish even established a <a href="/wiki/Jesuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Jesuit">Jesuit</a> <a href="/wiki/Ajac%C3%A1n_Mission" title="Ajacán Mission">mission</a> in <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">Chesapeake Bay</a> 37 years before the founding of Jamestown.
</p><p>Two iconic American stories have Spanish antecedents, too. Almost 80 years before <a href="/wiki/John_Smith_of_Jamestown" class="mw-redirect" title="John Smith of Jamestown">John Smith</a>'s alleged rescue by <a href="/wiki/Pocahontas" title="Pocahontas">Pocahontas</a>, a man by the name of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Ortiz_de_Z%C3%A1rate" title="Juan Ortiz de Zárate">Juan Ortiz</a> told of his remarkably similar rescue from execution by an Indian girl. Spaniards also held a <a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving" title="Thanksgiving">thanksgiving</a>—56 years before the famous Pilgrims <a href="/wiki/Harvest_festival" title="Harvest festival">festival</a>—when they feasted near St. Augustine with Florida Indians, probably on stewed pork and garbanzo beans. As late as 1783, at the end of the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>, Spain held claim to roughly half of today's continental United States (see <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">New Spain</a>); in 1775, Spanish ships even reached <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>. From 1819 to 1848, the United States and its army increased the nation's area by roughly a third at Spanish and Mexican expense, including today's three most populous states: <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>. Hispanics became the first American citizens in the newly acquired <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_United_States" title="Southwestern United States">Southwest</a> territory and remained the ancestral majority in several states until the 20th century.
</p><p>Hispanic Americans have fought in all the <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_States_military_history_events" class="mw-redirect" title="List of United States military history events">wars of the United States</a> and have earned some of the highest distinctions awarded to U.S. soldiers (<a href="/wiki/List_of_Hispanic_Medal_of_Honor_recipients" title="List of Hispanic Medal of Honor recipients">list of Hispanic Medal of Honor recipients</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Historic figures in the United States have been Hispanic from early times. Some recent famous people include actress <a href="/wiki/Rita_Hayworth" title="Rita Hayworth">Rita Hayworth</a> and baseball legends <a href="/wiki/Lefty_Gomez" title="Lefty Gomez">Lefty Gomez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ted_Williams" title="Ted Williams">Ted Williams</a>.
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Hispanic_Heritage_Month">National Hispanic Heritage Month</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: National Hispanic Heritage Month">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>The National Hispanic Heritage Month is celebrated in the United States from September 15 to October 15.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Racial_diversity">Racial diversity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Racial diversity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hispanic" title="Hispanic">Hispanic</a></div>
<p>The term <i>Hispanic</i> is cultural and not racial. The racial diversity found among Hispanics stems from the fact that Hispanic America has always been, since 1492, an area of immigration until late in the 20th century, when the region has increasingly become an area of emigration. Even outside the broad U.S. definition of Hispanic, the term encompasses a very racially and ethnically diverse population. While in the United States, Hispanics are often treated as a group apart from <a href="/wiki/White_(people)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (people)">whites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_(people)" class="mw-redirect" title="Black (people)">blacks</a> or other races, they actually include people who may identify with any or all of those racial groups.
</p><p>In the American mass media as well as popular culture, Hispanic is often incorrectly used to describe a subject's <a href="/wiki/Race_(classification_of_human_beings)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (classification of human beings)">race</a> or <a href="/wiki/Human_physical_appearance" title="Human physical appearance">physical appearance</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In general, Hispanics are assumed to have traits such as dark hair and eyes, and brown or olive skin. Many others are viewed as physically intermediate between <a href="/wiki/Whites" class="mw-redirect" title="Whites">whites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">blacks</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">Amerindians</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Asian_people" title="Asian people">Asians</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Hispanics with mostly <a href="/wiki/Caucasoid" class="mw-redirect" title="Caucasoid">Caucasoid</a> or <a href="/wiki/Negroid" title="Negroid">Negroid</a> features may not be recognized as such in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in spite of the ethnic and racial diversity of most Hispanic American populations. Hispanics who do not look like the stereotypical Hispanic may have their ancestral status questioned or even challenged by others.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Actors <a href="/wiki/Martin_Sheen" title="Martin Sheen">Martin Sheen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Bledel" title="Alexis Bledel">Alexis Bledel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cameron_Diaz" title="Cameron Diaz">Cameron Diaz</a>, for example, are Hispanic even though they may be presumed not to be so because, being white, they do not fit the stereotype. If Hispanics with mostly <a href="/wiki/Caucasoid" class="mw-redirect" title="Caucasoid">Caucasoid</a> features are to be considered Hispanic, they have stereotypical <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_race" title="Mediterranean race">Mediterranean</a>/Southern European appearance - <a href="/wiki/Olive_skin" title="Olive skin">olive skin</a>, dark hair, and dark eyes.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A great proportion of Hispanics identify as <a href="/wiki/Mestizo" title="Mestizo">half-caste</a> (mixed European and Amerindian) regardless of national origin.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> This is largely because most Hispanics have their origins in majority half-caste Hispanic American countries. <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> are examples of mostly half-caste populations, with 90% of Salvadorans, 95% of Paraguayans, and 70% <sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> of Mexicans identifying as mestizo, with Mexico having the largest total mestizo population at over 66 million.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many individuals identified as Hispanics (based on the U.S. definition) are of unmixed <a href="/wiki/Amerindian" class="mw-redirect" title="Amerindian">Amerindian</a> ancestry. For example, many of those from <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> constitute a majority or plurality of the population as do a considerable proportion in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Many Hispanics born in or with descent from the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, and other countries may be of African descent, be it <a href="/wiki/Mulatto" title="Mulatto">mulatto</a> (mixed European and black African), <a href="/wiki/Zambo" title="Zambo">zambo</a> (mixed Amerindian and black African), <a href="/wiki/Multiracial" title="Multiracial">triracial</a> (specifically European, black African, and Amerindian), or unmixed black African. Recently, Hispanics of unmixed black African blood are perceived and defined by American mass media and popular culture as Hispanic, because of existing mulatto Hispanics of Negroid phenotypes and the dark-skinned stereotype of Hispanics.
</p><p>The majority of people in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> are largely of European descent; not only of <a href="/wiki/Spaniards" title="Spaniards">Spanish</a> Europeans, but <a href="/wiki/Italian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Italian people">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_people" title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_people" class="mw-redirect" title="German people">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Polish people">Polish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a>, etc. In countries like <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, there was a process of <a href="/wiki/Miscegenation" title="Miscegenation">miscegenation</a>, which resulted in many people having both indigenous and European origins. Many <a href="/wiki/White_Mexicans" class="mw-redirect" title="White Mexicans">white Mexicans</a>, though labeled <i>Hispanic</i> by the U.S. definition because of their assimilated culture and country of birth, trace their ancestries to European countries other than Spain, and some to non-European countries (see next paragraph). Nevertheless, in most cases, they have some Spanish ancestry, as the waves of European immigrants to these countries tended to quickly assimilate, intermarrying with the country's local population. (From 1850 to 1920, the U.S. Census form did not distinguish between whites and Mexican Americans.<sup id="cite_ref-rutgers_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rutgers-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>  In 1930, the U.S. Census form asked for "color or race," and census enumerators were instructed to write <i>W</i> for white and <i>Mex</i> for Mexican.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>  In 1940 and 1950, the census reverted its decision and made Mexicans be classified as white again and thus the instructions were to "Report <i>white</i> (W) for Mexicans unless they were definitely of full Indigenous Indian or other non-white races (such as Black or Asian)."<sup id="cite_ref-rutgers_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rutgers-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>) Initially colonial <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> had predominately mestizo population, but because of a massive European migration (mostly Spaniards and Italians, with Germans, British, Polish, etc.) in the 19th century, and the repeated intermarriage with white Europeans and Middle Easterners, like white Mexicans, they also have some Spanish blood, wherein the mestizo population became a so-called <a href="/wiki/Castizo" title="Castizo">castizo</a> population; with more European settlers arriving in the early 20th century, the population of Argentina, Uruguay, and Chile has overwhelmingly become white and European (some Middle Eastern) in race, culture and tradition.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Like mentioned above, the Hispanics of Caucasian race, including white Mexicans, white Argentines, white Uruguayans, and white Chileans, and Spaniards themselves, who are perceived by U.S. mass media as well as general U.S. popular culture as Hispanic usually possess stereotypical Mediterranean appearance (Hispanics of other Southern European descent, mostly descendants of Italian and Portuguese settlers and quite a large number descendants of Greek settlers, are even perceived by U.S. definition as Hispanic)
</p><p>Likewise, a percentage of Hispanics as defined by the U.S. government trace their ancestries to the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, for example <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuadorians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexicans</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanese</a> or <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Palestinian people">Palestinian</a> ancestry; Hispanics of Middle Eastern ancestry are usually to be considered Hispanic by the U.S. government, because they possess stereotypical Southern European appearance. Many Hispanics are of <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asian</a> ancestry, as in the case of <a href="/wiki/Cubans" title="Cubans">Cubans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexicans" title="Mexicans">Mexicans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panamanians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peruvians</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> descent. If they were to migrate to the United States, the definition most frequently advocated would consider them Hispanic (<i>see also: <a href="/wiki/Asian_Latin_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian Latin American">Asian Hispanic American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asian_Hispanic_and_Latino_Americans" title="Asian Hispanic and Latino Americans">Asian Hispanic and Latino Americans</a></i>). A percentage of Hispanics are from <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander" title="Pacific Islander">Pacific Islander</a> descent, mostly <a href="/wiki/Rapa_Nui_people" title="Rapa Nui people">Rapa Nui</a> from Chilean territorial possession of <a href="/wiki/Easter_Island" title="Easter Island">Easter Island</a>; Hispanics of Pacific Islander descent are also considered as Hispanic based on definition of U.S. government because they are brown-skinned and black-haired.
</p><p>The presence of these mentioned ethnic groups are not country-specific, since they can be found in every Hispanic American country, whether as larger of smaller proportions of their respective populations. Even in Spain, the European <a href="/wiki/Motherland" class="mw-redirect" title="Motherland">motherland</a> of Hispanicity, recent decades has seen a growing population of mestizos and mulattoes and of unmixed Amerindian and African descent due to the reversal of the historic <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a>-to-<a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a> migration pattern, i.e.: Latin American immigration to Spain.
</p><p>Of the over 35 million Hispanics counted in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2000" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Census, 2000">Federal 2000 Census</a>, 47.9% identified as <a href="/wiki/White_(people)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (people)">White</a> (termed <a href="/wiki/White_Hispanic_and_Latino_Americans" title="White Hispanic and Latino Americans">White Hispanic</a> by the Census Bureau); 42.2% some other race; 6.3% two or more races; 2% Black or African American; 1.2% American Indian and Alaska Native; 0.3% Asian; and 0.1% Native Hawaiian and Other Pacific Islander.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Note that even among those Hispanics who reported one race only, most would also possess at least some ancestral lineage from one or more other races, despite the fact that only 6.3% reported as such (this is also applicable to the non-Hispanics counted in the U.S. Census, although maybe in less proportion).
</p><p>According to one study (Stephens et al. 2001), from the genetic perspective, Hispanics generally represent a differential mixture of European, Native American, and African ancestry, with the proportionate mix typically depending on country of origin.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The populations of Iberia (both Spain and Portugal), like all European populations, have received multiple other influences, even though they are still largely descended from the prehistoric European populations, and to a greater degree than any other major group.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>  The ancestry of Iberians has thus received many, (limited and often very localized) influences from the many people who settled on its territory throughout history, including <a href="/wiki/Phoenicians" class="mw-redirect" title="Phoenicians">Phoenicians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greeks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Romans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punics" title="Punics">Punics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suebi" title="Suebi">Suebi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buri_(Germanic_tribe)" class="mw-redirect" title="Buri (Germanic tribe)">Buri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alans" title="Alans">Alans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" class="mw-redirect" title="Slavic peoples">Slavs</a> (<a href="/wiki/Saqaliba" title="Saqaliba">saqaliba</a>), <a href="/wiki/Berber_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Berber people">Berbers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magyars" class="mw-redirect" title="Magyars">Magyars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a>, and—particularly in Andalusia—<a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Roma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Angola">Angola</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Angola">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The former Portuguese colony has a community of Afro-Cubans known as Amparos. They descend from Cuban soldiers brought to the country in 1975 as a result of the Cuban involvement in the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Castro" title="Fidel Castro">Fidel Castro</a> deployed thousands of troops to the country during the <a href="/wiki/Angolan_Civil_War" title="Angolan Civil War">Angolan Civil War</a>. As a result of this era, there exists a small Spanish-speaking community in <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> of Afro-Cubans numbering about 100,000.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Equatorial_Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Equatorial Guinea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Guinea" title="Spanish Guinea">Spanish Guinea</a></div>
<p>In the former Spanish province of <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>, although Portuguese and French are co-official languages, the majority of the population speak Spanish.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> There is a small minority of African people who possessed Spanish and other European ancestry. These individuals form less than 1% of the population.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Morocco">Morocco</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Morocco">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Morocco" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Morocco">Spanish Morocco</a></div>
<p>In the former Spanish protectorate of <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, Spanish speakers are present in small numbers, located in the northern coastal region of the country. However the majority of Moroccan people are predominantly Arabic speaking Muslims of <a href="/wiki/Berber_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Berber people">Berber</a> and African ancestry.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nigeria">Nigeria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Nigeria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The small Amaro population are descendants of repatriated <a href="/wiki/Afro-Cuban" title="Afro-Cuban">Afro-Cuban</a> indentured servants, they were called <i><a href="/wiki/Saro_(Nigeria)#Amaros" title="Saro (Nigeria)">Amaros</a></i>.  Despite being free to return to <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> when their tenure was over, they remained in these countries, marrying into the local native population.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish_territories_in_North_Africa">Spanish territories in North Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Spanish territories in North Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_cities_of_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous cities of Spain">Autonomous cities of Spain</a></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Plazas_de_soberan%C3%ADa" title="Plazas de soberanía">Plazas de soberanía</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ceuta" title="Ceuta">Ceuta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melilla" title="Melilla">Melilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chafarinas_Islands" title="Chafarinas Islands">Chafarinas Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pe%C3%B1%C3%B3n_de_Alhucemas" class="mw-redirect" title="Peñón de Alhucemas">Peñón de Alhucemas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pe%C3%B1%C3%B3n_de_V%C3%A9lez_de_la_Gomera" title="Peñón de Vélez de la Gomera">Peñón de Vélez de la Gomera</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a></div>
<p>Since the <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Reconquista</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> has held numerous emplacements in <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>. Most of them were promptly lost, but to date, with an approximate population of 143,000 people, the <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_Communities_of_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous Communities of Spain">autonomous cities</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ceuta" title="Ceuta">Ceuta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Melilla" title="Melilla">Melilla</a>, which constitute the two <i>plazas de soberanía mayores</i> (Major Territories under [Spanish] Sovereignty) remained Spanish, and the <a href="/wiki/Chafarinas_Islands" title="Chafarinas Islands">Chafarinas Islands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pe%C3%B1%C3%B3n_de_Alhucemas" class="mw-redirect" title="Peñón de Alhucemas">Peñón de Alhucemas</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pe%C3%B1%C3%B3n_de_V%C3%A9lez_de_la_Gomera" title="Peñón de Vélez de la Gomera">Peñón de Vélez de la Gomera</a>, which constitute the three <i>plazas de soberanía menores</i> (Minor Territories under [Spanish] Sovereignty), still forming part of Spain. The <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, a constituent part of Spain's main territorial subdivisions, are also located in North Africa.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Western_Sahara">Western Sahara</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Western Sahara">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Sahara" title="Spanish Sahara">Spanish Sahara</a></div>
<p>In the former Spanish province of <a href="/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a>, Spanish is <i>de facto</i> official (however, in the <a href="/wiki/Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic" title="Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic">Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic</a>, one of the claimants to the territory, it is <i>de jure</i> co-official).<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Most Arabic speakers speak Spanish as second language.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Philippines">Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Filipino" title="Spanish Filipino">Spanish Filipino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Spanish" title="Philippine Spanish">Philippine Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chavacano" title="Chavacano">Chavacano</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_the_Philippines" title="Captaincy General of the Philippines">Captaincy General of the Philippines</a></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Filipino" title="Spanish Filipino">Spanish Filipino</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Filipino_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Filipino people">Filipino</a> who has Spanish or Hispanic lineage and descent, mostly born and raised in the Philippines. Most common languages spoken today by Spanish Filipinos are <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Spanish" title="Philippine Spanish">Philippine Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>; <a href="/wiki/Chavacano" title="Chavacano">Chavacano</a>, the only Spanish-based creole language in Asia and is spoken by over a million people; and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, which is used in the public sphere. A number of Spanish Filipinos also speak other <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_the_Philippines" title="Languages of the Philippines">Philippine languages</a>.
</p><p>Section 7, Article XIV of the <a href="/wiki/1987_Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 Constitution">1987 Philippine Constitution</a> specifies Spanish (along with <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a>) a language to "be promoted on a voluntary and optional basis", while the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Academy_of_the_Spanish_Language" title="Philippine Academy of the Spanish Language">Philippine Academy of the Spanish Language</a> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Academia Filipina de la Lengua Española</i>) remains the state regulating body for the language. <a href="/wiki/Castilian_Spanish" title="Castilian Spanish">Castilian Spanish</a> is the sole dialectal standard taught in schools, while <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Spanish" title="Philippine Spanish">Philippine Spanish</a> currently has a few thousand native speakers left.
</p><p>Despite its rapid decline in the 20th century, there has been a revival of interest in the Spanish language in the first decade of the 21st century. Since the rule of President <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Macapagal-Arroyo" class="mw-redirect" title="Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo">Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo</a> (herself a fluent speaker), Spanish is slowly being re-introduced into the <a href="/wiki/Education_in_the_Philippines" title="Education in the Philippines">educational system</a>, with a revival of Spanish-language media including <i>Filipinas Ahora Mismo</i> (<a href="/wiki/Tagalog_language" title="Tagalog language">Tagalog</a>: <i lang="tl">Filipinas Ora Mismo</i>), a radio programme broadcast on <a href="/wiki/Radio_Manila_FM" title="Radio Manila FM">Radio Manila FM</a>.
</p><p>Many <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_the_Philippines" title="Languages of the Philippines">Philippine languages</a> including <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">Filipino</a> notably contain numerous <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language_in_the_Philippines#Influence_on_the_languages_of_the_Philippines" title="Spanish language in the Philippines">loanwords of Spanish origin</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pacific_Islands">Pacific Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Pacific Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="Easter_Island_.28Rapa_Nui.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Easter_Island_(Rapa_Nui)">Easter Island (Rapa Nui)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Easter Island (Rapa Nui)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Spanish is the official language of <a href="/wiki/Easter_Island" title="Easter Island">Easter Island</a>, a territorial possession of <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> in <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesia</a>, Easter Island is the only place in Austronesian world where people fluently speak Spanish.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mariana_Islands">Mariana Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hispanophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Mariana Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mariana_Islands" title="Mariana Islands">Mariana Islands</a> (today split between the <a href="/wiki/United_States_territory" title="United States territory">United States territory</a> of <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_the_Northern_Mariana_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands">Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands</a>) were formerly governed as a part of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_East_Indies" title="Spanish East Indies">Spanish East Indies</a>, and thus many <a href="/wiki/Chamorros" class="mw-redirect" title="Chamorros">Chamorros</a> possess some degree of Spanish admixture.
</p><p>While most people living on these islands no longer speak Spanish, the native <a href="/wiki/Chamorro_language" title="Chamorro language">Chamorro language</a> exhibits a noticeable Spanish influence in its vocabulary. Many Chamorros have also preserved Hispanic cultural elements such as <a href="/wiki/Party" title="Party">fiestas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cockfight" title="Cockfight">cockfighting</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> faith despite having integrated with the American way of life.
</p><p>Spanish surnames are still prevalent on Guam, it is spoken by Catholic people and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Ricans" title="Puerto Ricans">Puerto Ricans</a>, and the custom of women keeping their <a href="/wiki/Maiden_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Maiden name">maiden names</a> after marriage is a both byproduct of Spanish culture on these islands as well as the <a href="/wiki/Matrilineal" class="mw-redirect" title="Matrilineal">matrilineal</a> structure of indigenous <a href="/wiki/Chamorro_people" title="Chamorro people">Chamorro</a> culture.
</p>
