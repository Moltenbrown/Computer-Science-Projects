<p><b>Schneider</b> (German for "tailor", literally "someone who cuts", from the verb <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/schneiden" class="extiw" title="wikt:schneiden">schneiden</a></i> "to cut") is a very common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Alternative spellings include: Schneyder, Schnieder, <a href="/wiki/Snyder_(surname)" title="Snyder (surname)">Snyder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snider_(surname)" title="Snider (surname)">Snider</a>, Sneider, <a href="/wiki/Schnyder" title="Schnyder">Schnyder</a>, Znaider,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Schnaider, <a href="/wiki/Schneiter" title="Schneiter">Schneiter</a>, Shneider, <a href="/wiki/Sneijder" title="Sneijder">Sneijder</a> (Dutch), Snither (English), <a href="/wiki/Snyman" title="Snyman">Snyman</a> (Afrikaans), Schnider (Swiss German), Sznajder (Polish), Szneider, Snaider.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_distribution">Geographical distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schneider_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geographical distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2014, 57.9% of all known bearers of the surname <i>Schneider</i> were residents of Germany (frequency 1:184), 18.8% of the United States (1:2,554), 6.1% of Brazil (1:4,446), 3.6% of Switzerland (1:299), 3.6% of France (1:2,452), 2.6% of Austria (1:443), 1.3% of Canada (1:3,837) and 1.0% of Argentina (1:5,820).
</p><p>In Germany, the frequency of the surname was higher than national average (1:184) in the following states:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
