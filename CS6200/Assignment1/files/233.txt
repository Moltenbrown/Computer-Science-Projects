<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_and_variants">Origins and variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=King_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins and variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The English name may be related to the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> word for a tribal leader, <i>cyning</i>, which derives from the <a href="/wiki/Proto-Germanic" class="mw-redirect" title="Proto-Germanic">Proto-Germanic</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Kuningaz" class="mw-redirect" title="Kuningaz">kuningaz</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Irish_name" title="Irish name">Irish</a> surname "King" is associated with <a href="/wiki/County_Galway" title="County Galway">County Galway</a> and was often given to <a href="/wiki/Irish-American" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish-American">Irish-American</a> immigrants as an <a href="/wiki/Anglicized" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicized">anglicized</a> form of the surname "<a href="/wiki/Conroy_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect" title="Conroy (disambiguation)">Conroy</a>", among others.
</p><p>The Scottish surname "King" is a <a href="/wiki/Sept" title="Sept">sept</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Clan_Gregor" title="Clan Gregor">Clan Gregor</a> / MacGregor.
</p>
