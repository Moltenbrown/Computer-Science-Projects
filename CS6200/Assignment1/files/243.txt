<p><b>Chandler</b>, and its variant spellings, is a family name that originated as an <a href="/wiki/Occupational_surname" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupational surname">occupational surname</a> in medieval England. It applied to a person involved in making or selling <a href="/wiki/Candle" title="Candle">candles</a> and similar articles. The earliest records as a surname are of Matthew le Candeler in London in 1274 and William le Chandeler in Essex in 1275.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In the 1881 census of England, the surname Chandler was apparently used by over 0.3% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-one-name_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-one-name-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
