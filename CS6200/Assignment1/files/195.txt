	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Fox (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p><b>Fox</b> or <b>Foxe</b> or <b>Foxx</b> is a surname originating in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. 
The derivation is from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a> "fox", itself coming from the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> pre 7th century "fox". The surname first appears on record in the latter part of the 13th century, with the first recorded spelling in 1273 to be that of John Fox in the "Hundred Rolls of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>", England.<sup id="cite_ref-REC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-REC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In Ireland, Fox is mainly a translation of the Old Gaelic "Mac a'tSionnaigh" (son of the Fox).<sup id="cite_ref-REC_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-REC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
