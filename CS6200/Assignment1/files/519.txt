<p><b>National Family Week</b> is an annual event in the <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a> which celebrates family life and encourages families to spend more time together while also highlighting the important role families play in society. The week consists of thousands of local events organised across the UK by supporting organisations including local authorities, sports clubs, charities, community and religious groups. <i>National Family Week</i> is endorsed by but does not receive funding from the <a href="/wiki/UK_government" class="mw-redirect" title="UK government">UK government</a> so funding for the initiative comes through commercial sponsorship.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisers">Organisers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organisers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>National Family Week</i> was founded in 2009 by Nick Henry and Jane Nethersole of Henley Media Group<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and is run by Family Week Limited. It mirrors the established <a href="/wiki/National_Family_Week" title="National Family Week">Canadian National Family Week</a> which has been established since 1985. Unlike the Canadian week which is funded by taxpayers, the UK week is supported wholly by commercial sponsorship and the support of a large network of local, regional and national organisations with Family Week Ltd co-ordinating the activity .<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Family_Week_2009">National Family Week 2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: National Family Week 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>National Family Week</i> 2009 consisted of over 4,000 events nationwide including a favourite family recipe competition, Family of the Year competition and search to find the next Big Family Idea where the <a href="/wiki/Big_Lottery_Fund" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Lottery Fund">Big Lottery Fund</a> donated £150,000 to go towards turning the winning ideas into a reality.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 2009 also included a successful <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Record" class="mw-redirect" title="Guinness World Record">Guinness World Record</a> attempt for the largest picnic across multiple venues which was attended by ambassadors <a href="/wiki/Sally_Gunnell" title="Sally Gunnell">Sally Gunnell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antony_Worrall_Thompson" title="Antony Worrall Thompson">Antony Worrall Thompson</a> amongst other celebrities achieving 12,934 participants in 79 venues across the UK. Sponsors of the 2009 campaign included <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury&#39;s">Sainsbury's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bupa" title="Bupa">Bupa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vue_(cinema)" class="mw-redirect" title="Vue (cinema)">Vue (cinema)</a>, Outwell, Seat and <a href="/wiki/Legoland" title="Legoland">Legoland</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Family_Week_2010">National Family Week 2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: National Family Week 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Over 5,000 events took place during <i>National Family Week</i> 2010 and competitions were launched to find the best family photo and most talented family pet. A new <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Record" class="mw-redirect" title="Guinness World Record">Guinness World Record</a> was also set for the largest three-legged race<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> across multiple venues which included Twickenham stadium and the City of Manchester stadium with at total of 1,860 participants at 18 venues across the UK, on 5 June 2010. Sponsors of 2010 included <a href="/wiki/Tesco_Mobile" title="Tesco Mobile">Tesco Mobile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hasbro" title="Hasbro">Hasbro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vue_(cinema)" class="mw-redirect" title="Vue (cinema)">Vue (cinema)</a>.
</p>
<dl><dt>2010 Family Survey</dt></dl>
<p>UK parents spend on average 49 minutes a day together with their children.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
Websites such as Facebook have become a major influence in the lives of teenage girls - much more influential than television, magazines, celebrities and even their own brothers and sisters.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Family_Week_2011">National Family Week 2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: National Family Week 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>National Family Week 2011 was a huge success; one headline event in Liverpool, ten flagship events and over 6,000 local events took place all over the UK attracting more than one million people. The campaign's research once again raised mass awareness of issues impacting family life in the media and highlighted the importance of family life. The official Headline Sponsor of the 2011 campaign was French's Mustard.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Merchandise">Merchandise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Merchandise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Free <i>National Family Week</i> goodie bags are handed out at major events and contain freebies for kids, a voucher booklet and Family Time! magazine. Organisations co-ordinating events under the umbrella of <i>National Family Week</i> are also supplied with free merchandise, balloons, stickers, posters and banners to help promote their events.
</p>
