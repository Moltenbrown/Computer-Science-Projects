	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Spouse</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Holy_Matrimony.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Holy_Matrimony.JPG/220px-Holy_Matrimony.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="249" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Holy_Matrimony.JPG/330px-Holy_Matrimony.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/57/Holy_Matrimony.JPG 2x" data-file-width="336" data-file-height="380" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Holy_Matrimony.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A religious <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marriage</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>A <b>spouse</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Significant_other" title="Significant other">significant other</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marriage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Civil_union" title="Civil union">civil union</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Common-law_marriage" title="Common-law marriage">common-law marriage</a>. The term is <a href="/wiki/Gender_neutral" class="mw-redirect" title="Gender neutral">gender neutral</a>, whereas a male spouse is a <a href="/wiki/Husband" title="Husband">husband</a> and a female spouse is a <a href="/wiki/Wife" title="Wife">wife</a>. Although a spouse is a form of <a href="/wiki/Significant_other" title="Significant other">significant other</a>, the latter term also includes non-marital partners who play a social role similar to that of a spouse, but do not have rights and duties reserved by law to a spouse.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_status">Legal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Legal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Coverture" title="Coverture">Coverture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marital_power" title="Marital power">Marital power</a></div>
<p>The legal status of a spouse, and the specific rights and obligations associated with that status, vary significantly among the jurisdictions of the world. These regulations are usually described in <a href="/wiki/Family_law" title="Family law">family law</a> statutes. However, in many parts of the world, where <a href="/wiki/Civil_marriage" title="Civil marriage">civil marriage</a> is not that prevalent, there is instead customary marriage, which is usually regulated informally by the community. In many parts of the world, spousal rights and obligations are related to the payment of <a href="/wiki/Bride_price" title="Bride price">bride price</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dowry" title="Dowry">dowry</a> or <a href="/wiki/Dower" title="Dower">dower</a>. Historically, many societies have given sets of rights and obligations to male marital partners that have been very different from the sets of rights and obligations given to female marital partners. In particular, the control of marital property, inheritance rights, and the right to dictate the activities of children of the marriage, have typically been given to male marital partners. However, this practice was curtailed to a great deal in many countries in the twentieth century, and more modern statutes tend to define the rights and duties of a spouse without reference to gender.  Among the last European countries to establish full <a href="/wiki/Gender_equality" title="Gender equality">gender equality</a> in marriage were Switzerland,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Greece,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Spain,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and France <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> in the 1980s. In various <a href="/wiki/Marriage_laws" class="mw-redirect" title="Marriage laws">marriage laws</a> around the world, however, the husband continues to have authority; for instance the Civil Code of <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> states at Article 1105: <i>"In relations between husband and wife; the position of the head of the family is the exclusive right of the husband"</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Depending on jurisdiction, the refusal or inability of a spouse to perform the marital obligations may constitute a <a href="/wiki/Ground_for_divorce" class="mw-redirect" title="Ground for divorce">ground for divorce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Legal_separation" title="Legal separation">legal separation</a> or <a href="/wiki/Annulment" title="Annulment">annulment</a>. The latter two options are more prevalent in countries where the dominant religion is <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</a>, some of which introduced divorce only recently (i.e. <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> in 1970, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> in 1975, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> in 1977,  <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in 1981, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> in 1987,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a> in 1991,<sup id="cite_ref-auto_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> in 1991,<sup id="cite_ref-auto_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> in 1996, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> in 2004<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a> in 2011). In recent years, many Western countries have adopted <a href="/wiki/No_fault_divorce" class="mw-redirect" title="No fault divorce">no fault divorce</a>. In some parts of the world, the formal dissolution of a marriage is complicated by the payments and goods which have been exchanged between families (this is common where <a href="/wiki/Arranged_marriage" title="Arranged marriage">marriages are arranged</a>). This often makes it difficult to leave a marriage, especially for the woman: in some parts of Africa, once the bride price has been paid, the wife is seen as belonging to the husband and his family; and if she wants to leave, the husband may demand back the bride price that he had paid to the girl's family. The girl's family often cannot or does not want to pay it back.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Regardless of legislation, personal relations between spouses may also be influenced by local <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religion</a>, which may promote male authority over the wife: for instance the word <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D7%91%D7%A2%D7%9C" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:בעל">בעל</a> (ba`al), Hebrew for <i>husband</i>, used throughout the Bible, is synonymous with <i>owner</i> and <i>master</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Minimum_age">Minimum age</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Minimum age">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is often a minimum legal <a href="/wiki/Marriageable_age" title="Marriageable age">marriageable age</a>. The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Population_Fund" title="United Nations Population Fund">United Nations Population Fund</a> stated the following:<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd>"In 2010, 158 countries reported that 18 years was the minimum legal age for marriage for women without parental consent or approval by a pertinent authority. However, in 146 countries, state or customary law allows girls younger than 18 to marry with the consent of parents or other authorities; in 52 countries, girls under age 15 can marry with parental consent. In contrast, 18 is the legal age for marriage without consent among males in 180 countries. Additionally, in 105 countries, boys can marry with the consent of a parent or a pertinent authority, and in 23 countries, boys under age 15 can marry with parental consent."</dd></dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Procreation">Procreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Procreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although in Western countries spouses sometimes <a href="/wiki/Childfree" class="mw-redirect" title="Childfree">choose not to have children</a>, such a choice is not accepted in some parts of the world.  In some cultures and religions, the quality of a spouse imposes an obligation to have children. In <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ghana" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Ghana">northern Ghana</a>, for example, the payment of bride price signifies a woman's requirement to bear children, and women using birth control are at risks of threats and coercion.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Choosing_a_spouse">Choosing a spouse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Choosing a spouse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are many ways in which a spouse is chosen, which vary across the world, and include <a href="/wiki/Love_marriage" title="Love marriage">love marriage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arranged_marriage" title="Arranged marriage">arranged marriage</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Forced_marriage" title="Forced marriage">forced marriage</a>. The latter is in some jurisdictions a <a href="/wiki/Void_marriage" title="Void marriage">void marriage</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Voidable_marriage" title="Voidable marriage">voidable marriage</a>. Forcing someone to marry is also a criminal offense in some countries.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
