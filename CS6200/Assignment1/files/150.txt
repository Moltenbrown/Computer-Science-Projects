	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Olsen (surname)</h1>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Olsen</b> (<small>Danish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Danish" title="Help:IPA/Danish">[ˈoːˀlsn̩]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>-<a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a> <a href="/wiki/Patronymic_surname" title="Patronymic surname">patronymic surname</a> meaning "son of <a href="/wiki/Ole_(name)" title="Ole (name)">Ole</a>". The surname <b>Olesen</b> (<small>pronounced&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Danish" title="Help:IPA/Danish">[ˈoːləsn̩]</a></span>) has a similar origin. The <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a> parallel form is <a href="/wiki/Olsson" title="Olsson">Olsson</a>&#160;– "son of <a href="/wiki/Ola_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Ola (disambiguation)">Ola</a>". <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a> immigrants to <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>-speaking countries often changed the spelling to <a href="/wiki/Olson_(surname)" title="Olson (surname)">Olson</a> in order to accommodate <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English orthographic rules</a>.
</p><p>Notable people with the surname include:
