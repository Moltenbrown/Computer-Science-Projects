<p><b>Clark</b> is an <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a> <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>, ultimately derived from the <a href="/wiki/Latin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin language">Latin</a> <i>clericus</i> meaning "scribe", "secretary" or a scholar within a religious order, referring to someone who was educated. <i>Clark</i> evolved from "<a href="/wiki/Clerk_(position)" class="mw-redirect" title="Clerk (position)">clerk</a>". First records of the name are found in 12th-century England. The name has many variants.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Clark</i> is the twenty-seventh most common surname in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-BRE_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BRE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> including placing fourteenth in Scotland.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Clark is also an occasional <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>, as in the case of <a href="/wiki/Clark_Gable" title="Clark Gable">Clark Gable</a>.
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/1990_United_States_Census" title="1990 United States Census">1990 United States Census</a>, <i>Clark</i> was the twenty-first most frequently encountered surname, accounting for 0.23% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Notable people with the surname include:
