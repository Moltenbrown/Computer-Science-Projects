		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Hawkins</caption><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Meaning</th><td>"<a href="/wiki/Hawking_(falconry)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hawking (falconry)">falconry</a>"</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Region of origin</th><td>Kent, England</td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Other names</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Variant form(s)</th><td>Hawking</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a> <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> <b>Hawkins</b> originated in the 11th century in <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. Its meaning comes from the word "<a href="/wiki/Falconry" title="Falconry">hawking</a>", meaning "falconry". Hawkins may have evolved to the variant "Haughan" or "O'haughan" due to migration of peoples to <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> during the Civil War in the 16th century. It is rare as a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>.
</p>
