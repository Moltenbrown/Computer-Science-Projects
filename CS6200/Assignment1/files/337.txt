	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">White (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Whyte_(surname)" title="Whyte (surname)">Whyte (surname)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wight_(surname)" title="Wight (surname)">Wight (surname)</a></div>
<p><b>White</b> is a surname either of English<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> or of Scottish and Irish origin, the latter being an anglicisation of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a> <i>MacGillebhàin</i>, "Son of the fair gillie" and the Irish "Mac Faoitigh" or "de Faoite".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It is the seventeenth most common surname in England.<sup id="cite_ref-fbd_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fbd-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the 1990 United States Census, "White" ranked fourteenth among all reported surnames in frequency, accounting for 0.28% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> By 2000, White had fallen to position 20 in the United States<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and 22nd position by 2014<sup id="cite_ref-fbd_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fbd-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
