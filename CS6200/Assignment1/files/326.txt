<p><b>Luis</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> form of the originally <a href="/wiki/Germanic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Germanic language">Germanic</a> name <i>Hludowig</i> or <i>Chlodovech</i>. Other <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Romance_languages" title="Iberian Romance languages">Iberian Romance languages</a> have comparable forms: <b>Luís</b> (with an accent mark on the i) in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">Galician</a>, <b>Lluís</b> in <a href="/wiki/Aragonese_language" title="Aragonese language">Aragonese</a> and <b>Lluís</b> in <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a>, while <b>Luiz</b> is archaic in Portugal, but common in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Germanic name <i>Chlodowig</i> (and its variants) is usually said to be composed of the words for "fame" (<i>hlūd</i>) and "warrior" (<i>wīg</i>) and hence may be translated to <i>famous warrior</i> or "famous in battle".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> According to Dutch onomatologists however, it is more likely that the first stem was <i>(h)lod-</i>, meaning fame, which would give the meaning <i>warrior for the gods (or: </i>warrior who captured stability<i>) for the full name.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></i>
</p><p>Modern forms of the name are the <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> name <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_(given_name)" title="Ludwig (given name)">Ludwig</a> and the Dutch form <a href="/wiki/Lodewijk" title="Lodewijk">Lodewijk</a>. <i>Luis</i> and the other Iberian forms more closely resemble the <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> name <a href="/wiki/Louis_(given_name)" title="Louis (given name)">Louis</a>, a derivation of the <a href="/wiki/Latinisation_of_names" title="Latinisation of names">Latinized</a> form <a href="/wiki/Clovis_(given_name)" title="Clovis (given name)">Clovis</a>, known from the kings of the Franks. The sanctified French king <a href="/wiki/Louis_IX_of_France" title="Louis IX of France">Louis IX</a> (Saint Louis) contributed much to the popularity of the name.
</p>
