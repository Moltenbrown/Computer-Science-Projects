<p><b>Kerch</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Керч</span>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Керчь</span>, <small>pronounced&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Russian" title="Help:IPA/Russian">[ˈkʲɛrtɕ]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Old_East_Slavic" title="Old East Slavic">Old East Slavic</a>: Кърчевъ, <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a>: <span lang="grc" title="Ancient Greek language text">Παντικάπαιον</span>, <i>Pantikápaion</i>; <a href="/wiki/Crimean_Tatar_language" title="Crimean Tatar language">Crimean Tatar</a>: <span lang="crh"><i lang="crh-Latn" title="Crimean Tatar language text">Keriç</i>, <span lang="crh-Cyrl" title="Crimean Tatar language text">Керич</span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <i lang="tr">Kerç</i>) is a city of regional significance on the <a href="/wiki/Kerch_Peninsula" title="Kerch Peninsula">Kerch Peninsula</a> in the east of the <a href="/wiki/Crimea" title="Crimea">Crimea</a>.
Population: <span class="nowrap">147,033&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Crimean_Federal_District_Census_(2014)" title="Crimean Federal District Census (2014)">2014 Census</a>)</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-2014Census_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2014Census-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Founded 2,600 years ago as an <a href="/wiki/Colonies_in_antiquity#Greek_colonies" title="Colonies in antiquity">ancient Greek colony</a>, Kerch is considered to be one of the most ancient cities in Crimea. The city experienced rapid growth starting in the 1920s and was the site of a <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Kerch_Peninsula" title="Battle of the Kerch Peninsula">major battle</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.
</p><p>Today, it is one of the largest cities in Crimea and is among the republic's most important industrial, transport and tourist centres.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_times">Ancient times</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ancient times">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Archeological digs at Mayak village near the city ascertained that the area had already been inhabited in 17th–15th centuries BC.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kerch_Pantikapaeum.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Kerch_Pantikapaeum.jpg/250px-Kerch_Pantikapaeum.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Kerch_Pantikapaeum.jpg/375px-Kerch_Pantikapaeum.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/26/Kerch_Pantikapaeum.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="375" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kerch_Pantikapaeum.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ruins of <a href="/wiki/Panticapaeum" title="Panticapaeum">Panticapaeum</a>. 6th century BC</div></div></div>
<p>Kerch as a city starts its history in 7th century BC, when <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greek colonists</a> from <a href="/wiki/Miletus" title="Miletus">Miletus</a> founded a city-state named <a href="/wiki/Panticapaeum" title="Panticapaeum">Panticapaeum</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> on <a href="/wiki/Mount_Mithridat" title="Mount Mithridat">Mount Mithridat</a> near the mouth of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Melek-Chesme&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Melek-Chesme (page does not exist)">Melek-Chesme river</a>. Panticapaeum subdued nearby cities and by 480 BC became a capital of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bosporus" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Bosporus">Kingdom of Bosporus</a>. Later, during the rule of <a href="/wiki/Mithradates_VI_Eupator" class="mw-redirect" title="Mithradates VI Eupator">Mithradates VI Eupator</a>, Panticapaeum for a short period of time became the capital of the much more powerful and extensive <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Pontus" title="Kingdom of Pontus">Kingdom of Pontus</a>.
</p><p>The city was located at the intersection of trade routes between the <a href="/wiki/Steppe" title="Steppe">steppe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. This caused it to grow rapidly. The city's main exports were grain and salted fish; wine-making was also common. Panticapaeum minted its own coins. According to extant documents the Melek-Chesme river (small and shallow nowadays) was navigable in Bosporan times, and sea <a href="/wiki/Galley" title="Galley">galleys</a> were able to enter the river. A large portion of the city's population was ethnically <a href="/wiki/Scythian" class="mw-redirect" title="Scythian">Scythian</a>, later <a href="/wiki/Sarmatian" class="mw-redirect" title="Sarmatian">Sarmatian</a>, as the large royal barrow (<a href="/wiki/Kurgan" title="Kurgan">Kurgan</a>) at <a href="/wiki/Kul-Oba" title="Kul-Oba">Kul-Oba</a> testifies.
</p><p>In the 1st century AD Panticapaeum and the Kingdom of Bosporus suffered from <a href="/wiki/Ostrogoth" class="mw-redirect" title="Ostrogoth">Ostrogoth</a> raids; then the city was devastated by the <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a> in AD 375.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Myrmekion" title="Myrmekion">Myrmekion</a> was founded in the eastern part of the Kerch, 4&#160;km NE of ancient <a href="/wiki/Panticapaeum" title="Panticapaeum">Panticapaeum</a>. The settlement was founded by <a href="/wiki/Ionians" title="Ionians">Ionians</a> in the first half of the 6th c. BC.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_Ages">Middle Ages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Middle Ages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Yenikale_fortress_in_Kerch.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Yenikale_fortress_in_Kerch.jpg/300px-Yenikale_fortress_in_Kerch.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Yenikale_fortress_in_Kerch.jpg/450px-Yenikale_fortress_in_Kerch.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Yenikale_fortress_in_Kerch.jpg/600px-Yenikale_fortress_in_Kerch.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="1253" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Yenikale_fortress_in_Kerch.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Yenikale fortress in Kerch</div></div></div>
<p>From the 6th century the city was under the control of the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>. By order of Emperor <a href="/wiki/Justinian_I" title="Justinian I">Justinian I</a>, a citadel named Bospor was built there. Bospor was the centre of a <a href="/wiki/Bishopric" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishopric">bishopric</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Bosporus" class="mw-redirect" title="Diocese of Bosporus">diocese of Bosporus</a> and developed under the influence of Greek <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>. In 576, it withstood a siege by the <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6kt%C3%BCrks" title="Göktürks">Göktürks</a> under Bokhan, aided by Anagai, the last <a href="/wiki/Khan_(title)" title="Khan (title)">khan</a> (ruler) of the <a href="/wiki/Uturgurs" class="mw-redirect" title="Uturgurs">Uturgurs</a> (tribe of <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a>).
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kerch_ChurchOfStJohn.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Kerch_ChurchOfStJohn.jpg/300px-Kerch_ChurchOfStJohn.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="244" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Kerch_ChurchOfStJohn.jpg/450px-Kerch_ChurchOfStJohn.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Kerch_ChurchOfStJohn.jpg/600px-Kerch_ChurchOfStJohn.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2461" data-file-height="2000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kerch_ChurchOfStJohn.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Church of St John the Baptist, 8th century</div></div></div>
<p>In the 7th century, the <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turkic</a> <a href="/wiki/Khazars" title="Khazars">Khazars</a> took control of Bospor, and the city was named <b>Karcha</b> from Turkic "karşı" meaning 'opposite, facing.' The main local government official during Khazar times was the <a href="/wiki/Tudun" title="Tudun">tudun</a>. Christianity was a major religion in Kerch during the period of Khazar rule. Kerch's Church of <a href="/wiki/John_the_Baptist" title="John the Baptist">St. John the Baptist</a> was founded in 717; thus, it is the oldest church in Ukraine.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  The "Church of the Apostles" existed during the late 8th and early 9th centuries, according to the "Life of the Apostle Andrew" by <a href="/wiki/Epiphanius_of_Salamis" title="Epiphanius of Salamis">Epiphanius of Salamis</a>.
</p><p>Following the fall of Khazaria to <a href="/wiki/Kievan_Rus%27" title="Kievan Rus&#39;">Kievan Rus'</a> in the late 10th century, Kerch became the centre of a Khazar <a href="/wiki/Successor-state" class="mw-redirect" title="Successor-state">successor-state</a>. Its ruler, <a href="/wiki/Georgius_Tzul" title="Georgius Tzul">Georgius Tzul</a>, was deposed by a <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a>-<a href="/wiki/Kievan_Rus%27" title="Kievan Rus&#39;">Rus</a> expedition in 1016.
</p><p>From the 10th century, the city was a <a href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" class="mw-redirect" title="Slavic peoples">Slavic</a> settlement named Korchev, which belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Tmutarakan" title="Tmutarakan">Tmutarakan</a> principality. Kerch was a center of trade between Russia', <a href="/wiki/Crimea" title="Crimea">Crimea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Orient" title="Orient">Orient</a>.
</p><p>In the 13th century, the Crimea including Korchev was invaded by <a href="/wiki/Mongol_invasion_of_Rus" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongol invasion of Rus">Mongols</a>. After Mongols, the city became the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">Genoese</a> colony of Cerco (Cherkio) in 1318 and served as a sea harbour, where townspeople worked at salt-works and fishery.
</p><p>In 1475, city was passed to the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>. During the Turkish rule Kerch fell into decay and served as a <a href="/wiki/Slave_trade" class="mw-redirect" title="Slave trade">slave-market</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It repeatedly suffered from raids of <a href="/wiki/Zaporizhian_Sich" class="mw-redirect" title="Zaporizhian Sich">Zaporizhian Cossacks</a>.
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span id="18th_.E2.80.93_20th_centuries"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="18th_–_20th_centuries">18th – 20th centuries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 18th – 20th centuries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In response to strengthening of <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian</a> military forces in <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Azov" title="Sea of Azov">Azov</a> area, the Turks built a fortress, named <a href="/wiki/Yenikale" class="mw-redirect" title="Yenikale">Yenikale</a>, near Kerch on the shore of <a href="/wiki/Kerch_Strait" title="Kerch Strait">Kerch Strait</a>. The fortress was completed by 1706. In 1771 the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Army" title="Imperial Russian Army">Imperial Russian Army</a> invaded <a href="/wiki/Crimea" title="Crimea">Crimea</a> and approached Yenikale. The Turks decided to abandon the fortress, though reinforcements from the Ottoman Empire had arrived a few days earlier. By the Peace <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Kuchuk-Kainarji" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Kuchuk-Kainarji">Treaty of Kuchuk-Kainarji</a> in 1774, Kerch and Yenikale were ceded to Russia. As a result, the Turkish heritage has been almost completely wiped out.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kerch_aivazovsky.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Kerch_aivazovsky.jpg/300px-Kerch_aivazovsky.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Kerch_aivazovsky.jpg/450px-Kerch_aivazovsky.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Kerch_aivazovsky.jpg/600px-Kerch_aivazovsky.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1910" data-file-height="1400" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kerch_aivazovsky.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Kerch</i> by <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Aivazovsky" title="Ivan Aivazovsky">Ivan Aivazovsky</a>, 1839</div></div></div>
<p>In 1790 Russian naval forces under the command of admiral <a href="/wiki/Fyodor_Fyodorovich_Ushakov" class="mw-redirect" title="Fyodor Fyodorovich Ushakov">Fyodor Ushakov</a> defeated the Turkish fleet at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kerch_Strait_(1790)" title="Battle of Kerch Strait (1790)">Battle of Kerch Strait</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Because of its location, from 1821 Kerch developed into an important trade and fishing port. The state museum of ancient times and a number of educational institutions were opened in the city. The ironwork factory was built in 1846 based on a huge <a href="/wiki/Iron_ore" title="Iron ore">iron ore</a> deposit found on Kerch Peninsula.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Crimean_War" title="Crimean War">Crimean War</a> the city was devastated by <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> forces in 1855.
</p><p>In the late 19th century, mechanical and <a href="/wiki/Cement" title="Cement">cement</a> factories were built, and tinned food and <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a> factories were established.
By 1900, Kerch was connected to a <a href="/wiki/Railroad" class="mw-redirect" title="Railroad">railroad</a> system, and the fairway of Kerch Strait was deepened and widened. At this time, the population had reached 33,000.
</p><p>After suffering a decline during the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">First World War</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Civil_War" title="Russian Civil War">Russian Civil War</a>, the city resumed its growth in the late 1920s, with the expansion of various industries, iron ore and metallurgy in particular, and by 1939 its population had reached 104,500.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kerch_in_World_War_II">Kerch in World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Kerch in World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Front_(World_War_II)" title="Eastern Front (World War II)">Eastern Front of World War II</a> from 1941 to 1945, Kerch was the site of heavy fighting between <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> and <a href="/wiki/Axis_powers" title="Axis powers">Axis</a> forces.
After fierce fighting, the city was taken by the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germans</a> in November 1941. On 31 December 1941 the <a href="/wiki/302nd_Rifle_Division_(Soviet_Union)" title="302nd Rifle Division (Soviet Union)">302nd Mountain Rifle Division</a> recaptured the city following a naval landing operation at Kamysh Burun, to the south of the city, five days earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1942 the Germans occupied the city again. The Red Army lost over 160,000 men, either killed or taken <a href="/wiki/POW" class="mw-redirect" title="POW">POW</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Kerch_Peninsula_(1942)" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of the Kerch Peninsula (1942)">Battle of the Kerch Peninsula</a>. On 31 October 1943 another Soviet naval landing operation was launched. Kerch returned to Soviet control on 11 April 1944.
</p><p>The German invaders killed about 15,000 citizens and deported another 14,000 during their occupation.
Evidence of German atrocities in Kerch was presented in the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_trials" title="Nuremberg trials">Nuremberg trials</a>. After the war, the city was awarded the title <a href="/wiki/Hero_City" title="Hero City">Hero City</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Defense_of_the_Adzhimushkay_quarry" title="Defense of the Adzhimushkay quarry">Adzhimushkay</a> <a href="/wiki/Catacombs" title="Catacombs">catacombs</a> (mines) in the city's suburbs were the site of <a href="/wiki/Guerrilla_warfare" title="Guerrilla warfare">guerrilla warfare</a> against the occupation. Thousands of soldiers and refugees found shelter inside, and were involved in counterattacks. Many of them died underground, including those who died of numerous alleged poison gas attacks. Later, a memorial was established on the site.
</p><p>On 11 November 2007 there was a great storm that passed through the city, causing much damage and an ecological disaster as a few ships, including an oil tanker, were shipwrecked and blocked the <a href="/wiki/Kerch_Strait" title="Kerch Strait">Kerch Strait</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_Russian_annexation">2014 Russian annexation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014 Russian annexation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Annexation_of_Crimea_by_the_Russian_Federation" title="Annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation">Annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Crimea" title="Crimea">Crimea</a> was annexed by <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> in early 2014 and the <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a>, formerly <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukrainian</a> territory since 1991, is now administered as two <a href="/wiki/Federal_subjects_of_Russia" title="Federal subjects of Russia">Russian federal subjects</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Crimea" title="Republic of Crimea">Republic of Crimea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Federal_cities_of_Russia" title="Federal cities of Russia">federal city</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sevastopol" title="Sevastopol">Sevastopol</a>. The international community has overwhelmingly condemned the military takeover. Five United Nations General Assembly Resolutions (<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.un.org/en/ga/search/view_doc.asp?symbol=A/RES/68/262">[1]</a><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.un.org/en/ga/search/view_doc.asp?symbol=A/RES/71/205">[2]</a><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.un.org/en/ga/search/view_doc.asp?symbol=A/RES/72/190">[3]</a><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.un.org/en/ga/search/view_doc.asp?symbol=A/RES/73/194">[4]</a><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.un.org/en/ga/search/view_doc.asp?symbol=A/RES/73/263">[5]</a>) confirmed the status of Crimea as part of the territory of Ukraine, condemned the occupation of Crimea by the Russian Federation and stated that the Russian annexation of Crimea would not be recognized. The United Nations also called upon all states, international organizations and specialized agencies not to recognize any alteration of the status of the Autonomous Republic of Crimea and the city of Sevastopol and to refrain from any action or dealing that might be interpreted as recognizing any such altered status.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kerch has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Cfa</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> with cool to cold winters and warm to hot summers.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city municipality stretches over a substantial area and includes several separate neighborhoods that are part of the Kerch city: Eltigen (Heroyevskoe), Kamysh-Burun (Arshyntsevo), <a href="/wiki/Port_Krym" title="Port Krym">Port Krym</a>, Adzhimushkai, and <a href="/wiki/Tuzla_Island" title="Tuzla Island">Tuzla Island</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Today Kerch is considered as a city of <a href="/wiki/Metallurgist" class="mw-redirect" title="Metallurgist">metallurgists</a>, shipbuilders and fishermen, and also has a significant tourism sector.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The largest enterprises in the city are:
</p>
<ul><li>Kerch Metallurgical Works Factory launched in 1900</li>
<li>Kamysh-Burun Iron Ore Plant</li>
<li>"Zaliv" ("Gulf") shipbuilding factory that produces and repairs tankers and cargo ships.</li></ul>
<p>Construction-materials, food processing, and light industries play a significant role in the city's economy. Kerch is also a fishing fleet base and an important processing centre for numerous fish products.
</p>
<ul><li>Kerch Aircraft Repair Plant KeARZ</li>
<li>KMZ KMK Metallurgical Plant, Kerch east</li>
<li>Oil and Gas extraction industry petrochemical and storage</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Zaliv_Shipbuilding_yard" class="mw-redirect" title="Zaliv Shipbuilding yard">Zaliv Shipyard</a>, (Kamysh Burun Zheleznogoroda)</li>
<li>Kvartz Quartz Glass Factory Plant KSZ (Glass (various) and optics, optical materials and instruments) (Kerch)</li>
<li>PSZ Albatros, PSZ KMPZ Vityaz Priladobudivni (Instruments and Instruments making, Machinery, Engineering)</li>
<li>Kamysh Burun Iron Metallurgy Plant</li>
<li>KMZ KMTP SV Fregat floating docks yard and ship repair yard</li>
<li>KSRZ uvas-trans floating docks yards and ship repair (also lesser Kerch ship repair yards around)</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kerch_MithridatesStair.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Kerch_MithridatesStair.jpg/300px-Kerch_MithridatesStair.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="274" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Kerch_MithridatesStair.jpg/450px-Kerch_MithridatesStair.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Kerch_MithridatesStair.jpg/600px-Kerch_MithridatesStair.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1275" data-file-height="1164" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kerch_MithridatesStair.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mithridates Staircase (19th century)</div></div></div>
<p>Because of its location on the shores of the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Azov" title="Sea of Azov">Azov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black</a> seas, Kerch became a popular summer resort among people of the former <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">USSR</a>. Also, several mud-cure sources are located near the city. Despite the seaside location, the tourist appeal of Kerch today is limited because of the industrial character of the city and associated pollution.
Despite the lack of beaches in the town's area, there are a lot of them at a distance of 20 minutes' travel by bus, train or taxi.
</p><p>Kerch has a number of impressive architectural and historical monuments. Ancient historical heritage of the city makes it attractive for scientific tourism. The most notable of Kerch's sights are:
</p>
<ul><li>Site of ancient settlement Pantikapaeum (5th century BC–3rd century AD).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Royal_Kurgan_(Kerch)" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Kurgan (Kerch)">Tsarskiy Kurghan</a> (4th century BC) – burial mound for one of the Bosporian kings</li>
<li>Church of <a href="/wiki/John_the_Baptist" title="John the Baptist">St. John the Baptist</a> (AD 717)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yenikale" class="mw-redirect" title="Yenikale">Fortress of Yenikale</a> (18th century)</li>
<li>The Great Mithridates Staircase leading on top of the <a href="/wiki/Mount_Mithridat" title="Mount Mithridat">Mount Mithridat</a>, contains 428 footsteps, built in 1833–1840 under the guidance of <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> architect <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexander_Digbi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alexander Digbi (page does not exist)">Alexander Digbi</a></li>
<li>Obelisk of Glory on the <a href="/wiki/Mount_Mithridat" title="Mount Mithridat">Mount Mithridat</a>, built after World War II</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lapidarium,_Kerch" title="Lapidarium, Kerch">Lapidarium</a></li>
<li>Memorial of heroic <a href="/wiki/Defense_of_the_Adzhimushkay_quarry" title="Defense of the Adzhimushkay quarry">guerilla warfare in Adzhimushkay mines</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kerch_(fortress)" title="Kerch (fortress)">Kerch Fortress</a>; restricted area in Soviet times but free to enter in present days. The fortress was built by the Russian military architect <a href="/wiki/Eduard_Totleben" title="Eduard Totleben">Totleben</a> in the middle of 19th century.</li>
<li>Sites of ancient settlements Mirmecium, Tiritaka and Nimphei. There are also some settlements which have gone underwater due to earthquakes.</li>
<li>So called <a href="/wiki/Demeter" title="Demeter">Demetra</a>'s Crypt, a crypt with numerous frescos dated 1st century BC.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kerch_Housing.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Kerch_Housing.jpg/200px-Kerch_Housing.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="182" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Kerch_Housing.jpg/300px-Kerch_Housing.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Kerch_Housing.jpg/400px-Kerch_Housing.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1426" data-file-height="1300" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kerch_Housing.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Private houses are common in Kerch</div></div></div>
<p>Kerch has a harbour on the Kerch Strait, which makes it a key to the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Azov" title="Sea of Azov">Sea of Azov</a>, several railroad terminals and a small airport. The <a href="/wiki/Kerch_Strait_ferry_line" title="Kerch Strait ferry line">Kerch Strait ferry line</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Kerch_Strait" title="Kerch Strait">Kerch Strait</a> was established in 1953, connecting Crimea and the <a href="/wiki/Krasnodar_Krai" title="Krasnodar Krai">Krasnodar Krai</a> (<a href="/wiki/Port_Krym" title="Port Krym">Port Krym</a> – <a href="/wiki/Port_Kavkaz" title="Port Kavkaz">Port Kavkaz</a> line); (as of November 2009) there are also plans for a Kerch-<a href="/wiki/Poti" title="Poti">Poti</a> ferry route.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
Tavrida Highway work in progress along Kerch railway (two rails) highway (four lanes) bridge to connect Rostov Krasnodar with peninsula.
</p><p>There are several ports in Kerch, including Kerch Maritime Trading Port, Kerch Maritime Fishing Port, <a href="/wiki/Port_Krym" title="Port Krym">Port Krym</a> (ferry crossing), Kamysh-Burun Port.
</p><p>The railroad terminals include: Kerch, Kerch I, Kerch Factory, Arshyntsevo, and Krym.
</p><p>Bus network connects Kerch to other cities in <a href="/wiki/Crimea" title="Crimea">Crimea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Krasnodar_Krai" title="Krasnodar Krai">Krasnodar Krai</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Crimean_Bridge">Crimean Bridge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Crimean Bridge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Crimean_Bridge" title="Crimean Bridge">Crimean Bridge</a></div>
<p>On 25 April 2010, Ukrainian President <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Yanukovych" title="Viktor Yanukovych">Viktor Yanukovych</a> and Russian President <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Medvedev" title="Dmitry Medvedev">Dmitry Medvedev</a> signed an agreement to build a bridge across the Kerch Strait. In January 2015, with Russia now in control, the contract for the construction of the bridge was awarded to <a href="/wiki/Arkady_Rotenberg" title="Arkady Rotenberg">Arkady Rotenberg</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Stroygazmontazh" title="Stroygazmontazh">S.G.M. Group</a>. In May 2015 construction commenced, and the road section of the bridge was opened to traffic in May 2018, with the rail section expected to be opened in 2019.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Archaeology">Archaeology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Archaeology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Panticapaea_excavations.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Panticapaea_excavations.JPG/300px-Panticapaea_excavations.JPG" decoding="async" width="300" height="225" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Panticapaea_excavations.JPG/450px-Panticapaea_excavations.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Panticapaea_excavations.JPG/600px-Panticapaea_excavations.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Panticapaea_excavations.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Excavations in Pantikapaeum</div></div></div>
<p>Archaeological digs in Kerch were launched under Russian auspices in the middle of the 19th century. Since then the site of ancient <a href="/wiki/Panticapaeum" title="Panticapaeum">Panticapaeum</a> city on <a href="/wiki/Mount_Mithridat" title="Mount Mithridat">Mount Mithridat</a> has been systematically excavated. Located nearby are several ancient burial mounds (<a href="/wiki/Kurgan" title="Kurgan">kurgans</a>) and excavated cities. Kerch takes part in <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a>" programme.<br />
Treasures and historical findings of Kerch adorn the collections of major museums around the world. Such as: the Hermitage, the Louvre, the British Museum, the Berlin Museum, the Moscow State Museum of fine arts and many others.<br />
Currently, excavations at ancient fortresses of Kerch are led by scientists from Russia, Ukraine, and Poland.
</p>
