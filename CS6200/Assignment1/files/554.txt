	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Pedigree chart</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p>A <b>pedigree chart</b> is a diagram that shows the occurrence and appearance of <a href="/wiki/Phenotype" title="Phenotype">phenotypes</a> of a particular gene or <a href="/wiki/Organism" title="Organism">organism</a> and its <a href="/wiki/Ancestor" title="Ancestor">ancestors</a> from one generation to the next,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> most commonly <a href="/wiki/Humans" class="mw-redirect" title="Humans">humans</a>, show <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">dogs</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and race <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a>.
</p><p>The word pedigree is a corruption of the Anglo-Norman French <i>pé de grue</i> or "crane's foot", either because the typical lines and split lines (each split leading to different offspring of the one parent line) resemble the thin leg and foot of a <a href="/wiki/Crane_(bird)" title="Crane (bird)">crane</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> or because such a mark was used to denote succession in pedigree charts<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Properties">Properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedigree_chart&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A pedigree results in the presentation of family information in the form of an easily readable chart. Pedigrees use a standardized set of symbols, squares represent males and circles represent females. Pedigree construction is a family history, and details about an earlier generation may be uncertain as memories fade. If the sex of the person is unknown a diamond is used. Someone with the phenotype in question is represented by a filled-in (darker) symbol. <a href="/wiki/Heterozygotes" class="mw-redirect" title="Heterozygotes">Heterozygotes</a>, when identifiable, are indicated by a shade dot inside a symbol or a half-filled symbol.
</p><p>Relationships in a pedigree are shown as a series of lines. Parents are connected by a horizontal line and a vertical line leads to their offspring. The offspring are connected by a horizontal sibship line and listed in birth order from left to right. If the offspring are twins then they will be connected by a triangle. If an offspring dies then its symbol will be crossed by a line. If the offspring is still born or aborted it is represented by a small triangle.
</p><p>Each generation is identified by a <a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">Roman numeral</a> (I, II, III, and so on), and each individual within the same generation is identified by an <a href="/wiki/Arabic_numerals" title="Arabic numerals">Arabic numeral</a> (1, 2, 3, and so on). Analysis of the pedigree using the principles of <a href="/wiki/Mendelian_inheritance" title="Mendelian inheritance">Mendelian inheritance</a> can determine whether a trait has a dominant or recessive pattern of inheritance. Pedigrees are often constructed after a family member afflicted with a genetic disorder has been identified. This individual, known as the <a href="/wiki/Proband" title="Proband">proband</a>, is indicated on the pedigree by an arrow.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> These changes may occur yearly or monthly.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_human_use">In human use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedigree_chart&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In human use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><span id="In_human_use"></span>In <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> pedigrees are officially recorded in the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Arms" title="College of Arms">College of Arms</a>, which has records going back to the Middle Ages, including pedigrees collected during roving inquiries by its <a href="/wiki/Herald" title="Herald">heralds</a> during the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries. The purpose of these <a href="/wiki/Heraldic_visitation" title="Heraldic visitation">heraldic visitations</a> was to register and regulate the use of <a href="/wiki/Coats_of_arms" class="mw-redirect" title="Coats of arms">coats of arms</a>. Those who claimed the right to bear arms had to provide proof either of a grant of arms to them by the College, or of descent from an ancestor entitled to arms. It was for this reason that pedigrees were recorded by the visitations. Pedigrees continue to be registered at the College of Arms and kept up to date on a voluntary basis but they are not accessible to the general public without payment of a fee.
</p><p>More visible, therefore, are the pedigrees recorded in published works, such as <a href="/wiki/Burke%27s_Peerage" title="Burke&#39;s Peerage">Burke's Peerage</a> and Burke's <a href="/wiki/Landed_gentry" title="Landed gentry">Landed Gentry</a> in the United Kingdom and, in continental <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Almanach_de_Gotha" title="Almanach de Gotha">Almanach de Gotha</a>.
</p><p>A pedigree may be used to establish the probability of a child having a particular disorder or condition. It may be used to discover where the genes in question are located (x, y, or autosome chromosome), and to determine whether a trait is dominant or recessive. When a pedigree shows a condition appearing in a 50:50 ratio between men and women it is considered <a href="/wiki/Autosome" title="Autosome">autosomal</a>. When the condition predominantly affects males in the pedigree it is considered <a href="/wiki/X-linked_recessive_inheritance" title="X-linked recessive inheritance">x-linked</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some examples of dominant traits include: <a href="/wiki/Male_baldness" class="mw-redirect" title="Male baldness">male baldness</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astigmatism" title="Astigmatism">astigmatism</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dwarfism" title="Dwarfism">dwarfism</a>. Some examples of recessive traits include: small eyes, little body hair, and tall stature.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_animal_husbandry">In animal husbandry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedigree_chart&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In animal husbandry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pedigree-sh-1895.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Pedigree-sh-1895.jpg/220px-Pedigree-sh-1895.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="133" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Pedigree-sh-1895.jpg/330px-Pedigree-sh-1895.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Pedigree-sh-1895.jpg/440px-Pedigree-sh-1895.jpg 2x" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="394" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pedigree-sh-1895.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pedigree of horse Shagya IX b. 1895</div></div></div>
<p>In the practice of <a href="/wiki/Selective_breeding" title="Selective breeding">selective breeding</a> of animals, particularly in <a href="/wiki/Animal_fancy" title="Animal fancy">animal fancy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a>, pedigree charts are used to track the ancestry of animals and assist in the planning of suitable <a href="/w/index.php?title=Breeding_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Breeding programs (page does not exist)">breeding programs</a> to enhance desirable traits. <a href="/wiki/Breed_registry" title="Breed registry">Breed registries</a> are formed and are dedicated to the accurate tracking of pedigrees and maintaining accurate records of birth, death and identifying characteristics of each registered animal.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
