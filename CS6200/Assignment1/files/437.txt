<p>The <b>Kabardians</b> (<a href="/wiki/Kabardian_Adyghe_dialect" class="mw-redirect" title="Kabardian Adyghe dialect">Highland Adyghe</a>: Къэбэрдей адыгэхэр; <a href="/wiki/West_Adyghe_dialect" class="mw-redirect" title="West Adyghe dialect">Lowland Adyghe</a>: Къэбэртай адыгэхэр; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Кабардинцы</span>), or <b>Kabardinians</b>, are the largest of the twelve <a href="/wiki/Circassians" title="Circassians">Adyghe</a> (Circassian) tribes (sub-ethnic groups). They are also commonly known by the plural terms <b>Kabardin</b>, <b>Kebertei</b>, or <b>Kabarday</b>. Along with the <a href="/wiki/Besleney" title="Besleney">Besleney</a> tribe, they speak a distinctive dialect of the <a href="/wiki/East_Circassian" class="mw-redirect" title="East Circassian">Adyghe language</a>.
</p><p>Despite the <a href="/wiki/Soviet-era" class="mw-redirect" title="Soviet-era"> Soviet</a> administrative divisions that placed Circassians under four different designations and political units, namely <i>Adygeans</i> (Adyghe in <a href="/wiki/Adygea" title="Adygea">Adygea</a>), <i>Cherkessians</i> (Adyghe in <a href="/wiki/Karachay-Cherkessia" title="Karachay-Cherkessia">Karachay-Cherkessia</a>), <i>Kabardians</i> (Adyghe in <a href="/wiki/Kabardino-Balkaria" title="Kabardino-Balkaria">Kabardino-Balkaria</a>), <i>Shapsugians</i> (Adyghe in <a href="/wiki/Krasnodar_Krai" title="Krasnodar Krai">Krasnodar Krai</a>), all four groups are essentially the same people (Adyghe). One of the 12 stars on the green and gold <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Adygea" title="Flag of Adygea"> Adyghe flag</a> represents the Kabardian people.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kabardians&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Kabardian tribe are the largest Circassian (Adyghe) branch in the world in general, and form the largest Circassian tribe in Russia, Turkey,  Egypt, and in some other countries in the region. However, in Israel and Jordan, the <a href="/wiki/Shapsug" class="mw-redirect" title="Shapsug">Shapsug</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abzakh" class="mw-redirect" title="Abzakh">Abzakh</a> tribes respectively are the largest.  As of 2002<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Kabardians&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> Kabardians numbered around 520,000 in <a href="/wiki/Kabardino-Balkaria" title="Kabardino-Balkaria">Kabardino-Balkaria</a>, Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Significant populations of Kabardians also live in <a href="/wiki/Jordan_(country)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jordan (country)">Jordan</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-ethnologue_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ethnologue-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
and there are communities in the United States. In Turkey, where more than 1 million of them live,<sup id="cite_ref-Jineps_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jineps-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> they are concentrated on the Uzunyayla plateau of <a href="/wiki/Kayseri_Province" title="Kayseri Province">Kayseri Province</a> and around (Central Turkey), though there are Kabardian villages in <a href="/wiki/Bal%C4%B1kesir" title="Balıkesir">Balıkesir</a>, <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCzce" title="Düzce">Düzce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eski%C5%9Fehir" title="Eskişehir">Eskişehir</a> (Northwest Turkey), <a href="/wiki/%C3%87orum" title="Çorum">Çorum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samsun" title="Samsun">Samsun</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tokat" title="Tokat">Tokat</a> (Black Sea region), amongst many others.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kabardians&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Religions historically practiced by Kabardins include the native <a href="/wiki/Habze" class="mw-redirect" title="Habze">Habze</a> faith, Christianity, and Islam. A majority had converted to Islam by the early 19th century. There are also still some adherents to traditional Habze beliefs, although most Kabardin are now <a href="/wiki/Hanafi" title="Hanafi">Hanafi</a> Sunni <a href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a>.
</p><p>Kabardians also constituted one of the earliest <a href="/wiki/Christianity_in_Europe" title="Christianity in Europe">Christian communities in Europe</a>, converting in the late 2nd and early 3rd Centuries.  There are also some <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> Kabardians (possibly descended from families who reportedly converted from Orthodoxy during the 13th century). Kabardians living in <a href="/wiki/Mozdoksky_District" title="Mozdoksky District">Mozdoksky District</a> in the <a href="/wiki/North_Ossetia%E2%80%93Alania" title="North Ossetia–Alania">Republic of North Ossetia–Alania</a> are <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodoxy" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Orthodoxy">Orthodox Christians</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Greenwood_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greenwood-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Some of the Kabardians living in the southern part of the neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Kursky_District,_Stavropol_Krai" title="Kursky District, Stavropol Krai">Kursky district of Stavropol Krai</a> are also <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Christian" class="mw-redirect" title="Orthodox Christian">Orthodox Christians</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Greenwood_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greenwood-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
