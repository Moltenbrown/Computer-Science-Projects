	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Young (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The surname <b>Young</b> has several origins.
</p><p>In some cases – particularly in England, Northern Ireland, and Scotland – the surname is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a> <i>yunge</i>, <i>yonge</i> ("young"); this particular surname usually originated to distinguish a younger son.
</p><p>In other cases, the surname is an <a href="/wiki/Anglicisation" title="Anglicisation">anglicisation</a> of any number of like-sounding, or <a href="/wiki/Cognate" title="Cognate">cognate</a> surnames in other languages. For example: the <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> <i>Jung</i> and <i>Junk</i>; the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> <i>Jong</i>; in <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Sweden</a>, <i>Ljung</i>; and the <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> <i>Lejeune</i> and <i>Lajeunesse</i>. The surname can also be a form of the French <i>Dion</i> or <i>Guyon</i>.
</p><p>In yet other cases, it is a <a href="/wiki/Romanization" title="Romanization">romanization</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Yang_(surname)" title="Yang (surname)">Yang (楊/杨)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Very rarely, it may be a romanization of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_surname" class="mw-redirect" title="Korean surname">Korean surnames</a> <a href="/wiki/Yong_(Korean_name)" title="Yong (Korean name)">Yong</a> (용; 龍) or <a href="/wiki/Young_(Korean_name)" title="Young (Korean name)">Yeong</a> (영; 永/榮/影); however, if seen in a Korean name, it is far more likely that Young is a portion of the <i>given</i> name instead.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There have been five baronetcies created for persons with the surname Young, one in the Baronetage of England, one in the Baronetage of Great Britain and three in the Baronetage of the United Kingdom. As of 2014 four of the creations are extant. They are <a href="/wiki/Young_baronets" title="Young baronets">Young Baronetcy</a>.
</p><p>Young is the 49th-most common surname in England; 22nd in Scotland and 11th in New Zealand.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
