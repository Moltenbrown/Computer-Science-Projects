	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Li (surname 李)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For other Chinese surnames romanized as "Li", see <a href="/wiki/Li_(surname)" title="Li (surname)">Li (surname)</a>. <p><b>Li</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%9D%8E" class="extiw" title="wikt:李">李</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>&#58; <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Lǐ</span></i>) is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_common_Chinese_surnames" title="List of common Chinese surnames">second most common surname</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, behind only <a href="/wiki/Wang_(surname)" title="Wang (surname)">Wang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-xinhua_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is one of the most common surnames in the world, shared by 92.76 million people in China,<sup id="cite_ref-stats_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and more than 100 million worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is the fourth name listed in the <a href="/wiki/Song_dynasty" title="Song dynasty">Song dynasty</a> <a href="/wiki/Chinese_classics" title="Chinese classics">classic text</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hundred_Family_Surnames" title="Hundred Family Surnames">Hundred Family Surnames</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The name is pronounced as "Lei" in <a href="/wiki/Cantonese" title="Cantonese">Cantonese</a>, but is often spelled as <b>Lee</b> in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> and many other <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Chinese" title="Overseas Chinese">overseas Chinese</a> communities. In Macau, it is also spelled as <b>Lei</b>. In <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> it is commonly spelled as <b>Lie</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The common <a href="/wiki/Korean_name#Family_names" title="Korean name">Korean surname</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_(Korean_surname)" title="Lee (Korean surname)">Lee</a> (also romanized as Yi, Ri, or Rhee), and the <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_name#Family_name" title="Vietnamese name">Vietnamese surname</a>, <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_(Vietnamese_name)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lý (Vietnamese name)">Lý</a>, are both derived from Li and are historically written with the same Chinese character, 李.<sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The character also means "plum" or "plum tree".
</p><p>According to tradition, the Li surname originated from the title <i>Dali</i> held by <a href="/wiki/Gao_Yao_(minister)" title="Gao Yao (minister)">Gao Yao</a>, a legendary minister of the <a href="/wiki/Xia_dynasty" title="Xia dynasty">Xia dynasty</a>, and was originally written with the different character, 理. <a href="/wiki/Laozi" title="Laozi">Laozi</a> (Li Er), the founder of <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">Taoism</a>, was the first historical person known to have the surname and is regarded as the founding ancestor of the surname.
</p><p>As the surname of the emperors of the <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang dynasty</a>, Li was bestowed upon or adopted by numerous people, including many foreigners, during the period, and became one of the most common <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">Chinese surnames</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics_and_distribution">Demographics and distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_(surname_%E6%9D%8E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics and distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Li 李 is one of the most common surnames in China and the world, shared by more than 93 million people in China,<sup id="cite_ref-xinhua_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> or about 7.4% of the Chinese population.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Worldwide, more than 100 million people bear the surname.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was formerly thought to be the most common surname in China, but a 2013 analysis of the names of 1.33 billion Chinese citizens has concluded that Li is the second most common surname, behind Wang with 95 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-xinhua_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Li 李 is the most common surname for the <a href="/wiki/Hakka_people" title="Hakka people">Hakka people</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Geographically, Li is the most common surname in <a href="/wiki/North_China" title="North China">North China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southwest_China" title="Southwest China">Southwest China</a>. In provinces such as <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shanxi" title="Shanxi">Shanxi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jilin" title="Jilin">Jilin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heilongjiang" title="Heilongjiang">Heilongjiang</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yunnan" title="Yunnan">Yunnan</a>, more than 8.8% of the local population are surnamed Li. Among all Chinese provinces, Henan has the largest number of Lis, accounting for 10.3% of the total.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Li is less common in southern and southeastern China. In <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zhejiang" title="Zhejiang">Zhejiang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, only 2.2 to 6.6% of the population share the surname.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_(surname_%E6%9D%8E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legendary_beginning">Legendary beginning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_(surname_%E6%9D%8E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legendary beginning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the ninth-century Tang dynasty text <i><a href="/wiki/Yuanhe_Xing_Zuan" class="mw-redirect" title="Yuanhe Xing Zuan">Yuanhe Xing Zuan</a></i>, Li is a branch of the ancient ancestral name <a href="/wiki/Y%C3%ADng_(surname)" class="mw-redirect" title="Yíng (surname)">Ying (嬴)</a> and descends from Emperor <a href="/wiki/Zhuanxu" title="Zhuanxu">Zhuanxu</a>, grandson of the mythical <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Emperor" title="Yellow Emperor">Yellow Emperor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> During the reign of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Yao" title="Emperor Yao">Emperor Yao</a>, Zhuanxu's descendant <a href="/wiki/Gao_Yao_(minister)" title="Gao Yao (minister)">Gao Yao</a> served as <i>Dali</i> (大理), or Minister of Law. During the <a href="/wiki/Xia_dynasty" title="Xia dynasty">Xia dynasty</a>, Gao Yao's descendants adopted <a href="/wiki/Li_(surname_%E7%90%86)" title="Li (surname 理)">Li (理)</a> as their surname, from the title <i>Dali</i> (meaning "great judge").<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is uncertain how the character for Li changed from the original 理 to the current 李. According to popular folklore, at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Shang_dynasty" title="Shang dynasty">Shang dynasty</a>, the minister Li Zheng (理征) was executed by <a href="/wiki/King_Zhou_of_Shang" title="King Zhou of Shang">King Zhou of Shang</a> who was known for his cruelty. Li Zheng's son Li Lizhen (理利贞) escaped with his mother to the ruins of Yihou (伊侯之墟), where they survived by eating plums. In gratitude, Li Lizhen changed his surname to 李, a character that means "plum" and is a <a href="/wiki/Homophone" title="Homophone">homophone</a> of 理. Li Lizhen was said to have settled at Ku County (苦县), in modern <a href="/wiki/Luyi_County" title="Luyi County">Luyi County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> province, which is regarded as the original hometown of the Li surname.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Laozi">Laozi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_(surname_%E6%9D%8E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Laozi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first historical person known to have the surname is Li Er (李耳), better known as <a href="/wiki/Laozi" title="Laozi">Laozi</a> (fl. 6th century BC), the philosopher who founded <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">Taoism</a>. He was said to have been an eleventh-generation descendant of Li Lizhen. Laozi is widely revered as the founding ancestor of the Li surname.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ba_people">Ba people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_(surname_%E6%9D%8E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ba people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Another early origin of Li is the non-<a href="/wiki/Huaxia" title="Huaxia">Huaxia</a> Ba people, who established the <a href="/wiki/Ba_(state)" title="Ba (state)">Ba state</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Zhou_dynasty" title="Zhou dynasty">Zhou dynasty</a> in modern western <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> province and <a href="/wiki/Chongqing" title="Chongqing">Chongqing</a> municipality. In 316 BC, Ba was conquered by the <a href="/wiki/Qin_(state)" title="Qin (state)">state of Qin</a>, which would eventually conquer all the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Warring_States" title="Seven Warring States">warring states</a> to establish the <a href="/wiki/Qin_dynasty" title="Qin dynasty">Qin dynasty</a>. Many Ba people adopted Li as their surname, as it sounded similar to the Ba word for <a href="/wiki/Tiger" title="Tiger">tiger</a>, which was a <a href="/wiki/Totem" title="Totem">totem</a> for the Ba.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 304 AD, the Ba leader <a href="/wiki/Li_Xiong" title="Li Xiong">Li Xiong</a> (Emperor Wu) established <a href="/wiki/Cheng_Han" title="Cheng Han">Cheng Han</a>, the first Li-surnamed dynasty in history.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tang_dynasty">Tang dynasty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_(surname_%E6%9D%8E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tang dynasty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TangGaozu.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/TangGaozu.jpg/170px-TangGaozu.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="363" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/TangGaozu.jpg/255px-TangGaozu.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/TangGaozu.jpg/340px-TangGaozu.jpg 2x" data-file-width="929" data-file-height="1983" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:TangGaozu.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Li Yuan (Emperor Gaozu), founder of the Tang dynasty</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_emperors_of_the_Tang_dynasty" title="List of emperors of the Tang dynasty">List of emperors of the Tang dynasty</a></div>
<p>Li was the imperial surname of the <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang dynasty</a>, founded by <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Gaozu_of_Tang" title="Emperor Gaozu of Tang">Li Yuan</a> in 618 AD. One of the most prosperous and influential dynasties in Chinese history, Tang was ruled by 20 emperors surnamed Li.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The Tang dynasty imperial family belonged to the northwest military aristocracy prevalent during the <a href="/wiki/Sui_dynasty" title="Sui dynasty">Sui dynasty</a> and claimed to be <a href="/wiki/Patrilineality" title="Patrilineality">paternally descended</a> from <a href="/wiki/Laozi" title="Laozi">Laozi</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Woolf2007_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Woolf2007-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hargett2006_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hargett2006-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Qin_(state)" title="Qin (state)">Qin</a> general <a href="/wiki/Li_Xin_(Qin)" title="Li Xin (Qin)">Li Xin</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han dynasty</a> general <a href="/wiki/Li_Guang" title="Li Guang">Li Guang</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Western_Liang_(Sixteen_Kingdoms)" title="Western Liang (Sixteen Kingdoms)">Western Liang</a> ruler <a href="/wiki/Li_Gao" title="Li Gao">Li Gao</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Tang emperors liberally granted the royal surname to favoured generals, officials, and their clans, such as <a href="/wiki/Xu_Shiji" class="mw-redirect" title="Xu Shiji">Xu Shiji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Du_Fuwei" title="Du Fuwei">Du Fuwei</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Guo_Zihe" title="Guo Zihe">Guo Zihe</a> (郭子和). Many non-Chinese people under Tang's rule were also granted the Li surname, including the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Tujue" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Tujue">Eastern Tujue</a> khan <a href="/wiki/Ashina_Simo" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashina Simo">Ashina Simo</a> (Li Simo), <a href="/wiki/Mohe_people" title="Mohe people">Mohe</a> leaders <a href="/wiki/Li_Duozuo" title="Li Duozuo">Li Duozuo</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_Jinxing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Li Jinxing (page does not exist)">Li Jinxing</a> (李谨行), <a href="/wiki/Khitan_people" title="Khitan people">Khitan</a> leaders <a href="/wiki/Li_Guangbi" title="Li Guangbi">Li Guangbi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Li_Jinzhong" title="Li Jinzhong">Li Jinzhong</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Goguryo" class="mw-redirect" title="Goguryo">Goguryo</a> general <a href="/wiki/Li_Zhengji" title="Li Zhengji">Li Zhengji</a>. Many <a href="/wiki/Tibetans" class="mw-redirect" title="Tibetans">Tibetans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uyghurs" title="Uyghurs">Uyghurs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Persian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Persian people">Persians</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> were also granted or adopted the Li surname. The number of people surnamed Li skyrocketed during the Tang dynasty.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_dynasties_ruled_by_Li_families">Other dynasties ruled by Li families</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_(surname_%E6%9D%8E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Other dynasties ruled by Li families">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Five_Dynasties_and_Ten_Kingdoms_period" title="Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period">Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period</a> following the fall of Tang in 907, the <a href="/wiki/Shatuo" title="Shatuo">Shatuo</a> general <a href="/wiki/Li_Keyong" title="Li Keyong">Li Keyong</a>, who had been granted the Li surname, established the <a href="/wiki/Jin_(Later_Tang_precursor)" title="Jin (Later Tang precursor)">Jin State</a>, precursor of the <a href="/wiki/Later_Tang" title="Later Tang">Later Tang</a>. <a href="/wiki/Li_Bian" title="Li Bian">Li Bian</a> (Xu Zhigao), the founder of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Tang" title="Southern Tang">Southern Tang</a>, also changed his surname to Li. The emperors of Later Tang and Southern Tang continued the Tang tradition of liberally bestowing the Li surname on their favoured people.<sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other Li-surnamed dynasties include <a href="/wiki/Li_Gui" title="Li Gui">Liang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Western_Xia" title="Western Xia">Western Xia</a> (Tangut Empire), and <a href="/wiki/Shun_dynasty" title="Shun dynasty">Shun</a>. All told, there have been 64 Li-surnamed emperors in Chinese history, ruling all or part of China for 650 years.<sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adoption_by_non-Han_Chinese_peoples">Adoption by non-Han Chinese peoples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_(surname_%E6%9D%8E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Adoption by non-Han Chinese peoples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:T%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng_L%C3%BD_Th%C3%A1i_T%E1%BB%95.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/T%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng_L%C3%BD_Th%C3%A1i_T%E1%BB%95.jpg/170px-T%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng_L%C3%BD_Th%C3%A1i_T%E1%BB%95.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/T%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng_L%C3%BD_Th%C3%A1i_T%E1%BB%95.jpg/255px-T%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng_L%C3%BD_Th%C3%A1i_T%E1%BB%95.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/T%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng_L%C3%BD_Th%C3%A1i_T%E1%BB%95.jpg/340px-T%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng_L%C3%BD_Th%C3%A1i_T%E1%BB%95.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2080" data-file-height="2774" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:T%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng_L%C3%BD_Th%C3%A1i_T%E1%BB%95.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lý Thái Tổ, founder of the Vietnamese Lý dynasty</div></div></div>
<p>Influenced by <a href="/wiki/Chinese_culture" title="Chinese culture">Chinese culture</a>, many non-<a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a> peoples living in and near China have adopted Chinese-style names throughout history, and Li is one of the most common surnames adopted. Li has been used by the <a href="/wiki/Bai_people" title="Bai people">Bai people</a> for more than 1,000 years, and is one of the top three surnames among the Bai. Li is the fifth of the twelve most common surnames of the <a href="/wiki/Yao_people" title="Yao people">Yao people</a>, who adopted the name more than 800 years ago. Li also has history of 500 years among the <a href="/wiki/Miao_people" title="Miao people">Miao people</a>. Among the 55 <a href="/wiki/List_of_ethnic_groups_in_China" title="List of ethnic groups in China">officially recognized ethnic minorities</a> in China, 33 are known to use the Li surname.<sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Li (spelled <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_(Vietnamese_name)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lý (Vietnamese name)">Lý</a> in modern Vietnamese) has been used in <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> for more than 2,000 years since the <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han dynasty</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Northern_Vietnam" title="Northern Vietnam">Northern Vietnam</a> was ruled as a province of the Han Empire. In 544, Vietnam gained temporary independence from China when <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_Nam_%C4%90%E1%BA%BF" title="Lý Nam Đế">Lý Nam Đế</a> founded the <a href="/wiki/Early_L%C3%BD_dynasty" title="Early Lý dynasty">Early Lý dynasty</a>. Lý Nam Đế (Li Nan Di), an ethnic Chinese, is often considered the first emperor of Vietnam. In 1009, <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_Th%C3%A1i_T%E1%BB%95" title="Lý Thái Tổ">Lý Thái Tổ</a> established the Later <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_dynasty" title="Lý dynasty">Lý dynasty</a>, which ruled Vietnam for more than 200 years.<sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1232, after usurping the Lý dynasty, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Preceptor" title="Grand Preceptor">Grand Preceptor</a> <a href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BA%A7n_Th%E1%BB%A7_%C4%90%E1%BB%99" title="Trần Thủ Độ">Trần Thủ Độ</a> forced the descendants of the Lý royal family to change their surname to <a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n" class="mw-redirect" title="Nguyễn">Nguyễn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nvk_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nvk-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Koreans</a> began using Chinese surnames during the Tang dynasty, with Li (romanized as <a href="/wiki/Lee_(Korean_surname)" title="Lee (Korean surname)">Lee</a>, Yi, or Rhee in Korea) being one of the top four. In 1392, <a href="/wiki/Yi_Seong-gye" class="mw-redirect" title="Yi Seong-gye">Yi Seong-gye</a> established the <a href="/wiki/Joseon" title="Joseon">Joseon</a> dynasty, which ruled Korea for more than 500 years.<sup id="cite_ref-zhang_6-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zhang-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prominent_clans">Prominent clans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_(surname_%E6%9D%8E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Prominent clans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are historically twelve prominent clans (<i>junwang</i>, 郡望) of Li, the most famous being those of the <a href="/wiki/Longxi_Commandery" title="Longxi Commandery">Longxi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zhao_Commandery" class="mw-redirect" title="Zhao Commandery">Zhao</a> <a href="/wiki/Commandery_(China)" title="Commandery (China)">commanderies</a>. The Zhao clan, based in modern <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Zhao_County" title="Zhao County">Zhao County</a>, traces its origin from <a href="/wiki/Li_Mu" title="Li Mu">Li Mu</a> (died 229 BC), Lord Wu'an of the <a href="/wiki/State_of_Zhao" class="mw-redirect" title="State of Zhao">State of Zhao</a>, who was one of the most famous generals of the <a href="/wiki/Warring_States_period" title="Warring States period">Warring States period</a>. The Zhao clan produced 17 <a href="/wiki/Chinese_chancellor" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese chancellor">prime ministers</a> during the Tang dynasty.<sup id="cite_ref-stnn_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stnn-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, a group of nine large tombs of the Zhao clan were discovered in <a href="/wiki/Zanhuang_County" title="Zanhuang County">Zanhuang County</a>, dating from the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Dynasties" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Dynasties">Northern Dynasties</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Zhao clan remained the most prominent branch of the Li until it was surpassed by the Longxi during the Tang dynasty. The Longxi clan is named after the Longxi Commandery in southern <a href="/wiki/Gansu" title="Gansu">Gansu</a> province. <a href="/wiki/Li_Chong_(general)" title="Li Chong (general)">Li Chong</a> (李崇), the first Qin governor of Longxi, is revered as its founder. The Han general <a href="/wiki/Li_Guang" title="Li Guang">Li Guang</a>, famous for defeating the <a href="/wiki/Xiongnu" title="Xiongnu">Xiongnu</a>, came from Longxi. Centuries later, the Tang emperors traced their ancestry to the Longxi clan, making it the most prominent branch ever since.<sup id="cite_ref-stnn_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stnn-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The Longxi Li is considered one of the four great cultural traditions of Gansu province. <a href="/wiki/Longxi_County" title="Longxi County">Longxi County</a> has built a museum for the Li clan, and hosted the Longxi Li cultural festival in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are 2,157 <a href="/wiki/Genealogy_book" title="Genealogy book">genealogy books</a> of Li families known to be extant.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_distribution_and_migration">Historical distribution and migration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_(surname_%E6%9D%8E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Historical distribution and migration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a href="/wiki/File:World_Headquarters_of_the_Lee%27s_Family,_Taipei._2012.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/World_Headquarters_of_the_Lee%27s_Family%2C_Taipei._2012.jpg/350px-World_Headquarters_of_the_Lee%27s_Family%2C_Taipei._2012.jpg" decoding="async" width="350" height="224" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/World_Headquarters_of_the_Lee%27s_Family%2C_Taipei._2012.jpg/525px-World_Headquarters_of_the_Lee%27s_Family%2C_Taipei._2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/World_Headquarters_of_the_Lee%27s_Family%2C_Taipei._2012.jpg/700px-World_Headquarters_of_the_Lee%27s_Family%2C_Taipei._2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="994" data-file-height="637" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:World_Headquarters_of_the_Lee%27s_Family,_Taipei._2012.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>"World Headquarter of the Lee's Family", <a href="/wiki/Taipei,_Taiwan" class="mw-redirect" title="Taipei, Taiwan">Taipei, Taiwan</a></div></div></div>
<p>Having originated in what is now Henan province, the Lis had spread to <a href="/wiki/Shanxi" title="Shanxi">Shanxi</a>, Hebei, <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi" title="Shaanxi">Shaanxi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hubei" title="Hubei">Hubei</a> provinces by the end of the <a href="/wiki/Warring_States_period" title="Warring States period">Warring States period</a>. During the Qin dynasty, the Lis spread to <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a> provinces, while Li Chong established the Longxi Li clan in Gansu.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the early Tang dynasty many Lis migrated to <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a> provinces. After the mass adoption of the imperial Li surname during the Tang period, Li became the second most common surname during the subsequent Song dynasty, after Wang. There were approximately 5.6 million people with the surname, or 7.2% of the total population at the time, with large concentrations in the northern provinces of Hebei, Henan, and <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Mongol_invasion_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongol invasion of China">Mongol invasion of China</a> in the 13th century caused widespread depopulation in northern China. Li, being overrepresented in the north, was hit especially hard. In the <a href="/wiki/Ming_dynasty" title="Ming dynasty">Ming dynasty</a> following the Mongol <a href="/wiki/Yuan_dynasty" title="Yuan dynasty">Yuan dynasty</a>, the Li-surnamed population had declined to 5.1 million, constituting 5.5% of the total population. It fell behind <a href="/wiki/Zhang_(surname)" title="Zhang (surname)">Zhang</a> to become the third most common surname of the time. Many of the Lis had migrated to <a href="/wiki/South_China" title="South China">South China</a> by this period.<sup id="cite_ref-eastday_3-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
