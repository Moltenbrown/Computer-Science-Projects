	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Schmidt (surname)</h1>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Schmidt</b> is a common German occupational surname derived from the German word "<a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Schmied" class="extiw" title="wikt:Schmied">Schmied</a>" meaning "blacksmith" and/or "metalworker". This surname is the German equivalent of "Smith" in the English-speaking world.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_distribution">Geographical distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schmidt_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geographical distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2014, 64.9% of all known bearers of the surname <i>Schmidt</i> were residents of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> (frequency 1:113), 18.2% of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> (1:1,809), 3.7% of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> (1:5,058), 1.7% of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> (1:1,936), 1.5% of <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> (1:503), 1.3% of <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> (1:398), 1.1% of <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> (1:4,469), 1.1% of <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> (1:3,635) and 1.0% of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> (1:6,167).
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, the frequency of the surname was higher than national average (1:113) in the following states:
</p>
<ul><li>1. <a href="/wiki/Thuringia" title="Thuringia">Thuringia</a> (1:60)</li>
<li>2. <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg-Vorpommern" title="Mecklenburg-Vorpommern">Mecklenburg-Vorpommern</a> (1:65)</li>
<li>3. <a href="/wiki/Saxony-Anhalt" title="Saxony-Anhalt">Saxony-Anhalt</a> (1:66)</li>
<li>4. <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a> (1:70)</li>
<li>5. <a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a> (1:84)</li>
<li>6. <a href="/wiki/Saarland" title="Saarland">Saarland</a> (1:87)</li>
<li>7. <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a> (1:92)</li>
<li>8. <a href="/wiki/Schleswig-Holstein" title="Schleswig-Holstein">Schleswig-Holstein</a> (1:95)</li>
<li>9. <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a> (1:110)</li>
<li>10. <a href="/wiki/Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Rhineland-Palatinate">Rhineland-Palatinate</a> (1:112)</li></ul>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, the frequency of the surname was higher than national average (1:398) only in one region:<sup id="cite_ref-schmidt2014_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-schmidt2014-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>1. <a href="/wiki/Region_of_Southern_Denmark" title="Region of Southern Denmark">Southern Denmark</a> (1:215)</li></ul>
