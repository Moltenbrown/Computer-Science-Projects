<p><b>Kristen Mary Jenner</b> (<a href="/wiki/Maiden_and_married_names" title="Maiden and married names">née</a> <b>Houghton</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;h&#39; in &#39;hi&#39;">h</span><span title="/oʊ/: &#39;o&#39; in &#39;code&#39;">oʊ</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/ən/: &#39;on&#39; in &#39;button&#39;">ən</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">HOH</span>-tən</i></a>, formerly <b>Kardashian</b>; born November 5, 1955)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an American television personality, entertainment manager, producer, businesswoman, and author. She rose to fame starring in the <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Keeping_Up_with_the_Kardashians" title="Keeping Up with the Kardashians">Keeping Up with the Kardashians</a></i> (2007–present).
</p><p>She has four children from her first marriage to lawyer <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kardashian" title="Robert Kardashian">Robert Kardashian</a>: <a href="/wiki/Kourtney_Kardashian" title="Kourtney Kardashian">Kourtney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Kardashian" title="Kim Kardashian">Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khlo%C3%A9_Kardashian" title="Khloé Kardashian">Khloé</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rob_Kardashian" title="Rob Kardashian">Robert</a>, and two children from her second marriage to television personality and retired <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a> <a href="/wiki/Olympic_medal" title="Olympic medal">medalist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caitlyn_Jenner" title="Caitlyn Jenner">Bruce Jenner</a> (now Caitlyn): <a href="/wiki/Kendall_Jenner" title="Kendall Jenner">Kendall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Jenner" title="Kylie Jenner">Kylie</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Jenner was born in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. She is the first child of Mary Jo Shannon (<i>née</i> Campbell; born 1934) and Robert True Houghton,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> an engineer.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She is of Dutch, English, Irish, German and Scottish descent. When Jenner was seven years old, her parents divorced and she and her younger sister, Karen, were raised by their mother until a few years later when her mother married businessman Harry Shannon.
</p><p>Three months after moving to <a href="/wiki/Oxnard,_California" title="Oxnard, California">Oxnard, California</a>, Shannon's business partner allegedly left with all the company's capital, so the family moved back to San Diego.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Jenner attended <a href="/wiki/Clairemont_High_School" title="Clairemont High School">Clairemont High School</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and graduated in 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She worked as a flight attendant for a year in 1976.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<p>Jenner runs her own production company, Jenner Communications, which is based in Los Angeles. Since before the start of <i><a href="/wiki/Keeping_Up_with_the_Kardashians" title="Keeping Up with the Kardashians">Keeping Up with the Kardashians</a></i>, she has managed her daughter Kim's career. She also is involved with the business management of her other daughters and son.
</p><p>Jenner opened a children's boutique in 2004 with her eldest daughter, <a href="/wiki/Kourtney_Kardashian" title="Kourtney Kardashian">Kourtney</a>. The boutique was called "Smooch" and was open for almost six years before closing down in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing">Writing</span></h3>
<p>Jenner's autobiography, <i>Kris Jenner... and All Things Kardashian</i>, was released in November 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She later wrote a <a href="/wiki/Cookbook" title="Cookbook">cookbook</a> entitled <i>In the Kitchen with Kris: A Kollection of Kardashian-Jenner Family Favorites</i>, which was released in October 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_show">Television show</span></h3>
<p>Jenner hosted a <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">pop culture</a>-driven daytime <a href="/wiki/Talk_show" title="Talk show">talk show</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kris_Jenner_Show" title="Kris Jenner Show">Kris</a></i>. The series began its six-week trial summer run on several <a href="/wiki/Fox_Television_Stations" title="Fox Television Stations">Fox-owned</a> stations on July 15, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, her son-in-law, revealed the first public picture of daughter North West on the show. The show's six-week trial run was not extended.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Keeping_Up_with_the_Kardashians"><i>Keeping Up with the Kardashians</i></span></h3>
<p>Jenner met with <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Seacrest" title="Ryan Seacrest">Ryan Seacrest</a> in 2007 to pursue a reality television show based on her family. Seacrest, who had his own production company, decided to develop the idea, having the popular family-based show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Osbournes" title="The Osbournes">The Osbournes</a></i> in his mind. Jenner further commented on the possible series:<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: &#39;Times New Roman&#39;, Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“
</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">Like, there's the little girls, and there's the older girls, and then there's my son. [...] Everybody thinks that they could create a bunch of drama in their lives, but it's something that I felt I didn't even have to think about. It would be natural.

<p>The show eventually was picked up to air on the <a href="/wiki/E!" title="E!">E!</a> cable network with Jenner acting as the executive producer. The series focuses on the personal and professional lives of the Kardashian–Jenner blended families.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The series debuted on October 14, 2007, and has become one of the longest-running reality television shows in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The twelfth season of <i>Keeping Up with the Kardashians</i> started airing on May 1, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The show has resulted in the development of several <a href="/wiki/Spin-off_(media)" title="Spin-off (media)">spin-offs</a>, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Kourtney_and_Khlo%C3%A9_Take_Miami" class="mw-redirect" title="Kourtney and Khloé Take Miami">Kourtney and Khloé Take Miami</a></i> (2009), <i><a href="/wiki/Kourtney_and_Kim_Take_New_York" title="Kourtney and Kim Take New York">Kourtney and Kim Take New York</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Khlo%C3%A9_%26_Lamar" title="Khloé &amp; Lamar">Khloé &amp; Lamar</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Rob_%26_Chyna" title="Rob &amp; Chyna">Rob &amp; Chyna</a></i> (2016) and <i><a href="/wiki/Life_of_Kylie" title="Life of Kylie">Life of Kylie</a></i> (2017)<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span id="Marriages.2C_relationships.2C_and_family"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriages,_relationships,_and_family">Marriages, relationships, and family</span></h3>
<p>Jenner's first marriage was to lawyer <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kardashian" title="Robert Kardashian">Robert Kardashian</a> (who later became widely known for his early legal representation of <a href="/wiki/O._J._Simpson" title="O. J. Simpson">O. J. Simpson</a>) on July 8, 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> They have four children: daughters <a href="/wiki/Kourtney_Kardashian" title="Kourtney Kardashian">Kourtney</a> (born 1979), <a href="/wiki/Kim_Kardashian" title="Kim Kardashian">Kim</a> (born 1980), <a href="/wiki/Khlo%C3%A9_Kardashian" title="Khloé Kardashian">Khloé</a> (born 1984), and son <a href="/wiki/Rob_Kardashian" title="Rob Kardashian">Rob</a> (born 1987). They divorced in March 1991, but remained close friends until his death from <a href="/wiki/Esophageal_cancer" title="Esophageal cancer">esophageal cancer</a> in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Kris confessed that she had an affair with former soccer player and animator Todd Waterman during her marriage to Kardashian. She referred to Waterman as "Ryan" in her autobiography, but he revealed his identity on his own. They had an encounter on <i>Keeping up with the Kardashians</i> while Kris was having a tennis lesson.<sup id="cite_ref-todd_waterman_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-todd_waterman-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 1991, one month after her divorce from Kardashian, Jenner married her second spouse, retired Olympian <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Jenner" class="mw-redirect" title="Bruce Jenner">Bruce Jenner</a>, who publicly came out as a transgender woman in 2015, taking the name Caitlyn.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> They have two daughters together: <a href="/wiki/Kendall_Jenner" title="Kendall Jenner">Kendall</a> (born 1995) and <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Jenner" title="Kylie Jenner">Kylie</a> (born 1997);<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> in her autobiography, Jenner explained that she named her daughter Kendall Nicole after the late <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Brown_Simpson" title="Nicole Brown Simpson">Nicole Brown Simpson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> By marriage to Bruce, Kris also had four stepchildren: Burt, Cassandra "Casey", <a href="/wiki/Brandon_%26_Leah#Brandon_Jenner" title="Brandon &amp; Leah">Brandon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brody_Jenner" title="Brody Jenner">Brody</a>.
</p><p>The Jenners announced their separation in October 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and on September 22, 2014, she filed for divorce, citing <a href="/wiki/Irreconcilable_differences" title="Irreconcilable differences">irreconcilable differences</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The divorce became final on March 23, 2015, because of a six-month state legal requirement.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-March_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-March-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Kris Jenner has talked about the breakup with Caitlyn as "the most passive-aggressive thing", saying that while she had known of her use of hormones in the 1980s, "there wasn't a gender issue. Nobody mentioned a gender issue."<sup id="cite_ref-Independent_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
Through her children, Jenner has nine grandchildren.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jenner has been in a relationship with Corey Gamble since around 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="O._J._Simpson_trial">O. J. Simpson trial</span></h3>
<p>Jenner and her family suffered emotional turmoil during the <a href="/wiki/O._J._Simpson_murder_case" title="O. J. Simpson murder case">O. J. Simpson trial</a> (1994-1995), later described as the "Trial of the Century." Jenner was a good friend of O.J.'s ex-wife, <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Brown_Simpson" title="Nicole Brown Simpson">Nicole Brown</a>, and Jenner's first husband, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kardashian" title="Robert Kardashian">Robert Kardashian</a>, was one of O.J. Simpson's "<a href="/wiki/Dream_Team_(law)" title="Dream Team (law)">Dream Team</a>" of defense lawyers during the trial.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She was portrayed by American actress <a href="/wiki/Selma_Blair" title="Selma Blair">Selma Blair</a> in the <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a> limited series <i><a href="/wiki/American_Crime_Story:_The_People_v._O._J._Simpson" class="mw-redirect" title="American Crime Story: The People v. O. J. Simpson">American Crime Story: The People v. O. J. Simpson</a></i>, which premiered in February 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="California_Community_Church">California Community Church</span></h3>
<p>Jenner and Pastor Brad Johnson founded the California Community Church in 2012. It originally was called the Life Change Community Church, located in <a href="/wiki/Agoura_Hills" class="mw-redirect" title="Agoura Hills">Agoura Hills</a>, California.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
