	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Evans (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Evans</b> is a male name &amp; <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Welsh</a>, and possibly <a href="/wiki/Cornish_people" title="Cornish people">Cornish</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> origin.  Within <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> it is the fifth most common surname and is the tenth most common in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Within the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, it is ranked as the 48th-most common surname.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Evans</b>: In <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> the male name Evans is pronounced Evangelos. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evans_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Evans is of <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Welsh</a> origin. In its <a href="/wiki/Anglicised" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicised">anglicised</a> form the name means "son of Evan".  Regarding its Welsh roots, it is a derivative of the name <i><b>Ifan</b></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Cognate" title="Cognate">cognate</a> of <a href="/wiki/John_(name)" class="mw-redirect" title="John (name)">John</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh language</a>, the <i>f</i> produces the <i>v</i> sound; <i>Ifan</i> (<i>Ivan</i>) became <i>Evan</i>.
</p><p>The similarity to the <a href="/wiki/Slavic_languages" title="Slavic languages">Slavic</a> name <a href="/wiki/Ivan_(name)" title="Ivan (name)">Ivan</a> is not accidental, as the latter is a cognate of John too.
</p><p>In the Welsh language the patronymic "ab Evan" resulted in the anglicized surname "<a href="/wiki/Bevan" title="Bevan">Bevan</a>", which is also common in Wales.
</p>
