<p><b>Stepsiblings</b> (sometimes abbreviated informally as stepsibs)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is the generic term for <b>stepsisters</b> and  <b>stepbrothers</b>. Step-siblings  are usually regarded as connected only by law and/or social norms, and are not necessarily <a href="/wiki/Consanguinity" title="Consanguinity">genetically related</a> – unless they are also <a href="/wiki/Half-sibling" class="mw-redirect" title="Half-sibling">half-siblings</a> or another kind of <a href="/wiki/Blood_relative" class="mw-redirect" title="Blood relative">blood relative</a>. Technically, a step-sibling can be related to a stepbrother or stepsister only if they are born before their <a href="/wiki/Biological_parent" class="mw-redirect" title="Biological parent">biological parents</a> are divorced or widowed, and a <a href="/wiki/Remarriage" title="Remarriage">remarriage</a> occurs, involving one biological parent of each step-sibling.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stepsibling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In many fairy tales, the central character has a stepmother, after whom their stepsiblings take.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In <i><a href="/wiki/Cinderella" title="Cinderella">Cinderella</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Ugly_sisters" title="Ugly sisters">ugly sisters</a> are the main character's stepsisters. <i><a href="/wiki/Mother_Hulda" class="mw-redirect" title="Mother Hulda">Mother Hulda</a></i> also features wicked stepsisters, but  <i><a href="/wiki/Kate_Crackernuts" title="Kate Crackernuts">Kate Crackernuts</a></i> loving stepsisters.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Many <a href="/wiki/Romance_novel" title="Romance novel">romance novels</a> feature heroes who are the stepbrother of the heroine. The step-relationship generally stems from a marriage when the hero and heroine are at least in their adolescence. 
</p><p>Some family <a href="/wiki/Films" class="mw-redirect" title="Films">films</a> and television <a href="/wiki/Sitcoms" class="mw-redirect" title="Sitcoms">sitcoms</a> feature a blended nuclear family including siblings as the center premise. In many cases, the step-family is large and full of children causing situations such as sibling rivalry, rooming, falling in love, and getting along amongst the children as popular plotlines. This premise dates back as far as the 1968 film <i><a href="/wiki/Yours,_Mine_and_Ours_(1968_film)" title="Yours, Mine and Ours (1968 film)">Yours, Mine and Ours</a></i>. This film gave way to a classic family television sitcom about a blended family known as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Brady_Bunch" title="The Brady Bunch">The Brady Bunch</a></i>. Some contemporary family sitcoms have made the blended family sitcom more popular with the <a href="/wiki/TGIF_(ABC)" class="mw-redirect" title="TGIF (ABC)">TGIF</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Step_by_Step_(TV_series)" title="Step by Step (TV series)">Step by Step</a></i> bringing about other shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/Aliens_in_the_Family" title="Aliens in the Family">Aliens in the Family</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Life_with_Derek" title="Life with Derek">Life with Derek</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Drake_%26_Josh" title="Drake &amp; Josh">Drake &amp; Josh</a></i>, and the short-lived <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> family sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Something_So_Right_(TV_series)" title="Something So Right (TV series)">Something So Right</a></i>. <i><a href="/wiki/Life_of_Riley_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Life of Riley (TV series)">The Life of Riley</a></i> is a 2009 British comedy television series, shown on BBC One &amp; BBC HD. It focuses on the lives of a blended family. <i><a href="/wiki/Kevin_and_Kell" title="Kevin and Kell">Kevin and Kell</a></i> is a comic strip that focuses on a blended family. The <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Phineas_and_Ferb" title="Phineas and Ferb">Phineas and Ferb</a></i> also prominently features a blended family, chosen by co-creator <a href="/wiki/Jeff_%22Swampy%22_Marsh" title="Jeff &quot;Swampy&quot; Marsh">Jeff "Swampy" Marsh</a> in part due to its underuse in children's programming, and his personal experiences growing up in such a family.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
