<p><b>"Ben Adam"</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span lang="he" dir="rtl">בן אדם</span>&#8206;, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> translation: "Human Being", literally "Son of <a href="/wiki/Adam_(Bible)" class="mw-redirect" title="Adam (Bible)">Adam</a>") was the <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> entry in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_1988" title="Eurovision Song Contest 1988">Eurovision Song Contest 1988</a>, performed in <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a> by <a href="/wiki/Yardena_Arazi" title="Yardena Arazi">Yardena Arazi</a>. 
</p><p>The song deals with the ordinariness of humanity, as Arazi sings that "no man is perfect in all virtues" and tells her listeners "don't always judge him for the worse". With this in mind, she exhorts her listeners to "let him dream and give him breath", that is, to treat everyone with dignity. Musically, the song is perhaps best known for its final chorus. Beginning at a slow tempo, the song builds to a fast-paced conclusion.
</p><p>The song was performed eighth on the night (following the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>' <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Joling" title="Gerard Joling">Gerard Joling</a> with "<a href="/wiki/Shangri-La_(Gerard_Joling_song)" title="Shangri-La (Gerard Joling song)">Shangri-La</a>" and preceding <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>'s entry and winning song "<a href="/wiki/Ne_Partez_Pas_Sans_Moi" class="mw-redirect" title="Ne Partez Pas Sans Moi">Ne Partez Pas Sans Moi</a>", sung by <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Céline Dion</a>). Arazi has always been highly superstitious and consults an astrologer on all matters in her life. The astrologer told her the song performed 9th would win the competition in Dublin, Ireland. Israel had drawn 9th in the running order, so Arazi agreed to represent Israel. However, when Cyprus withdrew from the contest (having been drawn to sing second), Israel's position shifted to 8th. The 9th song did win the competition; with the Swiss triumphing from that starting point.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> At the close of voting, it had received 85 points, placing 7th in a field of 21.
</p><p>In 2010, the Israeli artist <a href="/wiki/Yoni_Eilat" title="Yoni Eilat">Yoni Eilat</a> recorded a <a href="/wiki/Yiddish" title="Yiddish">Yiddish</a> cover of the song, "Der Mentsch" (<a href="/wiki/Yiddish_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Yiddish language">Yiddish</a>: <span lang="yi" dir="rtl">דער מענטש</span>&#8206;), for his album "Tzigayner Neshume".
</p>
