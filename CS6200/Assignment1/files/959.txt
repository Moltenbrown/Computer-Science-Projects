<div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Concept of quality of life</div>
<p>An individual's or a <a href="/wiki/Socioeconomic_class" class="mw-redirect" title="Socioeconomic class">socioeconomic class</a>'s <b>standard of living</b> is the level of <a href="/wiki/Wealth" title="Wealth">wealth</a>, comfort, material goods, and necessities available to them in a certain geographical area, usually a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a>.  The standard of living includes factor as a whole quality and availability of <a href="/wiki/Employment" title="Employment">employment</a>, class disparity, <a href="/wiki/Poverty_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Poverty rate">poverty rate</a>, quality and housing affordability, hours of work are required to purchase necessities, <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">gross domestic product</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inflation_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Inflation rate">inflation rate</a>, amount of leisure time every year, affordable (or free) access to quality healthcare, quality and availability of <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, <a href="/wiki/Literacy" title="Literacy">literacy</a> rates, life expectancy, occurrence of diseases, cost of goods and services, infrastructure, national <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a>, economic and political stability, freedom, environmental quality, climate and safety. The standard of living is closely related to <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">quality of life</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Measurement">Measurement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_of_living&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Measurement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Standard of living is generally measured by standards such as realistic (i.e. <a href="/wiki/Inflation_(economics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Inflation (economics)">inflation</a> adjusted) income per person and poverty rate. Other measures such as access and quality of <a href="/wiki/Health_care" title="Health care">health care</a>, <a href="/wiki/Economic_inequality" title="Economic inequality">income growth inequality</a>, and educational standards are also used. Examples are access to certain goods (such as number of refrigerators per 1000 people), or measurement of health such as life expectancy. It is the ease by which people living in a time or place are able to satisfy their needs and/or wants.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The main idea of a 'standard' may be contrasted with the <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">quality of life</a>, which takes into account not only the material standard of living, but also other more intangible aspects that make up human life, such as leisure, safety, cultural resources, social life, <a href="/wiki/Physical_health" class="mw-redirect" title="Physical health">physical health</a>, environmental quality issues etc.  More complexed means of measuring well-being must be employed to make such judgements, and these are very often political, thus controversial. Even between two nations or societies that have similar material standards of living, quality of life factors may in fact make one of these places more attractive to a given individual or group.
</p>
