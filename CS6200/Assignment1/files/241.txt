<p><b>Carpenter</b> is a surname. Its use as a forename or middle name is rare. Within the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, it is ranked as the 231st-most common surname as of the 2010 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The English meaning of <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/carpenter" class="extiw" title="wikt:carpenter">carpenter</a></i> is from the occupation of one who makes wooden objects and structures by shaping wood.<sup id="cite_ref-ALL_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ALL-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carpenter_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Common use of the Carpenter surname in the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a> is seen circa 1275-1325 in <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a>. Its use prior to this time as a surname has roots in the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Norman_French" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-Norman French">Anglo-Norman French</a> introduced into England about the time of the <a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_England" title="Norman conquest of England">Norman conquest of England</a> of 1066. The earliest attested use as a surname in English is from 1121,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> though its use as a secondary name or description in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> of 1086 might have precedence.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a>, the surname was commonly written as "Carpentier" and its earlier form as "Charpentier".<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Its use as a surname may have derived as a <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nickname</a> or description of one's occupation circa 900-1000.<sup id="cite_ref-ALL_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ALL-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Citation given is vague and does not appear to be relevant to French surnames (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>All of these variations come from the <a href="/wiki/Late_Latin" title="Late Latin">Late Latin</a> <i>carpentārĭus</i>, denoting use as an <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/artifex" class="extiw" title="wikt:artifex"><i>artifex</i></a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> - a wagon or carriage-maker equal to a <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/wainwright" class="extiw" title="wikt:wainwright">wainwright</a>. The roots of <i>carpentārĭus</i> come from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/carpentum" class="extiw" title="wikt:carpentum">carpentum</a></i>, meaning a two-wheeled carriage or a form of <a href="/wiki/Chariot" title="Chariot">chariot</a> not used directly for warfare in the community by women and others, plus <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-arius" class="extiw" title="wikt:-arius">arius</a></i> - used in the masculine form as a noun denoting an agent of use from other nouns. It may be related to the <a href="/wiki/Old_Irish" title="Old Irish">Old Irish</a> <i>carpat</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Gaulish_language" title="Gaulish language">Gaulish</a> <i>carbad</i> for carriage or cart, and is probably related to the Gaulish <i>karros</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ALL_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ALL-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Carpenter_name_variants">Carpenter name variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carpenter_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Carpenter name variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carpenter name variants include:
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Wright" title="Wright">Wright</a></b> - Woodwright in old England Such as a "wood wright" (wood worker).  See also <a href="/wiki/Wainwright_(name)" title="Wainwright (name)">Wainwright (name)</a>, from "wain wright" (a wooden wagon maker).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="says who (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Carpentier" title="Carpentier">Carpentier</a></b> and <b><a href="/wiki/Charpentier" title="Charpentier">Charpentier</a></b> - From the French Norman Carpentier (le Carpentier, le Charpentier), a worker of wood, derived from the late Latin "carpentarius", a maker of wooden carriages. French Surnames &gt; CARPENTIER ++, Forme norm.-picarde de Charpentier; var. du Sud-Ouest et roussillonnase Carpentier.  Avecart. Le Carpentier.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="says who (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  The surname Charpentier could be the source of other surnames due to the adventurousness of its early French bearers; for example, Francois Charpentier and Joseph Charpentier were <a href="/wiki/Jacobitism" title="Jacobitism">Jacobites</a> captured during the <a href="/wiki/1745_rising" class="mw-redirect" title="1745 rising">1745 rising</a> while in the French Service. Francois was a native of <a href="/wiki/Dieppe,_Seine-Maritime" class="mw-redirect" title="Dieppe, Seine-Maritime">Dieppe</a>, France captured at <a href="/wiki/Carlisle" title="Carlisle">Carlisle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marshalsea" title="Marshalsea">Marshalsea</a>, and Joseph was captured at sea and imprisoned at <a href="/wiki/Berwick-upon-Tweed" title="Berwick-upon-Tweed">Berwick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Carpender" title="Carpender">Carpender</a></b> - An English phonetic name variant of Carpenter. Also seen as Carpendar.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="says who (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li></ul>

result of a 1465 law enacting that "every Irishman that dwells betwixt or amongst Englishmen in the County of Dublin, Myeth, Vriell, and Kildare ... shall take to him an English Surname of ... arte or style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="source quotes the law without saying this is how the Carpenter surname came to be (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> the surname was recorded there as early as 1636<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup research (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and as late as 1890.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  In <a href="/wiki/County_Kerry" title="County Kerry">County Kerry</a>, the surname is said to be that of an English family who settled on estates near <a href="/wiki/Tralee" title="Tralee">Tralee</a> as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Rebellion_of_1641" title="Irish Rebellion of 1641">Irish Rebellion of 1641</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  After the <a href="/wiki/Restoration_(England)" class="mw-redirect" title="Restoration (England)">Restoration</a> in 1660, John Carpenter, Philip Carpenter, Capt. Phillip Carpenter, and Lt. Thomas Carpenter were among the "Forty-Nine (i.e, 1649) Officers" who supported the Royalist cause in the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Confederate_Wars" title="Irish Confederate Wars">Irish Confederate Wars</a> rewarded with grants of land in Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-JO&#39;H_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JO&#39;H-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  The 1659 census of County Limerick listed Carpenter as a family surname in Balliea townland, Small County Barony, and among the tituladoes (principal residents) in the barony of Cosmay in Limerick.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  In 1890, 10 entries for Carpenter were made in Ireland's birth indexes, with 8 in Leinster Province (County Dublin), and 1 each in Munster and Ulster provinces.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>  Many of the MacIntyres of Northern Ireland are believed to be descended from the Scottish <a href="/wiki/Clan_MacIntyre" title="Clan MacIntyre">Clan MacIntyre</a> whose ancient seat was in <a href="/wiki/Lorne,_Scotland" title="Lorne, Scotland">Lorne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  A documented instance of the surname Carpenter being adopted by an Irish McIntyre in America is that of Ireland-born brothers Owen Patrick McIntyre of Placer County, California and Michael Carpenter of Ottawa County, Michigan as shown in McIntyre's last will &amp; testament dated August 25, 1875 and filed September 11, 1875 naming his brother Michael to direct the education of his nephew and namesake Michael's son Owen Patrick Carpenter.<sup id="cite_ref-CSS_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSS-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="appears to be primary source/original research (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>

Can be used for either the occupation or surname.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="says who (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>

<li><b>De Carpenter</b> or <b>De Carpentier</b> - Dutch for "the carpenter", a worker of wood, from the French Carpentier.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="says who (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>
