	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Perry (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the surname Perry. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Perry_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Perry (disambiguation)">Perry (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Perry</caption><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Language(s)</th><td><a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Region of origin</th><td><a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Perry</b> is a surname with several distinct origins. In <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, deriving from the Old English <i lang="ang" title="Old English language text">pyrige</i> (pear tree), referring to one who dwells by a pear tree,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> while in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> Perry, along with Parry, arose as <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymics</a>, via a shortening of "ap Harry" (son of Harry). There are some variants in the <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">Romance</a> or <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">Romantic</a> languages (derived from the Latin): <a href="/wiki/Pereira_(surname)" title="Pereira (surname)">Pereira</a>
, Pereyra, Pereyro, Pereiro, Pereiros Pereire, Perera, Perer, Perero, Pereros;<sup id="cite_ref-genealogy.about.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-genealogy.about.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the Norman French <i lang="fro" title="Old French language text">perrieur</i> (quarry), possibly referring to a quarryman.<sup id="cite_ref-genealogy.about.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-genealogy.about.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Perry was recorded as a surname from the late 16th century in villages near <a href="/wiki/Colchester" title="Colchester">Colchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>, East England, such as Lexden and Copford.<sup id="cite_ref-AYT_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AYT-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Perry has some resemblance with the Portuguese common surname <a href="/wiki/Pereira_(surname)" title="Pereira (surname)">Pereira</a>, which means <a href="/wiki/Pear_tree" class="mw-redirect" title="Pear tree">pear tree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese language</a>. Because of that, many <a href="/wiki/Portuguese-Americans" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese-Americans">Portuguese immigrants to the USA</a> (especially <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>) chose to "<a href="/wiki/Americanization_(immigration)" title="Americanization (immigration)">Americanize</a>" their <a href="/wiki/Pereira_(surname)" title="Pereira (surname)">Pereira</a> surname to Perry. The Italian surname, Perri, related to "Peter", is also often Americanized to Perry.
</p>
