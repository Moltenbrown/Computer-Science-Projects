<p><b>Oxford Dictionaries</b>, formerly <b>Oxford Dictionaries Online</b> (<b>ODO</b>), is a collection of <a href="/wiki/Online_dictionary" class="mw-redirect" title="Online dictionary">dictionary websites</a> produced by <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Press" title="Oxford University Press">Oxford University Press</a> (OUP), the <a href="/wiki/Publishing_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Publishing house">publishing house</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a>, which also publishes a number of print dictionaries, among other works.
</p><p>The collection includes <a href="/wiki/Bilingual_dictionary" title="Bilingual dictionary">bilingual dictionaries</a> between English and a variety of languages (Greek, <a href="/wiki/Gujarati_language" title="Gujarati language">Gujarati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hindi_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindi language">Hindi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvian_language" title="Latvian language">Latvian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Sotho_language" title="Northern Sotho language">Northern Sotho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southern_Quechua_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Southern Quechua language">Southern Quechua</a>, Spanish, <a href="/wiki/Swahili_language" title="Swahili language">Swahili</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tajik_language" title="Tajik language">Tajik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tatar_language" title="Tatar language">Tatar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telugu_language" title="Telugu language">Telugu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tok_Pisin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Tok Pisin language">Tok Pisin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tswana_language" title="Tswana language">Tswana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkmen_language" title="Turkmen language">Turkmen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urdu_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xhosa_language" title="Xhosa language">Xhosa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zulu_language" title="Zulu language">Zulu</a>) known as <b>Oxford Living Dictionaries</b>, which are provided free of charge, as well as Oxford Dictionaries Premium, a <a href="/wiki/Subscription_service" class="mw-redirect" title="Subscription service">subscription service</a>. Oxford Living Dictionaries previously included English and Spanish dictionaries, which have been moved to <b>Lexico</b>, a collaboration between OUP and <a href="/wiki/Dictionary.com" title="Dictionary.com">Dictionary.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-faqs_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-faqs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Oxford Dictionaries' definitions appear in <a href="/wiki/Google_Dictionary" title="Google Dictionary">Google definition search</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dictionary_(software)" title="Dictionary (software)">Dictionary</a> application on <a href="/wiki/MacOS" title="MacOS">macOS</a>, etc., licensed through Oxford Dictionaries <a href="/wiki/Application_programming_interface" title="Application programming interface">API</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oxford_Dictionaries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hosted under oxforddictionaries.com, Oxford Dictionaries Online was launched in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Buyers of the third edition of the <i><a href="/wiki/Oxford_Dictionary_of_English" title="Oxford Dictionary of English">Oxford Dictionary of English</a></i>, also published in 2010, were granted a one-year subscription to the website's subscription content.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The website's English dictionaries incorporated content of the <i>Oxford Dictionary of English</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/New_Oxford_American_Dictionary" title="New Oxford American Dictionary">New Oxford American Dictionary</a></i>, <i>Oxford Thesaurus of English</i>, and <i>Oxford American Writer’s Thesaurus</i>. It also provided a Spanish monolingual dictionary and bilingual dictionaries between English and several languages.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As of June 2014<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Oxford_Dictionaries&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, it was updated every three months.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, OUP launched Oxford Global Languages, an initiative to build lexical resources (bilingual dictionaries) of the world's languages, starting with <a href="/wiki/Zulu_language" title="Zulu language">Zulu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Sotho_language" title="Northern Sotho language">Northern Sotho</a> online dictionaries released in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, the free content of Oxford Dictionaries Online was rebranded as Oxford Living Dictionaries, and the subscription content was moved to a different <a href="/wiki/Subdomain" title="Subdomain">subdomain</a>, premium.oxforddictionaries.com.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2019, the free-of-charge monolingual dictionaries of English and Spanish were moved to Lexico.com, a collaboration between OUP and <a href="/wiki/Dictionary.com" title="Dictionary.com">Dictionary.com</a>. The Oxford Living Dictionaries in other languages and the subscription content of Oxford Dictionaries are still offered under oxforddictionaries.com. The offer of the US English dictionary on Oxford Living Dictionaries was completely terminated, as Lexico only hosts UK English and Spanish dictionaries.<sup id="cite_ref-faqs_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-faqs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> "Lexico" was itself part of the former name of the company Dictionary.com, Lexico Publishing Group, LLC.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Comparison_with_the_Oxford_English_Dictionary">Comparison with the <i>Oxford English Dictionary</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oxford_Dictionaries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Comparison with the Oxford English Dictionary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>
The website of the <i><a href="/wiki/Oxford_English_Dictionary" title="Oxford English Dictionary">Oxford English Dictionary</a></i> described its difference from Oxford Dictionaries as follows:</p><blockquote><p>The dictionary content in Oxford Dictionaries focuses on current English and includes modern meanings and uses of words. Where words have more than one meaning, the most important and common meanings in modern English are given first, and less common and more specialist or technical uses are listed below. The <i>OED</i>, on the other hand, is a historical dictionary and it forms a record of all the core words and meanings in English over more than 1,000 years, from Old English to the present day, and including many obsolete and historical terms. Meanings are ordered chronologically in the <i>OED</i>, according to when they were first recorded in English&#160;...<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
