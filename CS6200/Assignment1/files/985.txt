	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Roberts (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Roberts</b> is a British surname of patronymic origin or reflecting servile status, deriving from the given name <a href="/wiki/Robert" title="Robert">Robert</a>, meaning "bright renown" – from the <a href="/wiki/Germanic_languages" title="Germanic languages">Germanic</a> elements "hrod" meaning renown and "beraht" meaning bright. Roberts may mean either "servant of Robert" or "son of Robert"; the latter is more common in Wales, while the former is more common in England.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cottle_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cottle-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Today the surname Roberts is common in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>, with the biggest concentration by far occurring in North Wales. The name is also common in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.
</p>
