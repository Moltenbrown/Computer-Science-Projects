	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Nisba (onomastics)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nisbat_(onomastics)&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Nisbat (onomastics)">Nisbat (onomastics)</a>)</span></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>In <a href="/wiki/Arabic_name" title="Arabic name">Arabic names</a>, a <b>nisba</b> (also spelled <i>nesba</i>, sometimes <i>nesbat</i>&#160;; <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl">نسبة</span>&#8206; <i><i lang="ar-Latn" title="American Library Association – Library of Congress transliteration">nisbah</i></i>, "attribution") is an adjective indicating the person's place of origin, tribal affiliation, or ancestry, used at the end of the name and occasionally ending in the <a href="/wiki/Suffix" title="Suffix">suffix</a> <i>-iyy(ah)</i>. Nisbah is originally an Arabic word that was passed to many other languages such as <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Urdu_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>.
</p><p>In the usage of Persian, Turkish and Urdu, it is pronounced/written exclusively <i>nisbat</i>. In <a href="/wiki/Arabic_grammar" title="Arabic grammar">Arabic usage</a>, that pronunciation occurs when the word is uttered in its <a href="/wiki/Construct_state#Arabic" title="Construct state">construct state</a> only.
</p><p>The practice has been adopted in <a href="/wiki/Iranian_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Iranian name">Iranian names</a> and South Asian Muslim names.  The nisba has sometimes become a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Original_use">Original use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nisba_(onomastics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Original use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Arabic_nouns_and_adjectives#Nisba" title="Arabic nouns and adjectives">Arabic nouns and adjectives §&#160;Nisba</a></div>
<p>A <i>nisba</i> "relation" is a grammatical term referring to the suffixation of masculine -<i>iyy</i>, feminine <i>-iyyah</i> to a word to make it an adjective. As an example, the word <i>‘Arabiyy</i> (<span lang="ar" dir="rtl" title="Arabic language text">عربي</span>) means "Arab, related to Arabic, Arabian". Nisba forms are very common in Arabic names.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_in_onomastics">Use in onomastics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nisba_(onomastics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Use in onomastics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Traditional Arabic names do not include <a href="/wiki/Family_names" class="mw-redirect" title="Family names">family names</a> or <a href="/wiki/Surnames" class="mw-redirect" title="Surnames">surnames</a>, but rather <a href="/wiki/Patronymics" class="mw-redirect" title="Patronymics">patronymics</a> (<i>nasab</i>), where the name of the person is followed by the name of his father, usually linked by <i>ibn</i> or <i>bin</i> ('son').  Patronymics may be long as they may include all known forefathers.  When a name is simplified to one or two ancestors, it may become confused with other persons' names, so an additional specifier, a <i>nisba</i>, may be added as an attribute.
</p><p>A nisba is usually prefixed by <i>al</i> ('the') and may be to almost anything:
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Multiples">Multiples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nisba_(onomastics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Multiples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>One can have more than one nisba, one can be related to a city, a clan, a profession and a person at the same time. Examples include:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Ibn_al-Nafis" title="Ibn al-Nafis">Ali ibn Abi-Hazm al-Qarshi al-Dimashqi</a>, from the tribe of Quraish and from <a href="/wiki/Damascus" title="Damascus">Damascus</a> (Dimashq).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ibn_Tahir_al-Baghdadi" class="mw-redirect" title="Ibn Tahir al-Baghdadi">Abd al-Qahir ibn Tahir al-Tamimi al-Shafi`i al-Baghdadi</a>, from the tribe of Bani Tamim, from the city of Baghdad and a follower of <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_ibn_Idris_ash-Shafi%60i" class="mw-redirect" title="Muhammad ibn Idris ash-Shafi`i">Muhammad ibn Idris ash-Shafi`i</a>.</li></ul>
<p>The nisba is optional but is quite widespread.
</p>
