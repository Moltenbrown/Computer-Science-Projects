	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Slater</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Slater_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Slater (disambiguation)">Slater (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Slater_at_work,_Rothesay,_Isle_of_Bute.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Slater_at_work%2C_Rothesay%2C_Isle_of_Bute.jpg/220px-Slater_at_work%2C_Rothesay%2C_Isle_of_Bute.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Slater_at_work%2C_Rothesay%2C_Isle_of_Bute.jpg/330px-Slater_at_work%2C_Rothesay%2C_Isle_of_Bute.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Slater_at_work%2C_Rothesay%2C_Isle_of_Bute.jpg/440px-Slater_at_work%2C_Rothesay%2C_Isle_of_Bute.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Slater_at_work,_Rothesay,_Isle_of_Bute.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Slater working on a roof</div></div></div>
<p>A <b>slater</b>, or <b>slate mason</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Tradesman" title="Tradesman">tradesman</a> who covers buildings with <a href="/wiki/Slate" title="Slate">slate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tools_of_the_trade">Tools of the trade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Tools of the trade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The various <a href="/wiki/Hand_tool" title="Hand tool">tools</a> of the slater's trade are all drop-forged.<sup id="cite_ref-RoofingHandbook_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RoofingHandbook-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
The main trimming and holing bar is called a Bench Break or Bench Dresser, the portable steel is called a Roof Break (Bryan N Rowbotham FIoR)
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Taille_d%27une_ardoise.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Taille_d%27une_ardoise.jpg/220px-Taille_d%27une_ardoise.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Taille_d%27une_ardoise.jpg/330px-Taille_d%27une_ardoise.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Taille_d%27une_ardoise.jpg/440px-Taille_d%27une_ardoise.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1910" data-file-height="1352" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Taille_d%27une_ardoise.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Cutting a slate with a slater's hammer on a slater's stake</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Zwaluwen_op_dakgoot_schaliedak.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Zwaluwen_op_dakgoot_schaliedak.JPG/220px-Zwaluwen_op_dakgoot_schaliedak.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Zwaluwen_op_dakgoot_schaliedak.JPG/330px-Zwaluwen_op_dakgoot_schaliedak.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Zwaluwen_op_dakgoot_schaliedak.JPG/440px-Zwaluwen_op_dakgoot_schaliedak.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1920" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Zwaluwen_op_dakgoot_schaliedak.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A slate roof</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>slater's hammer</b> is forged in one single piece, from crucible-cast steel, and has a 12-inch (30&#160;cm) leather handle.  It consists of a claw for drawing nails, a sheer edge for cutting slate, and a head with a sharp point at one end for punching holes in slate and with a hammer head at the other.<sup id="cite_ref-RoofingHandbook_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RoofingHandbook-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Roofing_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roofing-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <b>ripper</b> or Rip is also forged from crucible-cast steel and is 24 inches (60&#160;cm) long.  It consists of a blade and a hook, and is used for removing broken slate.<sup id="cite_ref-RoofingHandbook_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RoofingHandbook-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The hook can be used to cut and remove slating nails.<sup id="cite_ref-RIA_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <b>slater's stake</b> is T-shaped.  The vertical bar of the "T" is pointed to allow it to be driven into a rafter or other woodworking surface.  The horizontal bar of the "T" is used to support slates whilst working on them (cutting, punching, or smoothing) with other tools.  The long bar of the stake can also be used as a straight edge for marking.<sup id="cite_ref-RoofingHandbook_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RoofingHandbook-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Roofing_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roofing-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <b>zax</b> (also called a <b>sack</b> or <b><a href="/wiki/Seax" title="Seax">sax</a></b><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>) is a hand tool for cutting, trimming, and punching nail holes in slate. It consists of a heavy rectangular knife blade with a large point, of square cross section, protruding from the <a href="/wiki/Axe" title="Axe">poll</a> (or butt). The blade edge is used to split slate, while the point is used to pierce square holes for mounting the slate on the roof (with square copper nails) or making a series of small holes marking a line where the slate is broken over a <b>slater's iron</b>. The tool is mounted to a laterally offset handle to protect the user's hand from sharp slate chips.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
