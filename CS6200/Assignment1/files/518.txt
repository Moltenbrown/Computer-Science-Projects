<p><b>National Family Week</b> originated in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and takes place the week before Thanksgiving every year. It was proclaimed an official week by the government of Canada in 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> National Family Week is celebrated throughout the country with special events put on by community organizations. National Family Week also exists in the <a href="/wiki/National_Family_Week_(UK)" title="National Family Week (UK)">United Kingdom</a>, the United States, and Australia.
</p><p>It is a distinct event from <a href="/wiki/Family_Day" title="Family Day">Family Day</a> which is a statutory holiday created by the provincial governments of <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>National Family Week is largely a public awareness campaign, the purpose of which is to celebrate families. Each year FRP Canada, in partnership with other national organizations, creates downloadable resources for families and those who work with families to access when planning an event in their community. With a different theme each year, National Family week highlights various important aspects of family life.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Organizers">Organizers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organizers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Canadian Association of Family Resource Programs (FRP Canada) is a non-profit organization that aims to help families by providing resources, national leadership and consultation to those who care for and support them.  Their vision is "to build a Canada that cares for its children, its families and its communities." <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  There are over 2000 <a href="/wiki/Family_resource_programs" class="mw-redirect" title="Family resource programs">family resource programs</a> in Canada serving approximately 500,000 families.
</p><p>For more than 20 years, Family Service Canada coordinated National Family Week activities.  At the invitation of Family Service Canada in 2007, Families Canada (formerly FRP Canada) took on the lead role in national programming and coordination.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_perspective">Government perspective</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Government perspective">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>National Family Week offers an opportunity for the federal government to honour families and to introduce new legislation or modify existing laws in ways that it deems will help families.
</p><p>In 2007, Minister of Human Resources and Social Development, Monte Solberg, stated that "Canada's New Government is proud to celebrate National Family Week and to recognize the important role that families play in this country. Families are the building blocks of our society, and we will continue delivering on our commitment to support and strengthen them.”<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Family_Week_UK">National Family Week UK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: National Family Week UK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/National_Family_Week_(UK)" title="National Family Week (UK)">National Family Week (UK)</a></div>
<p>National Family Week takes place during the Summer half term holiday and is backed by over 180 not-for-profit organisations <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and has broad political support.  It is organized by Family Week Ltd, part of the Henley Media Group. Although it received Lottery funding in its inaugural year it receives no government funding and operates through commercial sponsorship and support by not-for-profit partners.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Family_Week_United_States">National Family Week United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: National Family Week United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>National Family Week</i> in the United States takes place the week of American Thanksgiving.  It was first proclaimed by Richard Nixon in 1972 pursuant to a resolution introduced in Congress by Representative John Myers (R-IN) upon the suggestion of a constituent, Sam Wiley.  It is led annually by the National Family Week Foundation, established by Mr. Wiley and now managed by John Palatiello, a former staff assistant to Rep. Myers. President <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> proclaimed <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/presidential-proclamation-national-family-week-2018/">the week of November 18 through November 24, 2018, as National Family Week</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Families_Week_Aust">National Families Week Aust</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Family_Week&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: National Families Week Aust">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Australian_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian Government">Australian Government</a>, through the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Families,_Housing,_Community_Services_and_Indigenous_Affairs" title="Department of Families, Housing, Community Services and Indigenous Affairs">Department of Families, Housing, Community Services and Indigenous Affairs</a>, sponsors National Families Week with Families Australia. Centrelink also promotes National Families Week and provides support through its national network.
</p>
