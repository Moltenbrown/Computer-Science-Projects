		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Holmes</b> is an <a href="/wiki/English-language" class="mw-redirect" title="English-language">English-language</a> surname with several origins.
</p><p>The name can be a variant of the surname <i>Holme</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Holmes_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Holmes-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This surname has several etymological origins: it can be derived from a name for someone who lived next to a <a href="/wiki/Holly" title="Holly">holly</a> tree, from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a> <i>holm</i>; it can also be derived from the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>holm</i> and <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a> <i>holmr</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Holme_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Holme-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Another origin of Holmes is from a placename near <a href="/wiki/Dundonald,_South_Ayrshire" title="Dundonald, South Ayrshire">Dundonald</a>, or else a place located in the barony of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inchestuir&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Inchestuir (page does not exist)">Inchestuir</a>. The surname is also sometimes an <a href="/wiki/Anglicised" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicised">Anglicised</a> form of the <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaelic language">Gaelic</a> <i>Mac Thomáis</i>; similarly, <i>Holmes</i> can also be a variant of <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cavish&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cavish (page does not exist)">Cavish</a></i>, derived as an Anglicised form of <i>Mac Thámhais</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Holmes_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Holmes-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
