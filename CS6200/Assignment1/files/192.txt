<p><b>Lewis</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> in the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a>. It has several independent origins.
</p><p>One of the origins of the surname, in England and Wales, is from the Norman <a href="/wiki/Personal_name" title="Personal name">personal name</a> <i>Lowis</i>, <i>Lodovicus</i>. This name is from the post-Classical <a href="/wiki/Latin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin language">Latin</a> name <i>Ludovicus</i> meaning victorious in athletic games (<i>ludus</i>, game; <i>vicus</i>, var. of <i>victor</i>, winner). The name developed into the <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>Clovis</i>, <i>Clouis</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Louis_(given_name)" title="Louis (given name)">Louis</a></i>; as well as into the <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Ludwig_(given_name)" title="Ludwig (given name)">Ludwig</a></i>. The name Lowis spread to England through the <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Normans</a>. Lewis has since become confused with other unrelated names. One such name is <i><a href="/wiki/Llewellyn_(name)" class="mw-redirect" title="Llewellyn (name)">Llywelyn</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a> which has been <a href="/wiki/Anglicised" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicised">Anglicised</a> into <i>Lewis</i>, with among the earliest examples being the Lewis family of <a href="/wiki/Glamorgan" title="Glamorgan">Glamorgan</a> in the 1540s.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Another is the <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaelic language">Gaelic</a> surname <i>Mac Lughaidh</i>, meaning "son of <i>Lughaidh</i>", which has also been Anglicised as <i>Lewis</i>. The surname <i>Lewis</i> is also an <a href="/wiki/Anglicisation_of_names" title="Anglicisation of names">Anglicisation</a> of several like-sounding <a href="/wiki/Jewish_surnames" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish surnames">Jewish surnames</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> such as "<a href="/wiki/Levy_(surname)" title="Levy (surname)">Levy</a>" or "<a href="/wiki/Levi_(surname)" title="Levi (surname)">Levi</a>", and of the Arab form of the name "<a href="/wiki/Elias" title="Elias">Elias</a>".
</p><p>Lewis is the 4th most common surname in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines" title="Saint Vincent and the Grenadines">Saint Vincent and the Grenadines</a>, 6th most common surname in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, 16th most common in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, 22nd most common in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, 24th most common in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, 61st most common in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and 129th most common in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-fb_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
