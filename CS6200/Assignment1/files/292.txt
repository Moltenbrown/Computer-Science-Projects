	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Wainwright (name)</h1>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the name Wainwright. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Wainwright_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Wainwright (disambiguation)">Wainwright</a>.</div>
<table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Wainwright</caption><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Word/name</th><td>England</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Meaning</th><td>"wagon builder"</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Region of origin</th><td>England</td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Other names</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Variant form(s)</th><td>Wainewright, Wainright, Waynewright, Wainwrigt and Winwright.</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Wainwright</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-Saxon">Anglo-Saxon</a> <a href="/wiki/Occupational_surname" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupational surname">occupational surname</a> derived from the pre-7th century <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> word <i>waegnwyrhta</i>.  The prefix, <i>"waeg(e)n</i>/<i>waen</i>, refers to a vehicle/wagon, common in its time as being horse-driven and four-wheeled.  The suffix, <i>wyrhta</i>/<i>wright</i>, refers to a maker/builder.  The earliest public record of the name dates to 1237 in <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>.  There are many variations.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> People with the name include:
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surname">Surname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wainwright_(name)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Surname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Adam_Wainwright" title="Adam Wainwright">Adam Wainwright</a> (born 1981), American baseball pitcher for the St. Louis Cardinals</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alfred_Wainwright" title="Alfred Wainwright">Alfred Wainwright</a> (1907–1991), writer of guide books to the fells of the English Lake District, including <i>List of Wainwrights</i></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Arthur_Wainwright" title="Arthur Wainwright">Arthur Wainwright</a>, English footballer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Charles_S._Wainwright" title="Charles S. Wainwright">Charles S. Wainwright</a> (1826–1907), American artillery officer during the American Civil War</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/George_L._Wainwright,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="George L. Wainwright, Jr.">George L. Wainwright, Jr.</a> (born 1943), American judge, Associate Justice of the North Carolina Supreme Court</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Wainwright" title="Geoffrey Wainwright">Geoffrey Wainwright</a> (born 1939), British Methodist theologian</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Harry_Wainwright" title="Harry Wainwright">Harry Wainwright</a> Locomotive, Carriage and Wagon Superintendent of the South Eastern and Chatham Railway</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Helen_Wainwright" title="Helen Wainwright">Helen Wainwright</a>,  American diver and freestyle swimmer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Henry_Wainwright" title="Henry Wainwright">Henry Wainwright</a> (died 1875), English brushmaker and murderer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hilary_Wainwright" title="Hilary Wainwright">Hilary Wainwright</a> (born 1949), British socialist and feminist</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Mayhew_Wainwright_(bishop)" title="Jonathan Mayhew Wainwright (bishop)">Jonathan Mayhew Wainwright (bishop)</a> (1792–1854), b. Liverpool, England, Americal Episcopal Bishop and father of Jonathan Mayhew Wainwright II</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Mayhew_Wainwright_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Jonathan Mayhew Wainwright II">Jonathan Mayhew Wainwright II</a> (1821–1863), Lieutenant in the United States Navy during the US Civil War, grandfather of Jonathan Mayhew Wainwright IV.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Mayhew_Wainwright_IV" class="mw-redirect" title="Jonathan Mayhew Wainwright IV">Jonathan Mayhew Wainwright IV</a> (1883–1953), United States Army general during World War II, Recipient of the Medal of Honor, Japanese Prisoner of War, and the last "fighting general."</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Loudon_Wainwright_Jr." title="Loudon Wainwright Jr.">Loudon Wainwright Jr.</a> (1924–1988), American columnist and editor.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Loudon_Wainwright_III" title="Loudon Wainwright III">Loudon Wainwright III</a> (born 1946), American songwriter, folk singer, humorist, actor, father of Martha and Rufus Wainwright</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Louie_L._Wainwright" title="Louie L. Wainwright">Louie L. Wainwright</a>, Secretary of Florida Division of Corrections; respondent in Gideon v. Wainwright and other famous Supreme Court cases</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lyndon_Wainwright" title="Lyndon Wainwright">Lyndon Wainwright</a> (1919–2018), English ballroom and Latin exhibition dancer.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mark_Wainwright" title="Mark Wainwright">Mark Wainwright</a>, Australian academic</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Martha_Wainwright" title="Martha Wainwright">Martha Wainwright</a> (born 1976), Canadian-American singer-songwriter, daughter of Loudon Wainwright III</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Martin_Wainwright" title="Martin Wainwright">Martin Wainwright</a>, British journalist</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Michael_Wainwright_(musician)" title="Michael Wainwright (musician)">Michael Wainwright (musician)</a>, Canadian musician</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Richard_Wainwright_(politician)" title="Richard Wainwright (politician)">Richard Wainwright (politician)</a> (1918–2003), British Liberal politician</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Richard_Wainwright_(Civil_War_naval_officer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Richard Wainwright (Civil War naval officer)">Richard Wainwright (Civil War naval officer)</a> (1817–1862), United States Navy Commander, father of Admiral Richard Wainwright</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Richard_Wainwright_(Spanish-American_War_naval_officer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Richard Wainwright (Spanish-American War naval officer)">Richard Wainwright (Spanish-American War naval officer)</a> (1849–1926), United States Navy Admiral, son of Commander Richard Wainwright</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Richard_Wainwright_(World_War_I_naval_officer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Richard Wainwright (World War I naval officer)">Richard Wainwright (World War I naval officer)</a> (1881–1944), United States Navy Commander, son of Admiral Richard Wainwright</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rob_Wainwright_(rugby_union)" title="Rob Wainwright (rugby union)">Rob Wainwright (rugby union)</a> (born 1965), rugby union player, capped 37 times for Scotland (captain 16 times) and once for the British Lions</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Wainwright" title="Rufus Wainwright">Rufus Wainwright</a> (born 1973), Canadian-American singer-songwriter, son of Loudon Wainwright III</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sally_Wainwright" title="Sally Wainwright">Sally Wainwright</a>, English dramatist and television writer</li>
<li>William Wainwright, British musician better known under his artist name <a href="/wiki/William_Orbit" title="William Orbit">William Orbit</a>. Other aliases include Wayne Wright</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_Edward_Wainwright" class="mw-redirect" title="William Edward Wainwright">William Edward Wainwright</a> (1873–1959), mine manager in Broken Hill, New South Wales, Australia</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ted_Wainwright" title="Ted Wainwright">Ted Wainwright</a> (1865–1919), English cricketer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Wainwright_(cricketer)" title="Thomas Wainwright (cricketer)">Thomas Wainwright (cricketer)</a> (1940–2019), English cricketer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_L._Wainwright" title="William L. Wainwright">William L. Wainwright</a>, American politician, member of the North Carolina General Assembly</li></ul>
