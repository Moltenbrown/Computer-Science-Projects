<p><b>Gitxsan</b> (also spelled <b>Gitksan</b>) are an <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_in_Canada" title="Indigenous peoples in Canada">indigenous people</a> of Canada whose home territory comprises most of the area known as the <a href="/wiki/Skeena_Country" title="Skeena Country">Skeena Country</a> in English (Git: means "people of" and Xsan: means "the River of Mist"). Gitksan territory encompasses approximately 53,000&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (20,000&#160;sq&#160;mi) of land, from the basin of the upper <a href="/wiki/Skeena_River" title="Skeena River">Skeena River</a> from about Legate Creek to the Skeena's headwaters and its surrounding tributaries.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>    Part of the <a href="/wiki/Tsimshianic_languages" title="Tsimshianic languages">Tsimshianic language group</a>, their culture is considered to be part of the civilization of the <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Pacific_Northwest_Coast" title="Indigenous peoples of the Pacific Northwest Coast">Indigenous peoples of the Pacific Northwest Coast</a>, although their territory lies in the <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia_Interior" title="British Columbia Interior">Interior</a> rather than on the <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia_Coast" title="British Columbia Coast">Coast</a>.  They were at one time also known as the <i>Interior Tsimshian</i>, a term which also included the <a href="/wiki/Nisga%27a" title="Nisga&#39;a">Nisga'a</a>, the Gitxsan's neighbours to the north.  Their neighbours to the west are the <a href="/wiki/Tsimshian" title="Tsimshian">Tsimshian</a> (a.k.a. the Coast Tsimshian) while to the east the <a href="/wiki/Wet%CA%BCsuwet%CA%BCen" title="Wetʼsuwetʼen">Wetʼsuwetʼen</a>, an Athapaskan people, with whom they have a long and deep relationship and shared political and cultural community.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Society_and_culture">Society and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gitxsan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Society and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gitxsan are a <a href="/wiki/Matrilineality" title="Matrilineality">matrilineal</a> society that consists of Frog, Eagle, Wolf, and Fireweed <a href="/wiki/Clan" title="Clan">Clans</a>. Each clan consists of a series of independent Houses (Wilp), each with their own High Chief, and traditional territories and fishing sites. Marriage within a clan is forbidden.
</p><p>There are approximately 5,000 people <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> wide with many living in traditional Gitxsan territory. Many also live elsewhere in British Columbia, in places such as <a href="/wiki/Terrace,_British_Columbia" title="Terrace, British Columbia">Terrace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smithers,_British_Columbia" title="Smithers, British Columbia">Smithers</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, as well as around the world.
</p><p>Eighty per cent of the people living on the lands surrounding Legate creek to the Skeena headwaters are Gitxsan ('People of the River Mist') and archaeological evidence supports a continuous habitation of at least 10,000 years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  Their traditional language is called <a href="/wiki/Gitxsan_language" title="Gitxsan language">Gitxsanimaax</a>.
</p><p>A museum known as <a href="/wiki/%27Ksan" title="&#39;Ksan">'Ksan</a> displaying some traditional and modern Gitksan art and history is located on the <a href="/wiki/Gitanmaax" class="mw-redirect" title="Gitanmaax">Gitanmaax</a> reserve near <a href="/wiki/Hazelton,_British_Columbia" title="Hazelton, British Columbia">Hazelton</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Title_and_treaties">Title and treaties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gitxsan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Title and treaties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The aboriginal title rights of the Gitxsan and their neighbours, the <a href="/wiki/Wet%CA%BCsuwet%CA%BCen" title="Wetʼsuwetʼen">Wetʼsuwetʼen</a>, were affirmed by the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Canada" title="Supreme Court of Canada">Supreme Court of Canada</a> in its 1997 <a href="/wiki/Delgamuukw_v_British_Columbia" title="Delgamuukw v British Columbia">Delgamuukw</a> decision.
</p><p>To date, a treaty agreement between the <a href="/wiki/Gitxsan_Nation" title="Gitxsan Nation">Gitxsan Nation</a> and the Federal <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Canada" title="Government of Canada">Government of Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Executive_Council_of_British_Columbia" title="Executive Council of British Columbia">Provincial Government of British Columbia</a> has not been reached.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Communities">Communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gitxsan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Communities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some of the Gitxsan (Gitksan) villages are, divided by dialect:
</p><p>Gitxsan dialect or Gitxsanimax̱, also known as Eastern Gitxsan dialect speaking bands in Gigeenix (eastern region):
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Hazelton,_British_Columbia#Old_Hazelton_and_Two_Mile" title="Hazelton, British Columbia">Old Hazelton</a> (traditional name: Gitanmaax, <a href="/wiki/Gitanmaax_Band" title="Gitanmaax Band">Gitanmaax Band</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kispiox" title="Kispiox">Kispiox</a> (traditional name: Anspa'yaxw, <a href="/wiki/Kispiox_Band_Council" title="Kispiox Band Council">Kispiox Band Council</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Glen_Vowell" title="Glen Vowell">Glen Vowell</a> (traditional name: Sik-e-Dakh, <a href="/wiki/Gitxsan_Treaty_Society" title="Gitxsan Treaty Society">Glen Vowell Indian Band</a>)</li></ul>
<p>Gitsken dialect or Gitsenimx̱ , also known as Western Gitksan dialect speaking bands in Gyeets (western region):
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Gitanyow" title="Gitanyow">Gitanyow</a> (formerly Kitwancool, <a href="/wiki/Gitanyow_Band" title="Gitanyow Band">Gitanyow Band</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gitsegukla" title="Gitsegukla">Gitsegukla</a> (formerly: Kitsegeucla, <a href="/wiki/Gitsegukla_Indian_Band" title="Gitsegukla Indian Band">Gitsegukla Indian Band (formerly Kitsegugkla)</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kitwanga" title="Kitwanga">Kitwanga</a> (traditional name: Gitwangak, <a href="/wiki/Gitwangak_Indian_Band" title="Gitwangak Indian Band">Gitwangak Indian Band (formerly Kitwanga)</a>) - the Gitksan community of <a href="/wiki/Cedarvale,_British_Columbia" title="Cedarvale, British Columbia">Cedarvale</a> (traditional name: Minskinish or Meanskaniist) belong as Koonwat Indian Reserve No. 7 to the Gitwangak.</li></ul>
