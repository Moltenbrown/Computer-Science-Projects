<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name Archer is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a> <i>archere</i>, and <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>archer</i>, <i>archier</i>. The surname originated as an <a href="/wiki/Occupational_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupational name">occupational name</a> denoting an <a href="/wiki/Archery" title="Archery">archer</a>. By the 14th century, the mentioned Middle English and Old French words replaced the native English <i>bowman</i>. In <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, the surname <i>Archer</i> has absorbed many like-sounding names and <a href="/wiki/Cognate" title="Cognate">cognates</a> (for example, the <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> <i>Archier</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
