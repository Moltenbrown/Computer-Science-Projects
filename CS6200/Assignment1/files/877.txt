<p>The ruins of <b>Spiš Castle</b> (<a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a>: <i lang="sk">Spišský hrad</i>, <span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sk-Spi%C5%A1sk%C3%BD_hrad.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f3/Sk-Spi%C5%A1sk%C3%BD_hrad.ogg" class="internal" title="Sk-Spišský hrad.ogg">pronunciation</a></span>&#160;<small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Sk-Spi%C5%A1sk%C3%BD_hrad.ogg" title="File:Sk-Spišský hrad.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span>; <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <i lang="hu">Szepesi vár</i>; <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Zipser Burg</i>) in eastern <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a> form one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Castle" title="Castle">castle</a> sites in Central Europe. The castle is situated above the town of <a href="/wiki/Spi%C5%A1sk%C3%A9_Podhradie" title="Spišské Podhradie">Spišské Podhradie</a> and the village of <a href="/wiki/%C5%BDehra" title="Žehra">Žehra</a>, in the region known as <a href="/wiki/Spi%C5%A1" title="Spiš">Spiš</a> (<a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <i lang="hu">Szepes</i>, German: <i lang="de">Zips</i>, <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <i lang="pl">Spisz</i>, <a href="/wiki/Latin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <i lang="la">Scepusium</i>). It was included in the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> list of <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Sites" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage Sites">World Heritage Sites</a> in 1993 (together with the adjacent locations of <a href="/wiki/Spi%C5%A1sk%C3%A1_Kapitula" title="Spišská Kapitula">Spišská Kapitula</a>, Spišské Podhradie and Žehra). This is one of the biggest European castles by area (41 426 m²).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spi%C5%A1_Castle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Szepescivertanlegi4.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Szepescivertanlegi4.jpg/220px-Szepescivertanlegi4.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="316" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Szepescivertanlegi4.jpg/330px-Szepescivertanlegi4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1a/Szepescivertanlegi4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="418" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Szepescivertanlegi4.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aerial photograph of the castle</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Spi%C5%A1_Castle_4.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Spi%C5%A1_Castle_4.jpg/200px-Spi%C5%A1_Castle_4.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Spi%C5%A1_Castle_4.jpg/300px-Spi%C5%A1_Castle_4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Spi%C5%A1_Castle_4.jpg/400px-Spi%C5%A1_Castle_4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1056" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Spi%C5%A1_Castle_4.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Spiš castle - the central part.</div></div></div>
<p>Spiš Castle was built in the 12th century on the site of an earlier castle. It was the political, administrative, economic and cultural centre of <a href="/wiki/Szepes_County" title="Szepes County">Szepes County</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Kingdom of Hungary</a>. Before 1464, it was owned by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Hungary" class="mw-redirect" title="List of rulers of Hungary">kings of Hungary</a>, afterwards (until 1528) by the <a href="/wiki/John_Z%C3%A1polya" title="John Zápolya">Zápolya</a> family, the <a href="/wiki/Thurz%C3%B3" class="mw-redirect" title="Thurzó">Thurzó</a> family (1531–1635), the <a href="/wiki/Imre_Cs%C3%A1ky_(cardinal)" title="Imre Csáky (cardinal)">Csáky</a> family (1638–1945), and (since 1945) by the state of <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a> then <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>.
</p><p>Originally a <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> stone castle with fortifications, a two-story Romanesque palace and a three-nave Romanesque-<a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a> basilica were constructed by the second half of the 13th century. A second extramural settlement was built in the 14th century, by which the castle area was doubled. The castle was completely rebuilt in the 15th century; the castle walls were heightened and a third extramural settlement was constructed. A late Gothic chapel was added around 1470. The <a href="/wiki/John_Z%C3%A1polya" title="John Zápolya">Zápolya</a> clan performed late Gothic transformations, which made the upper castle into a comfortable family residence, typical of late Renaissance residences of the 16th and 17th centuries. 
The last owners of the Spiš Castle, the Csáky family, abandoned the castle in the early 18th century because they considered it too uncomfortable to live in. They moved to the newly built nearby village castles/palaces in Hodkovce near <a href="/wiki/%C5%BDehra" title="Žehra">Žehra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spi%C5%A1sk%C3%BD_Hrhov" title="Spišský Hrhov">Spišský Hrhov</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Decline_and_reconstruction">Decline and reconstruction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spi%C5%A1_Castle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Decline and reconstruction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1780, the castle was destroyed in a fire. The cause of the blaze is unknown, but there are a few theories. One is that the Csáky family purposely burned it down to reduce taxes as at the time additional taxes applied to roofed buildings. Another is that it was struck by lightning, which started the fire. A third is that some soldiers in the castle were making moonshine and in the process accidentally started the fire. Whatever the case, after the fire, the castle was no longer occupied and began to fall into disrepair.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The castle was partly reconstructed in the second half of the 20th century, and extensive archaeological research was carried out on the site. The reconstructed sections house displays of the Spiš Museum, and artifacts such as torture devices formerly used in the castle.
</p>
