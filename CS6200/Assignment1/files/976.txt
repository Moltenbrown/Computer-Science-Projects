<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_and_variants">Origins and variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murphy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins and variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The surname is a variant of two Irish surnames: "Ó Murchadha"/"Ó Murchadh" (descendant of "Murchadh"), and "Mac Murchaidh"/"<a href="/wiki/Mac_Murchadh" class="mw-redirect" title="Mac Murchadh">Mac Murchadh</a>" (son of "Murchadh")<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> derived from the Irish <a href="/wiki/Personal_name" title="Personal name">personal name</a> "Murchadh", which meant sea-warrior or sea-battler<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (<i>muir</i> meaning sea and <i>cath</i> meaning battle).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is said of Murrough (Murchadh) as he entered the thick of the fight and prepared to assail the foreign invaders, the Danes, when they had repulsed the Dal-Cais, that 'he was seized with a boiling terrible anger, an excessive elevation and greatness of spirit and mind. A bird of valour and championship rose in him, and fluttered over his head and on his breath.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In modern <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>, "Ó Murchú", rather than "Ó Murchadha", is used.
</p><p>"Murphy" is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_most_common_surnames_in_Europe#Republic_of_Ireland" title="List of most common surnames in Europe">most common surname in Ireland</a>, the fourteenth most common surname in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, and the fifty-eighth most common surname in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
The last name "Murphy" is also what Irish travellers called themselves when people asked for names.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
