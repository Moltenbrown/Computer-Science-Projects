<p><b>Francophonie</b> is the quality of speaking <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>. It is not to be confused with the <a href="/wiki/Organisation_internationale_de_la_Francophonie" title="Organisation internationale de la Francophonie">Organisation internationale de la Francophonie</a>, sometimes also informally called "la Francophonie". The term designates the ensemble of people, organisations and governments that share the use of French on a daily basis and as administrative language, teaching language or chosen language.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Denominations">Denominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francophonie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Denominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Francophonie</b>, <b>francophonie</b> and <b>francophone space</b> are <a href="/wiki/Syntagmatic" class="mw-redirect" title="Syntagmatic">syntagmatic</a> expressions that are sometimes misunderstood or misused. They can be synonymous but most of the time they are complementary.
</p>
<ul><li>"francophonie", with a small "f", refers to populations and people who speak French for communication or/and in their daily lives.<sup id="cite_ref-francophonie.org_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-francophonie.org-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>"Francophonie", with a capital "F", can be defined as referring to the governments, governmental and non-governmental organisations or governing officials that share the use of French in their work and exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-francophonie.org_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-francophonie.org-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>"Francophone space", "Francosphere" represents not only a linguistic or geographic reality, but also a cultural entity: for example describing any individual who identifies with one of the francophone cultures, may it be <a href="/wiki/Slavs" title="Slavs">Slavic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Creole_peoples" title="Creole peoples">Creole</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_French" title="American French">North American</a> or <a href="/wiki/Oceanian" class="mw-redirect" title="Oceanian">Oceanian</a> for example.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
