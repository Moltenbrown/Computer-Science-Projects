	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Hayward (profession)</h1>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For other uses of "Hayward", see <a href="/wiki/Hayward_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Hayward (disambiguation)">Hayward</a>.</div>
<p><b>Hayward</b>, or "<a href="/wiki/Hedge_(gardening)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hedge (gardening)">hedge</a> warden", was an officer of an English parish dating from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> in charge of <a href="/wiki/Fence" title="Fence">fences</a> and enclosures; also, a herdsman in charge of cattle and other animals grazing on common land. Their main job was to protect the crops of the village from livestock.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Sussex" title="Sussex">Sussex</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surrey" title="Surrey">Surrey</a> the form <b>Haybittle</b> occurs (from <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a>, <i>haia</i>, enclosure and <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Anglo-Saxon</a> <i>bydel</i>, <a href="/wiki/Bailiff" title="Bailiff">bailiff</a>). This has survived as a locally common surname, especially in and around <a href="/wiki/Reigate" title="Reigate">Reigate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
