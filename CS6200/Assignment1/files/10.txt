	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">English-speaking world</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Countries and regions where English is everyday language and people (or peoples) who speak English</div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Countries_with_English_as_Official_Language.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Countries_with_English_as_Official_Language.png/300px-Countries_with_English_as_Official_Language.png" decoding="async" width="300" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Countries_with_English_as_Official_Language.png/450px-Countries_with_English_as_Official_Language.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Countries_with_English_as_Official_Language.png/600px-Countries_with_English_as_Official_Language.png 2x" data-file-width="1366" data-file-height="710" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Countries_with_English_as_Official_Language.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nations in which English is an official language (<i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/De_jure" title="De jure">de jure</a></i>). Anglosphere countries are a subset of those where English is the main native language. <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #00d921; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Anglosphere" title="Anglosphere">Anglosphere</a></div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #088a4b; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Official as majority language</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #16c46d; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Official as minority language</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #045fb4; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Co-official as majority language</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #5599ff; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Co-official as minority language</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #f1ff59; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Unofficial<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2018)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #ffc34a; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Not official as majority language</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #ffffff; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Not official as minority language</div> The term "majority language" is here defined as a language of which more than 50% of the population have some command. It does not mean that this command is native, nor that English is the most widely used language of the respective country.</div></div></div>
<p>Over 2 billion people speak <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Two_thousand_million_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Two_thousand_million-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> making English the <a href="/wiki/List_of_languages_by_total_number_of_speakers" title="List of languages by total number of speakers">largest language by number of speakers</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Wikipedia_and_sources_that_mirror_or_use_it" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="This claim cites another Wikipedia article. Articles need references to reliable third-party sources. (May 2019)">circular reference</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_languages_by_number_of_native_speakers" title="List of languages by number of native speakers">third largest language by number of native speakers</a>. With 300 million native speakers, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States of America</a> is the largest English-speaking country. As pictured in the pie graph below, most native speakers of English are <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">Americans</a>.
</p><p>Additionally, there are 60 million native speakers in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, 29 million in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, 25.1 million in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> 4.7 million in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>, and 4.9 million in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, English is one of 24 official languages and is widely used by institutions, and by a majority of the population as the native language in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a> and as a second language in other member states.
</p><p>Estimates that include <a href="/wiki/Second_language" title="Second language">second language</a> speakers vary greatly, from 470 million to more than 2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Two_thousand_million_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Two_thousand_million-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/David_Crystal" title="David Crystal">David Crystal</a> calculates that, as of 2003, non-native speakers outnumbered native speakers by a ratio of 3 to 1.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> When combining native and non-native speakers, English is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_languages_by_total_number_of_speakers" title="List of languages by total number of speakers">most widely spoken language worldwide</a>.
</p><p>Besides the major <a href="/wiki/List_of_dialects_of_the_English_language" class="mw-redirect" title="List of dialects of the English language">varieties of English</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/American_English" title="American English">American English</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_English" title="British English">British English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canadian_English" title="Canadian English">Canadian English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australian_English" title="Australian English">Australian English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irish_English" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish English">Irish English</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_English" title="New Zealand English">New Zealand English</a> and their sub-varieties, countries such as <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a> also have millions of native speakers of <a href="/wiki/Dialect_continuum" title="Dialect continuum">dialect continua</a> ranging from English-based creole <a href="/wiki/Languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Languages">languages</a> to <a href="/wiki/Standard_English" title="Standard English">Standard English</a>. Other countries such as Ghana and Uganda also use English as their primary official languages.
</p><p>India now claims to be the world's <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_English-speaking_population" title="List of countries by English-speaking population">second-largest</a> English-speaking country. The most reliable estimate is around 10% of its population or 125 million people, second only to the US and expected to quadruple in the next decade from 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>There are six large countries with a majority of native English speakers that are sometimes grouped under the term <a href="/wiki/Anglosphere" title="Anglosphere">Anglosphere</a>. In numbers of English speakers they are: the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States of America</a> (at least 231 million),<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERyan2013Table_1_6-0" Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>) (60 million),<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEOffice_for_National_Statistics2013Key_Points_7-0" class="reference"><a (at least 20 million),<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStatistics_Canada2014_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStatistics_Canada2014-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Australia" </p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Countries_where_English_is_an_official_language">Countries where English is an official language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=English-speaking_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Countries where English is an official language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_territorial_entities_where_English_is_an_official_language" title="List of territorial entities where English is an official language">List of territorial entities where English is an official language</a></div>
<p>In some countries where English is not the most spoken language, it is an <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official language</a>. These countries include <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> (co-official with <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>), <a href="/wiki/Eswatini" title="Eswatini">Eswatini (Swaziland)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kiribati" title="Kiribati">Kiribati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">Lesotho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Federated_States_of_Micronesia" title="Federated States of Micronesia">Federated States of Micronesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palau" title="Palau">Palau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">the Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seychelles" title="Seychelles">Seychelles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>. There also are countries where in a part of the territory English became a co-official language, in Colombia's <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_y_Providencia" class="mw-redirect" title="San Andrés y Providencia">San Andrés y Providencia</a>, China's <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> and Nicaragua's <a href="/wiki/Mosquito_Coast" title="Mosquito Coast">Mosquito Coast</a>. This was a result of the influence of <a href="/wiki/British_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="British colonization of the Americas">British colonization</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_the_Philippines" title="Commonwealth of the Philippines">American colonization</a> in these areas.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> has the largest number of second-language speakers of English (<i>see</i> <a href="/wiki/Indian_English" title="Indian English">Indian English</a>); Crystal (2004) claims that combining native and non-native speakers, India has more people who speak or understand English than any other country in the world. However, most scholars and research that has been conducted dispute his assertions.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECrystal2004b_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrystal2004b-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> also has the English language (<a href="/wiki/Pakistani_English" title="Pakistani English">Pakistani English</a>) as a second official language after the <a href="/wiki/Urdu" title="Urdu">Urdu language</a> as the result of <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British rule (Raj)</a>, making Pakistan the only <a href="/wiki/Islam_by_country" title="Islam by country">Islamic country</a> in which English is official. <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">The Philippines</a> use the English language, too, as the second and third official language after <a href="/wiki/Sinhalese_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Sinhalese language">Sinhala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">Filipino</a>.
</p><p>English is one of the eleven official languages that are given equal status in South Africa (<a href="/wiki/South_African_English" title="South African English">South African English</a>). It is also the official language in current <a href="/wiki/Dependent_territory" title="Dependent territory">dependent territories</a> of Australia (<a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Island" title="Norfolk Island">Norfolk Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Island" title="Christmas Island">Christmas Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cocos_Island" title="Cocos Island">Cocos Island</a>) and of the United States of America (<a href="/wiki/American_Samoa" title="American Samoa">American Samoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> (in Puerto Rico, English is co-official with <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">US Virgin Islands</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Special_Administrative_Region_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People&#39;s Republic of China">Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China</a>.
</p><p>Although the United States federal government has no official languages, English has been given official status by 32 of the 50 <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">US state</a> governments.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, per <a href="/wiki/United_States_nationality_law" title="United States nationality law">United States nationality law</a>, the process of becoming a naturalized citizen of the US entails a basic English proficiency test, which may be the most prominent example of the claim of the nation not having an official language being belied by policy realities.
</p><p>Although falling short of official status, English is also an important language in several former colonies and <a href="/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">protectorates</a> of the United Kingdom, such as <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brunei" title="Brunei">Brunei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="English_as_a_global_language">English as a global language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=English-speaking_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: English as a global language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/English_in_computing" title="English in computing">English in computing</a>, <a href="/wiki/International_English" title="International English">International English</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Englishes" title="World Englishes">World Englishes</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_language" title="World language">World language</a>, and <a href="/wiki/English_as_a_second_or_foreign_language" title="English as a second or foreign language">English as a second or foreign language</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Anglospeak.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Anglospeak.png/400px-Anglospeak.png" decoding="async" width="400" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Anglospeak.png/600px-Anglospeak.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Anglospeak.png/800px-Anglospeak.png 2x" data-file-width="2664" data-file-height="1468" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Anglospeak.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><span style="background-color:#004288; color:white; border:1px solid darkgray; text-align:center;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</span> States and territories in which English or an <a href="/wiki/English_creole" class="mw-redirect" title="English creole">English creole</a> is the first language of the majority of the population.<br /><span style="background-color:#79c1ff; color:black; border:1px solid darkgray; text-align:center;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</span> States and territories in which English is an official, but not the majority language.</div></div></div>
<p>Because English is so widely spoken, it has often been referred to as a "<a href="/wiki/World_language" title="World language">world language</a>", the <a href="/wiki/Lingua_franca" title="Lingua franca">lingua franca</a> of the modern era,<sup id="cite_ref-Graddol_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Graddol-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and while it is not an official language in most countries, it is currently the language most often taught as a <a href="/wiki/Foreign_language" title="Foreign language">foreign language</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>  It is, by international treaty, the official language for aeronautical<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and maritime<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> communications. English is one of the official languages of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> and many other international organizations, including the <a href="/wiki/List_of_IOC_country_codes" title="List of IOC country codes">International Olympic Committee</a>. It is also one of two co-official languages for astronauts (besides the Russian language) serving on board the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>English is studied most often in the European Union, and the perception of the usefulness of foreign languages among Europeans is 67 percent in favour of English ahead of 17 percent for German and 16 percent for French (as of 2012<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=English-speaking_world&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>). Among some of the non-English-speaking EU countries, the following percentages of the adult population claimed to be able to converse in English in 2012: 90 percent in the Netherlands, 89 percent in Malta, 86 percent in Sweden and Denmark, 73 percent in Cyprus, Croatia, and Austria, 70 percent in Finland, and over 50 percent in Greece, Belgium, Luxembourg, Slovenia, and Germany. In 2012, excluding native speakers, 38 percent of Europeans consider that they can speak English.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Books, magazines, and newspapers written in English are available in many countries around the world, and English is the most commonly used language in the sciences<sup id="cite_ref-Graddol_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Graddol-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Science_Citation_Index" title="Science Citation Index">Science Citation Index</a> reporting as early as 1997 that 95% of its articles were written in English, even though only half of them came from authors in English-speaking countries.
</p><p>In publishing, English literature predominates considerably with 28 percent of all books published in the world [leclerc 2011]<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#What_information_to_include" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="A complete citation is needed (June 2014)">full citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and 30 percent of web content in 2011 (down from 50 percent in 2000).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENorthrup2013_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENorthrup2013-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This increasing use of the English language globally has had a large impact on many other languages, leading to <a href="/wiki/Language_shift" title="Language shift">language shift</a> and even <a href="/wiki/Language_death" title="Language death">language death</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and to claims of <a href="/wiki/Linguistic_imperialism" title="Linguistic imperialism">linguistic imperialism</a>. English itself has become more open to <a href="/wiki/Language_shift" title="Language shift">language shift</a> as multiple <a href="/wiki/List_of_dialects_of_the_English_language" class="mw-redirect" title="List of dialects of the English language">regional varieties</a> feed back into the language as a whole.<sup id="cite_ref-one_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-one-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
