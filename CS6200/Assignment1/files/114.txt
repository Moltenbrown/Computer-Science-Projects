<p>A <b>testator</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/ɛ/: &#39;e&#39; in &#39;dress&#39;">ɛ</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/eɪ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;face&#39;">eɪ</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/ɔːr/: &#39;ar&#39; in &#39;war&#39;">ɔːr</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Person" title="Person">person</a> who has written and executed a <a href="/wiki/Will_(law)" class="mw-redirect" title="Will (law)">last will and testament</a> that is in effect at the time of his/her <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is any "person who makes a will."<sup id="cite_ref-Gordon_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gordon-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Related_terms">Related terms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Testator&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Related terms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>A female testator is sometimes referred to as a <b>testatrix</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/ɛ/: &#39;e&#39; in &#39;dress&#39;">ɛ</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/eɪ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;face&#39;">eɪ</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="&#39;r&#39; in &#39;rye&#39;">r</span><span title="/ɪ/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;kit&#39;">ɪ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>), plural <b>testatrices</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/ɛ/: &#39;e&#39; in &#39;dress&#39;">ɛ</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/ə/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="&#39;r&#39; in &#39;rye&#39;">r</span><span title="/aɪ/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;tide&#39;">aɪ</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="/iː/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">iː</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>), particularly in older cases.<sup id="cite_ref-Gordon_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gordon-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Ahmadiyya_Islam" class="mw-redirect" title="Ahmadiyya Islam">Ahmadiyya Islam</a>, a testator is referred to as a <b>moosi</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-Khairallah_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Khairallah-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> who is someone that has signed up for Wasiyyat or a will, under the plan initiated by the <a href="/wiki/Mirza_Ghulam_Ahmad" title="Mirza Ghulam Ahmad">Promised Messiah</a>, thus committing a portion, not less than one-tenth, of his lifetime earnings and any property to a cause.</li></ul>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Adjective" title="Adjective">adjectival</a> form of the word is <b>testamentary</b>, as in:</li></ul>
<ol><li><a href="/wiki/Testamentary_capacity" title="Testamentary capacity">Testamentary capacity</a>, or mental capacity or ability to execute a will and</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Testamentary_disposition" title="Testamentary disposition">Testamentary disposition</a>, or gift made in a will (see that article for types).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Testamentary_trust" title="Testamentary trust">Testamentary trust</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Trust_(law)" class="mw-redirect" title="Trust (law)">trust</a> that is created in a will.</li></ol>
<ul><li>A <a href="/wiki/Will_(law)" class="mw-redirect" title="Will (law)">will</a> is also known as a <b>last will and testament</b>.</li>
<li><b>Testacy</b> means the status of being <b>testate</b>, that is, having executed a will.  The property of such a person goes through the <a href="/wiki/Probate" title="Probate">probate</a> process.</li>
<li><b>Intestacy</b> means the status of <i>not</i> having made a will, or to have died <i>without</i> a valid will.  The estate of a person who dies <b>intestate</b>, undergoes <a href="/wiki/Administration_of_an_estate_on_death" class="mw-redirect" title="Administration of an estate on death">administration</a>, rather than probate.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Attestation_clause" title="Attestation clause">attestation clause</a> of a will is where the witnesses to a will attest to certain facts concerning the making of the will by the testator, and where they sign their names as witnesses.</li></ul>
