	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Wedding anniversary</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>A <b>wedding anniversary</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Anniversary" title="Anniversary">anniversary</a> of the date a <a href="/wiki/Wedding" title="Wedding">wedding</a> took place. Traditional names exist for some of them: for instance, fifty years of marriage is called a "golden wedding anniversary" or simply a "golden anniversary" or "golden wedding".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_recognition">Official recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wedding_anniversary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Official recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The historic origins of wedding anniversaries date back to the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>, when husbands crowned their wives with a silver wreath on their twenty-fifth anniversary, and a gold wreath on the fiftieth.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Later, principally in the twentieth century, commercialism led to the addition of more anniversaries being represented by a named gift.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_realm" title="Commonwealth realm">Commonwealth realms</a>, one can receive a message from the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_realm_monarchies" class="mw-redirect" title="Commonwealth realm monarchies">monarch</a> for 60th, 65th, and 70th wedding anniversaries, and any wedding anniversary after that.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This is done by applying to <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_Palace" title="Buckingham Palace">Buckingham Palace</a> in the United Kingdom, or to the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor-General">Governor-General</a>'s office in the other Commonwealth realms.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Australia, where one can receive a letter of congratulations from the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_Australia" title="Governor-General of Australia">Governor-General</a> on the 50th and all subsequent wedding anniversaries; the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Australia" title="Prime Minister of Australia">Prime Minister</a>, the federal Opposition leader, local members of both state and federal parliaments, and state Governors may also send salutations for the same anniversaries.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Canada, one can also receive a message from the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor-General of Canada">Governor-General</a> for the 50th anniversary, and every fifth anniversary after that.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the United States, a couple can receive an anniversary greeting card from the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> for the 50th and all subsequent anniversaries.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholics</a> may apply for a <a href="/wiki/Papal_blessing" class="mw-redirect" title="Papal blessing">Papal blessing</a> through their local diocese for wedding anniversaries of a special nature (25th, 50th, 60th, etc.).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebration_and_gifts">Celebration and gifts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wedding_anniversary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Celebration and gifts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-More_citations_needed_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wedding_anniversary&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. <p>The names of some anniversaries provide guidance for appropriate or traditional gifts for the spouses to give each other; if there is a party these can be brought by the guests or influence the theme or decoration. These gifts vary in different countries, but some years have well-established connections now common to most nations: 5th <a href="/wiki/Wood" title="Wood">Wood</a>, 10th <a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">Tin</a>, 15th <a href="/wiki/Lead_glass" title="Lead glass">Crystal</a>, 20th <a href="/wiki/Tableware" title="Tableware">China</a>, 25th <a href="/wiki/Silver_Jubilee" class="mw-redirect" title="Silver Jubilee">Silver</a>, 30th <a href="/wiki/Pearl" title="Pearl">Pearl</a>, 35th <a href="/wiki/Jade" title="Jade">Jade</a>, 40th <a href="/wiki/Ruby_Jubilee" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruby Jubilee">Ruby</a>, 45th <a href="/wiki/Sapphire" title="Sapphire">Sapphire</a>, 50th <a href="/wiki/Golden_Jubilee" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Jubilee">Gold</a>, 60th <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Jubilee" class="mw-redirect" title="Diamond Jubilee">Diamond</a>, and 70th <a href="/wiki/Platinum_Jubilee" class="mw-redirect" title="Platinum Jubilee">Platinum</a>. In <a href="/wiki/English-speaking_world" title="English-speaking world">English speaking countries</a> the first, wooden, gift was cut on the day of celebration and then presented to the wife as a finished article before the next two <a href="/wiki/Quarter_days" title="Quarter days">quarter days</a> had passed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The modern tradition may have originated in medieval Germany where, if a married couple lived to celebrate the 25th anniversary of their wedding, the wife was presented by her friends and neighbours with a silver wreath to congratulate them for the good fortune that had prolonged the lives of the couple for so many years. On celebration of the 50th, the wife received a wreath of gold. Over time the number of symbols expanded and the German tradition came to assign gifts that had direct connections with each stage of married life. The symbols have changed over time. For example, in the United Kingdom, diamond was a well known symbol for the 75th anniversary, but this changed to the now more common 60th anniversary after <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>'s 60 years on the throne was widely marked as her <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Jubilee" class="mw-redirect" title="Diamond Jubilee">Diamond Jubilee</a>.
</p><p>The origins of the current gift conventions date to 1937.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Before that, only the 1st, 5th, 10th, 15th, 20th, 25th, 50th, and 75th anniversaries had an associated gift. In 1937, the American National Retail Jeweler Association (now known as <a href="/wiki/Jewelers_of_America" title="Jewelers of America">Jewelers of America</a> as a result of an organizational merger) introduced an expanded list of gifts. The revamped list gave a gift for each year up to the 25th, and then for every fifth anniversary after that.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
