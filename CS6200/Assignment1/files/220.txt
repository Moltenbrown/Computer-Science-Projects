<p><b>Cameron</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Scottish_surname" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish surname">Scottish surname</a> and thus somewhat common throughout the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English-speaking world</a>.
</p><p>There are several possible origins. One is from a <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Gaelic</a>-language <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nickname</a>, derived from <i>cam</i> ("crooked", "bent") and <i>sròn</i> ("nose"). Another is from any of the various places called Cameron, especially such places located in <a href="/wiki/Fife" title="Fife">Fife</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Cameron_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Cameron-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Another possible origin of the Scottish placename (from which the surname is thought to be derived in some cases) is from <a href="/wiki/Cambernon" title="Cambernon">Cambernon</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-H1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The English-language surname can be rendered into <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a> as: <i>Camarran</i><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (masculine), <i>Chamarran</i> (feminine); or as <i>Camshron</i><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> (masculine) and <i>Chamshron</i> (feminine).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
