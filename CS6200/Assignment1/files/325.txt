<p><i><b>Wood</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> in the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a>. It is common throughout the world, especially countries with historical links to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wood_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For the most part, the surname Wood originated as a topographic name used to describe a person who lived in, or worked in a wood or <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forest</a>. This name is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a> <i>wode</i>, meaning "wood" (from the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>wudu</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Wood_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Wood-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> An early occurrence of this surname (of a personal residing near a wood) is <i>de la Wode</i>, recorded in <a href="/wiki/Hertfordshire" title="Hertfordshire">Hertfordshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, in 1242.<sup id="cite_ref-Reaney-34743475_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reaney-34743475-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The locational name also appeared in early records <a href="/wiki/List_of_Latinised_names" title="List of Latinised names">Latinised</a> as <i>de Bosco</i><sup id="cite_ref-Black-822_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Black-822-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (from the <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>bois</i>, meaning "wood").<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Boyce_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Boyce-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Another derivation for the surname is from a nickname of an eccentric or violent person, derived from the Old English <i>wōd</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-Reaney-34743475_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reaney-34743475-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i>wad</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Wood_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Wood-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and Middle English <i>wod</i>, <i>wode</i>, all meaning "frenzied" or "wild".<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Wood_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Wood-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Reaney-34743475_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reaney-34743475-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This derivation is considered to be much less common than the locational origin.<sup id="cite_ref-Reaney-34743475_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reaney-34743475-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> An early occurrence of the surname derived in this fashion (from a nickname) is <i>le Wode</i>, recorded in <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire" title="Worcestershire">Worcestershire</a>, England, in 1221.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Wood_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Wood-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Reaney-34743475_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reaney-34743475-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variations">Variations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wood_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Variations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Variations of the name include the surnames <i>Woodd</i>, <i>Woode</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Woods_(surname)" title="Woods (surname)">Woods</a></i>, <i>Wod</i>, and <i>Wode</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Reaney-34743475_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reaney-34743475-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The equivalent name in German is <i>Wald</i>, and in many cases, emigrants from the German-speaking countries Anglicized this name to Wood when they settled in countries like the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, et cetera. The name "Wald" still exists, too.
</p><p>Also, the name Wood has been modified in many cases by the addition of suffixs to create: Woodard, Woodburn, Woodby, Woodcock, Woodforde, Woodham, Woodly, Woodman, Woodruff, Woodstock, Woodvine, and Woodward.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popularity">Popularity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wood_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Popularity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, and on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>, Wood is the 26th most-common surname,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> it is the 53rd most-common surname<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> the 78th.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
