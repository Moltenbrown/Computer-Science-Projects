<p><b>Bayeux</b> (<small>French pronunciation:&#160;&#8203;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[bajø]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France">commune</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Calvados_(department)" title="Calvados (department)">Calvados</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a> in <a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a> in northwestern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.
</p><p>Bayeux is the home of the <a href="/wiki/Bayeux_Tapestry" title="Bayeux Tapestry">Bayeux Tapestry</a>, which depicts the events leading up to the <a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_England" title="Norman conquest of England">Norman conquest of England</a>. It is also known as the first major town secured by the Allies during <a href="/wiki/Operation_Overlord" title="Operation Overlord">Operation Overlord</a>. <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Gaulle" title="Charles de Gaulle">Charles de Gaulle</a> made <a href="/wiki/The_Bayeux_speeches" title="The Bayeux speeches">two famous speeches</a> in this town.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayeux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bayeux is a <a href="/wiki/Sub-prefecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Sub-prefecture">sub-prefecture</a> of Calvados. It is the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Arrondissement" title="Arrondissement">arrondissement</a> of Bayeux and of the <a href="/wiki/Cantons_of_France" title="Cantons of France">canton</a> of Bayeux.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayeux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bayeux is located 7 kilometres (4 miles) from the coast of the English Channel and 30&#160;km (19&#160;mi) north-west of <a href="/wiki/Caen" title="Caen">Caen</a>. The city, with elevations varying from 32 to 67 metres (105 to 220 feet) <a href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" class="mw-redirect" title="Above mean sea level">above sea level</a> – with an average of 46 metres (151 feet) – is bisected by the <a href="/wiki/Aure_(river)" title="Aure (river)">River Aure</a>. Bayeux is located at the crossroads of <a href="/wiki/Route_nationale_13" title="Route nationale 13">RN 13</a> and the train route Paris-Caen-<a href="/wiki/Cherbourg" class="mw-redirect" title="Cherbourg">Cherbourg</a>. The city is the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Bessin" title="Bessin">Bessin</a>, which extends north-west of <a href="/wiki/Calvados_(department)" title="Calvados (department)">Calvados</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayeux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city was known as <b><a href="/wiki/Augustodurum" class="mw-redirect" title="Augustodurum">Augustodurum</a></b> in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>. It means the <i>durum</i> (Celtic word <i>duro-</i> 'door', 'gate', Welsh <i><i lang="cy" title="Welsh language text">dor</i></i>, Breton <i><i lang="br" title="Breton language text">dor</i></i> 'door', 'gate') dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Augustus</a>, Roman Emperor. The Celtic word <i>duron</i>, Latinised as <i>durum</i>, was probably used to translate the Latin word <i><a href="/wiki/Forum_(Roman)" title="Forum (Roman)">forum</a></i> (Compare <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9jus" title="Fréjus">Fréjus</a> <i>Forum Julii</i>, dedicated to Julius (Caesar)).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the Late Empire it took the name of the Celtic tribe who lived here: the <i>Bodiocassi</i>, Latinized in <i>Bajocassi</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bajocasses" class="mw-redirect" title="Bajocasses">Bajocasses</a></i>, and this word explains the place-names Bayeux and <a href="/wiki/Bessin" title="Bessin">Bessin</a>. <i>Bodiocassi</i> has been compared with Old Irish <i><i lang="sga" title="Old Irish language text">Buidechass</i></i> 'with blond locks'.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayeux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayeux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Founded as a Gallo-Roman settlement in the 1st century BC under the name Augustodurum, Bayeux is the capital of the former territory of the <a href="/wiki/Baiocasses" title="Baiocasses">Baiocasses</a> people of Gaul, whose name appears in <a href="/wiki/Pliny%27s_Natural_History" class="mw-redirect" title="Pliny&#39;s Natural History">Pliny's Natural History</a> (iv.107). Evidence of earlier human occupation of the territory comes from fortified Celtic camps, but there is no evidence of any major pre-existing Celtic town before the organization of Gaul in Roman <i><a href="/wiki/Civitas" title="Civitas">civitates</a></i>. Any settlement was more likely confined to scattered Druid huts along the banks of the Aure and Drome rivers or on Mount Phaunus where they worshiped. Cemeteries have been found on the nearby Mount Phaunus indicating the area as a Druid centre. Titus Sabinus, a lieutenant of <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a>, subjected the Bessin region to Roman domination. The 5th-century <i>Notitia provinciarum et civitatum Galliae</i> mentions <a href="/wiki/Suevi" class="mw-redirect" title="Suevi">Suevi</a> that had been officially settled here (<i><a href="/wiki/Laeti" title="Laeti">laeti</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town is mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy" title="Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a>, writing in the reign of Antoninus Pius, under the name <i>Noemagus Biducassium</i> (for <i>*Noviomagus Badiocassium</i> 'New market of the Badiocassi') and remained so until the time of the Roman Empire. The main street was already the heart of the city. Two baths, under the Church of St. Lawrence and the post office in rue Laitière, and a sculpted head of the goddess Minerva have been found, attesting to the adoption of Roman culture. In 1990 a closer examination of huge blocks discovered in the cathedral in the 19th century indicated the presence of an old Roman building. Bayeux was built on a crossroads between <a href="/wiki/Lisieux" title="Lisieux">Lisieux</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valognes" title="Valognes">Valognes</a>, developing first on the west bank of the river. By the end of the 3rd century a walled enclosure surrounded the city and remained until it was removed in the 18th century. Its layout is still visible and can be followed today. The citadel of the city was located in the southwest corner and the cathedral the southeast. An important city in Normandy, Bayeux was part of the coastal defence of the Roman Empire against the pirates of the region, and a Roman legion was stationed there.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_Ages">Middle Ages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayeux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Middle Ages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BayeuxTapestryScene22.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/BayeuxTapestryScene22.jpg/220px-BayeuxTapestryScene22.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="131" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/BayeuxTapestryScene22.jpg/330px-BayeuxTapestryScene22.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/BayeuxTapestryScene22.jpg/440px-BayeuxTapestryScene22.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="357" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:BayeuxTapestryScene22.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bayeux (Bagias), depicted in <a href="/wiki/Bayeux_Tapestry_tituli#22" title="Bayeux Tapestry tituli">scene 22</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Bayeux_Tapestry" title="Bayeux Tapestry">Bayeux Tapestry</a></div></div></div>
<p>The city was largely destroyed during the <a href="/wiki/Viking_expansion" title="Viking expansion">Viking raids</a> of the late 9th century but was rebuilt in the early 10th century under the reign of Bothon. In the middle of the 10th century Bayeux was controlled by <a href="/wiki/Hagrold" title="Hagrold">Hagrold</a>, a pagan Viking who defended the city against the Franks. The 12th-century poet <a href="/wiki/Beno%C3%AEt_de_Saint-Maure" class="mw-redirect" title="Benoît de Saint-Maure">Benoît de Saint-Maure</a>, in his verse history of the dukes of Normandy, remarked on the "Danish" commonly spoken at Bayeux.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 11th century saw the creation of five villages beyond the walls to the north east evidence of its growth during <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Normandy" title="Duchy of Normandy">Ducal Normandy</a>. <a href="/wiki/William_the_Conqueror" title="William the Conqueror">William the Conqueror</a>'s half brother <a href="/wiki/Odo,_Earl_of_Kent" class="mw-redirect" title="Odo, Earl of Kent">Odo, Earl of Kent</a> completed the cathedral in the city and it was dedicated in 1077. However the city began to lose prominence when William placed his capital at <a href="/wiki/Caen" title="Caen">Caen</a>. When King <a href="/wiki/Henry_I_of_England" title="Henry I of England">Henry I of England</a> defeated his brother <a href="/wiki/Robert_Curthose" title="Robert Curthose">Robert Curthose</a> for the rule of Normandy, the city was burned to set an example to the rest of the duchy. Under <a href="/wiki/Richard_the_Lionheart" class="mw-redirect" title="Richard the Lionheart">Richard the Lionheart</a>, Bayeux was wealthy enough to purchase a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_charter" title="Municipal charter">municipal charter</a>. From the end of Richard's reign to the end of the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Years%27_War" title="Hundred Years&#39; War">Hundred Years' War</a>, Bayeux was repeatedly pillaged until <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_of_England" title="Henry V of England">Henry V of England</a> captured the city in 1417. After the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Formigny" title="Battle of Formigny">Battle of Formigny</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_VII_of_France" title="Charles VII of France">Charles VII of France</a> recaptured the city and granted a general amnesty to its populace in 1450. The capture of Bayeux heralded a return to prosperity as new families replaced those decimated by war and these built some 60 mansions scattered throughout the city, with stone supplanting wood.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-medieval">Post-medieval</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayeux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-medieval">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:272px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bayeux-centre-ville.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Bayeux-centre-ville.jpg/270px-Bayeux-centre-ville.jpg" decoding="async" width="270" height="179" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Bayeux-centre-ville.jpg/405px-Bayeux-centre-ville.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Bayeux-centre-ville.jpg/540px-Bayeux-centre-ville.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="1272" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bayeux-centre-ville.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bayeux city centre (2011)</div></div></div>
<p>The area around Bayeux is called the Bessin, which was the <a href="/wiki/Bailiwick" title="Bailiwick">bailiwick</a> of the province Normandy until the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, Bayeux was the first city of the <a href="/wiki/Operation_Overlord" title="Operation Overlord">Battle of Normandy</a> to be liberated, and on 16 June 1944 General <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Gaulle" title="Charles de Gaulle">Charles de Gaulle</a> made <a href="/wiki/The_Bayeux_speeches" title="The Bayeux speeches">the first of two major speeches in Bayeux</a> in which he made clear that France sided with the Allies. The buildings in Bayeux were virtually untouched during the Battle of Normandy, the German forces being fully involved in defending <a href="/wiki/Caen" title="Caen">Caen</a> from the Allies.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Bayeux_War_Cemetery" class="mw-redirect" title="Bayeux War Cemetery">Bayeux War Cemetery</a> with its memorial includes the largest British cemetery dating from the Second World War in France. There are 4,648 graves, including 3,935 British and 466 Germans. Most of those buried there were killed in the invasion of Normandy.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_British_Legion" title="The Royal British Legion">Royal British Legion National</a>, every 5 June at 1530 hrs, attends the 3rd Division Cean Memorial Service and beating retreat ceremony. On the 6th of June, it holds a remembrance service in <a href="/wiki/Bayeux_Cathedral" title="Bayeux Cathedral">Bayeux Cathedral</a> starting at 1015 hrs, and later at 1200 hrs, the Royal British Legion National holds a service of remembrance at the Bayeux Cemetery. All services are open to the public, all Standards <a href="/wiki/The_Royal_British_Legion" title="The Royal British Legion">RBL</a>, NVA, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">RN</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">ARMY</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">RAF</a> service and Regimental Associations are welcome to attend and parade. Details can be found at <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120326180301/http://www.rblsomme.org/">www.rblsomme.org</a>
</p><p>The French town of Bayeux is also the home of a memorial to all of the journalists who have lost their lives while reporting. The memorial was designed by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Craquelin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Samuel Craquelin (page does not exist)">Samuel Craquelin</a>, who is a French architect. The memorial lists the names of 1,889 journalists killed between 1944 and 2007. The memorial was established in Bayeux because of its historic liberation on 7 June 1944.<sup id="cite_ref-bayeux_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayeux-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_features">Natural features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayeux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Natural features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The river <a href="/wiki/Aure_(river)" title="Aure (river)">Aure</a> flows through Bayeux, offering panoramic views from a number of locations. The Aure has a relatively high level of <a href="/wiki/Turbidity" title="Turbidity">turbidity</a> and the speed of its brownish water is moderate because of the slight slope of the watercourse, although where it is narrow in places like the centre of Bayeux, higher surface speeds are generated. In the centre of Bayeux near the Bayeux Tapestry Museum, <a href="/wiki/PH" title="PH">pH</a> levels were measured at 8.35 and the electrical conductivity of water was tested at 37 microsiemens per centimetre. Turbidity was measured at 13 centimetres by the <a href="/wiki/Secchi_disk" title="Secchi disk">Secchi disk</a> method. At this point of reference, flows are generally of the order of 50 cubic feet per second (1.4&#160;m<sup>3</sup>/s).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Bajocian" title="Bajocian">Bajocian</a> Age in the <a href="/wiki/Jurassic" title="Jurassic">Jurassic</a> Period of geological time takes its name from the Latinised name of the inhabitants of Bayeux (the Bajocassi)
</p>

<p>The inhabitants of Bayeux are called <i>Bayeusains</i> <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[bajøzɛ̃]</a></span> or <i>Bajocasses</i> <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[baʒokas]</a></span>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights">Sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayeux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bayeux_Cathedral.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Bayeux_Cathedral.JPG/220px-Bayeux_Cathedral.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="275" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Bayeux_Cathedral.JPG/330px-Bayeux_Cathedral.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Bayeux_Cathedral.JPG/440px-Bayeux_Cathedral.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="2398" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bayeux_Cathedral.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bayeux Cathedral</div></div></div>
<p>Bayeux is a major tourist attraction, best known to British and French visitors for the <a href="/wiki/Bayeux_tapestry" class="mw-redirect" title="Bayeux tapestry">Bayeux tapestry</a>, made to commemorate events in the <a href="/wiki/Norman_Conquest" class="mw-redirect" title="Norman Conquest">Norman Conquest</a> of England in 1066. According to the legend, the tapestry was made by <a href="/wiki/Matilda_of_Flanders" title="Matilda of Flanders">Reine Mathilde</a>, wife of William the Conqueror. In fact, it may have been designed and woven in England.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  It is displayed in a museum in the town centre. The large <a href="/wiki/Norman_architecture" title="Norman architecture">Norman</a>-<a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cath%C3%A9drale_Notre-Dame_de_Bayeux" class="mw-redirect" title="Cathédrale Notre-Dame de Bayeux">Cathédrale Notre-Dame de Bayeux</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> consecrated in 1077, was arguably the original home of the tapestry where William's half-brother <a href="/wiki/Odo_of_Bayeux" title="Odo of Bayeux">Odo of Bayeux</a> (represented on the tapestry with a wooden club at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hastings" title="Battle of Hastings">Battle of Hastings</a>), would have had it displayed.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Jardin_botanique_de_Bayeux" title="Jardin botanique de Bayeux">Jardin botanique de Bayeux</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Botanical_garden" title="Botanical garden">botanical garden</a> dating from 1864.
</p>
