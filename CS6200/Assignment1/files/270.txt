	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Hunt (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Hunt</b> is an occupational surname related with <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a>, originating in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, the surname Hunt is also very common, it means <a href="/wiki/Gray_wolf" class="mw-redirect" title="Gray wolf">wolf</a> in <a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian language</a>. 
</p><p>The Irish family surname is derived from Ó Fiachna and the significance is <i>fiach</i> means "the chase"; the clan itself was part of the <a href="/wiki/Connachta" title="Connachta">Connachta</a>'s <a href="/wiki/S%C3%ADol_Muireadaigh" title="Síol Muireadaigh">Síol Muireadaigh</a> (kindred to the <a href="/wiki/%C3%93_Conchubhair" class="mw-redirect" title="Ó Conchubhair">Ó Conchubhair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mac_Diarmada" title="Mac Diarmada">Mac Diarmada</a>), located in <a href="/wiki/County_Roscommon" title="County Roscommon">County Roscommon</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Sligo" title="County Sligo">County Sligo</a>.
</p>
