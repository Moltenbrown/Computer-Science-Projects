	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Henderson (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Henderson</caption><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Meaning</th><td>1. "Son of Hendry"<br />2. "Son of Henry"</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Region of origin</th><td><a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Henderson</b> is a common <a href="/wiki/Scottish_surnames" title="Scottish surnames">Scottish</a> <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>. The name is derived from patronymic form of the name <i><a href="/wiki/Hendry" title="Hendry">Hendry</a></i>, which is a Scottish form of <i><a href="/wiki/Henry_(surname)" title="Henry (surname)">Henry</a></i>. Some Hendersons also derive their name from <i>Henryson</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The surname <i>Henderson</i> is borne by numerous unrelated <a href="/wiki/Scottish_clans" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish clans">families</a> in Scotland. For example, the Hendersons of Fordell, in Fife, were the chief <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Lowlands" title="Scottish Lowlands">Lowland</a> family of the name. This family descended from a family of Henrysons, from Dumfriesshire. A branch of <a href="/wiki/Clan_Gunn" title="Clan Gunn">Clan Gunn</a> also bears the name. According to tradition, this family descends from Henry Gunn, a younger son of a <a href="/wiki/Scottish_clan_chief" title="Scottish clan chief">chief</a> of the Gunns who lived in the 15th century. The Hendersons of Glencoe derive their surname from the <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaelic language">Gaelic</a> <i>MacEanruig</i>.
</p><p>The surname was unknown in England prior to the 17th century and is first mentioned in a marriage document between one of the Borders Hendersons and the daughter of a <a href="/wiki/Carlisle,_Cumbria" class="mw-redirect" title="Carlisle, Cumbria">Carlisle</a> merchant at <a href="/wiki/Hexham" title="Hexham">Hexham</a>. The surname is rendered in <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a> as: <i>MacEanraig</i> or <i>MacEanruig</i> (masculine), and <i>NicEanraig</i> or <i>NicEanruig</i> (feminine).
</p>
