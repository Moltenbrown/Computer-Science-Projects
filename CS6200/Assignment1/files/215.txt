<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crawford_(name)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The surname <i>Crawford</i> originates from a locative name,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> possibly derived from a <a href="/wiki/Place_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Place name">place name</a> composed of the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> elements <i>crāwe</i> ("<a href="/wiki/Crow" title="Crow">crow</a>") and <i>ford</i> ("<a href="/wiki/Ford_(crossing)" title="Ford (crossing)">ford</a>").<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Examples of such place names include: <a href="/wiki/Crawford,_South_Lanarkshire" title="Crawford, South Lanarkshire">Crawford, South Lanarkshire</a>, Scotland,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Crawford,_Dorset" class="mw-redirect" title="Crawford, Dorset">Crawford, Dorset</a>, England,<sup id="cite_ref-combine1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-combine1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Crawford,_Lancashire" class="mw-redirect" title="Crawford, Lancashire">Crawford, Lancashire</a>, England.<sup id="cite_ref-combine1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-combine1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The surname is most probably derived from the Scottish place name.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;note 1&#93;</a></sup> In some cases, the surname may be a variant of <i>Crowfoot</i>, a surname derived from a <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nickname</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The surname <i>Crawford</i> corresponds to the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a> <i>MacCreamhain</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a> <i>de Cráfort</i>, <i>Mac Crábhagáin</i>, and <i>Mac Raith</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Early examples of forms of the surname include: John de Crauford, in 1147–1160 (Scotland),<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Galfridus de Crauford, in 1188–1202 (Scotland), and Nicolaus de Crauford, in 1205 (England).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> <i>Crawford</i>, generally a masculine name, is derived from the surname.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crawford_(name)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a surname, Crawford is the 289th most common name in Great Britain, with 30,292 bearers. It is most common in <a href="/wiki/South_Lanarkshire" title="South Lanarkshire">South Lanarkshire</a> where it is the 5th most common surname with 3,384 bearers and in <a href="/wiki/Highland_(council_area)" title="Highland (council area)">Highland</a> where it is the 8th most common surname with 3,394 bearers. Other concentrations include <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a> (25th, 3,328), <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a> (58th, 3,378), <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> (327th, 1,852), <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(county)" title="West Midlands (county)">West Midlands</a> (341st, 1,796) and <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a> (374th, 1,722), and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The countries with the highest percentages of Crawford families are <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
