<p><b>Fuxi</b> (伏羲), also known as <b>Paoxi</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Culture_hero" title="Culture hero">culture hero</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_legend" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese legend">Chinese legend</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_mythology" title="Chinese mythology">mythology</a>, credited along with his sister <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCwa" title="Nüwa">Nüwa</a> with <a href="/wiki/List_of_protoplasts" class="mw-redirect" title="List of protoplasts">creating humanity</a> and the invention of hunting, fishing, domestication, and cooking as well as the <a href="/wiki/Cangjie#Legend_of_character_creation" title="Cangjie">Cangjie</a> system of writing <a href="/wiki/Chinese_characters" title="Chinese characters">Chinese characters</a> around 2,000<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>BCE. Fuxi was counted as the first of the <a href="/wiki/Three_August_Ones_and_Five_Emperors" class="mw-redirect" title="Three August Ones and Five Emperors">Three Sovereigns</a> at the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Chinese_dynasties" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Chinese dynasties">Chinese dynastic period</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fuxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pangu" title="Pangu">Pangu</a> was said to be the creation god in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_mythology" title="Chinese mythology">Chinese mythology</a>. He was a giant sleeping within an egg of chaos. As he awoke, he stood up and divided the sky and the earth. Pangu then died after standing up, and his body turned into rivers, mountains, plants, animals, and everything else in the world, among which is a powerful being known as Hua Hsu.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2019)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Hua Hsu gave birth to a twin brother and sister, Fuxi and Nüwa. Fuxi and Nüwa are said to be creatures that have faces of human and bodies of snakes.
</p><p>Fuxi was known as the "original human", and he was said to have been born in the lower-middle reaches of the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a> in a place called Chengji (possibly modern <a href="/wiki/Lantian_County" title="Lantian County">Lantian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi" title="Shaanxi">Shaanxi</a> province, or <a href="/wiki/Tianshui" title="Tianshui">Tianshui</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gansu" title="Gansu">Gansu</a> province).<sup id="cite_ref-flp1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flp1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In reality, many Chinese people believe<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> that Hua Hsu was a leader during the matriarchal society (<abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;">&#8201;2,600</span><span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>BC) as early Chinese developed language skill while Fuxi and Nüwa were leaders in the early patriarchal society (<abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;">&#8201;2,600</span><span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>BCE) while Chinese began the marriage rituals.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation_legend">Creation legend</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fuxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Creation legend">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Classic_of_Mountains_and_Seas" title="Classic of Mountains and Seas">Classic of Mountains and Seas</a></i>, Fuxi and Nüwa were the original humans who lived on the mythological <a href="/wiki/Kunlun_Mountain_(mythology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kunlun Mountain (mythology)">Kunlun Mountain</a> (today's Huashan). One day they set up two separated piles of fire, and the fire eventually became one. Under the fire, they decided to become husband and wife. Fuxi and Nüwa used clay to create offspring, and with the divine power they made the clay figures come alive.<sup id="cite_ref-flp1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flp1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> These clay figures were the earliest human beings. Fuxi and Nüwa were usually recognized by Chinese as two of the Three Emperors in the early patriarchal society in China (<abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;">&#8201;2,600</span><span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>BCE), based on the myth about Fuxi establishing marriage ritual in his tribe. The creation of human beings was a symbolic story of having a larger family structure that included the figure of a father.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_importance">Social importance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fuxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Social importance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On one of the columns of the Fuxi Temple in Gansu Province, the following couplet describes Fuxi's importance: "Among the three primogenitors of <a href="/wiki/Huaxia" title="Huaxia">Huaxia</a> civilization, Fu Xi in Huaiyang Country ranks first."<sup id="cite_ref-flp1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flp1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> During the time of his predecessor <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCwa" title="Nüwa">Nüwa</a>, society was matriarchal.
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>In the beginning there was as yet no moral or social order.  Men knew their mothers only, not their fathers.  When hungry, they searched for food; when satisfied, they threw away the remnants.  They devoured their food hide and hair, drank the blood, and clad themselves in skins and rushes.  Then came Fu Xi and looked upward and contemplated the images in the heavens, and looked downward and contemplated the occurrences on earth.  He united man and wife, regulated the five stages of change, and laid down the laws of humanity.  He devised the eight <a href="/wiki/Bagua_(concept)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bagua (concept)">trigrams</a>, in order to gain mastery over the world.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite>Ban Gu, Baihu tongyi<sup id="cite_ref-iching_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iching-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>Fuxi taught his subjects to cook, to fish with nets, and to hunt with weapons made of bone, wood, or bamboo. He instituted marriage and offered the first open-air sacrifices to heaven. A stone tablet, dated AD 160, shows Fuxi with Nüwa.
</p><p>Traditionally, Fuxi is considered the originator of the <i><a href="/wiki/I_Ching" title="I Ching">I Ching</a></i>, which work is attributed to his reading of the <i>He Map</i> (or the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River_Map" title="Yellow River Map">Yellow River Map</a>). According to this tradition, Fuxi had the arrangement of the <a href="/wiki/Bagua_(concept)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bagua (concept)">trigrams</a> of the <i>I Ching</i> revealed to him in the markings on the back of a mythical <a href="/wiki/Longma" title="Longma">dragon horse</a> (sometimes said to be a <a href="/wiki/Tortoise" title="Tortoise">tortoise</a>) that emerged from the <a href="/wiki/Luo_River_(Henan)" title="Luo River (Henan)">Luo River</a>. This arrangement precedes the compilation of the <i>I Ching</i> during the <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Zhou Dynasty">Zhou dynasty</a>. This discovery is said to have been the origin of calligraphy. Fuxi is also credited with the invention of the <a href="/wiki/Guqin" title="Guqin">Guqin</a> musical instrument, though credit for this is also given to <a href="/wiki/Shennong" title="Shennong">Shennong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Emperor" title="Yellow Emperor">Yellow Emperor</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Figurism" title="Figurism">Figurists</a> viewed Fuxi as <a href="/wiki/Enoch_(ancestor_of_Noah)" title="Enoch (ancestor of Noah)">Enoch</a>, the Biblical patriarch. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fuxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fuxi is said to have lived for 197 years altogether and died at a place called <a href="/wiki/Chen_(state)" title="Chen (state)">Chen</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Huaiyang_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Huaiyang County">Huaiyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>), where a monument to him can still be found and visited as a tourist attraction.<sup id="cite_ref-flp1_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flp1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
