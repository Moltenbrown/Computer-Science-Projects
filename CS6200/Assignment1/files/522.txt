<p>The <b>Museum of Motherhood</b>, also known as M.O.M, was conceived in 2003 and first opened to the public on Main St. in Dobbs Ferry, NY with a small exhibition gallery. The focus of the museum is to explore and understand American mothers, fathers, and families. M.O.M. was founded under the non-profit Motherhood Foundation Inc. 501c3, and has participated in events throughout New York state, including a village-wide display called "The Moms of Rock" in <a href="/wiki/Seneca_Falls,_New_York" title="Seneca Falls, New York">Seneca Falls</a>, home of the <a href="/wiki/Suffragette" title="Suffragette">Suffragette Movement</a> (2010), as well as family activities each year at the <a href="/wiki/Museum_Mile_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Museum Mile Festival">Museum Mile Festival</a> in New York City.
</p><p>In 2011, local Gymboree franchise owners Deb Whitefield and Barry Hanson donated a <a href="/wiki/Museum#Pop-up_museums" title="Museum">popup museum</a> space in the <a href="/wiki/Yorkville,_Manhattan" title="Yorkville, Manhattan">Yorkville</a> neighborhood of Manhattan's <a href="/wiki/Upper_East_Side" title="Upper East Side">Upper East Side</a>. During that time the museum leveraged students from surrounding universities: <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>, Marymount Manhattan University, and Columbia, Teachers' College to source internships and conduct tours. It is believed to be the only museum to focus on motherhood and the role of the mother.<sup id="cite_ref-mother_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mother-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> M.O.M. also features the work of international artists and student-made art. An article in the July 2013 issue of the Wall Street Journal<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> addressed the museum's goals of ascribing value to the role of caregiving.
</p><p>The museum's founder, Martha <a href="/wiki/Joy_Rose" title="Joy Rose">Joy Rose</a>, lead singer of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Housewives_on_Prozac&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Housewives on Prozac (page does not exist)">Housewives on Prozac</a> also founded the concept of a "Mom Rock Movement" and coined the phrase "Moms Rock" after founding the Mamapalooza Festival. The festival highlights the value of mothers and their talents,<sup id="cite_ref-roryh_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-roryh-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and is organized in conjunction with New York Parks Dept. Rose is believed to be the first person to receive a Master's in Mother Studies,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> graduating CUNY, <a href="/wiki/Graduate_Center,_CUNY" title="Graduate Center, CUNY">The Graduate Center</a> in 2015.
</p><p>Originally scheduled to be open in Upper East Side location through December 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-roryh_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-roryh-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  the museum remained open on the UES through the summer of 2014. The museum took its exhibits online in 2015 and M.O.M. participated in several international arts presentations including The Art of Motherhood as part of the 2nd annual Jerusalem Biennale for Contemporary Jewish Art at the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://eng.hechalshlomo.org.il/">Hechal Sclomo Museum</a> in Israel. Each May the Museum organizes and produces academic conferences on the subject of motherhood. The 2016 conference is the Museum's 11th annual gathering, taking place for the second year at <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_College" title="Manhattan College">Manhattan College</a>.
</p><p>On June 1, 2016 the Museum of Motherhood relocated to an exhibition and teaching space in the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.historickenwood.org/">Historic Kenwood</a> Artists Enclave at 538 27th St. N. St. Petersburg, Florida 33713. Hours are on Thursday, Friday, and Saturday by appointment only.<sup id="cite_ref-wilk_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wilk-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
