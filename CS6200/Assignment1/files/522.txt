	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Parent-in-law</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"Mother-in-law" redirects here. For the song, see <a href="/wiki/Mother-in-Law_(song)" title="Mother-in-Law (song)">Mother-in-Law (song)</a>. For the sandwich, see <a href="/wiki/Mother-in-law_(sandwich)" title="Mother-in-law (sandwich)">Mother-in-law (sandwich)</a>.</div>
<p>A <b>parent-in-law</b> is a person who has a legal <a href="/wiki/Affinity_(law)" title="Affinity (law)">affinity</a> with another by being the <a href="/wiki/Parent" title="Parent">parent</a> of the other's <a href="/wiki/Spouse" title="Spouse">spouse</a>. Many cultures and legal systems impose duties and responsibilities on persons connected by this relationship. A person is a son-in-law or daughter-in-law to the parents of the spouse, who are in turn also the parents of those <a href="/wiki/Sisters-in-law" class="mw-redirect" title="Sisters-in-law">sisters-in-law</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brothers-in-law" class="mw-redirect" title="Brothers-in-law">brothers-in-law</a> (if any) who are siblings of the spouse (as opposed to spouses of siblings). Together the members of this family affinity group are called the <a href="/wiki/Affinal_kin" class="mw-redirect" title="Affinal kin">in-laws</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fathers-in-law">Fathers-in-law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parent-in-law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fathers-in-law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <b>father-in-law</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Father" title="Father">father</a> of a person's spouse.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Two men who are fathers-in-law to each other's children may be called <b>co-fathers-in-law</b>, or, if there are grandchildren, <b>co-grandfathers</b>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mothers-in-law">Mothers-in-law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parent-in-law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mothers-in-law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <b>mother-in-law</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Mother" title="Mother">mother</a> of a person's spouse.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Two women who are mothers-in-law to each other's children may be called <b>co-mothers-in-law</b>, or, if there are grandchildren, <b>co-grandmothers</b>. 
</p><p>In comedies, the mother-in-law is sometimes shown as the bane of the husband, who is married to the mother-in-law's daughter. Mothers-in-law are often <a href="/wiki/Stereotype" title="Stereotype">stereotyped</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mother-in-law_joke" title="Mother-in-law joke">mother-in-law jokes</a>.
</p><p>Some <a href="/wiki/Australian_Aboriginal_languages" title="Australian Aboriginal languages">Australian Aboriginal languages</a> use <a href="/wiki/Avoidance_speech" title="Avoidance speech">avoidance speech</a>, so-called "mother-in-law languages", special sub-languages used when in hearing distance of <a href="/wiki/Taboo" title="Taboo">taboo</a> relatives, most commonly the mother-in-law.
</p><p>A <i><a href="/wiki/Secondary_suite#Mother-in-law_house" title="Secondary suite">mother-in-law suite</a></i> is also a type of dwelling, usually guest accommodations within a family home that may be used for members of the extended family.
</p>
