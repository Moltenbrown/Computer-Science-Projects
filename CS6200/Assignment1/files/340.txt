<p><b>Sudono Salim</b> (July 16, 1916 – June 11, 2012), also known as <b>Liem Sioe Liong</b>, was a Chinese-born Indonesian businessman of <a href="/wiki/Fuqing" title="Fuqing">Fuqing</a> origin. He was the richest individual in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Suryadinata_139_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suryadinata_139-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was the founder and chairman of the conglomerate <a href="/wiki/Salim_Group" title="Salim Group">Salim Group</a> before turning over its management to his youngest son <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthoni_Salim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Anthoni Salim (page does not exist)">Anthoni Salim</a> (now the fifth wealthiest person in Indonesia<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>) in 1992.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sudono_Salim&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1916, Salim was born as Lim Sioe Liong (Lin Shaoliang), in <a href="/wiki/Fuqing" title="Fuqing">Fuqing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a>, China, the second son of a father. According to the Chinese zodiac, he was born in the Year of the Dragon, on the seventh day of the seventh month.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1936, he left Fujian to join his brother Lim Ke Lok and brother-in-law Zheng Xusheng in <a href="/wiki/Medan" title="Medan">Medan</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Sumatra" title="North Sumatra">North Sumatra</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Suryadinata_139_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suryadinata_139-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Salim diversified their <a href="/wiki/Peanut_oil" title="Peanut oil">peanut oil</a> trading business into the <a href="/wiki/Clove" title="Clove">clove</a> market, which was growing rapidly from demand for production.<sup id="cite_ref-SCMP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCMP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> While in Medan, he supplied soldiers of the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_National_Revolution" title="Indonesian National Revolution">Indonesian National Revolution</a> with medical supplies and came into contact with <a href="/wiki/Suharto" title="Suharto">Suharto</a>, an officer of the army. Salim denied allegations that he also provided arms to Indonesian soldiers to resist Dutch forces.<sup id="cite_ref-Suryadinata_140_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suryadinata_140-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As soldiers seized Dutch businesses following independence, his business absorbed many of their assets and gained a monopoly in the clove market,<sup id="cite_ref-SCMP_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCMP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> but he denied working with Suharto in expanding his ventures.<sup id="cite_ref-Suryadinata_141_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suryadinata_141-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_career_and_family">Business career and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sudono_Salim&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business career and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Salim_Group" title="Salim Group">Salim Group</a></div>
<p>In 1952, after moving to <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>, Salim expanded his trading business by establishing connections with other ethnic Chinese businessmen in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>. His soap factory became one of the primary suppliers to the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_National_Armed_Forces" title="Indonesian National Armed Forces">Indonesian National Armed Forces</a>. He later expanded into textiles and banking, eventually establishing the largest private bank in Indonesia—the <a href="/wiki/Bank_Central_Asia" title="Bank Central Asia">Bank Central Asia</a> (BCA). The bank was nationalized following the <a href="/wiki/1997_Asian_financial_crisis" title="1997 Asian financial crisis">Asian financial crisis</a>.
</p><p>In 1968, after a merger, he gained the right to a <a href="/wiki/Monopoly" title="Monopoly">monopoly</a> on clove importation. Bogasari, a joint venture with another Hokchia businessman became the largest producer of <a href="/wiki/Flour" title="Flour">flour</a> in Indonesia. These two companies were said to have provided him with the capital to establish the <a href="/wiki/Cement" title="Cement">cement</a> giant <a href="/wiki/Indocement_Tunggal_Prakarsa" class="mw-redirect" title="Indocement Tunggal Prakarsa">Indocement</a> in 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-Suryadinata_140_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suryadinata_140-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1990, he established the food manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Indofood" title="Indofood">Indofood</a>, the country's largest maker of instant noodles.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1992, Salim handed over management of the conglomerate <a href="/wiki/Salim_Group" title="Salim Group">Salim Group</a> to his son Anthony Salim.
</p><p>By 1997, the Salim Group possessed <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>20 billion in assets and included more than 500 companies employing over 200,000 Indonesians. When the <a href="/wiki/1997_Asian_Financial_Crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="1997 Asian Financial Crisis">Asian Financial Crisis</a> hit, the conglomerate incurred US$4.8 billion in debts<sup id="cite_ref-BusinessWeek_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusinessWeek-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and had to give up control of Bank Central Asia in 1998 to the government.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> BCA was 30% owned by two offspring of Suharto.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/May_1998_Indonesian_riots" class="mw-redirect" title="May 1998 Indonesian riots">May 1998 riots</a>, Salim fled to Singapore after a mob burned his home in <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>; his son remained to fight off the mobs and formed the Salim Group.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He eventually settled in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-BusinessWeek_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusinessWeek-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> magazine listed him as the 25th wealthiest businessperson in Southeast Asia in 2004 with a <a href="/wiki/Net_worth" title="Net worth">net worth</a> of <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>655 million.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Salim has three sons and one daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-Suryadinata_140_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suryadinata_140-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sudono_Salim&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On June 10, 2012, a month before his 96th birthday, Salim died from natural causes in <a href="/wiki/Raffles_Hospital" title="Raffles Hospital">Raffles Hospital</a>, Singapore. He is buried at the <a href="/wiki/Lim_Chu_Kang" title="Lim Chu Kang">Lim Chu Kang</a> Cemetery.
</p>
