	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Stringer (name)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stringer_(surname)&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Stringer (surname)">Stringer (surname)</a>)</span></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Stringer</caption><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Word/name</th><td><a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Old_English_(language)" class="mw-redirect" title="Old English (language)">Old English</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Meaning</th><td>Maker of strings, bowstrings</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Region of origin</th><td><a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Stringer</b> (pronounced to rhyme with "ringer") is an <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> occupational surname and occasionally used as a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>. It originally denoted a maker of rope or strings, and especially those for the famous English longbows used for both hunting and war. It is based on an agent derivative of the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>streng</i>, meaning "string," which is in turn based on the <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a> <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/streng#Old_English" class="extiw" title="wikt:streng">strengr</a></i>. In <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, where it is still particularly common, George (?) Redmonds<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (December 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> argues that the surname may have been connected with <a href="/wiki/Blacksmith" title="Blacksmith">ironworking</a>, a stringer having operated some form of specialist hearth.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Early examples of the surname recorded in authentic registers and charters of the medieval period include:
</p>
<dl><dd><i>Roger le Strenger</i> in 1293, Yorkshire;</dd>
<dd><i>Lady Godwyna Strenger</i> in 1328, <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>;</dd>
<dd><i>Richard Stringer</i>, in 1679, a footsoldier of <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ISD_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ISD-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
<p>The first recorded spelling of the family name is believed to be that of Walter Stringere, which was dated 1194, in the <a href="/wiki/Curia_Regis_Rolls" class="mw-redirect" title="Curia Regis Rolls">Curia Regis Rolls</a> for <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire" title="Wiltshire">Wiltshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ISD_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ISD-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
