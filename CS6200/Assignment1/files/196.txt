<p><b>Harrison</b> is a common <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> origin.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It may also be spelled <b>Harrisson</b>, <b>Harryson</b> or <b>Harrysson</b>. Harrison means "son of Harry". Early records suggest that the surnames Harrison and <a href="/wiki/Harris_(surname)" title="Harris (surname)">Harris</a> were used interchangeably by some families. Harrison is the 42nd most common surname in England and 123rd most common in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-fb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The first known recording of the surname had been dated from 1355 in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is also a masculine <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> derived from the surname, of fairly recent origin.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
