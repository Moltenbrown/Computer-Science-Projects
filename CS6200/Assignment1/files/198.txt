	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Madison (name)</h1>

<p><b>Madison</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> of English origin that has become a popular <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> in the United States. Madison, commonly spelled <a href="/wiki/Maddison" title="Maddison">Maddison</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_East_England" title="North East England">Northeastern England</a>, is a variant of <i><a href="/wiki/Mathieson" title="Mathieson">Mathieson</a></i>, meaning <i>son of <a href="/wiki/Matthew_(name)" class="mw-redirect" title="Matthew (name)">Matthew</a></i>, although possibly occasionally standing for <i>son of <a href="/wiki/Maddy" title="Maddy">Maddy</a></i>, where <i>Maddy</i> is a pet form of <i><a href="/wiki/Maud_(name)" class="mw-redirect" title="Maud (name)">Maud</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Madison is also used as a given name. As a name, it has become popular for girls in recent decades. Its rise is generally attributed to the 1984 release of the film <i><a href="/wiki/Splash_(film)" title="Splash (film)">Splash</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> From a practically non-existent given name before 1985, Madison rose to being the second-most-popular name given to baby girls in the US in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-ssa-babynames_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ssa-babynames-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It has since declined in popularity as a name for girls, however, slipping to seventh place by 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-ssa-babynames_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ssa-babynames-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a masculine given name, Madison can be found within the top 1,000 names for boys in the United States up until about 1952. Madison returned to the top 1,000 ranked names for boys in 1987, remaining there through 1999, and it also was the 858th-most-common name for boys in 2004, but it remains uncommon as a masculine given name.<sup id="cite_ref-ssa-babynames_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ssa-babynames-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
