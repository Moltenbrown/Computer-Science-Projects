<p><b>Camp</b> is an <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> taken from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> roots.  The name is found in <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">Great Britain</a> and in other places throughout the world settled by the English.  According to the 2000 census there are fewer than 1300 Camps in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-sofem_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sofem-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The 2000 <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> census puts the number at over 27,000, making it the 1087th most common name in America, after <a href="/wiki/McDermott" class="mw-redirect" title="McDermott">McDermott</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  The <a href="/wiki/Australian" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian">Australian</a> government currently reports 465 persons named Camp.<sup id="cite_ref-Oz_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oz-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The governments of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> do not currently provide lists of surnames as the UK and others do.  Totals outside the <a href="/wiki/English-speaking_world" title="English-speaking world">English-speaking world</a> are also unknown.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camp_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>People with the surname Camp have no single origin or ancestor, the name instead having been chosen by different families over a few centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-about_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  The name is sometimes a variant spelling of "<a href="/wiki/Kemp_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Kemp (disambiguation)">Kemp</a>", which comes from the <a href="/wiki/West_Germanic_languages" title="West Germanic languages">Old West Germanic</a> <i>"kampo-z"</i>, meaning "a contest, or fight", which in turn became the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>"kemp"</i>, for a "fighter" or "soldier."<sup id="cite_ref-campmaster_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-campmaster-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-kemp_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kemp-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  However, the name usually denotes a family whose house was on an open field, or "camp", rather than in the woods or elsewhere.<sup id="cite_ref-coat_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coat-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  The word was borrowed from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_French" title="Middle French">Middle French</a> <i>"camp"</i>, its first use in English, in 1528, being for a "place where an army lodges temporarily", only later transferring to a non-military use sometime after 1560.  The French <i>"camp"</i> (later <i>"champ"</i>) is itself derived from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>"campus"</i>, which also meant an "open field", but also and especially an "open space for military exercise".  Coincidentally, the word's <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/martial" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:martial">martial</a> sense had been borrowed by the Germanic tribes during their <a href="/wiki/Migration_Period" title="Migration Period">conflicts with the Romans</a> to become the aforementioned <i>"kampo-z"</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-campmaster_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-campmaster-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><br /> 
Throughout the English-speaking world, but especially the United States, the name is sometimes derived from other, often longer, European names (e.g. the <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italian</a> "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Campesi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Campesi (page does not exist)">Campesi</a>" or the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_(ethnic_group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch (ethnic group)">Dutch</a> "<a href="/wiki/Van_de_Kamp" title="Van de Kamp">Van de Kamp</a>") which were changed during the process of <a href="/wiki/Cultural_assimilation" title="Cultural assimilation">cultural assimilation</a>.  Camp can also be a shortening or <a href="/wiki/Anglicizing" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicizing">anglicizing</a> of similar French names, such as "<a href="/wiki/De_Camp_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect" title="De Camp (disambiguation)">de Camp</a>" and "du Camp".
</p>
