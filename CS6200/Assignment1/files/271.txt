	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Sawyer (occupation)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sawyers_by_Hokusai.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Sawyers_by_Hokusai.jpg/220px-Sawyers_by_Hokusai.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Sawyers_by_Hokusai.jpg/330px-Sawyers_by_Hokusai.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Sawyers_by_Hokusai.jpg/440px-Sawyers_by_Hokusai.jpg 2x" data-file-width="720" data-file-height="472" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sawyers_by_Hokusai.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sawyers in Japan, circa 1800. <a href="/wiki/Nishiki-e" title="Nishiki-e">Nishiki-e</a> print after <a href="/wiki/Katsushika_Hokusai" class="mw-redirect" title="Katsushika Hokusai">Katsushika Hokusai</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><b>Sawyer</b> is an occupational term referring to someone who saws wood, particularly using a <a href="/wiki/Pit_saw" class="mw-redirect" title="Pit saw">pit saw</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> either in a <a href="/wiki/Saw_pit" title="Saw pit">saw pit</a> or with the log on trestles above ground or operates a <a href="/wiki/Sawmill" title="Sawmill">sawmill</a>. One such job is the occupation of someone who cuts lumber to length for the consumer market, a task now often done by end users or at lumber and home improvement stores.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  The term is still widely used in the <a href="/wiki/Logging" title="Logging">logging</a> industry to refer to the operator of a <a href="/wiki/Chainsaw" title="Chainsaw">chainsaw</a> (or still in some limited applications, a <a href="/wiki/Crosscut_saw" title="Crosscut saw">crosscut saw</a>) for harvesting, <a href="/wiki/Wildfire_suppression" title="Wildfire suppression">wildfire suppression</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trail" title="Trail">trail</a> construction and related work. In the construction industry, the term is applied to the operator of a <a href="/wiki/Concrete_saw" title="Concrete saw">concrete saw</a>.
</p>
