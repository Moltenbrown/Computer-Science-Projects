	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Lee (English surname)</h1>

<p><b>Lee</b> is a common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>-speaking countries.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> during the year <a href="/wiki/2000_US_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 US Census">2000 census</a>, "Lee" was the <a href="/wiki/Most_common_American_surnames" class="mw-redirect" title="Most common American surnames">22nd-most-common</a> surname; although this conflates a number of unrelated surnames with the same spelling (see below).<sup id="cite_ref-US_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-US-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_(English_surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are several distinct origins of the Lee surname. The most common is derived from <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/leah#Old_English" class="extiw" title="wikt:leah">lēah</a></i>, meaning a <a href="/wiki/Meadow" title="Meadow">meadow</a> or forest clearing.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This developed variously into the surnames Lee, <a href="/wiki/Lea_(surname)" title="Lea (surname)">Lea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Leigh_(name)" title="Leigh (name)">Leigh</a>. The Lees of <a href="/wiki/Shropshire" title="Shropshire">Shropshire</a> were notable as the forebears of the colonial American <a href="/wiki/Lee_family" title="Lee family">Lee family</a> which produced <a href="/wiki/Richard_Henry_Lee" title="Richard Henry Lee">Richard Henry Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Edward_Lee" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Edward Lee">Robert Edward Lee</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zachary_Taylor" title="Zachary Taylor">Zachary Taylor</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Unrelated_surnames_with_the_same_spelling">Unrelated surnames with the same spelling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_(English_surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Unrelated surnames with the same spelling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_(English_surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The name in Ireland has several diverse origins, resulting in widely dispersed clusters of the name in South Western, Western (Galway) and North Eastern Counties.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
One recognized root was the <a href="/wiki/Anglicization" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicization">anglicization</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a> surname "Ó Laoidaigh" which resulted in a number of variants, such as Lee, Lea, and Leigh.<sup id="cite_ref-IGF_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IGF-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  Other Lees have English roots and still others may have derived from the Norman "Du Lea".  
The 1901 Irish census<sup id="cite_ref-INA_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-INA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> list 4912 entries primarily in the counties of Galway, Dublin, Cork, Antrim, Limerick and Down.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Asian">East Asian</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_(English_surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: East Asian">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Lee_(Korean_surname)" title="Lee (Korean surname)">Lee (Korean surname)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Li_(Chinese_surname)" class="mw-redirect" title="Li (Chinese surname)">Li (Chinese surname)</a></div>
<p>Lee is the anglicized spelling of a number of East Asian surnames used by <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Chinese" title="Overseas Chinese">Chinese</a> or <a href="/wiki/Korean_diaspora" title="Korean diaspora">Korean</a> diaspora translating numerous surnames also romanized as <a href="/wiki/Li_(surname)" title="Li (surname)">Li</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-US_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-US-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> particularly the one deriving from the Chinese word for "<a href="/wiki/Plum" title="Plum">plum</a>" (<span lang="zh" title="Chinese language text"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%9D%8E" class="extiw" title="wikt:李">李</a></span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a></small> <i><a href="/wiki/Li_(%E6%9D%8E)" class="mw-redirect" title="Li (李)">Lǐ</a></i>; <small><a href="/wiki/South_Korean_language" class="mw-redirect" title="South Korean language">S.Kor.</a></small> <span lang="ko" title="Korean language text"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%EC%9D%B4" class="extiw" title="wikt:이">이</a></span>, <i><a href="/wiki/Lee_(Korean_name)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lee (Korean name)">Lee</a></i>; <small><a href="/wiki/North_Korean_language" class="mw-redirect" title="North Korean language">N.Kor.</a></small> <span lang="ko" title="Korean language text"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%EB%A6%AC" class="extiw" title="wikt:리">리</a></span>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rhee_(surname)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhee (surname)">Rhee</a>/<a href="/wiki/Lee_(Korean_surname)" title="Lee (Korean surname)">Lee</a></i>; <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese</a> <span lang="zh" title="Chinese language text"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%9D%8E" class="extiw" title="wikt:李">李</a></span>, <a href="/wiki/Qu%E1%BB%91c_ng%E1%BB%AF" class="mw-redirect" title="Quốc ngữ">quốc ngữ</a> <a href="/wiki/L%C3%AA" class="mw-redirect" title="Lê">Lê</a>, <a href="/wiki/Li_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Li (disambiguation)">Lý</a> or <a href="/wiki/Li_(surname)" title="Li (surname)">Lí</a>) which is among the most common surnames in the world.
In the United States, Lee is the third common surname for Chinese and Korean Americans and the second-most-common surname for <a href="/wiki/Asian_and_Pacific_Islanders" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian and Pacific Islanders">Asian and Pacific Islanders</a>, behind the <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_surname" class="mw-redirect" title="Vietnamese surname">Vietnamese</a> <a href="/wiki/Nguyen_(surname)" class="mw-redirect" title="Nguyen (surname)">Nguyen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-US_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-US-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Similarly, Lee is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_common_Chinese_surnames#Singapore" title="List of common Chinese surnames">third-most-common surname</a> among <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Singaporeans" title="Chinese Singaporeans">Chinese Singaporeans</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Norway">Norway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_(English_surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Norway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Originating from Norway as "Lie", this surname was altered when it arrived in the Americas in the late 1800s to fit the English language's pronunciation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
