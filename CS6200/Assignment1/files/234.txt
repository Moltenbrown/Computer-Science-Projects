<p>A <b>miller</b> is a person who operates a <a href="/wiki/Gristmill" title="Gristmill">mill</a>, a machine to grind a grain (for example corn or wheat) to make <a href="/wiki/Flour" title="Flour">flour</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mill_(grinding)" title="Mill (grinding)">Milling</a> is among the oldest of human occupations. "Miller", "Milne", and other variants are common surnames,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> as are their equivalents in other languages around the world ("<a href="/wiki/Melnyk_(surname)" class="mw-redirect" title="Melnyk (surname)">Melnyk</a>" in <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Meunier_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect" title="Meunier (disambiguation)">Meunier</a>" in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, "<a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCller_(surname)" title="Müller (surname)">Müller</a>" or "<a href="/wiki/German_surname" class="mw-redirect" title="German surname">Mueller</a>" in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Mulder" title="Mulder">Mulder</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Molenaar" title="Molenaar">Molenaar</a>" in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Moln%C3%A1r" title="Molnár">Molnár</a>" in <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Molinero" title="Molinero">Molinero</a>" in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Molinaro" title="Molinaro">Molinaro</a>" or "<a href="/wiki/Molinari" title="Molinari">Molinari</a>" in <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> etc.). Milling existed in <a href="/wiki/Hunter-gatherer" title="Hunter-gatherer">hunter-gatherer</a> communities, and later millers were important to the <a href="/wiki/History_of_agriculture" title="History of agriculture">development of agriculture</a>.
</p><p>The materials ground by millers are often <a href="/wiki/Foodstuff" class="mw-redirect" title="Foodstuff">foodstuffs</a> and particularly <a href="/wiki/Cereal" title="Cereal">grain</a>.  The physical grinding of the food allows for the easier digestion of its <a href="/wiki/Nutrient" title="Nutrient">nutrients</a> and saves wear on the teeth. Non-food substances needed in a fine, powdered form, such as <a href="/wiki/Building_materials" class="mw-redirect" title="Building materials">building materials</a>, may be processed by a miller.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Quern-stone">Quern-stone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Quern-stone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bedandrind.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Bedandrind.jpg/220px-Bedandrind.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Bedandrind.jpg/330px-Bedandrind.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Bedandrind.jpg/440px-Bedandrind.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2288" data-file-height="1712" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bedandrind.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A bedstone and rind. <a href="/wiki/Dalgarven_Mill" class="mw-redirect" title="Dalgarven Mill">Dalgarven Mill</a>, Scotland.</div></div></div>
<p>The most basic tool for a miller was the <a href="/wiki/Quern-stone" title="Quern-stone">quern-stone</a>—simply a large, fixed stone as a base and another movable stone operated by hand, similar to a <a href="/wiki/Mortar_and_pestle" title="Mortar and pestle">mortar and pestle</a>.  As technology and <a href="/wiki/Millstones" class="mw-redirect" title="Millstones">millstones</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Bedstone" title="Bedstone">bedstone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Millrind" title="Millrind">rynd</a>) improved, more elaborate machines such as <a href="/wiki/Watermill" title="Watermill">watermills</a> and <a href="/wiki/Windmill" title="Windmill">windmills</a> were developed to do the grinding work. These mills harnessed available energy sources including animal, water, wind, and electrical power.  Mills are some of <a href="/wiki/Mill_(factory)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mill (factory)">the oldest factories</a> in human history, so factories making other items are sometimes known as mills, for example, <a href="/wiki/Cotton_mill" title="Cotton mill">cotton mills</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steel_mill" title="Steel mill">steel mills</a>.  These factory workers are also called millers.
</p><p>The rynd in <a href="/wiki/History_of_Scotland" title="History of Scotland">pre-reformation Scotland</a> was often carved on millers' gravestones as a symbol of their trade.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a traditional <a href="/wiki/Rural" class="mw-redirect" title="Rural">rural</a> society, a miller is often wealthier than ordinary peasants, which can lead to jealousy. Millers are often accused of associating with thieves, and were targeted in <a href="/wiki/Bread_riots" class="mw-redirect" title="Bread riots">bread riots</a> at times of <a href="/wiki/Famine" title="Famine">famine</a>. Conversely, millers might be in a stronger position vis-a-vis <a href="/wiki/Feudal" class="mw-redirect" title="Feudal">feudal</a> land owners than are ordinary peasants.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Carnival">Carnival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Carnival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Monselice_z21.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Monselice_z21.JPG/220px-Monselice_z21.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Monselice_z21.JPG/330px-Monselice_z21.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Monselice_z21.JPG/440px-Monselice_z21.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Monselice_z21.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A man dressed as a <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">medieval</a> miller at a festival in <a href="/wiki/Monselice" title="Monselice">Monselice</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The traditional carnival held annually in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ivrea#Culture" title="Ivrea">Ivrea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, commemorates a spirited "Mugnaia" (miller's daughter) who supposedly  refused to let a local duke exercise his <a href="/wiki/Right_of_the_first_night" class="mw-redirect" title="Right of the first night">right of the first night</a>, and proceeded to chop the duke's head off and spark a revolution. Whatever the historical validity  of the story, it is significant it was the daughter of a miller to whom folk tradition assigned this rebellious role.
</p>
<h2><span id="Miller.27s_thumb"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Miller's_thumb">Miller's thumb</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Miller&#039;s thumb">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As an important part of his job, the miller repeatedly takes into his hand samples of the ground meal coming out of the spout in order to feel the quality and character of the product. The miller rubs the grain between his thumb and forefinger. After years of doing this the miller's thumb changes shape and becomes broad and flattened. This is known as a "miller's thumb".
</p><p>Sayings such as "worth a millerˈs thumb" and "an honest miller hath a golden thumb" refers to the profit the miller makes as a result of this skill.
</p><p>The shape of a miller's thumb is said to have the appearance of the head of a fish. The <a href="/wiki/European_bullhead" title="European bullhead">European bullhead</a> (<i>Cottus gobio</i>), a freshwater fish, is commonly called a miller's thumb for this reason.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surname">Surname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Surname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Miller_(name)" class="mw-redirect" title="Miller (name)">Miller</a> (also known as Millar) is a common surname derived from the old English surname <i>Milleiir</i>.
