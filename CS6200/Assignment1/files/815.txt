	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Onomastics</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Study of proper names of all kinds and the origins of names</div>
<p><b>Onomastics</b> or <b>onomatology</b> is the study of the <a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">etymology</a>, history, and use of <a href="/wiki/Proper_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Proper name">proper names</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Onomastics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Onomastics</i> originates from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Greek</a> ὀνομαστικός (<i>onomastikós</i>), which translates to "of or belonging to naming",<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> from ὄνομα (<i>ónoma</i>) meaning "name".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Toponymy" title="Toponymy">Toponymy or toponomastics</a>, the study of place names, is one of the principal branches of onomastics. <a href="/wiki/Anthroponomastics" class="mw-redirect" title="Anthroponomastics">Anthroponomastics</a> is the study of personal names. Onomastics can be helpful in <a href="/wiki/Data_mining" title="Data mining">data mining</a>, with applications such as <a href="/wiki/Named-entity_recognition" title="Named-entity recognition">named-entity recognition</a>, or recognition of the origin of names.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It has also been used in historical research to identify ethnic minorities within wider populations.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Literary_onomastics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Literary onomastics (page does not exist)">Literary onomastics</a> is the branch that researches the names in works of literature and other fiction.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> An <i>orthonym</i> is the proper name of the object in question, the object of onomastic study.
</p>
