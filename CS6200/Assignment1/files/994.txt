<p>A <b>portrait</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Portrait_painting" title="Portrait painting">painting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portrait_photography" title="Portrait photography">photograph</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculpture</a>, or other artistic representation of a person, in which the face and its expression is predominant. The intent is to display the likeness, <a href="/wiki/Personality_type" title="Personality type">personality</a>, and even the mood of the person. For this reason, in photography a portrait is generally not a <a href="/wiki/Snapshot_(photography)" title="Snapshot (photography)">snapshot</a>, but a composed image of a person in a still position. A portrait often shows a person looking directly at the painter or photographer, in order to most successfully engage the subject with the viewer.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portrait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prehistorical_portraiture">Prehistorical portraiture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portrait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Prehistorical portraiture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Plastered_Skull,_c._9000_BC.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Plastered_Skull%2C_c._9000_BC.jpg/220px-Plastered_Skull%2C_c._9000_BC.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Plastered_Skull%2C_c._9000_BC.jpg/330px-Plastered_Skull%2C_c._9000_BC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Plastered_Skull%2C_c._9000_BC.jpg/440px-Plastered_Skull%2C_c._9000_BC.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6000" data-file-height="4000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Plastered_Skull,_c._9000_BC.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Plastered_skull" class="mw-redirect" title="Plastered skull">Plastered skull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tell_es-Sultan" title="Tell es-Sultan">Tell es-Sultan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jericho" title="Jericho">Jericho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pre-Pottery_Neolithic_B" title="Pre-Pottery Neolithic B">Pre-Pottery Neolithic B</a>, circa 9000 BC</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Prehistoric_art" title="Prehistoric art">Prehistoric art</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Plastered_human_skulls" title="Plastered human skulls">Plastered human skulls</a> were reconstructed human skulls that were made in the ancient <a href="/wiki/Levant" title="Levant">Levant</a> between 9000 and 6000 BC in the <a href="/wiki/Pre-Pottery_Neolithic_B" title="Pre-Pottery Neolithic B">Pre-Pottery Neolithic B</a> period. They represent some of the oldest forms of art in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and demonstrate that the prehistoric population took great care in burying their <a href="/wiki/Ancestors" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancestors">ancestors</a> below their homes. The skulls denote some of the earliest sculptural examples of portraiture in the <a href="/wiki/History_of_art" title="History of art">history of art</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_portraiture">Historical portraiture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portrait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Historical portraiture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Portrait_painting" title="Portrait painting">Portrait painting</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fayum02.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Fayum02.jpg/170px-Fayum02.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="349" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Fayum02.jpg/255px-Fayum02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/de/Fayum02.jpg 2x" data-file-width="299" data-file-height="613" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fayum02.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Roman-Egyptian <a href="/wiki/Fayum_mummy_portraits" title="Fayum mummy portraits">funeral portrait</a> of a young boy</div></div></div>
<p>Most early representations that are clearly intended to show an individual are of rulers, and tend to follow idealizing artistic conventions, rather than the individual features of the subject's body, though when there is no other evidence as to the ruler's appearance the degree of idealization can be hard to assess.  Nonetheless, many subjects, such as <a href="/wiki/Akhenaten" title="Akhenaten">Akhenaten</a> and some other Egyptian <a href="/wiki/Pharaoh" title="Pharaoh">pharaohs</a>, can be recognised by their distinctive features. The 28 surviving rather small <a href="/wiki/Statues_of_Gudea" title="Statues of Gudea">statues of Gudea</a>, ruler of <a href="/wiki/Lagash" title="Lagash">Lagash</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sumeria" class="mw-redirect" title="Sumeria">Sumeria</a> between c. 2144&#8211;2124 BC, show a consistent appearance with some individuality.
</p><p>Some of the earliest surviving painted portraits of people who were not rulers are the Greco-Roman <a href="/wiki/Fayum_mummy_portraits" title="Fayum mummy portraits">funeral portraits</a> that survived in the dry climate of Egypt's <a href="/wiki/Fayum" class="mw-redirect" title="Fayum">Fayum</a> district. These are almost the only paintings from the classical world that have survived, apart from <a href="/wiki/Fresco" title="Fresco">frescos</a>, though many sculptures and portraits on coins have fared better.  Although the appearance of the figures differs considerably, they are considerably idealized, and all show relatively young people, making it uncertain whether they were painted from life.
</p><p>The art of the portrait flourished in Ancient Greek and especially <a href="/wiki/Roman_sculpture" title="Roman sculpture">Roman sculpture</a>, where sitters demanded individualized and realistic portraits, even unflattering ones. During the 4th century, the portrait began to retreat in favor of an idealized symbol of what that person looked like. (Compare the portraits of Roman Emperors <a href="/wiki/Constantine_I_(emperor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Constantine I (emperor)">Constantine I</a> and <a href="/wiki/Theodosius_I" title="Theodosius I">Theodosius I</a> at their entries.) In the <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Early_Middle_Ages" title="Early Middle Ages">Early Middle Ages</a> representations of individuals are mostly generalized.  True portraits of the outward appearance of individuals re-emerged in the late <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Tomb_monument" class="mw-redirect" title="Tomb monument">tomb monuments</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donor_portrait" title="Donor portrait">donor portraits</a>, miniatures in <a href="/wiki/Illuminated_manuscript" title="Illuminated manuscript">illuminated manuscripts</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Panel_painting" title="Panel painting">panel paintings</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:MochePortrait.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/MochePortrait.jpg/220px-MochePortrait.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="205" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/MochePortrait.jpg/330px-MochePortrait.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/MochePortrait.jpg/440px-MochePortrait.jpg 2x" data-file-width="441" data-file-height="411" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:MochePortrait.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Moche_culture" title="Moche culture">Moche</a> ceramic portrait. <a href="/wiki/Larco_Museum" title="Larco Museum">Larco Museum</a> Collection. Lima-Peru</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Moche_(culture)" class="mw-redirect" title="Moche (culture)">Moche</a> culture of Peru was one of the few ancient civilizations which produced portraits. These works accurately represent anatomical features in great detail. The individuals portrayed would have been recognizable without the need for other symbols or a written reference to their names. The individuals portrayed were members of the ruling elite, priests, warriors and even distinguished artisans.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  They were represented during several stages of their lives. The faces of gods were also depicted. To date, no portraits of women have been found. There is particular emphasis on the representation of the details of headdresses, hairstyles, body adornment and face painting.
</p><p>One of the best-known portraits in the Western world is <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci" title="Leonardo da Vinci">Leonardo da Vinci</a>'s painting titled <i><a href="/wiki/Mona_Lisa" title="Mona Lisa">Mona Lisa</a></i>, which is a painting of <a href="/wiki/Lisa_del_Giocondo" title="Lisa del Giocondo">Lisa del Giocondo</a>. What has been claimed as the world's oldest known portrait was found in 2006 in the <a href="/wiki/Vilhonneur" title="Vilhonneur">Vilhonneur</a> grotto near <a href="/wiki/Angoul%C3%AAme" title="Angoulême">Angoulême</a> and is thought to be 27,000 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Self-portraiture">Self-portraiture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portrait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Self-portraiture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Maria_Razumovskaya_by_Vig%C3%A9e-Lebrun.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Maria_Razumovskaya_by_Vig%C3%A9e-Lebrun.jpg/170px-Maria_Razumovskaya_by_Vig%C3%A9e-Lebrun.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="207" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Maria_Razumovskaya_by_Vig%C3%A9e-Lebrun.jpg/255px-Maria_Razumovskaya_by_Vig%C3%A9e-Lebrun.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Maria_Razumovskaya_by_Vig%C3%A9e-Lebrun.jpg/340px-Maria_Razumovskaya_by_Vig%C3%A9e-Lebrun.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1317" data-file-height="1602" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Maria_Razumovskaya_by_Vig%C3%A9e-Lebrun.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Late 18th Century portrait by <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_Vig%C3%A9e-Lebrun" class="mw-redirect" title="Elisabeth Vigée-Lebrun">Elisabeth Vigée-Lebrun</a></div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Self-portrait" title="Self-portrait">Self-portrait</a></div>
<p>When the artist creates a portrait of him- or herself, it is called a <i>self-portrait</i>. Identifiable examples become numerous in the late Middle Ages. But if the definition is extended, the first was by the <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptian</a> <a href="/wiki/Pharaoh" title="Pharaoh">Pharaoh</a> <a href="/wiki/Akhenaten" title="Akhenaten">Akhenaten</a>'s sculptor Bak, who carved a representation of himself and his wife Taheri c. 1365 BC. However, it seems likely that self-portraits go back to the <a href="/wiki/Cave_paintings" class="mw-redirect" title="Cave paintings">cave paintings</a>, the earliest representational art, and literature records several classical examples that are now lost.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_portrait">Official portrait</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portrait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Official portrait">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Reproduction-of-the-1805-Rembrandt-Peale-painting-of-Thomas-Jefferson-New-York-Historical-Society_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Reproduction-of-the-1805-Rembrandt-Peale-painting-of-Thomas-Jefferson-New-York-Historical-Society_1.jpg/170px-Reproduction-of-the-1805-Rembrandt-Peale-painting-of-Thomas-Jefferson-New-York-Historical-Society_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="204" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Reproduction-of-the-1805-Rembrandt-Peale-painting-of-Thomas-Jefferson-New-York-Historical-Society_1.jpg/255px-Reproduction-of-the-1805-Rembrandt-Peale-painting-of-Thomas-Jefferson-New-York-Historical-Society_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Reproduction-of-the-1805-Rembrandt-Peale-painting-of-Thomas-Jefferson-New-York-Historical-Society_1.jpg/340px-Reproduction-of-the-1805-Rembrandt-Peale-painting-of-Thomas-Jefferson-New-York-Historical-Society_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="846" data-file-height="1013" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Reproduction-of-the-1805-Rembrandt-Peale-painting-of-Thomas-Jefferson-New-York-Historical-Society_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rembrandt_Peale" title="Rembrandt Peale">Rembrandt Peale</a>, <i>Portrait of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson" title="Thomas Jefferson">Thomas Jefferson</a></i>, 1805. <a href="/wiki/New-York_Historical_Society" title="New-York Historical Society">New-York Historical Society</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The official portrait is a photographic production of record and dissemination of important personalities, notably kings, presidents and governors. It is usually decorated with official colors and symbols such as flag, presidential stripes and coat of arms of countries, states or municipalities. There is also connotation as an image of events, products and meetings<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Portrait_photography">Portrait photography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portrait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Portrait photography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Portrait_photography" title="Portrait photography">Portrait photography</a></div>
<p>Portrait photography is a popular commercial industry all over the world. Many people enjoy having professionally made <i>family portraits</i> to hang in their homes, or special portraits to commemorate certain events, such as graduations or weddings.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DSC_7424_(34067852616).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/DSC_7424_%2834067852616%29.jpg/200px-DSC_7424_%2834067852616%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="133" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/DSC_7424_%2834067852616%29.jpg/300px-DSC_7424_%2834067852616%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/DSC_7424_%2834067852616%29.jpg/400px-DSC_7424_%2834067852616%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6016" data-file-height="4000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DSC_7424_(34067852616).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A portrait of a family</div></div></div>
<p>Since the dawn of photography, people have made portraits. The popularity of the <a href="/wiki/Daguerreotype" title="Daguerreotype">daguerreotype</a> in the middle of the 19th century was due in large part to the demand for inexpensive portraiture. Studios sprang up in cities around the world, some cranking out more than 500 plates a day. The style of these early works reflected the technical challenges associated with 30-second exposure times and the <a href="/wiki/Painterly" class="mw-redirect" title="Painterly">painterly</a> aesthetic of the time. Subjects were generally seated against plain backgrounds and lit with the soft light of an overhead window and whatever else could be reflected with mirrors.
</p><p>As photographic techniques developed, an intrepid group of photographers took their talents out of the studio and onto battlefields, across oceans and into remote wilderness. <a href="/wiki/William_Shew" title="William Shew">William Shew</a>'s <i>Daguerreotype Saloon</i>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Fenton" title="Roger Fenton">Roger Fenton</a>'s <i>Photographic Van</i> and <a href="/wiki/Mathew_Brady" title="Mathew Brady">Mathew Brady</a>'s <i>What-is-it?</i> wagon set the standards for making portraits and other photographs in the field.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portrait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Politics" title="Politics">politics</a>, portraits of the leader are often used as a <a href="/wiki/Symbol" title="Symbol">symbol</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">state</a>. In most countries it is common protocol for a portrait of the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> to appear in important <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> buildings. Excessive use of a leader's portrait, such as that done of <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a>, can be indicative of a <a href="/wiki/Personality_cult" class="mw-redirect" title="Personality cult">personality cult</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Literature">Literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portrait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Portrait_(literature)" title="Portrait (literature)">Portrait (literature)</a></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Literature" title="Literature">literature</a> the term <i>portrait</i> refers to a written description or analysis of a person or thing. A written portrait often gives deep insight, and offers an analysis that goes far beyond the superficial. For example, American author <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Cornwell" title="Patricia Cornwell">Patricia Cornwell</a> wrote a best-selling book entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Portrait_of_a_Killer" class="mw-redirect" title="Portrait of a Killer">Portrait of a Killer</a></i> about the personality, background, and possible motivations of <a href="/wiki/Jack_the_Ripper" title="Jack the Ripper">Jack the Ripper</a>, as well as the media coverage of his murders, and the subsequent police investigation of his crimes.
</p>
