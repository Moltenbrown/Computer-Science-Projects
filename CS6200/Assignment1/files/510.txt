<p><b>Father–Daughter Day</b> (sometimes called <b>National Father–Daughter Day</b>) is a holiday recognized annually on the second Sunday of October in the United States, honoring the relationship between a father and a daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Unlike <a href="/wiki/Mother%27s_Day" title="Mother&#39;s Day">Mother's Day</a> and <a href="/wiki/Father%27s_Day" title="Father&#39;s Day">Father's Day</a>, it is not federally recognized.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Father%E2%80%93Daughter_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The U.S. holiday was originally conceived by <a href="/wiki/Smokey_Robinson" title="Smokey Robinson">Smokey Robinson</a> to honor his relationship with his six daughters. In human development the relationship between fathers and sons overshadows the bond with daughters. This holiday promotes the development of young women through their father.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Robinson stated: “There are many different kinds of families today, and we know that all parental relationships are important to the healthy development of children, but the father/daughter bond is unique and one that is near to my heart. The father/daughter relationship shapes a young woman’s perspective of men and what to expect from them. I believe that female empowerment begins in the home and fathers must set a healthy example through their personal actions and interactions.”
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebration">Celebration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Father%E2%80%93Daughter_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Celebration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Examples of commemoration during Father–Daughter Day can be both commercial or intangible. Nonmaterial examples of observances during this day include embracing one another with hugs, spending quality time together, and honoring their presence in your life.
</p><p>In September 2017, <a href="/wiki/Rockabye_Baby!" title="Rockabye Baby!">Rockabye Baby! Music</a> released a <a href="/wiki/Lullaby" title="Lullaby">lullaby</a> version of Smokey Robinson's "<a href="/wiki/My_Girl_(The_Temptations_song)" title="My Girl (The Temptations song)">My Girl</a>" to support the holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In October 2017, greeting card company <a href="/wiki/American_Greetings" title="American Greetings">American Greetings</a> announced their plan to release a line of eCards with Smokey Robinson to celebrate the launch of Father-Daughter Day.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Father_absence_in_the_United_States">Father absence in the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Father%E2%80%93Daughter_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Father absence in the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Father_absence" title="Father absence">Father absence</a></div>
<p>Fifteen million U.S. children live without a father.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> While father absence mainly results from parental divorce and separation,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> other factors such as family poverty, developmental difficulties have been associated with father absence, the effects of which have been explained by various theoretical approaches. Fathers are traditionally deemed a provider of protection and support for the child's development.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Early maturing girls have been found to be at risk for teenage pregnancy,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> drinking<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and weight problems,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:5_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and giving birth to low birth weight infants.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
