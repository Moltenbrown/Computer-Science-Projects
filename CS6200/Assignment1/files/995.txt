<p><b>Stewart</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> (also used as a <a href="/wiki/Masculine" class="mw-redirect" title="Masculine">masculine</a> <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>) possibly of pre-7th century <a href="/wiki/Old_English_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Old English language">Old English</a> origin, derived from <i>stigeweard</i>, the genitive prefix <i>stige</i> meaning "hall", and the suffix <i>weard</i> meaning "guardian" or "warden" (whence also the word <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/steward" class="extiw" title="wikt:steward">steward</a></i>). Alternative spellings are <a href="/wiki/Stuart_(name)" title="Stuart (name)">Stuart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steward_(surname)" title="Steward (surname)">Steward</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steuart_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Steuart (disambiguation)">Steuart</a>. The surname Stewart has large concentrations in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> (mainly in the <a href="/wiki/Deep_South" title="Deep South">Deep South</a>, and the other <a href="/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">southern</a> states), <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> (mainly in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_East_England" title="North East England">North East England</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_West_England" title="South West England">South West England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>), <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, and elsewhere that has large <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Scots_people" title="Ulster Scots people">Ulster Scots</a> diaspora.
</p><p>The progenitor of the Stewart family was <a href="/wiki/Alan_fitz_Flaad" title="Alan fitz Flaad">Alan fitz Flaad</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Breton_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Breton people">Breton</a> knight who settled in England after the <a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_England" title="Norman conquest of England">Norman Conquest</a>. His son, <a href="/wiki/Walter_fitz_Alan" title="Walter fitz Alan">Walter fitz Alan</a>, relocated to <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> during <a href="/wiki/The_Anarchy" title="The Anarchy">the Anarchy</a>, became the <a href="/wiki/High_Steward_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="High Steward of Scotland">High Steward of Scotland</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> hence the origin of the surname. Stewart is the 66th-most common surname in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="House_of_Stewart">House of Stewart</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stewart_(name)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: House of Stewart">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/House_of_Stuart" title="House of Stuart">House of Stuart</a></div>
<p>One of the hereditary Stewart stewards, <a href="/wiki/Walter_Stewart,_6th_High_Steward_of_Scotland" title="Walter Stewart, 6th High Steward of Scotland">Walter Stewart</a>, married <a href="/wiki/Marjorie_Bruce" title="Marjorie Bruce">Marjorie Bruce</a>, daughter of King <a href="/wiki/Robert_I_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert I of Scotland">Robert I</a>, and founded the royal <a href="/wiki/House_of_Stewart" class="mw-redirect" title="House of Stewart">House of Stewart</a>, beginning with their son King <a href="/wiki/Robert_II_of_Scotland" title="Robert II of Scotland">Robert II</a>. The House of Stewart was the longest serving royal dynasty of Scotland. <a href="/wiki/Mary,_Queen_of_Scots" title="Mary, Queen of Scots">Mary, Queen of Scots</a> formalised the use of the spelling Stuart, while resident in France, in order to preserve the correct pronunciation. In 1603, the Stewart King <a href="/wiki/James_VI_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="James VI of Scotland">James VI of Scotland</a> became King James I of England and Wales by his <a href="/wiki/Succession_to_Queen_Elizabeth_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Succession to Queen Elizabeth I">succession to Queen Elizabeth I</a>. The Stewart dynasty ruled Scotland, England and Wales (with an interruption during Cromwell's Commonwealth after the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a>) until 1714, when <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Queen_of_Great_Britain" title="Anne, Queen of Great Britain">Queen Anne</a> died and the British Crown passed to the German Electors of Hanover.
</p><p>The grandson of <a href="/wiki/James_II_of_England" title="James II of England">James II</a>, Prince <a href="/wiki/Charles_Edward_Stuart" title="Charles Edward Stuart">Charles Edward Stuart</a>, led the last attempt to restore the Stewart dynasty to the British Crown in 1745-6 and became known to history as "<a href="/wiki/Bonnie_Prince_Charlie" class="mw-redirect" title="Bonnie Prince Charlie">Bonnie Prince Charlie</a>". This attempted coup d'état ended in the slaughter of Charles' army at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Culloden" title="Battle of Culloden">Battle of Culloden</a> in April, 1746.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stewart_peers">Stewart peers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stewart_(name)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stewart peers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to the Royal House of Stuart, various branches of the Stewart family became Scottish peers, at various times holding the <a href="/wiki/Marquis_of_Bute" class="mw-redirect" title="Marquis of Bute">Marquisate of Bute</a>, the earldoms of <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Atholl" title="Earl of Atholl">Atholl</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Mar" title="Earl of Mar">Mar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Moray" title="Earl of Moray">Moray</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Angus" title="Earl of Angus">Angus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Galloway" title="Earl of Galloway">Galloway</a>, as well as several <a href="/wiki/List_of_Lordships_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Lordships of Parliament">Lordships of Parliament</a>. Several families of Stewarts became Highland clans in their own right, including the <a href="/wiki/Stewarts_of_Appin" class="mw-redirect" title="Stewarts of Appin">Stewarts of Appin</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stewarts_of_Ardvorlich&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stewarts of Ardvorlich (page does not exist)">Stewarts of Ardvorlich</a> the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stewarts_of_Atholl&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stewarts of Atholl (page does not exist)">Stewarts of Atholl</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stewarts_of_Garth&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stewarts of Garth (page does not exist)">Stewarts of Garth</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diaspora">Diaspora</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stewart_(name)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Diaspora">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many Stewart emigrants from the lowlands of Scotland settled in the Irish Province of Ulster in the seventeenth century.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  Stewarts also emigrated from other parts of Scotland and settled throughout the rest of Ireland, all the way over to the west coast of Ireland in cities as far-west as Galway.  The Stewarts left lasting families in Ireland, some modern-day Irish still claiming Stewart lineage.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
