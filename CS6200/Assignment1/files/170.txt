<p><b>Richardson</b> is an English surname of <a href="/wiki/Anglo_Saxon" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo Saxon">Anglo Saxon</a> origin.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
The prefix <a href="/wiki/Richard" title="Richard">Richard</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> derived from the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>ric</i> ("power") and <i>hard</i> ("brave"/"hardy"). The suffix <i>-son</i> denotes "son/descendant of". The name Richard and Richardson is found in records as early as 1381 in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is the 64th most common surname in England.  There are variant spellings including the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Swedish people">Swedish</a> Richardsson. People with the name Richardson or its variants include:
