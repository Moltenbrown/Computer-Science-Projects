<p>The <b>University of the West of England, Bristol</b> (<b>UWE Bristol</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public</a> <a href="/wiki/Research_universities" class="mw-redirect" title="Research universities">research university</a>, located in and around <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>, England, which received university status in 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In common with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bristol" title="University of Bristol">University of Bristol</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bath" title="University of Bath">University of Bath</a> it can trace its origins to the Merchant Venturers' Technical College, founded as a school in 1595 by the <a href="/wiki/Merchant_Venturers" class="mw-redirect" title="Merchant Venturers">Society of Merchant Venturers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>UWE Bristol is made up of several campuses in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Bristol" title="Greater Bristol">Greater Bristol</a>. Frenchay Campus is the largest campus in terms of student numbers as most of its courses are based there. City campus provides courses in the creative and cultural industries, and is made up of Bower Ashton Studios, <a href="/wiki/Arnolfini" title="Arnolfini">Arnolfini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spike_Island,_Bristol" title="Spike Island, Bristol">Spike Island</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Watershed_(Bristol)" title="Watershed (Bristol)">Watershed</a>. The institution is affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Old_Vic_Theatre_School" title="Bristol Old Vic Theatre School">Bristol Old Vic Theatre School</a> and validates its higher education courses. Frenchay Campus and Glenside Campus are home to most of the Faculty of Health and Applied Sciences, with a further Adult Nursing cohort based at <a href="/wiki/Gloucester_Docks" title="Gloucester Docks">Gloucester</a> Campus. <a href="/wiki/Hartpury_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Hartpury College">Hartpury</a> Campus provides training in animal sciences, sport, equine, agriculture and conservation. 
</p><p>UWE Bristol is ranked among the top 25 higher education institutions in England for its graduate employment prospects. Recent figures show 96% of recent graduates are in employment or further study, with 78% in professional roles. In 2018, the <a href="/wiki/Teaching_Excellence_Framework" title="Teaching Excellence Framework">Teaching Excellence Framework</a> (TEF) awarded the university with Gold rating, which is awarded for consistently outstanding and of the highest quality found in the UK Higher Education sector.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> UWE Bristol is only one of four universities in the UK to have a University Enterprise Zone providing space for over 70 businesses, and the largest UK robotics lab.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_foundations">Early foundations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early foundations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The University of the West of England can trace its roots back to the foundation of the <a href="/wiki/Merchant_Venturers" class="mw-redirect" title="Merchant Venturers">Merchant Venturers</a> Navigation School, which was founded in 1595.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1894, the school became the Merchant Venturers Technical College. The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bristol" title="University of Bristol">University of Bristol</a> was formed just a few years after this, leaving the college for the foundation of UWE Bristol.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The college was partly responsible for the creation of the Bristol College of Science and Technology (BCST) in 1960, which later gained a royal charter to form the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bath" title="University of Bath">University of Bath</a> in 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The technical college in turn became <i>Bristol Polytechnic</i> in 1970; the then-main campus was at Ashley Down, now a campus of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bristol_College" title="City of Bristol College">City of Bristol College</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UWEHistory_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UWEHistory-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bower Ashton Studios was formed in 1969 as the West of England College of Art which was formerly the art school of the Royal West of England Academy in Queens Road, Bristol. The <a href="/wiki/St_Matthias,_Bristol" title="St Matthias, Bristol">St Matthias</a> site (which is no longer owned by the university) was originally built in Victorian times and was a teacher training college. These campuses, together with campuses in Redland, Ashley Down, Unity Street and Frenchay became part of Bristol Polytechnic around 1976.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="University_status">University status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: University status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The institution gained university status and its present name as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Further_and_Higher_Education_Act,_1992" class="mw-redirect" title="Further and Higher Education Act, 1992">Further and Higher Education Act, 1992</a>. The Avon and Gloucestershire College of Health which is now <a href="/wiki/Glenside,_Bristol" title="Glenside, Bristol">Glenside Campus</a> and the Bath and Swindon College of Health Studies joined in January 1996. Hartpury campus joined in 1997. The university is a lead academic sponsor of <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Technology_and_Engineering_Academy" title="Bristol Technology and Engineering Academy">Bristol Technology and Engineering Academy</a>, a new <a href="/wiki/University_technical_college" title="University technical college">university technical college</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Frenchay_Campus">Frenchay Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Frenchay Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>UWE Bristol's largest and primary campus is named after the nearby village of <a href="/wiki/Frenchay" title="Frenchay">Frenchay</a> in the civil parish of <a href="/wiki/Winterbourne,_Gloucestershire" title="Winterbourne, Gloucestershire">Winterbourne</a>. It is located 4 miles north of Bristol city centre, with <a href="/wiki/Filton" title="Filton">Filton</a> to the West and <a href="/wiki/Stoke_Gifford" title="Stoke Gifford">Stoke Gifford</a> to the North.
</p><p>An £80&#160;million student village located at the Frenchay campus, which includes a sports centre and rooms for 2000 students, opened in 2006.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> 
</p><p>In August 2006, a new sports centre was opened at Frenchay.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In September 2008 UWE Bristol purchased the major part of neighbour <a href="/wiki/Hewlett_Packard" class="mw-redirect" title="Hewlett Packard">Hewlett Packard</a>'s adjoining land, resulting in a 70-acre (28-hectare) expansion to their current 80-acre (32&#160;ha) campus. 
</p><p>In 2012, major changes were introduced to the Frenchay campus at UWE Bristol. First, the <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Robotics_Laboratory" title="Bristol Robotics Laboratory">Bristol Robotics Laboratory</a>, the largest robotics laboratory in Europe, was opened and later on in the same year the UWE Bristol International College was opened to students.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The International College provides international students with the necessary academic, subject-based and English language skills needed to successfully progress on to a degree course at UWE Bristol.
</p><p>The Students' Union opened its new building in 2015; it is two interlinked buildings bringing all Students' Union services together.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In autumn 2016 Future Space, a <a href="/wiki/Business_incubator" title="Business incubator">business incubator</a> for hi-tech companies, was opened adjacent to the Bristol Robotics Laboratory on Frenchay Campus.<sup id="cite_ref-clensy_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clensy-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It is only one of four universities in the UK to have a University Enterprise Zone providing space for over 70 businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-farrell_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-farrell-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The new Bristol Business School building at Frenchay Campus was completed in 2017. It houses the Bristol Business School and <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Law_School" title="Bristol Law School">Bristol Law School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A new state-of-the-art engineering building with teaching and research facilities, located close to the new Bristol Business School in the heart of the Frenchay Campus, is due to open to students and staff in June 2020.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="City_Campus">City Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: City Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>City Campus is made up of <b>Bower Ashton Studios</b>, <b>Spike Island</b>, <b>Arnolfini</b> and <b>Watershed</b>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Bower_Ashton_Studios">Bower Ashton Studios</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Bower Ashton Studios">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Bower Ashton Studios is home to the creative and cultural subjects, which are part of the Faculty of Arts, Creative Industries and Education. Adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/Ashton_Court" title="Ashton Court">Ashton Court</a> estate, on the edge of the city of Bristol,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> the West of England College of Art was established in purpose-built premises in 1969, moving from its previous location as the art school of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_West_of_England_Academy" title="Royal West of England Academy">Royal West of England Academy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Clifton,_Bristol" title="Clifton, Bristol">Clifton</a>. In 1970 the college became part of Bristol Polytechnic, the precursor of the university.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Every year in June the campus houses a degree show attended by Bristol residents as well as friends and families of the graduating students.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Among its principals and deans were the war artist <a href="/wiki/Jack_Chalker_(artist)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jack Chalker (artist)">Jack Chalker</a>, the graphic designer <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_van_Der_Lem&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Paul van Der Lem (page does not exist)">Paul van Der Lem</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Gough&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Paul Gough (page does not exist)">Paul Gough</a> RWA, a fine artist who became the first pro-vice chancellor and executive dean of the faculty in its expanded form of over 2,600 students.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Glenside_Campus">Glenside Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Glenside Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Glenside,_Bristol" title="Glenside, Bristol">Glenside, Bristol</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Glenside_Hospital,_main_building,_front.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Glenside_Hospital%2C_main_building%2C_front.jpg/220px-Glenside_Hospital%2C_main_building%2C_front.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="161" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Glenside_Hospital%2C_main_building%2C_front.jpg/330px-Glenside_Hospital%2C_main_building%2C_front.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Glenside_Hospital%2C_main_building%2C_front.jpg/440px-Glenside_Hospital%2C_main_building%2C_front.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2936" data-file-height="2152" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Glenside_Hospital,_main_building,_front.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The main building of Glenside Hospital</div></div></div>
<p>Glenside Campus is the home of the Faculty of Health and Applied Sciences. It is located on Blackberry Hill in the suburb of <a href="/wiki/Fishponds" title="Fishponds">Fishponds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>The Faculty of Health and Applied Sciences (formerly the Faculty of Health and Social Care) was created in 1996 when the former Avon and Gloucestershire College of Health and Bath and Swindon College of Health Studies joined with the existing Faculty of Health and Community Studies at UWE Bristol.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The <a href="/wiki/Glenside_Museum" title="Glenside Museum">Glenside Museum</a> is situated within the campus.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Faculty of Health and Applied Sciences includes the following departments:
</p>
<ul><li>Department of Allied Health Professions</li>
<li>Department of Applied Sciences</li>
<li>Department of Health and Social Sciences</li>
<li>Department of Nursing and Midwifery</li></ul>
<p>It offers full- and part-time courses at all levels in the areas of <a href="/wiki/Midwifery" title="Midwifery">Midwifery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nursing" title="Nursing">Nursing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Occupational_Therapy" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupational Therapy">Occupational Therapy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Physiotherapy" class="mw-redirect" title="Physiotherapy">Physiotherapy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radiography" title="Radiography">Radiography</a>, Social Work and other health-related professions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gloucester_Campus">Gloucester Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Gloucester Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alexandra Warehouse is the Gloucester home of the Department of Nursing and Midwifery, Faculty of Health and Applied Sciences, at the University of the West of England (UWE), <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>. It is located on West Quay in the <a href="/wiki/Gloucester_Docks" title="Gloucester Docks">Gloucester Docks</a>. This campus delivers nurse training in the heart of <a href="/wiki/Gloucester" title="Gloucester">Gloucester</a> with UWE Bristol ~ Pre-registration Adult and Mental Health nursing; Return to Practice; Post-graduate and CPD courses. Alexandra Warehouse, a historic listed building, has been fully refurbished.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="St_Matthias_Campus">St Matthias Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: St Matthias Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/St_Matthias,_Bristol" title="St Matthias, Bristol">St Matthias, Bristol</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stmatthias.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Stmatthias.JPG/220px-Stmatthias.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Stmatthias.JPG/330px-Stmatthias.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Stmatthias.JPG/440px-Stmatthias.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2102" data-file-height="1389" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stmatthias.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The main building at St Matthias</div></div></div>
<p>St Matthias Campus was located in the suburb of Fishponds in Bristol. Built in the Victorian times by the Church of England, the campus has some <a href="/wiki/Gothic_Revival_architecture" title="Gothic Revival architecture">Victorian Gothic</a> buildings, set around a sunken lawn. St Matthias campus was home to various departments of the faculty of Creative Arts, Humanities and Education.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The University of the West of England closed the campus in September 2014 (with operations on the site ceasing on 4 July 2014) as a part of a relocation project. The various departments of the faculty of Creative Arts, Humanities and Education from St Matthias and Bower Ashton have moved to new facilities at Frenchay campus. In March 2014 it was announced that, subject to planning permission, the site would be sold and redeveloped by Barratt Developments for housing and the listed buildings would become a <a href="/wiki/Waldorf_education#United_Kingdom" title="Waldorf education">Steiner School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Steiner_Academy_Bristol_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Steiner_Academy_Bristol-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="School_of_Art_and_Design">School of Art and Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: School of Art and Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bower_Ashton_Campus,_UWE_(geograph_3712271).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Bower_Ashton_Campus%2C_UWE_%28geograph_3712271%29.jpg/220px-Bower_Ashton_Campus%2C_UWE_%28geograph_3712271%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Bower_Ashton_Campus%2C_UWE_%28geograph_3712271%29.jpg/330px-Bower_Ashton_Campus%2C_UWE_%28geograph_3712271%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Bower_Ashton_Campus%2C_UWE_%28geograph_3712271%29.jpg/440px-Bower_Ashton_Campus%2C_UWE_%28geograph_3712271%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="1440" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bower_Ashton_Campus,_UWE_(geograph_3712271).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bower Ashton Studios</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>School of Art and Design</b> became part of the Faculty of Arts, Creative Industries and Education (ACE) following the University's reorganisation in 2010/11.  Adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/Ashton_Court" title="Ashton Court">Ashton Court</a> estate in <a href="/wiki/Bower_Ashton" title="Bower Ashton">Bower Ashton</a>, the West of England College of Art was established in purpose-built premises in 1969, moving from its previous location as the art school of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_West_of_England_Academy" title="Royal West of England Academy">Royal West of England Academy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Clifton,_Bristol" title="Clifton, Bristol">Clifton</a>. 
</p><p>Among its principals and deans were the war artist <a href="/wiki/Jack_Bridger_Chalker" title="Jack Bridger Chalker">Jack Bridger Chalker</a>, the graphic designer Paul van Der Lem, and Paul Gough RWA, a researcher and art historian,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> who became the first pro-vice chancellor and executive dean of the former faculty in its expanded form of over 2,600 students.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Department_of_Education_and_Childhood">Department of Education and Childhood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Department of Education and Childhood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <b>Department of Education and Childhood</b> (formerly the School of Education) is part of the Faculty of Social Sciences and Humanities. Its origins lie in teacher training colleges at <a href="/wiki/Redland,_Bristol" title="Redland, Bristol">Redland</a> and <a href="/wiki/St_Matthias" class="mw-redirect" title="St Matthias">St Matthias</a> which became part of the former Bristol Polytechnic in 1969. The dean of the school is Ron Ritchie, who is also an assistant vice-chancellor of the university.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> A new purpose built home for the department was completed in 2000 for the department at the University's Frenchay campus.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The department offers undergraduate degrees in initial teacher education in early years education or primary education, as well as an education studies + PGCE (3+1) programme.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate_Certificate_in_Education" title="Postgraduate Certificate in Education">Postgraduate Certificate in Education</a> courses are offered as well as a range of professional development courses for teachers, further and higher education teachers and lecturers, and school support staff.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Echoing Bristol's long connection with the sea and the Merchant Venturers' Navigation School, the top of the crest depicts a ship's mainmast and rigging. The flaming fire basket indicates guidance, hope and the desire for learning.<sup id="cite_ref-coatofarms_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coatofarms-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The shield at the centre is adapted from that of the College of St Matthias with the wavy line representing the rivers of <a href="/wiki/River_Avon_(Bristol)" class="mw-redirect" title="River Avon (Bristol)">Avon</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Severn" title="River Severn">Severn</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Unicorn" title="Unicorn">unicorn</a> is taken from the arms of the City of Bristol and the sea stag from those of the former <a href="/wiki/County_of_Avon" class="mw-redirect" title="County of Avon">County of Avon</a>. Both these creatures wear a crown of <a href="/wiki/Edgar_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Edgar of England">King Edgar</a> around their necks. Edgar is regarded as a local monarch because he was crowned in <a href="/wiki/Bath_Abbey" title="Bath Abbey">Bath Abbey</a> in 973.<sup id="cite_ref-Edgar_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Edgar-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The wavy lines enclosed in circles on the shoulders represent the fountain of knowledge and learning.<sup id="cite_ref-coatofarms_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coatofarms-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The unicorn and sea stag each support an apple tree, known as the tree of knowledge and is taken from the coat of arms of the <a href="/wiki/Council_for_National_Academic_Awards" title="Council for National Academic Awards">Council for National Academic Awards</a> which used to authorise degrees awarded to students of Bristol Polytechnic.<sup id="cite_ref-coatofarms_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coatofarms-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The motto <i>Light, Liberty, Learning</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Disraeli,_1st_Earl_of_Beaconsfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Benjamin Disraeli, 1st Earl of Beaconsfield">Disraeli</a> quotation and corresponds directly to the symbolism of the coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The fire basket represents the Light, the Bristol and Avon supporters represent liberty, and the trees of knowledge and learning.<sup id="cite_ref-coatofarms_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coatofarms-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The volume of world-leading research at UWE Bristol has gone up by 170%, according to the results of the <a href="/wiki/Research_Excellence_Framework" title="Research Excellence Framework">Research Excellence Framework</a> (REF) 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Research_Excellence_Framework_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Research_Excellence_Framework-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The REF 2014 results reveal that 57 percent of the research submitted by UWE Bristol was judged to be either world leading or internationally excellent. The results highlight UWE Bristol's particular strengths in the areas of allied health and nursing, and communications, cultural and media studies. Results were also outstanding in areas such as architecture, built environment and planning; engineering; art and design; computer science; and business and management.<sup id="cite_ref-UWE_Research_Excellence_Framework_results_2014_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UWE_Research_Excellence_Framework_results_2014-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, UWE Bristol launched a research <a href="/wiki/Institutional_repository" title="Institutional repository">repository</a> in order to host electronic versions of the research of its academics. The UWE Bristol Research Repository is <a href="/wiki/Open_access_(publishing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Open access (publishing)">open access</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bristol_Robotics_Laboratory_and_Future_Space">Bristol Robotics Laboratory and Future Space</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Bristol Robotics Laboratory and Future Space">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bristol_Robotics_Laboratory" title="Bristol Robotics Laboratory">Bristol Robotics Laboratory</a> (BRL), the largest robotics laboratory of its type in the UK was officially opened on 10 May 2012 by <a href="/wiki/David_Willetts" title="David Willetts">David Willetts</a>, Minister for Universities and Science. The laboratory is a partnership between University of the West of England (UWE) and the University of Bristol.
</p><p>According to EE/Times, it is the largest robotics laboratory in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> The BRL is home to a community of 70 academics and businesses who are leading current thinking in nouvelle and service robotics, intelligent autonomous systems and bio-engineering. Over £1.65&#160;million has been spent on the new facilities. The total area of the BRL is circa 2,400&#160;m<sup>2</sup>, with over 300 square metres of specialised laboratory space and two Flying Arenas.
<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Future Space is a <a href="/wiki/Business_incubator" title="Business incubator">business incubator</a> adjacent to the Bristol Robotics Laboratory, in a former Hewlett Packard factory building which was bought by UWE Bristol in 2015 and converted. It can house up to 70 hi-tech <a href="/wiki/Startup_companies" class="mw-redirect" title="Startup companies">startup companies</a> and early-stage companies.<sup id="cite_ref-clensy_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clensy-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It is the £16.5&#160;million realisation of the West of England University Enterprise Zone (UEZ), one of four UEZs supported by the UK government,<sup id="cite_ref-farrell_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-farrell-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> which were initially announced by Chancellor <a href="/wiki/George_Osborne" title="George Osborne">George Osborne</a> in 2014. The main areas of focus of the UEZ are robotics, <a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">biotechnology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biomedicine" title="Biomedicine">biomedicine</a>. It is a collaboration with the West of England Local Enterprise Partnership and the University of Bristol, supported by <a href="/wiki/South_Gloucestershire_Council" title="South Gloucestershire Council">South Gloucestershire Council</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bath" title="University of Bath">University of Bath</a> and the West of England Academic Health Science Network.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Future Space opened in autumn 2016.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_College_for_Legal_Training">National College for Legal Training</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: National College for Legal Training">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The National College of Legal Training (NCLT) is a collaboration between UWE Bristol and Central Law Training, launched in January 2010 to provide postgraduate legal training.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> NCLT Study centres are located at <a href="/wiki/Coventry_University" title="Coventry University">Coventry University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Metropolitan_University" title="Manchester Metropolitan University">Manchester Metropolitan University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southampton_Solent_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Southampton Solent University">Southampton Solent University</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Westminster" title="University of Westminster">University of Westminster</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Bristol_Distinguished_Address_Series">The Bristol Distinguished Address Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: The Bristol Distinguished Address Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Based at the University of the West of England Campus in Frenchay the series of lectures provide a unique opportunity to hear about the challenges, issues and decisions being made at the highest level of strategic leadership. These free public lectures bring top level business leaders to Bristol.  The conference covers a wide range of topics including business, technology &amp; innovation, science and local &amp; global issues.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_life">Student life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Student life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Students.27_Union"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Students'_Union">Students' Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_West_of_England,_Bristol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Students&#039; Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Students%27_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Students&#39; Union">Students' Union at UWE</a>, formerly UWE Students' Union (‘UWESU’), is based at Frenchay campus and was established in 1971. It is run by a team of five <a href="/wiki/Sabbatical_officer" title="Sabbatical officer">sabbatical officers</a>, who are elected annually from the student population. The new Students' Union building was completed in Summer 2015 and operates a bar, a coffee shop and two convenience stores at Frenchay Campus. A Students' Union bar and shop is also available at Glenside Campus and Bower Ashton Studios. The student radio station, <a href="/wiki/Hub_Radio" title="Hub Radio">Hub Radio</a> operates out of a studio on campus.
</p>
