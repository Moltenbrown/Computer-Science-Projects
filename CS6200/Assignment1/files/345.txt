<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_and_meaning">Origins and meaning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins and meaning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DSC00987_-_Copy.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/DSC00987_-_Copy.jpg/220px-DSC00987_-_Copy.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/DSC00987_-_Copy.jpg/330px-DSC00987_-_Copy.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/DSC00987_-_Copy.jpg/440px-DSC00987_-_Copy.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DSC00987_-_Copy.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ryan coat of arms at Valley Inn pub, Glenstal, Co. Limerick</div></div></div>
<p>There are several different origins for the surname. In some cases it may be a shortened form of <i>O'Ryan</i>, which is an Anglicized adaptation of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Gaelic</a> surname <i>Ó Riain</i>, meaning "descendant of <i>Rian</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-American_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It can also be a shortened form of <i>Mulryan</i> or <i>O'Mulryan</i>, which are derived from the Gaelic <i><a href="/wiki/%C3%93_Maoilriain" title="Ó Maoilriain">Ó Maoilriain</a></i>, meaning "descendant of the follower of <i>Rian</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-American_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Grehan_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grehan-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The meaning of the <a href="/wiki/Old_Irish" title="Old Irish">Old Irish</a> name <i>Rian</i> is unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-MacLysaght_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MacLysaght-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Some sources have suggested that it is related to the Old Irish word <i>rían</i>, meaning "water" or "ocean".<sup id="cite_ref-SurnameDB_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SurnameDB-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Others have suggested that it is related to the Old Irish word <i><a href="/wiki/R%C3%AD" title="Rí">rí</a></i>, meaning "king".<sup id="cite_ref-O&#39;Boyle_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-O&#39;Boyle-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Both of these etymologies have been discounted by scholars, however.<sup id="cite_ref-MacLysaght_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MacLysaght-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Popular sources typically suggest that <i>Ryan</i> means "little king"<sup id="cite_ref-Smith_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rosenkrantz_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rosenkrantz-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> or "illustrious".<sup id="cite_ref-Grehan_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grehan-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Little_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Little-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are also cases in which <i>Ryan</i> is an Americanized form of the German surname <i>Rein</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-American_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
