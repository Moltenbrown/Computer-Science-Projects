<p><b>National Grandparents Day</b> is a secular holiday celebrated in various countries, with official recognition in some, and occurring on various days of the year, either as one holiday or sometimes as a separate Grandmothers' Day and Grandfathers' Day<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup> (<i>see below for dates by country</i>).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Globalize plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-globalize" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="Globe icon." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Ambox_globe_content.svg/48px-Ambox_globe_content.svg.png" decoding="async" width="48" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Ambox_globe_content.svg/73px-Ambox_globe_content.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Ambox_globe_content.svg/97px-Ambox_globe_content.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="350" data-file-height="290" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">The examples and perspective in this section <b>may not represent a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Countering_systemic_bias" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Countering systemic bias">worldwide view</a> of the subject</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> You may <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a>, discuss the issue on the <a href="/wiki/Talk:National_Grandparents_Day" title="Talk:National Grandparents Day">talk page</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_wizard" title="Wikipedia:Article wizard">create a new article</a>, as appropriate.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">October 2019</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>In the United States, Russell Capper (age 9 in 1969) sent a letter to President Nixon suggesting a special day be set aside as Grandparents Day. 
On June 12, 1969 he received a letter back from Rose Mary Woods (Personal Secretary to the President) reading, "Dear Russell, Thank you for your letter to President Nixon. Your suggestion regarding a Grandparent’s Day is appreciated, but the President ordinarily issues proclamations designating periods for special observance only when a Congressional resolution authorizes him to do so. With best wishes, Sincerely, Rose Mary Woods Personal Secretary to the President".
</p><p>Since the aforementioned letter, <a href="/wiki/Marian_McQuade" title="Marian McQuade">Marian McQuade</a> was recognized nationally by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> &#8211; in particular by Senators Jennings Randolph;<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and Robert Byrd &#8211; and by <a href="/wiki/United_States_President" class="mw-redirect" title="United States President">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Carter" title="Jimmy Carter">Jimmy Carter</a>, as the founder of National Grandparents Day. McQuade made it her goal to educate the youth in the community<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="What community? (September 2010)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> about the important contributions seniors have made throughout history. She also urged the youth to "adopt" a grandparent, not just for one day a year, but rather for a lifetime. Co-founder Cynthia Bennett, who worked for Marian's husband, contributed by writing letters of verification.
</p><p>In 1973, Senator <a href="/wiki/Jennings_Randolph" title="Jennings Randolph">Jennings Randolph</a> introduced a <a href="/wiki/Resolution_(law)" title="Resolution (law)">resolution</a> to the senate to make Grandparents' Day a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Public holidays of the United States">national holiday</a>. West Virginia's Governor Arch Moore had proclaimed an annual Grandparents' Day for the state, at the urging of Marian McQuade.  When Senator Randolph's resolution in the U.S. Senate died in committee, Marian McQuade organized supporters and began contacting governors, senators, and congressmen in all fifty states.  She urged each state to proclaim their own Grandparents' Day. Within three years, she had received Grandparents' Day proclamations from forty-three states. She sent copies of the proclamations to Senator Randolph.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In February 1977, Senator Randolph, with the concurrence of many other senators, introduced a joint resolution to the senate requesting the president to "issue annually a proclamation designating the first Sunday of September after Labor Day of each year as 'National Grandparents' Day'."  <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> passed the legislation proclaiming the first Sunday after <a href="/wiki/Labor_Day" title="Labor Day">Labor Day</a> as National Grandparents' Day and, on August 3, 1978,  then-President Jimmy Carter signed the <a href="/wiki/Proclamation" title="Proclamation">proclamation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The statute cites the day's purpose: "...to honor grandparents, to give grandparents an opportunity to show love for their children's children, and to help children become aware of strength, information, and guidance older people can offer".
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In at least some states of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, Grandparent's Day is celebrated annually, on the last Sunday in October.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bangladesh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, the National Grandparents Day will be celebrated this year on 9th September to honor and express gratitude towards their constant love, care, and support.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brazil">Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, Grandparents' Day (Portuguese: Dia dos avós) is celebrated on July 26.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>National Grandparents' Day began in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> in 1995 but was discontinued in 2014. Motion number 273 submitted in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_Canada" title="House of Commons of Canada">House of Commons</a> by <a href="/wiki/Sarkis_Assadourian" title="Sarkis Assadourian">Sarkis Assadourian</a> read:
</p>
<blockquote><p>That, in the opinion of this House, the government should consider designating the second Sunday in September of each year as Grandparents' Day in order to acknowledge their importance to the structure of the family in the nurturing, upbringing and education of children.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Estonia">Estonia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Estonia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, Grandparents' Day (<i>Vanavanemate päev</i>) is celebrated on the second Sunday in September.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, Grandmothers' Day (<i>La fête des grands-mères</i>) was launched in 1987 by a brand of coffee (<i>Café Grand'Mère</i>), part of the Kraft Jacobs Suchard Group. The date is now included in French calendars and is celebrated on the first Sunday in March.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, Grandmothers' Day was established in 2010 and is celebrated on the second Sunday in October.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hong_Kong">Hong Kong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Hong Kong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Junior_Chamber_International" title="Junior Chamber International">Junior Chamber International</a> Victoria<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> introduced the first Grandparents' Day in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> in 1990. It is celebrated on the second Sunday in October.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, Grandparents' Day (officially <i>Festa Nazionale dei Nonni</i>, "National Grandparents' Feast") was established in 2005 and is celebrated on October 2,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Guardian_Angel" class="mw-redirect" title="Guardian Angel">Guardian Angels'</a> Day in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, Grandparents' Day (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Día del Abuelo</i>) is celebrated on August 28.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Netherlands">Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/The_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="The Netherlands">the Netherlands</a>, "Opa en Oma Dag" was created in 2004 and falls on June 4. It is not widely celebrated and to many people in the Netherlands it is completely unknown. The day was envisioned as a day for grandparents/great-grandparents to spend time in enjoyable activities with their (great-)grandchildren, in order to strengthen the bond between the generations and to increase respect and appreciation for senior citizens in general. One idea that has been promoted is the "adoption" of a grandmother or grandfather - for the day, or for life.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philippines">Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, Grandparents' Day is celebrated every second Sunday of September. The county started celebrating this event in 1987.  
</p><p>Grandparents' Day in the Philippines is also a family day. Many commercial establishments organize special events and give special privileges for the elderly people: free concerts, meal discounts, free medical checkups, flowers, etc. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Poland">Poland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Poland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, "Grandma's Day" (<a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <i lang="pl">Dzień Babci</i>) was created in 1964 by the <i>Kobieta i Życie</i> magazine, and popularized from 1965 onwards. It is celebrated on January 21. "Grandpa's Day" (<a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <i lang="pl">Dzień Dziadka</i>) is celebrated a day later, on January 22.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Singapore">Singapore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Singapore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> started celebrating Grandparents' Day in 1979, a year after the U.S. started. It is celebrated on the fourth Sunday in November.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Sudan">South Sudan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: South Sudan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudan</a> started celebrating Grandparents' Day in 2013, with the date set as the second Sunday in November.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Portugal">Portugal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Portugal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, Grandparents' Day (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">Dia dos avós</i>) is celebrated on July 26.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, Grandparents' Day (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Día de los abuelos y las abuelas</i>) is celebrated on July 26, the feast day of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Joachim" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Joachim">Saint Joachim</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Anne" title="Saint Anne">Saint Anne</a>, parents of <a href="/wiki/Mary,_the_mother_of_Jesus" class="mw-redirect" title="Mary, the mother of Jesus">Mary, the mother of Jesus</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taiwan">Taiwan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Taiwan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Education_(Republic_of_China)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ministry of Education (Republic of China)">Ministry of Education (Republic of China)</a> initiated Grandparents' Day (祖父母節, <i>Zǔfùmǔ Jié</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> on 29 August 2010, on the last Sunday in August annually, shortly before school children would start a new semester.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The celebration was introduced to the UK in 1990 by the charity <a href="/wiki/Age_Concern" title="Age Concern">Age Concern</a>. It has been celebrated on the first Sunday in October since 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> although it is not widely advertised and has not been as commercially successful as <a href="/wiki/Mothers_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Mothers Day">Mother's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Father%27s_Day" title="Father&#39;s Day">Father's Day</a>. Businesses specialising in gifts and greeting cards have started merging the respective grandparents days with <a href="/wiki/Mother%27s_Day" title="Mother&#39;s Day">Mother's Day</a> and <a href="/wiki/Father%27s_Day" title="Father&#39;s Day">Father's Day</a> to try to boost sales.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Grandparents_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Congress passed the legislation proclaiming the first Sunday after Labor Day as National Grandparents' Day in the U.S. and, on August 3, 1978, then-President Jimmy Carter signed the proclamation.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The flower of the U.S. National Grandparents Day is the <a href="/wiki/Forget-me-not" class="mw-redirect" title="Forget-me-not">forget-me-not</a> which blooms in the spring. As a result, seasonal flowers are given in appreciation to grandparents on this day.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
