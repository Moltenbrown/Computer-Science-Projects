		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Artur</caption><tbody><tr><th scope="row">Pronunciation</th><td><small>Portuguese:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">[aʁˈtuʁ]</a></span><br /><small>Polish:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Polish" title="Help:IPA/Polish">[ˈartur]</a></span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Gender</th><td>Male</td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Language(s)</th><td><a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> or <a href="/wiki/Celtic_languages" title="Celtic languages">Celtic</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Meaning</th><td>Bear-like</td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Other names</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">See also</th><td><a href="/wiki/Arthur" title="Arthur">Arthur</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Artur</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Cognate_(etymology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cognate (etymology)">cognate</a> to the common male given name <a href="/wiki/Arthur" title="Arthur">Arthur</a>, meaning "<a href="/wiki/Bear" title="Bear">bear</a>-like," which is believed to possibly be descended from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> surname <a href="/wiki/Artoria_gens" title="Artoria gens">Artorius</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Celt" class="mw-redirect" title="Celt">Celtic</a> bear-goddess <a href="/wiki/Artio" title="Artio">Artio</a> or more probably from the Celtic word <i>artos</i> ("bear"). Other Celtic languages have similar first names, such as <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Old Irish</a> <i>Art, Artúur</i>,  <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a> <i>Arth</i> - which may also be the source for the modern name.
<i>Art</i> is also a diminutive form of the common name Arthur. In <a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>, and many Romance, Slavic and Germanic languages the name is spelled as Artur. The Finnish versions are Arttu and Artturi.
</p>
