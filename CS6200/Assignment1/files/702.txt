<p><i><b>Western Culture</b></i> is a studio <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a> by <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Avant-rock" class="mw-redirect" title="Avant-rock">avant-rock</a> <a href="/wiki/Musical_ensemble" title="Musical ensemble">group</a> <a href="/wiki/Henry_Cow" title="Henry Cow">Henry Cow</a>, recorded at Sunrise Studios in <a href="/wiki/Kirchberg,_St._Gallen" title="Kirchberg, St. Gallen">Kirchberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> in January, July and August 1978. It was their last album and was released on Henry Cow's own private label, Broadcast, in 1979. Later editions appeared on Interzone in the US and Celluloid in France. Only the UK Broadcast pressing used the custom label artwork design.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Culture_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Western Culture</i> is an instrumental album which came about as a result of disagreements in the band as to what the composition of their next album should be. Recording had already begun at Sunrise Studios in January 1978 and some members were not happy about the predominance of song-oriented material. As a compromise it was agreed that two albums would be made: one of "songs" (released soon after as <i><a href="/wiki/Hopes_and_Fears_(Art_Bears_album)" title="Hopes and Fears (Art Bears album)">Hopes and Fears</a></i> under the name of a new group <a href="/wiki/Art_Bears" title="Art Bears">Art Bears</a>) and one of purely instrumental compositions. The group returned to Sunrise Studios in July that year to record the instrumental pieces for <i>Western Culture</i>, except "½  the Sky", which had been recorded during the January sessions.
</p><p>Henry Cow split up shortly after recording this album.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recording">Recording</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Culture_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recording">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Cooper" title="Lindsay Cooper">Lindsay Cooper</a> composition "½ the Sky" was named for <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Chairman Mao</a>'s dictum "Women hold up half the sky".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECutler20098_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECutler20098-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> "Viva Pa Ubu" (an <a href="/wiki/Outtake" title="Outtake">outtake</a> from the January recording sessions) was written by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Hodgkinson" title="Tim Hodgkinson">Tim Hodgkinson</a> as the start of a musical production of <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Jarry" title="Alfred Jarry">Alfred Jarry</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Ubu_Roi" title="Ubu Roi">Ubu Roi</a></i> (Pa Ubu being a character in the play).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECutler200911_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECutler200911-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> "Viva Pa Ubu" includes singing by the whole group, making the CD reissue no longer an instrumental. "Viva Pa Ubu" and "Slice" (an outtake from the July–August recording sessions) had been previously released on the <i><a href="/wiki/Recommended_Records_Sampler" title="Recommended Records Sampler">Recommended Records Sampler</a></i> (1982).
</p><p>During the recording sessions in July and August 1978, Henry Cow also recorded "Waking Against Sleep", a <a href="/wiki/Fred_Frith" title="Fred Frith">Fred Frith</a> composition. This 2-minute piece had previously been performed live by the band under the title "The Herring People", and appeared in <i>Volume 9: Late</i> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_40th_Anniversary_Henry_Cow_Box_Set" title="The 40th Anniversary Henry Cow Box Set">The 40th Anniversary Henry Cow Box Set</a></i> (2009). It was later recorded by <a href="/wiki/Curlew_(band)" title="Curlew (band)">Curlew</a> under the title "Time and a Half", and appeared on their album, <i>North America</i> (1985), which was produced by Frith. "Waking Against Sleep" was never released by Henry Cow, but appeared on the 1990 CD re-issue of Frith's solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Gravity_(Fred_Frith_album)" title="Gravity (Fred Frith album)">Gravity</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECutler20098_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECutler20098-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="CD_reissues">CD reissues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Culture_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: CD reissues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Western Culture</i> was reissued on CD by Broadcast in 1988 and <a href="/wiki/East_Side_Digital_Records" title="East Side Digital Records">East Side Digital Records</a> in 1995. It was reissued and partially remastered by <a href="/wiki/Bob_Drake_(musician)" title="Bob Drake (musician)">Bob Drake</a> by <a href="/wiki/Recommended_Records" title="Recommended Records">Recommended Records</a> in 2001 and East Side Digital in 2002 with three bonus tracks (one previously unreleased), new liner notes and photographs. The bonus tracks are listed in the wrong order in the booklet.
</p>
