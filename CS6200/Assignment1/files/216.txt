	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Miller (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miller_(name)&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Miller (name)">Miller (name)</a>)</span></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For a list of people with the surname Miller, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_people_with_surname_Miller" title="List of people with surname Miller">List of people with surname Miller</a>.</div>
<table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Miller</caption><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Meaning</th><td>derived from the occupation of a <a href="/wiki/Miller" title="Miller">miller</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Region of origin</th><td><a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> (<a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>)</td></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;border-top: #a2a9b1 1px solid;"><sup id="cite_ref-worldnames_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worldnames-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gbnames_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gbnames-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-britishsurnames_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-britishsurnames-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-scotland_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotland-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mencken_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mencken-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Miller</b> and <b>Millar</b> are surnames of <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a>, <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> or <a href="/wiki/Scots_language" title="Scots language">Scottish</a> origin. There are two <a href="/wiki/Homonym" title="Homonym">homonymous</a> forms of <i>Miller</i>, one that began as an <a href="/wiki/Surname#Occupational_surname" title="Surname">occupational surname</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Miller" title="Miller">miller</a><sup id="cite_ref-worldnames_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worldnames-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gbnames_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gbnames-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-britishsurnames_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-britishsurnames-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-scotland_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotland-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and another that began as a <a href="/wiki/Toponymic_surname" title="Toponymic surname">toponymic surname</a> for people from a locale in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>. <i>Miller</i> of the occupational origin may also be translated from many <a href="/wiki/Cognate" title="Cognate">cognate</a> surnames from other <a href="/wiki/European_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="European languages">European languages</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mencken_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mencken-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> such as <i><a href="/wiki/Mueller" title="Mueller">Mueller</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCller_(surname)" title="Müller (surname)">Müller</a></i>, <i>Mühler</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Moller" title="Moller">Moller</a></i>, <i>Möller</i>, <i>Møller</i>, <a href="/wiki/Myller" title="Myller">Myller</a>, and others. There is also a form in the early English lingusitics as <i>Milleiir</i>.
</p><p>The standard modern word represents the northern <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a> term, an agent derivative of mille ‘mill’, reinforced by <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a> mylnari (see Milner). In southern, western, and central England, Millward (literally, ‘mill keeper’) was the usual term.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The origin of the Scottish surname is from a burn (rivulet) in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, namely the molindinar (Mo-lynn-dine-are), and the name has evolved over the years to molindar Mo-lynn-dar and to molinar mo-lynn-ar and to Millar and finally to Miller. The first record of the name was in <a href="/wiki/Dumfriesshire" title="Dumfriesshire">Dumfriesshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> If the surname has Highland Scottish origins, the bearers are associated with <a href="/wiki/Clan_MacFarlane" title="Clan MacFarlane">Clan MacFarlane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1995, Miller was the 22nd most common surname on the birth, death and marriage registers in Scotland; Millar was 77th.<sup id="cite_ref-scotland_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotland-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The name Miller also has a long history in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, notably <a href="/wiki/County_Antrim" title="County Antrim">County Antrim</a> where many migrants from Northern England and Scotland settled in the 16th and 17th centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Amongst the earliest recordings of the surname is Reginald Miller in the Subsidy Tax Rolls of <a href="/wiki/Sussex" title="Sussex">Sussex</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_East_England" title="South East England">South East England</a> in 1327.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_North_America">In North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miller_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The surname Miller in Canada and the United States can also be the result of <a href="/wiki/Anglicization" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicization">anglicization</a> of:
</p>
<ul><li>surnames of German origin as <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCller_(surname)" title="Müller (surname)">Müller</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Mueller" title="Mueller">Mueller</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Moeller" title="Moeller">Moeller</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Muller" title="Muller">Muller</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Mahler_(surname)" title="Mahler (surname)">Mahler</a>", all of which are cognates of Miller</li>
<li>surnames from other European languages, for example: French - <a href="/wiki/Meunier_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect" title="Meunier (disambiguation)">Meunier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dumoulin_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Dumoulin (disambiguation)">Dumoulin</a>, Demoulins and Moulin, Dutch - <a href="/wiki/Molenaar" title="Molenaar">Molenaar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mulder" title="Mulder">Mulder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Smulders" title="Smulders">Smulders</a>, Danish - <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B8ller" title="Møller">Møller</a>, Italian - <a href="/wiki/Molinaro" title="Molinaro">Molinaro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Molinari" title="Molinari">Molinari</a>, Spanish - Molinero or Molino (= mill), Romanian - <a href="/wiki/Moraru_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Moraru (disambiguation)">Morariu</a>, Hungarian - Molnár, Slavic - Mlinar or Melnik, Greek - Mylonas (Μυλωνάς) etc.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mencken_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mencken-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>Miller is also the third most common surname among <a href="/wiki/Jews_in_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Jews in the United States">Jews in the United States</a> (after Cohen and Levy), from the <a href="/wiki/Yiddish_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Yiddish language">Yiddish</a> cognate of Müller, which would be Miller (מיללער) or Milner <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>(מילנער).
</p><p>According to the 1990 U.S. Census, Miller was the 7th most common surname in the United States by 1990, accounting for 0.424% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, about 1 in every 25 <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">Americans</a> were named Smith, Johnson, Williams, Brown, Jones, Miller or Davis. Miller was the seventh most common surname.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
