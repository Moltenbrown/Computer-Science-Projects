<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Iceland">Iceland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surname_law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Iceland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Icelandic law enforces the conventions of <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_name" title="Icelandic name">Icelandic names</a>, which require that the last name be derived from a given name of the father or mother, suffixed with "-son" or "-dóttir".  The law allows both derivations to be used, and for foreign last names to be inherited or kept by foreigners. This means that a father, mother, and child will all typically have different last names.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Foreigners who marry an Icelander and get Icelandic citizenship can take the last name of their partner, or a patronym or matronym from the name of a parent or parent-in-law; these possibilities are not necessarily open to native Icelanders.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_Naming_Committee" title="Icelandic Naming Committee">Icelandic Naming Committee</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Iran">Iran</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surname_law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Iran">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Persian_name#Surnames" title="Persian name">Persian name §&#160;Surnames</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Reza_Shah_Pahlavi" class="mw-redirect" title="Reza Shah Pahlavi">Reza Shah Pahlavi</a> ordered Iranians to adopt Western style surnames in place of old Islamic names and titles during his reign.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surname_law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A law in Japan dating from 1896 requires a married couple to have a common surname. Most commonly it was the wife who took her husband's name. In 2011, this law was challenged as unconstitutional on <a href="/wiki/Gender_equality" title="Gender equality">gender equality</a> grounds, but the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Japan" title="Supreme Court of Japan">Supreme Court of Japan</a> upheld the law in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prussian_ruled_Poland">Prussian ruled Poland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surname_law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Prussian ruled Poland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prussia tasked the implementation of surnames to <a href="/wiki/E._T._A._Hoffmann" title="E. T. A. Hoffmann">E. T. A. Hoffmann</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Davies1996_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Davies1996-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Tyrmand2014_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tyrmand2014-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lennox2013_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lennox2013-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Thailand">Thailand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surname_law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Thailand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the current law, Person Name Act, BE 2505 (1962), to create a new <a href="/wiki/Thai_name" title="Thai name">Thai name</a>, it must not be longer than ten <a href="/wiki/Thai_alphabet" class="mw-redirect" title="Thai alphabet">Thai letters</a>, excluding vowel symbols and diacritics.<sup id="cite_ref-Nittaya-Royin-1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nittaya-Royin-1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The same law also forbids the creation of a surname that duplicated any existing surnames, but there are some duplicates dating to the time before computer databases were available to prevent this.<sup id="cite_ref-Nittaya-Royin-2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nittaya-Royin-2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Some creations added the name of their location (<a href="/wiki/Muban" title="Muban">muban</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tambon" title="Tambon">tambon</a> or <a href="/wiki/Amphoe" title="Amphoe">amphoe</a>) into surnames, similar to <a href="/wiki/Family_name_affixes" class="mw-redirect" title="Family name affixes">family name suffixes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Korat-surnamesuffixes1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Korat-surnamesuffixes1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Non_Sung-surnamesuffixes1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Non_Sung-surnamesuffixes1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Thaisong-surnamesuffix_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thaisong-surnamesuffix-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Turkey">Turkey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surname_law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Turkey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Surname_Law_(Turkey)" title="Surname Law (Turkey)">Surname Law (Turkey)</a></div>
<p>On 21 June 1934, Turkey adopted the Surname Law which required all its citizens to adopt and use Western-style surnames. Only names with Turkish origin were permitted, and non-Turks with pre-existing surnames were required to adopt new Turkish names.
</p>
