	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Parents' Day</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<table class="infobox vevent" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="hd" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold;background-color: transparent">Parents' Day</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Frequency</th><td>Annual</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Parents' Day</b> is observed in South Korea (May 8) and in the United States (fourth Sunday of July). The South Korean designation was established in 1973, replacing the <a href="/wiki/Mother%27s_Day" title="Mother&#39;s Day">Mother's Day</a> previously marked on May 8, and includes public and private celebrations. The United States day was created in 1994 under President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a>. June 1 has also been proclaimed as "Global Day of Parents" by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> as a mark of appreciation for the commitment of parents towards their children. In the Philippines, while it is not strictly observed or celebrated, the first Monday of December each year is proclaimed as Parents' Day.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parents%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> proclaimed June 1 to be the <b>Global Day of Parents</b> "to appreciate all parents in all parts of the world for their selfless commitment to children and their lifelong sacrifice towards nurturing this relationship".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is the same day as <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Day" title="Children&#39;s Day">International Children's Day</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_United_States">In the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parents%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="infobox vevent" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="hd" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold;background-color: transparent">Parents' Day</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Observed&#160;by</th><td>United States</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Type</th><td>Federal observance</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Date</th><td>Fourth Sunday in July</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">2018&#160;date</th><td>July 22   <span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="dtstart">2018-07-22</span>)</span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">2019&#160;date</th><td>July 28   <span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="dtstart">2019-07-28</span>)</span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">2020&#160;date</th><td>July 26   <span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="dtstart">2020-07-26</span>)</span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">2021&#160;date</th><td>July 25   <span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="dtstart">2021-07-25</span>)</span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Frequency</th><td>annual</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Related&#160;to</th><td><a href="/wiki/Mother%27s_Day" title="Mother&#39;s Day">Mother's Day</a> <br /> <a href="/wiki/Father%27s_Day" title="Father&#39;s Day">Father's Day</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, Parents' Day is held on the fourth Sunday of July.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This was established in 1994 when President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> signed a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congressional</a> <a href="/wiki/Resolution_(law)" title="Resolution (law)">Resolution</a> into law (<a href="/wiki/Title_36_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 36 of the United States Code">36 U.S.C.</a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/36/135">§&#160;135</a>) for "recognizing, uplifting, and supporting the role of parents in the rearing of children." The bill was introduced by Republican Senator <a href="/wiki/Trent_Lott" title="Trent Lott">Trent Lott</a>. It was supported by members of the <a href="/wiki/Unification_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Unification Church">Unification Church</a> which also celebrates a holiday called Parents' Day, although on a different date.<sup id="cite_ref-parents_day_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parents_day-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Parents' Day is celebrated throughout the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_South_Korea">In South Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parents%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In South Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, Parents' Day (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko">어버이날</span> <i>Eobeoinal</i>) is annually held on May 8.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Parents' Day is celebrated by both the public and the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_South_Korea" title="Government of South Korea">government</a>. Family events focus on the parents; popular actions include giving parents <a href="/wiki/Dianthus_caryophyllus" title="Dianthus caryophyllus">carnations</a>. Public events are led by the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Health_and_Welfare_(South_Korea)" title="Ministry of Health and Welfare (South Korea)">Ministry of Health and Welfare</a> and include public celebrations and awards.<sup id="cite_ref-Folkency_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Folkency-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The origins of Parents' Day can be traced back to the 1930s. Starting in 1930, some <a href="/wiki/Christians" title="Christians">Christian</a> communities began to celebrate Mother's Day or Parents' Day. This tradition was combined with Korea's traditional <a href="/wiki/Confucianism" title="Confucianism">Confucianism</a> <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Korea" title="Culture of Korea">culture</a> to eventually establish Mother's Day.<sup id="cite_ref-Folkency_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Folkency-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1956, the <a href="/wiki/State_Council_of_South_Korea" title="State Council of South Korea">State Council of South Korea</a> designated May 8 as an annual Mother's Day.<sup id="cite_ref-Doopedia_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Doopedia-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> However, the question of Father's Day was discussed<sup id="cite_ref-EncyKorea_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EncyKorea-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and on March 30, 1973, May 8 was designated as Parents' Day under Presidential Decree 6615, or the <i>Regulations Concerning Various Holidays</i> (각종 기념일 등에 관한 규정).<sup id="cite_ref-Doopedia_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Doopedia-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> When Parents' Day was first established, the entire week with the 8th day was designated to be a week to respect the elderly, but respecting elders in the month of May was abolished in 1997 with October becoming the month designated for respecting the elderly.<sup id="cite_ref-Folkency_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Folkency-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_Philippines">In the Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parents%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In the Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Mothers' Day is traditionally celebrated on the first Monday of December. On this day, children placed pink <a href="/wiki/Antigonon_leptopus" title="Antigonon leptopus">cadena de amor</a> on their chest. Children who no longer have mothers place white cadena de amor.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 1921, Circular No. 33 designating the first Monday every December as Mothers' day was issued, as a response to the appeal Ilocos Norte Federation of Woman's Clubs. During the Philippine Commonwealth Government, then <a href="/wiki/Manuel_L._Quezon" title="Manuel L. Quezon">President Quezon</a> issued Proclamation No. 213, s. 1937 declaring the day designated as Mothers'  Day as Parents' Day. This was due to finding petitions to set a special date for Fathers’ Day not advisable as there are already set of numerous holidays set, and deeming it more fitting to celebrate both Mothers' and Fathers' Day together and not apart. In 1980, a proclamation was issued declaring first Sunday and the first Monday of December as Father's Day and Mother's Day respectively. In 1988, the issued presidential proclamation followed the international day of celebration of Father's and Mother's Day which most Filipinos are familiar with. However, then <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Estrada" title="Joseph Estrada">President Estrada</a> tried to revive the tradition through Proclamation No. 58, s. 1998.
</p>
