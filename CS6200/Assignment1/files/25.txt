<p><b>Celebrity</b> is the fame and public attention accorded by the <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">mass media</a> to individuals or groups or, occasionally, animals, but is usually applied to the persons or groups of people (celebrity couples, families, etc.) themselves who receive such a status of fame and attention. Celebrity status is often associated with <a href="/wiki/Wealth" title="Wealth">wealth</a> (commonly referred to as <i>fame and fortune</i>), while fame often provides opportunities to earn revenue.
</p><p>Successful careers in <a href="/wiki/Sport" title="Sport">sports</a> and <a href="/wiki/Entertainment" title="Entertainment">entertainment</a> are commonly associated with celebrity status,<sup id="cite_ref-I_want_to_be_famous_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-I_want_to_be_famous-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Western_world_kids_want_to_grow_up_to_be_famous_Children_in_developing_countries_want_to_be_doctors_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Western_world_kids_want_to_grow_up_to_be_famous_Children_in_developing_countries_want_to_be_doctors-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Political_leaders" class="mw-redirect" title="Political leaders">political leaders</a> often become celebrities. People may also become celebrities due to media attention on their lifestyle, wealth, or controversial actions, or for their connection to a famous person.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Athletes in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Ancient Greece</a> were welcomed home as heroes, had songs and poems written in their honor, and received free food and gifts from those seeking <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_endorsement" class="mw-redirect" title="Celebrity endorsement">celebrity endorsement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Ancient Rome</a> similarly lauded actors and notorious gladiators, and <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a> appeared on a coin in his own lifetime (a departure from the usual depiction of battles and divine lineage).<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the early 12th century, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Becket" title="Thomas Becket">Thomas Becket</a> became famous following his murder. He was promoted by the Christian Church as a martyr and images of him and scenes from his life became widespread in just a few years. In a pattern often repeated, what started out as an explosion of popularity (often referred to with the suffix 'mania') turned into long-lasting fame: pilgrimages to Canterbury Cathedral where he was killed became instantly fashionable and the fascination with his life and death have inspired plays and films.
</p><p>The cult of personality (particularly in the west) can be traced back to the Romantics in the 18th century,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> whose livelihood as artists and poets depended on the currency of their reputation. The establishment of cultural hot-spots became an important factor in the process of generating fame: for example, London and Paris in the 18th and 19th centuries. Newspapers started including gossip columns <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and certain clubs and events became places to be seen in order to receive publicity.
</p><p>The movie industry spread around the globe in the first half of the 20th century and with it the now familiar concept of the instantly recognizable faces of its superstars. Yet, celebrity was not always tied to actors in films, especially when cinema was starting out as a medium. As Paul McDonald states in <i>The Star System: Hollywood's Production of Popular Identities</i>, "in the first decade of the twentieth century, American film production companies withheld the names of film performers, despite requests from audiences, fearing that public recognition would drive performers to demand higher salaries."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  Public fascination went well beyond the on-screen exploits of <a href="/wiki/Movie_star" title="Movie star">movie stars</a> and their private lives became headline news: for example, in Hollywood the marriages of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Taylor" title="Elizabeth Taylor">Elizabeth Taylor</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a> the affairs of <a href="/wiki/Raj_Kapoor" title="Raj Kapoor">Raj Kapoor</a> in the 1950s.
</p><p>The second half of the century saw television and popular music bring new forms of celebrity, such as the rock star and the pop group, epitomised by <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">the Beatles</a>, respectively. <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a>'s highly controversial 1966 quote: "We're more popular than <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a> now,"<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> which he later insisted was not a boast, and that he was not in any way comparing himself with Christ,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMiles1997295_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMiles1997295-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> gives an insight into both the adulation and notoriety that fame can bring. Unlike movies, television created celebrities who were not primarily actors; for example, presenters, talk show hosts, and news readers. However, most of these are only famous within the regions reached by their particular broadcaster, and only a few such as <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" title="Oprah Winfrey">Oprah Winfrey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Springer" title="Jerry Springer">Jerry Springer</a>, or <a href="/wiki/David_Frost" title="David Frost">David Frost</a> could be said to have broken through into wider stardom.
</p><p>In the '60s and early '70s, the book publishing industry began to persuade major celebrities to put their names on autobiographies and other titles in a genre called celebrity publishing. In most cases, the book was not written by the celebrity but by a ghost-writer, but the celebrity would then be available for a book tour and appearances on talk shows.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_and_cultural_implications">Regional and cultural implications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Regional and cultural implications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lydia_Canaan_in_Concert.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Lydia_Canaan_in_Concert.jpg/220px-Lydia_Canaan_in_Concert.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="180" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Lydia_Canaan_in_Concert.jpg/330px-Lydia_Canaan_in_Concert.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Lydia_Canaan_in_Concert.jpg/440px-Lydia_Canaan_in_Concert.jpg 2x" data-file-width="750" data-file-height="613" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lydia_Canaan_in_Concert.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lydia_Canaan" title="Lydia Canaan">Lydia Canaan</a>, first rock star of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a></div></div></div>
<p>Cultures and regions with a significant population may have their own independent celebrity systems, with distinct hierarchies. For example, the Canadian province of <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, which is French-speaking, has its own system of French-speaking television, movie and music celebrities. A person who garners a degree of fame in one culture may be considered less famous or obscure in another. Some nationwide celebrities might command some attention outside their own nation; for example, the singer <a href="/wiki/Lara_Fabian" title="Lara Fabian">Lara Fabian</a> is widely known in the <a href="/wiki/French-speaking" class="mw-redirect" title="French-speaking">French-speaking</a> world, but only had a couple of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> hits in the U.S., whereas the francophone Canadian singer <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a> is well known in both the French-speaking world and in the United States.
</p><p>Regions within a country, or cultural communities (linguistic, ethnic, or religious) can also have their own celebrity systems, especially in linguistically or culturally distinct regions such as <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>. Regional radio personalities, <a href="/wiki/Newscasters" class="mw-redirect" title="Newscasters">newscasters</a>, politicians or community <a href="/wiki/Leader" class="mw-redirect" title="Leader">leaders</a> may be local or regional celebrities.
</p><p>In politics, certain politicians are recognizable to many people, usually the head of state and the Prime Minister. Yet only heads of state who play a major role in international politics have a good chance of becoming famous outside their country's borders, since they are constantly featured in <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">mass media</a>. <a href="/wiki/The_President_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="The President of the United States">The President of the United States</a>, for instance, is famous by name and face to millions of people around the world. Since World War II the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Presidential_elections" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Presidential elections">U.S. Presidential elections</a> are followed closely all across the globe, making the elected candidate instantly world-famous as a result. In contrast, both the <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Dalai_Lama" class="mw-redirect" title="The Dalai Lama">The Dalai Lama</a> are far more famous under their official title than under their actual names. Usually when politicians leave active politics their recognizability tends to diminish among general audiences, as other politicians replace them in their official political functions. Certain politicians, however, are still famous today, even decades or centuries after they were in power. They owe their fame to historical deeds which are kept in memory in history classes, for instance people like <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Genghis_Khan" title="Genghis Khan">Genghis Khan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" class="mw-redirect" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">Napoleon Bonaparte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scandal" title="Scandal">Scandal</a> can also unwillingly make certain politicians famous, even among those who are not particularly interested in politics.
</p><p>English-speaking media commentators and journalists will sometimes refer to celebrities as belonging to the <i><a href="/wiki/A-List" class="mw-redirect" title="A-List">A-List</a></i> or state that a certain actor belongs to the <i>B-List</i>, the latter being a disparaging context. These informal rankings indicate a placing within a hierarchy. However, due to differing levels of celebrity in different regions, it is difficult to place people within one bracket. A Brazilian actor might be a B-list action film actor in the U.S., but an A-list star in Portugal.
</p><p>Some elements are associated with fame, such as appearing on the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i>, being spoofed in <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_(magazine)" title="Mad (magazine)">Mad</a></i>, having a wax statue in <i><a href="/wiki/Madame_Tussauds" title="Madame Tussauds">Madame Tussauds</a></i>, or receiving a star on the <i><a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a>.</i>
</p><p>Certain people are known even to people unfamiliar with the area in which they assembled their fame. If one has to name a famous boxer, they are more likely to name <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Ali" title="Muhammad Ali">Muhammad Ali</a> or <a href="/wiki/Mike_Tyson" title="Mike Tyson">Mike Tyson</a>, since their fame expanded beyond the sport itself and appealed not just to followers of the sport but also outsiders to the area. <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Picasso" title="Pablo Picasso">Pablo Picasso</a>'s style and name are known even to people who are not interested in art; likewise many know that <a href="/wiki/Harry_Houdini" title="Harry Houdini">Harry Houdini</a> was an illusionist, <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Woods" title="Tiger Woods">Tiger Woods</a> a golfer, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Branson" title="Richard Branson">Richard Branson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bill_Gates" title="Bill Gates">Bill Gates</a> are entrepreneurs, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Albert Einstein</a> a scientist; <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Mozart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_van_Beethoven" title="Ludwig van Beethoven">Beethoven</a> classical composers; <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Pavarotti" title="Luciano Pavarotti">Luciano Pavarotti</a> an opera singer, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Lee" title="Bruce Lee">Bruce Lee</a> a martial artist, <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a> a playwright, <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney" title="Walt Disney">Walt Disney</a> an animator and <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gagarin" title="Yuri Gagarin">Yuri Gagarin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neil_Armstrong" title="Neil Armstrong">Neil Armstrong</a> astronauts though they did not all excel in the same area.
</p><p>Criminals can also become world-famous if the media cover their crimes, arrest, trial and possible punishment extensively and/or if the crime itself is sensational enough. Assassins of high-profile celebrities can become famous, like <a href="/wiki/Brutus" title="Brutus">Brutus</a> who is remembered for murdering <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a>. People who commit extremely gruesome crimes can also achieve infamy, such as Nazi doctor <a href="/wiki/Josef_Mengele" title="Josef Mengele">Josef Mengele</a>. Certain criminals have achieved lasting fame thanks to romanticization in popular culture, such as <a href="/wiki/Guy_Fawkes" title="Guy Fawkes">Guy Fawkes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackbeard" title="Blackbeard">Blackbeard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billy_the_Kid" title="Billy the Kid">Billy the Kid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_%26_Clyde" class="mw-redirect" title="Bonnie &amp; Clyde">Bonnie &amp; Clyde</a>. Others owe their fame to never being identified or caught, like <a href="/wiki/Jack_the_Ripper" title="Jack the Ripper">Jack the Ripper</a>, or by regularly  being interviewed in jail, like <a href="/wiki/Charles_Manson" title="Charles Manson">Charles Manson</a>. However, certain criminals are covered far less extensively in media and, as such, do not become very famous at all. In other cases the huge media coverage disappears after the conclusion of their trial, causing them to fade in obscurity again. This has even happened to people who commit high-profile crimes, like <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Ravaillac" title="François Ravaillac">François Ravaillac</a> whose murder of <a href="/wiki/Henry_IV_of_France" title="Henry IV of France">Henry IV of France</a> in 1610 is nowadays only remembered by people with historic knowledge. In some cases people who've been acquitted of certain crimes are still remembered as being guilty today, like <a href="/wiki/Lizzie_Borden" title="Lizzie Borden">Lizzie Borden</a>, showing that the sensation occasionally overshadows the actual facts.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fictional_implications">Fictional implications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fictional implications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The same phenomenon is true for fictional characters. <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dracula" title="Dracula">Dracula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frankenstein%27s_Monster" class="mw-redirect" title="Frankenstein&#39;s Monster">Frankenstein's Monster</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> continue to be portrayed in film, television and literature decades after the original stories were published. <a href="/wiki/Superman" title="Superman">Superman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Hulk" class="mw-redirect" title="The Hulk">The Hulk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Woman" title="Wonder Woman">Wonder Woman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Batman" title="Batman">Batman</a> represent super heroes to a far wider audience than that of the comics and graphic novels in which they appear. The same can be said about other comics characters which enjoy international distribution and syndication such as <a href="/wiki/Popeye" title="Popeye">Popeye</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tintin_(character)" title="Tintin (character)">Tintin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoopy" title="Snoopy">Snoopy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ast%C3%A9rix" class="mw-redirect" title="Astérix">Astérix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garfield_(character)" title="Garfield (character)">Garfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Astro_Boy" title="Astro Boy">Astro Boy</a>. <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a> have theme parks around the world which rely on the fame of its creations headed by <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Mouse" title="Mickey Mouse">Mickey Mouse</a>. Thanks to the global reach of film and television characters like <a href="/wiki/King_Kong" title="King Kong">King Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Godzilla" title="Godzilla">Godzilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Flintstones" title="The Flintstones">The Flintstones</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Muppets" title="The Muppets">The Muppets</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a> are instantly recognizable to millions. Certain fictional characters known from TV series have become so famous that their names are more well known than those of the actors who perform them. A good example is <a href="/wiki/Larry_Hagman" title="Larry Hagman">Larry Hagman</a> who played <a href="/wiki/J.R._Ewing" class="mw-redirect" title="J.R. Ewing">J.R. Ewing</a> on the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Dallas_(1978_TV_series)" title="Dallas (1978 TV series)">Dallas</a></i>. When his character was shot during a <a href="/wiki/Cliffhanger" title="Cliffhanger">cliffhanger</a> episode without the viewers knowing who was the killer, it caused a media hype around the question: <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Shot_J.R.%3F" class="mw-redirect" title="Who Shot J.R.?">Who Shot J.R.?</a></i>. By the time the answer was given in the first episode of the next season millions of people instantly recognized Hagman's face as J.R. rather than that of himself. Some characters from video and computer games have developed a celebrity life beyond these media, such as <a href="/wiki/Mario" title="Mario">Mario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lara_Croft" title="Lara Croft">Lara Croft</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pikachu" title="Pikachu">Pikachu</a>. Certain advertising characters have also become iconic thanks to decades of constant merchandising, such as <a href="/wiki/Ronald_McDonald" title="Ronald McDonald">Ronald McDonald</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bibendum" class="mw-redirect" title="Bibendum">Bibendum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hello_Kitty" title="Hello Kitty">Hello Kitty</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Becoming_a_celebrity">Becoming a celebrity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Becoming a celebrity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Chief_Guest_and_renowned_actor_Shahrukh_Khan_addressing_at_the_inauguration_of_the_38th_International_Film_Festival_of_India_(IFFI-2007)_at_Panaji,_Goa_on_November_23,_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/The_Chief_Guest_and_renowned_actor_Shahrukh_Khan_addressing_at_the_inauguration_of_the_38th_International_Film_Festival_of_India_%28IFFI-2007%29_at_Panaji%2C_Goa_on_November_23%2C_2007.jpg/220px-thumbnail.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/The_Chief_Guest_and_renowned_actor_Shahrukh_Khan_addressing_at_the_inauguration_of_the_38th_International_Film_Festival_of_India_%28IFFI-2007%29_at_Panaji%2C_Goa_on_November_23%2C_2007.jpg/330px-thumbnail.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/The_Chief_Guest_and_renowned_actor_Shahrukh_Khan_addressing_at_the_inauguration_of_the_38th_International_Film_Festival_of_India_%28IFFI-2007%29_at_Panaji%2C_Goa_on_November_23%2C_2007.jpg/440px-thumbnail.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="1325" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Chief_Guest_and_renowned_actor_Shahrukh_Khan_addressing_at_the_inauguration_of_the_38th_International_Film_Festival_of_India_(IFFI-2007)_at_Panaji,_Goa_on_November_23,_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Shah_Rukh_Khan" title="Shah Rukh Khan">Shah Rukh Khan</a> is an Indian actor, producer, businessman, investor, author, philanthropist, activist, host and much more.</div></div></div>
<p>People may become celebrities in a wide range of ways; from their professions, following appearances in the media, or by complete accident. The term "instant celebrity" describes someone who becomes a celebrity in a very short period of time. Someone who achieves a small amount of <a href="/wiki/15_minutes_of_fame" title="15 minutes of fame">transient fame</a> (through, say, <a href="/wiki/Media_circus" title="Media circus">hype</a> or mass media) may become labeled a "B-grade celebrity". Often, the generalization extends to someone who falls short of mainstream or persistent fame but who seeks to extend or exploit it.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Success">Success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are, of course, no guarantees of success for an individual to become a celebrity. Though celebrities come from many different working fields, most celebrities are typically associated with the fields of sports and entertainment, or a person may be a public figure who is commonly recognizable in mass media with commercial and critical acclaim.
</p><p>Though glamour and wealth may certainly play a role for only famous celebrities, most people in the sports and entertainments spheres, be it music, film, television, radio, modelling, comedy, literature etc. live in obscurity and only a small percentage achieve fame and fortune.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Outside of the sports and entertainment sphere, the top inventors, professionals such as doctors, lawyers, and scientists, etc. are unlikely to become celebrities even if they are enormously successful in their field due to society's disinterest in science, <a href="/wiki/Invention" title="Invention">invention</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a>, and courtroom law which is not fictional. American microbiologist <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Hilleman" title="Maurice Hilleman">Maurice Hilleman</a> is credited with saving more lives than any other medical <a href="/wiki/Scientist" title="Scientist">scientist</a> of the 20th century.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> After Hilleman's death <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Nader" title="Ralph Nader">Ralph Nader</a> wrote, "Yet almost no one knew about him, saw him on television, or read about him in newspapers or magazines. His anonymity, in comparison with Madonna, Michael Jackson, <a href="/wiki/Jose_Canseco" title="Jose Canseco">Jose Canseco</a>, or an assortment of grade B actors, tells something about our society's and media's concepts of celebrity; much less of the heroic."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Difficulty">Difficulty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Difficulty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Letterman_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/David_Letterman_2.jpg/220px-David_Letterman_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="270" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/David_Letterman_2.jpg/330px-David_Letterman_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/David_Letterman_2.jpg/440px-David_Letterman_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="932" data-file-height="1145" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Letterman_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Letterman" title="David Letterman">David Letterman</a>, comedian and former <a href="/wiki/American_people" class="mw-redirect" title="American people">American</a> late night talk show host</div></div></div>
<p>A number of <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" class="mw-redirect" title="Sportsperson">athletes</a> who are unable to turn professional take a second job or even sometimes abandon their athletic aspirations in order to make ends meet. A small percentage of entertainers and athletes are able to make a decent living but a vast majority will spend their careers toiling from hard work, determination, rejection, and frequent unemployment. For minor league to amateur athletes, earnings are usually on the lower end of the pay-scale. Many of them take second jobs on the side or even venture into other occupations within the field of sports such as coaching, <a href="/wiki/General_management" class="mw-redirect" title="General management">general management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Referee" title="Referee">refereeing</a>, or recruiting and scouting up-and-coming athletes.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Becoming_a_celebrity_in_the_U.S.">Becoming a celebrity in the U.S.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Becoming a celebrity in the U.S.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild" title="Screen Actors Guild">Screen Actors Guild</a>, a union representing actors and actresses throughout <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a> reports that the average television and film actor earns less than US$50,000 annually; the median hourly wage for actors was $18.80 in May 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-Actors,_Producers,_and_Directors_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Actors,_Producers,_and_Directors-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Actors sometimes alternate between theater, television, and film or even branch into other occupations within the <a href="/wiki/List_of_entertainment_industry_topics" class="mw-redirect" title="List of entertainment industry topics">entertainment industry</a> such as becoming a singer, comedian, producer, or a television host in order to be monetarily diversified, as doing one gig pays comparatively very little. For instance, <a href="/wiki/David_Letterman" title="David Letterman">David Letterman</a> is well known for branching into <a href="/wiki/Late_night_television" title="Late night television">late night television</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Talk_show_host" class="mw-redirect" title="Talk show host">talk show host</a> while honing his skills as a stand-up comedian, <a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a> ventured into acting while operating as a singer, or <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a>, who achieved even greater fame in Hollywood for being a film director and a producer than for his acting credentials.
</p><p>According to American entertainment magnate <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a>, entertainers and professional athletes make up less than 1% of all millionaires in the entire world.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Less than 1% of all runway models are known to make more than US$1000 for every fashion showcase. According to the US <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Labor_Statistics" title="Bureau of Labor Statistics">Bureau of Labor Statistics</a>, the median wage for commercial and print models was only $11.22 per hour in 2006 and was also listed one of the top ten worst jobs in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-worst_jobs_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worst_jobs-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wealth">Wealth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Wealth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Forbes_Celebrity_100"><i>Forbes</i> Celebrity 100</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Forbes Celebrity 100">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><sup id="cite_ref-Western_world_kids_want_to_grow_up_to_be_famous_Children_in_developing_countries_want_to_be_doctors_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Western_world_kids_want_to_grow_up_to_be_famous_Children_in_developing_countries_want_to_be_doctors-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> Magazine releases an annual <a href="/wiki/Forbes_Celebrity_100" title="Forbes Celebrity 100"><i>Forbes</i> Celebrity 100</a> list of the highest paid celebrities in the world. The total earnings for all top celebrity 100 earners totaled $4.5 billion over the course of 2010 alone.
</p><p>For instance, Forbes ranked <a href="/wiki/Media_mogul" class="mw-redirect" title="Media mogul">media mogul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Talk_show_host" class="mw-redirect" title="Talk show host">talk show host</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" title="Oprah Winfrey">Oprah Winfrey</a> as the top earner "Forbes magazine’s annual ranking of the most powerful celebrities", with earnings of $290 million in the past year. Forbes cites that <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a> reportedly earned over $90 million in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-Lady_Gaga_Tops_Celebrity_100_List_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lady_Gaga_Tops_Celebrity_100_List-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, golfer <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Woods" title="Tiger Woods">Tiger Woods</a> was one of highest-earning celebrity athletes, with an income of $74 million and is consistently ranked one of the highest paid <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" class="mw-redirect" title="Sportsperson">athletes</a> in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-Lady_Gaga_Tops_Celebrity_100_List_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lady_Gaga_Tops_Celebrity_100_List-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, Madonna was ranked as the fifth most powerful and the highest earning celebrity of the year with earnings of $125 million. She has consistently been among the most powerful and highest earning celebrities in the world, occupying the third place in Forbes Celebrity 100 2009 with $110 million of earnings, and getting the tenth place in the 2011 edition of the list with annual earnings equal to $58 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Madonna_highest_earning_celebrity_of_2013_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Madonna_highest_earning_celebrity_of_2013-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Celebrity endorsements have proven very successful around the world where, due to increasing <a href="/wiki/Consumerism" title="Consumerism">consumerism</a>, an individual is considered to own a status symbol when they purchase a celebrity-endorsed product<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. Although it has become commonplace for celebrities to place their name with endorsements onto products just for quick money, some celebrities have gone beyond merely using their names and have put their entrepreneurial spirit to work by becoming entrepreneurs by attaching themselves in the business aspects of entertainment and building their own business brand beyond their traditional salaried activities. Along with investing their salaried wages into growing business endeavors, a number of celebrities have become innovative business leaders in their respective industries, gaining the admiration of their peers and contributing to the country's economy.
</p><p>Numerous celebrities have ventured into becoming <a href="/wiki/Business_mogul" class="mw-redirect" title="Business mogul">business moguls</a> and established themselves as entrepreneurs, idolizing many well known American business leaders such as <a href="/wiki/Bill_Gates" title="Bill Gates">Bill Gates</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warren_Buffett" title="Warren Buffett">Warren Buffett</a>. For instance, basketball legend <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jordan" title="Michael Jordan">Michael Jordan</a> became an active entrepreneur involved with many sports related ventures including investing a minority stake in the <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Bobcats" class="mw-redirect" title="Charlotte Bobcats">Charlotte Bobcats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Newman" title="Paul Newman">Paul Newman</a> started his own <a href="/wiki/Salad_dressing" class="mw-redirect" title="Salad dressing">salad dressing</a> business after leaving behind a distinguished acting career, and rap musician <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(rapper)" title="Birdman (rapper)">Birdman</a> started his own <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>, clothing line, and an <a href="/wiki/Oil_well" title="Oil well">oil business</a> while maintaining a career as a rap artist. Brazilian football legend and World Cup winner <a href="/wiki/Ronaldo_(Brazilian_footballer)" title="Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)">Ronaldo</a> became the majority owner of La Liga club <a href="/wiki/Real_Valladolid" title="Real Valladolid">Real Valladolid</a> in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Other celebrities such as <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Perry" title="Tyler Perry">Tyler Perry</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a>, and Steven Spielberg have become successful entrepreneurs through starting their own film production companies and running their own <a href="/wiki/Movie_studio" class="mw-redirect" title="Movie studio">movie studios</a> beyond their traditional activities of screenwriting, directing, animating, producing, and acting.<sup id="cite_ref-Paul_Newman_Donates_Salad_Dressing_Ownership_To_Charity_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paul_Newman_Donates_Salad_Dressing_Ownership_To_Charity-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Tabloid magazines and talk TV shows bestow a great deal of attention on celebrities. To stay in the public eye and build wealth in addition to their salaried labor, numerous celebrities have participating and branching into various business ventures and endorsements. Many celebrities have participated in many different endorsement opportunities that include: animation, publishing, fashion designing, cosmetics, <a href="/wiki/Consumer_electronics" title="Consumer electronics">consumer electronics</a>, household items and <a href="/wiki/Home_appliance" title="Home appliance">appliances</a>, cigarettes, <a href="/wiki/Soft_drink" title="Soft drink">soft drinks</a> and alcoholic beverages, hair care, hairdressing, <a href="/wiki/Jewelry_design" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewelry design">jewelry design</a>, fast food, credit cards, video games, writing, and toys.<sup id="cite_ref-Best_And_Worst_Celebrity_Side_Businesses_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Best_And_Worst_Celebrity_Side_Businesses-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to various endorsements, a number of celebrities have been involved with some business and investment related ventures also include:  and toddler related items, <a href="/wiki/List_of_professional_sports_team_owners" title="List of professional sports team owners">sports team ownership</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fashion_design" title="Fashion design">fashion retailing</a>, establishments such as restaurants, cafes, hotels, and casinos, movie theaters, advertising and event planning, management related ventures such as <a href="/wiki/Sports_management" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports management">sports management</a>, financial services, <a href="/wiki/Modeling_agency" title="Modeling agency">model management</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Talent_management" title="Talent management">talent management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record labels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film_production" class="mw-redirect" title="Film production">film production</a>, <a href="/wiki/Television_producer" title="Television producer">television production</a>, publishing such as <a href="/wiki/Book_publisher" class="mw-redirect" title="Book publisher">book</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music_publisher_(popular_music)" title="Music publisher (popular music)">music publishing</a>, massage therapy, <a href="/wiki/Beauty_salon" title="Beauty salon">salons</a>, health and fitness, and real estate.<sup id="cite_ref-Best_And_Worst_Celebrity_Side_Businesses_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Best_And_Worst_Celebrity_Side_Businesses-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although some celebrities have achieved additional financial success from various business ventures, the vast majority of celebrities are not successful businesspeople and still rely on salaried labored wages in order to earn a living. Most businesses and investments are well known to have a 90 to 95 percent failure rate within the first five years of operation. Not all celebrities eventually succeed with their own businesses and other related side ventures. Some celebrities either went <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/broke" class="extiw" title="wikt:broke">broke</a> or filed for bankruptcy as result of dabbling with such side businesses or endorsements. Though some might question such a validity since celebrities themselves are already well known, have mass appeal, and are well exposed to the general public. The average entrepreneur who is not well known and reputable to general public does not the same marketing flexibility and status-quo as most celebrities allow and have. Therefore, compared to the average person who starts a business, celebrities already have all the cards and odds stacked in their favor. This means they can have an unfair advantage to expose their business ventures and endorsements and can easily capture a more significant amount of market share than the average entrepreneur.<sup id="cite_ref-7_Most_Embarrassing_Celebrity_Business_Failures_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7_Most_Embarrassing_Celebrity_Business_Failures-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Celebrities often have fame comparable to <a href="/wiki/Royal_family" title="Royal family">royalty</a>. As a result, there is a strong public curiosity about their private affairs. The release of <a href="/wiki/Kim_Kardashian" title="Kim Kardashian">Kim Kardashian</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_sex_tape" title="Celebrity sex tape">sex tape</a> with rapper <a href="/wiki/Ray_J" title="Ray J">Ray J</a> in 2003 brought her to a new level of fame, leading to magazine covers, book deals, and <a href="/wiki/Keeping_Up_with_the_Kardashians" title="Keeping Up with the Kardashians">reality TV</a> series.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vivid_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vivid-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Celebrities may be resented for their accolades, and the public may have a love/hate relationship with celebrities. Due to the high visibility of celebrities' private lives, their successes and shortcomings are often made very public. Celebrities are alternately portrayed as glowing examples of perfection, when they garner awards, or as decadent or immoral if they become associated with a scandal. When seen in a positive light, celebrities are frequently portrayed as possessing skills and abilities beyond average people; for example, celebrity actors are routinely celebrated for acquiring new skills necessary for filming a role within a very brief time, and to a level that amazes the professionals who train them. Similarly, some celebrities with very little formal education can sometimes be portrayed as experts on complicated issues. Some celebrities have been very vocal with their political views. For example, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a> expressed his displeasure with 2008 US vice presidential nominee <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Palin" title="Sarah Palin">Sarah Palin</a>, as well as with the 2011 <a href="/wiki/United_States_debt-ceiling_crisis_of_2011" title="United States debt-ceiling crisis of 2011">United States debt-ceiling crisis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Famous_for_being_famous">Famous for being famous</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Famous for being famous">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Famous_for_being_famous" title="Famous for being famous">Famous for being famous</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Paris_Hilton_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Paris_Hilton_2009.jpg/220px-Paris_Hilton_2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Paris_Hilton_2009.jpg/330px-Paris_Hilton_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Paris_Hilton_2009.jpg/440px-Paris_Hilton_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1380" data-file-height="2070" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Paris_Hilton_2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>American <a href="/wiki/Socialite" title="Socialite">socialite</a> <a href="/wiki/Paris_Hilton" title="Paris Hilton">Paris Hilton</a> was one of the first celebrities to be described as '<a href="#Famous_for_being_famous">famous for being famous</a>', she has since expanded her brand into a  multibillion-dollar empire</div></div></div>
<p>Famous for being famous, in <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">popular culture</a> terminology, refers to someone who attains celebrity status for no particular identifiable reason, or who achieves fame through association with a celebrity.<sup id="cite_ref-Jenkins_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jenkins-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>  The term is a <a href="/wiki/Pejorative" title="Pejorative">pejorative</a>, suggesting that the individual has no particular talents or abilities.<sup id="cite_ref-JenJones_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JenJones-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Even when their fame arises from a particular talent or action on their part, the term will sometimes still apply if their fame is perceived as disproportionate to what they earned through their own talent or work.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The coinages "<a href="/wiki/Famesque" class="mw-redirect" title="Famesque">famesque</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Celebutante" class="mw-redirect" title="Celebutante">celebutante</a>" are of similar pejorative gist.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Internet_celebrities">Internet celebrities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Internet celebrities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Also known as being <i><a href="/wiki/Internet_celebrity" title="Internet celebrity">internet famous</a></i>, contemporary fame does not always involve a physical red carpet.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_fame_in_Asia">Online fame in Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Online fame in Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A report by <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> highlighted a longtime trend of Asian internet celebrities such as <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a> celebrity <a href="/wiki/Wang_Hong" title="Wang Hong">Wang Hong</a> (birth name Ling Ling).<sup id="cite_ref-Chinese_Internet_Fame_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chinese_Internet_Fame-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> According to BBC, there are two kinds of online celebrities in China—those who create original content, such as <a href="/wiki/Papi_Jiang" title="Papi Jiang">Papi Jiang</a>, who is regularly censored by Chinese authorities for cussing in her videos, and those such as Wang Hong and Zhang Dayi, who fall under the second category, as they have clothing and cosmetics businesses on Taobao, China's equivalent of <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Chinese_Internet_Fame_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chinese_Internet_Fame-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_networking_and_video_hosting">Social networking and video hosting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Social networking and video hosting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most high-profile celebrities participate in <a href="/wiki/Social_networking" class="mw-redirect" title="Social networking">social networking</a> and photo or video hosting platforms such as <a href="/wiki/List_of_YouTubers" title="List of YouTubers">YouTube</a>, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, and Snapchat.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Social networking sites allow celebrities to communicate directly with their fans, removing the middle-man known as traditional media. Social media humanizes celebrities in a way that arouses public fascination as evident by the success of magazines such as <i><a href="/wiki/Us_Weekly" title="Us Weekly">Us Weekly</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)" title="People (magazine)">People Weekly</a></i>. Celebrity blogging have also spawned stars such as <a href="/wiki/Perez_Hilton" title="Perez Hilton">Perez Hilton</a> who is well known for not only blogging, but also <a href="/wiki/Outing" title="Outing">outing</a> celebrities.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Social media and the rise of the smartphone has changed how celebrities are treated and how people gain the platform of fame. Not everything is as concealed as it was back in old Hollywood because now everything is put out on the internet by fans or even the celebrity themselves. Websites like Twitter, Facebook, Instagram and YouTube allow people to become a celebrity overnight. For example, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a> got his start on YouTube by posting videos of him singing and got discovered. All of his fans got direct contact to his content and were able to interact with him on several social media platforms. Social media has substantially changed what it means to be a celebrity. Instagram and YouTube gives regular people an opportunity to become rich and famous all from inside their home. It also gives fans the opportunity to connect with their favorite celebrity without ever meeting them in person. Everything is being shared on social media so it makes it harder for celebrities to live private lives.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Social media sites have also contributed to the fame of some celebrities, such as <a href="/wiki/Tila_Tequila" title="Tila Tequila">Tila Tequila</a> who became known through MySpace.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Families">Families</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Families">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Another example of celebrity is a family that has notable ancestors or is known for its wealth. In some cases, a well-known family is associated with a particular field. For example, the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_family" title="Kennedy family">Kennedy family</a> is associated with US politics; The <a href="/wiki/House_of_Windsor" title="House of Windsor">House of Windsor</a> with royalty; The <a href="/wiki/Hilton_family" title="Hilton family">Hilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rothschild_family" title="Rothschild family">Rothschild</a> families with business; the <a href="/wiki/Jackson_family" title="Jackson family">Jackson family</a> with popular music; and the <a href="/wiki/The_Osbournes" title="The Osbournes">Osbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chaplin_family" title="Chaplin family">Chaplin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keeping_Up_with_the_Kardashians" title="Keeping Up with the Kardashians">Kardashian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_family" title="Baldwin family">Baldwin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Barrymore_family" title="Barrymore family">Barrymore</a> families with television and film.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Restricted_access">Restricted access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Restricted access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Access to celebrities is strictly controlled by their entourage of staff which includes <a href="/wiki/Talent_manager" title="Talent manager">managers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Publicist" title="Publicist">publicists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talent_agent" title="Talent agent">agents</a>, <a href="/wiki/Personal_assistant" title="Personal assistant">personal assistants</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bodyguard" title="Bodyguard">bodyguards</a>. Even journalists find it difficult to access celebrities for interviews. An interview with writer and actor <a href="/wiki/Michael_Musto" title="Michael Musto">Michael Musto</a> cites:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>You have to go through many hoops just to talk to a major celebrity. You have to get past three different sets of publicists: the publicist for the event, the publicist for the movie, and then the celebrity's personal publicist. They all have to approve you.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite><a href="/wiki/Michael_Musto" title="Michael Musto">Michael Musto</a><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>Celebrities often hire one or more bodyguards (or close protection officer) to protect themselves and their families from threats ranging from the mundane (intrusive <a href="/wiki/Paparazzi" title="Paparazzi">paparazzi photographers</a> or <a href="/wiki/Autograph" title="Autograph">autograph</a>-seeking <a href="/wiki/Fan_(person)" title="Fan (person)">fans</a>) to serious (<a href="/wiki/Assault" title="Assault">assault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kidnapping" title="Kidnapping">kidnapping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assassination" title="Assassination">assassination</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Stalking" title="Stalking">stalking</a>). The bodyguard travels with the celebrity during professional activities (movie shoots or concerts) and personal activities such as recreation and errands.
</p><p>Celebrities also typically have security staff at their home, to protect them from similar threats.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Celebrity-stalking_has_common_threads_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Celebrity-stalking_has_common_threads-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cult_of_celebrity">Cult of celebrity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Cult of celebrity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="15_minutes_of_fame">15 minutes of fame</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: 15 minutes of fame">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/15_minutes_of_fame" title="15 minutes of fame">15 minutes of fame</a> and <a href="/wiki/One-hit_wonder" title="One-hit wonder">One-hit wonder</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Andy_Warhol" title="Andy Warhol">Andy Warhol</a> famously coined the phrase "<a href="/wiki/15_minutes_of_fame" title="15 minutes of fame">15 minutes of fame</a>" in reference to a short-lived publicity. Certain "15 minutes of fame" celebrities can be average people seen with an A-list celebrity, who are sometimes noticed on entertainment news channels such as <a href="/wiki/E!_News" title="E! News">E! News</a>. These persons are ordinary people becoming celebrities, often based on the ridiculous things they do.  "In fact, many reality show contestants fall into this category: the only thing that qualifies them to be on TV is that they're real."<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Certain people are only remembered today because of a movie portrayal, certain story or <a href="/wiki/Urban_legend" title="Urban legend">urban legend</a> surrounding their life and less for their accomplishments. <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Salieri" title="Antonio Salieri">Antonio Salieri</a> was a famous and well-known 18th-century composer, but his fictional portrayal as an <a href="/wiki/Antagonist" title="Antagonist">antagonist</a> (for example, in the musical and film <i><a href="/wiki/Amadeus_(film)" title="Amadeus (film)">Amadeus</a></i>) has been more famous than his music since the end of the 20th century. <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_%22Fatty%22_Arbuckle" class="mw-redirect" title="Roscoe &quot;Fatty&quot; Arbuckle">Roscoe "Fatty" Arbuckle</a> and <a href="/wiki/O._J._Simpson" title="O. J. Simpson">O. J. Simpson</a> are more notorious for their association with murder trials than for their respective movie and sports careers. Centuries after his death, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Mantegna" title="Andrea Mantegna">Andrea Mantegna</a> is now better known as the mentor of <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci" title="Leonardo da Vinci">Leonardo da Vinci</a> than for his own paintings.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_implications">Health implications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celebrity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Health implications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/John_Cleese" title="John Cleese">John Cleese</a> said being famous offers some advantages such as financial wealth and easier access to things that are more difficult for non-famous people to access, such as the ability to more easily meet other famous or powerful people, but that being famous also often comes with the disadvantage of creating the conditions in which the celebrity finds themselves acting, at least temporarily (although sometimes over extended periods of time), in a superficial, inauthentic fashion.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Common threats such as <a href="/wiki/Stalking" title="Stalking">stalking</a> have spawned <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_worship_syndrome" title="Celebrity worship syndrome">celebrity worship syndrome</a> where a person becomes overly involved with the details of a celebrity's personal life.<sup id="cite_ref-Schumaker_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schumaker-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>    Psychologists have indicated that though many people obsess over glamorous film, television, sport and music stars, the disparity in salaries in society seems to value <a href="/wiki/Professional_athlete" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional athlete">professional athletes</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_entertainment_industry_topics" class="mw-redirect" title="List of entertainment industry topics">entertainment industry based professionals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> One study found that singers, musicians, actors and athletes die younger on average than writers, composers, academics, politicians and businesspeople, with a greater incidence of cancer and especially lung cancer. However, it was remarked that the reasons for this remained unclear, with theories including <a href="/wiki/Innate" class="mw-redirect" title="Innate">innate</a> tendencies towards risk-taking as well as the pressure or opportunities of particular types of fame.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Furthermore, some have said fame might have negative <a href="/wiki/Psychological" class="mw-redirect" title="Psychological">psychological</a> effects, and may lead to increasingly selfish tendencies and psychopathy.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (March 2019)">vague</span></a></i>&#93;</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (March 2019)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Recently, there has been more attention toward the impact celebrities have on health decisions of the population at large.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> It is believed that the public will follow celebrities' health advice to some extent.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> This can have positive impacts when the celebrities give solid, evidence informed health advice, however it can also have detrimental affects if the health advice is not accurate enough.
</p>
