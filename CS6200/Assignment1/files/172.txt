	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Wilson (name)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilson_(surname)&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilson (surname)">Wilson (surname)</a>)</span></div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the name. For people who share this surname, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_people_with_surname_Wilson" title="List of people with surname Wilson">List of people with surname Wilson</a>. For people who share this given name, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_people_with_given_name_Wilson" title="List of people with given name Wilson">List of people with given name Wilson</a>. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Wilson_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Wilson (disambiguation)">Wilson (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Wilson</b> is an English and Scottish surname, common in the <a href="/wiki/English-speaking_world" title="English-speaking world">English-speaking world</a>. The name is derived from a <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> form of <i>Will</i>, a popular medieval name. The medieval <i>Will</i> is derived from any of several names containing the first <a href="/wiki/Germanic_languages" title="Germanic languages">Germanic</a> element <i>wil</i>, meaning "desire".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Possibly the most common of these names was <i><a href="/wiki/William_(name)" class="mw-redirect" title="William (name)">William</a></i>, derived from elements <i>wil</i> and <i>helm</i>, meaning "desire" and "helmet", "protection".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The surname <i>Wilson</i> is first recorded in England as <i>Willeson</i> in 1324，<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and in Scotland as <i>Wulson</i> in 1405.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is the seventh most common surname in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and tenth most common in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>, occurring 783,051 times as of 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Wilson is also now quite common as a surname in many other countries with a large English-speaking population such as <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.
</p><p>Wilson is the third most common surname in Scotland. <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the sixteenth and seventeenth century the surname was greatly increased in Ulster by the thousands of Scottish settlers and as a result of this settlement it is the most common Surname in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>   
</p><p><a href="/wiki/List_of_people_with_given_name_Wilson" title="List of people with given name Wilson">Wilson</a> can also be a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>. 
</p>
