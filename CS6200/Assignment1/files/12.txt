	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Lusophone</h1>

Lusophone countries are a subset of those where Portuguese is the main native language. <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #015A01; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Native language</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #339900; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Official and administrative language</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #98FC66; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Cultural or secondary language</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #00FF00; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Portuguese speaking minorities</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #FFFF40; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Portuguese-based creole</div></div></div></div>
<p><b>Lusophones</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">Lusófonos</i>) are an <a href="/wiki/Ethnolinguistic_group" title="Ethnolinguistic group">ethno-linguistic group</a> of peoples and nations across the world that speak the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese language</a>. The <b>Lusofonia</b>, also known as the <b>Lusophone World</b> (<i>Mundo Lusófono</i>), is the corresponding community of Lusophone nations, which exist in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>. Lusophone nations make up more than 270 million people globally, 
</p><p>The history of the Lusophone World is intrinsically linked with the history of the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Empire" title="Portuguese Empire">Portuguese Empire</a>, although <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_diaspora" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese diaspora">Portuguese diaspora</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_diaspora" title="Brazilian diaspora">Brazilian diaspora</a> communities have also played a role in spreading the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese language</a> and Lusophone culture. Today, Portuguese-speaking nations of the world have come together for cooperation in politics, culture, and the economy, through the <a href="/wiki/Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Community of Portuguese Language Countries">Community of Portuguese Language Countries</a> (CPLP), also known as the Lusophone Commonwealth.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lusophone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term <i>Lusophone</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Classical_compound" title="Classical compound">classical compound</a>, whereby the <a href="/wiki/Combining_form" class="mw-redirect" title="Combining form">combining form</a> "Luso-" derives from the Latin term for an area roughly corresponding to modern Portugal, called <a href="/wiki/Lusitania" title="Lusitania">Lusitania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The suffix "-phone" derives from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient Greek language">Ancient Greek</a> word <span lang="el" title="Greek language text"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%86%CF%89%CE%BD%CE%AE#Ancient_Greek" class="extiw" title="wikt:φωνή">φωνή</a></span> (<i>phōnē</i>), meaning "voice". The use of the term Lusophone mirrors similar terms, such as <a href="/wiki/English-speaking" class="mw-redirect" title="English-speaking">Anglophone</a> for English-speakers, <a href="/wiki/Francophone" class="mw-redirect" title="Francophone">Francophone</a> for French-speakers, <a href="/wiki/Hispanophone" title="Hispanophone">Hispanophone</a> for Spanish-speakers, and <a href="/wiki/Sinophone" title="Sinophone">Sinophone</a> for Chinese-speakers. The term is sometimes used in reference to the <a href="/wiki/Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Community of Portuguese Language Countries">Community of Portuguese Language Countries</a>, similar to the <a href="/wiki/Francophonie" title="Francophonie">Francophonie</a>.
</p>

<p>Notes:
</p>
<ol><li>Some <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">linguists</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_Lindley_Cintra" title="Luís Lindley Cintra">Lindley Cintra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teixeira_de_Pascoaes" title="Teixeira de Pascoaes">Teixeira de Pascoaes</a> argue that <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">Galician</a>, spoken in <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a>, is merely a dialect of Portuguese rather than an independent language; this would make northwestern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> a part of the Portuguese-speaking world.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a> is not a sovereign nation. It is one of the two <a href="/wiki/Special_administrative_regions_of_China" title="Special administrative regions of China">Special Administrative Regions of the People's Republic of China</a> (the other being <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">Anglophone</a> <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, a former <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> colony).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a> adopted Portuguese as one of its official languages in 2007, being admitted to CPLP in 2014. The use of the Portuguese language in this country is limited. However, a Portuguese-based creole language, <a href="/wiki/Annobonese_Creole" title="Annobonese Creole">Annobonese Creole</a>, is used, mainly on islands of <a href="/wiki/Annobon" class="mw-redirect" title="Annobon">Annobon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bioko" title="Bioko">Bioko</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Uruguayan_Portuguese" title="Uruguayan Portuguese">15% of Uruguay's population speaks Portuguese</a> (in the northern regions near Brazil) as a <a href="/wiki/Native_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Native language">native language</a> though it is not an <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official language</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This makes Portuguese the second most spoken language of the country. A number of Uruguayans living near the Brazilian border also speak a mixture of Spanish and Portuguese called <a href="/wiki/Riverense_Portu%C3%B1ol_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Riverense Portuñol language">Portuñol</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> A similar blending of Portuguese, Spanish, and <a href="/wiki/Guarani_language" title="Guarani language">Guarani</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jopara_language" title="Jopara language">Jopara</a>) occurs along the border with <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>.</li></ol>
