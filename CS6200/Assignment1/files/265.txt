	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Fowler (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For uses of "Fowler" other than as a surname, see <a href="/wiki/Fowler_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Fowler (disambiguation)">Fowler (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Fowler</b> is an English and/or Scots <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>. Its origin is the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i lang="ang" title="Old English language text">fugelere</i>, an occupational name for a bird-catcher or hunter of wild birds.<sup id="cite_ref-FowlerNewfoundland_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FowlerNewfoundland-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ancestry1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ancestry1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SurnameDB_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SurnameDB-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Old English <i lang="ang" title="Old English language text">fugel</i> or <i lang="ang" title="Old English language text">fugol</i> means "bird".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fowler_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The "Fowler" surname evolved from an original use of "<span lang="ang" style="font-style:normal;" title="Old English language text">Fugelere</span>" in the early 13th Century.<sup id="cite_ref-SurnameDB_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SurnameDB-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The surname is uncommon in the United States, appearing with a rank of 250 in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_1990" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Census, 1990">1990 Census</a> and a rank of 267 in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2000" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Census, 2000">2000 Census</a>, 27½% of the American population being accounted for surnames in the ranks of 1 to 250.<sup id="cite_ref-Ancestry2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ancestry2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  In 19th Century England, "Fowler" was widespread, appearing in 35 of the 39 <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">historic counties</a>, with higher density in the north of England, in the <a href="/wiki/Census_in_the_United_Kingdom#UK_Census_dates" title="Census in the United Kingdom">1891 Census of England and Wales</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ancestry3_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ancestry3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  Meanwhile, in 19th Century United States, "Fowler" appears in every surveyed state in both the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_1880" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Census, 1880">1880 US Census</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_1840" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Census, 1840">1840 US Census</a>, showing a higher concentration in <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> state in each case.<sup id="cite_ref-Ancestry4_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ancestry4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ancestry5_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ancestry5-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
