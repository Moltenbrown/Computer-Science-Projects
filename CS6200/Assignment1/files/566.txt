		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Family Day</b> is a public <a href="/wiki/Holiday" title="Holiday">holiday</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Provinces of Canada">Canadian provinces</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>,  <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> and soon <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> states of <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu" title="Vanuatu">Vanuatu</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Capital_Territory" title="Australian Capital Territory">Australian Capital Territory</a>, and as the second day of <a href="/wiki/Songkran" title="Songkran">Songkran</a> in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australian_Capital_Territory">Australian Capital Territory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Australian Capital Territory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Family and Community Day</i> was celebrated on the first Tuesday of November from 2007–2009, coinciding with the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Cup" title="Melbourne Cup">Melbourne Cup</a>. This public holiday was declared in 2007. <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Barr" title="Andrew Barr">Andrew Barr</a>, then <a href="/wiki/Australian_Capital_Territory" title="Australian Capital Territory">ACT</a> Minister for Industrial Relations, stated the purpose of the new public holiday was:
</p><p>"...to enable workers to take a break from their hectic work and to spend some quality time with their family and friends. ... Australians do work the longest hours of any country in the western world. We do deserve a break."<sup id="cite_ref-HansardLegislative2007_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HansardLegislative2007-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the years 2010–2017, the date was moved to the first Monday of the September/October school holidays. Where the first Monday of the school holidays falls on the ACT Labour Day <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Australia" title="Public holidays in Australia">public holiday</a> (first Monday in October), the holiday is moved to the second Monday of the ACT school holidays.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Family and Community Day will no longer be a public holiday in the ACT. Replacing it is <a href="/wiki/Reconciliation_Day" title="Reconciliation Day">Reconciliation Day</a>, held on the first Monday on or after May 27.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Family_Day_(Canada)" title="Family Day (Canada)">Family Day (Canada)</a></div>
<p>Nearly two-thirds of Canadians reside in a province that observes a statutory holiday on the third Monday of February. The holiday is called <i>Family Day</i> in five provinces (Alberta, British Columbia, New Brunswick, Ontario and Saskatchewan), <i>Louis Riel Day</i> in Manitoba, and <i>Islander Day</i> in Prince Edward Island. There is no federally established Family Day.
</p><p>This holiday was first observed in Alberta in 1990, followed by Saskatchewan in 2007 and Ontario in 2008. On May 28, 2012, the BC government announced that Family Day would be observed on the second Monday in February each year, starting February 11, 2013.  <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/departments/post-secondary_education_training_and_labour/news/news_release.2017.04.0568.html">https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/departments/post-secondary_education_training_and_labour/news/news_release.2017.04.0568.html</a> The week of its observance was changed from the second Monday of February to the third Monday starting in 2019. In April 2017, the NB government announced the family statutory holiday to begin in February 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-FamilyDayBCGov_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FamilyDayBCGov-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In February 2018, the BC government announced that Family Day would move to the third Monday in February from 2019 onward, aligning the holiday with other Canadian provinces.<sup id="cite_ref-FamilyDayBCGovChange_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FamilyDayBCGovChange-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a new holiday, there are no traditional activities that are associated with the day. Canadians use the long weekend for whatever purpose they like.  In at least British Columbia, the decision to add this public holiday is due to the lack of any public holiday between lengthy gap between the New Year's day and Good Friday public holidays.  Otherwise, Canadian provinces have a public holiday at least every other month.
</p><p>Family Day is unrelated to <a href="/wiki/National_Family_Week" title="National Family Week">National Family Week</a>, a campaign put on each October by the Canadian Association of Family Resource Programs.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After 1995, <a href="/wiki/Easter_Monday" title="Easter Monday">Easter Monday</a> was renamed Family Day.<sup id="cite_ref-PublicHolidays1994_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PublicHolidays1994-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-About2006_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About2006-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Family Day</b> is a state holiday in <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>, celebrated on the day after <a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving" title="Thanksgiving">Thanksgiving</a>, i.e. the Friday following the fourth Thursday in November.<sup id="cite_ref-NRS_236.015_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NRS_236.015-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/American_Family_Day" title="American Family Day">American Family Day</a> is a state holiday in <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a> and has been celebrated on the first Sunday in August since 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-AmericanFamily_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AmericanFamily-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uruguay">Uruguay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Uruguay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, the public holiday on December 25 is officially known as Family Day (<i lang="es" title="Spanish language text">Día de la Familia</i>) rather than <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>, for the sake of <a href="/wiki/Secularism" title="Secularism">secularism</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vanuatu">Vanuatu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Vanuatu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Family Day in <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu" title="Vanuatu">Vanuatu</a> is celebrated annually on December 26, traditionally <a href="/wiki/Boxing_Day" title="Boxing Day">Boxing Day</a>, as a day on which school and work are suspended in order to spend the day giving thanks for and enjoying time with one's family, often by engaging in civic and religious events and a festive meal.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vietnam">Vietnam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Family_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Vietnam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vietnamese Family Day (<a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese</a>: <i lang="vi">Ngày gia đình Việt Nam</i>) occurs on the 28th of June each year, having been established in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Family-oriented activities and events are organised throughout the country, though it is not a public holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
