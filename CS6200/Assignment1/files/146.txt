	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Hansen (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Hansen</b> or <b>Hanssen</b> (<small>Danish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Danish" title="Help:IPA/Danish">[ˈhænsn̩]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavian</a> <a href="/wiki/Patronymic_surname" title="Patronymic surname">patronymic surname</a>, meaning <i>son of <a href="/wiki/Hans_(name)" title="Hans (name)">Hans</a></i>. As of 2008, it is the third most common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, shared by 4.3% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-dst_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dst-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As of 2000, <i>Hansen</i> is the single most common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, not counting spelling variations such as <i>Hanssen</i>, which are also quite common.<sup id="cite_ref-ssb_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ssb-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a> <i>Hansen</i> is the second most common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>, while in the <a href="/wiki/North_German" class="mw-redirect" title="North German">North German</a> <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">federal states</a> of <a href="/wiki/Schleswig-Holstein" title="Schleswig-Holstein">Schleswig-Holstein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> <i>Hansen</i> is the third and fifth most common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-verwandt_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-verwandt-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
In Sweden the parallel form is <i><a href="/wiki/Hanson_(surname)" title="Hanson (surname)">Hansson</a></i>. The frequent occurrences of Hansen as a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> outside <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-navneforskning_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-navneforskning-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Schleswig-Holstein" title="Schleswig-Holstein">Schleswig-Holstein</a> is due to <a href="/wiki/Immigration" title="Immigration">immigration</a>, though Nordic immigrants to <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>-speaking countries often <a href="/wiki/Anglicisation_of_names" title="Anglicisation of names">anglicised their names</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hanson_(surname)" title="Hanson (surname)">Hanson</a> in order to accommodate <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English orthographic rules</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_distribution">Geographical distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hansen_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geographical distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2014, 33.5% of all known bearers of the surname <i>Hansen</i> were residents of <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> (frequency 1:28), 31.6% of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> (1:1,933), 10.5% of <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> (1:83), 10.2% of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> (1:1,330), 2.3% of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> (1:2,658), 2.3% of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> (1:1,773), 1.3% of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> (1:1,325) and 1.2% of <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> (1:7,460).
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, the frequency of the surname was higher than national average (1:28) in the following regions:
</p>
<ul><li>1. <a href="/wiki/Region_Zealand" title="Region Zealand">Region Zealand</a> (1:19)</li>
<li>2. <a href="/wiki/Region_of_Southern_Denmark" title="Region of Southern Denmark">Region of Southern Denmark</a> (1:22)</li></ul>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, the frequency of the surname was higher than national average (1:83) in the following regions:<sup id="cite_ref-hansen2014_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hansen2014-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>1. <a href="/wiki/Northern_Norway" title="Northern Norway">Northern Norway</a> (1:35)</li>
<li>2. <a href="/wiki/Svalbard_and_Jan_Mayen" title="Svalbard and Jan Mayen">Svalbard and Jan Mayen</a> (1:42)</li>
<li>3. <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Norway" title="Eastern Norway">Eastern Norway</a> (1:79)</li>
<li>4. <a href="/wiki/Southern_Norway" title="Southern Norway">Southern Norway</a> (1:82)</li></ul>
