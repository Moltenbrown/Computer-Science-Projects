<p>The name <b>Hamilton</b> probably originated in the village of <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Leicestershire" class="mw-redirect" title="Hamilton, Leicestershire">Hamilton, Leicestershire</a>, England,<sup id="cite_ref-Reaney_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reaney-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HH_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HH-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Cottle_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cottle-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> but bearers of that name became established in the 13th century in <a href="/wiki/Lanarkshire" title="Lanarkshire">Lanarkshire</a>, Scotland.<sup id="cite_ref-Cottle_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cottle-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The town of <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_South_Lanarkshire" title="Hamilton, South Lanarkshire">Hamilton, South Lanarkshire</a> was named after the family some time before 1445.<sup id="cite_ref-Dorward_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dorward-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Whyte_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Whyte-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Contemporary Hamiltons are either descended from the original noble family, or descended from people named after the town.<sup id="cite_ref-HH_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HH-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dorward_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dorward-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
