	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Argead dynasty</h1>

</p><p><a href="/wiki/King_of_Asia" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Asia">King of Asia</a>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Pharaoh_of_Egypt" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharaoh of Egypt">Pharaoh of Egypt</a>
</p>
<a href="/wiki/League_of_Corinth" title="League of Corinth">Hegemon of the Hellenic League,
Strategus Autokrator of Greece</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Religion</th><td><a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek_Religion" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient Greek Religion">Ancient Greek Religion</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Estate(s)</th><td><a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(ancient_kingdom)" title="Macedonia (ancient kingdom)">Macedonia</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Dissolution</th><td>310 BC</td></tr></tbody></table><p>The <b>Argead dynasty</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="grc" title="Ancient Greek language text">Ἀργεάδαι</span>, <i lang="grc-Latn" title="Ancient Greek language text">Argeádai</i>) was an <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Macedonians" title="Ancient Macedonians">ancient Macedonian</a> royal house claiming <a href="/wiki/Dorians" title="Dorians">Dorian Greek</a> provenance.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> They were the founders and the ruling <a href="/wiki/Dynasty" title="Dynasty">dynasty</a> of the kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(ancient_kingdom)" title="Macedonia (ancient kingdom)">Macedon</a> from about 700 to 310 BC.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Their tradition, as described in <a href="/wiki/Greek_historiography" class="mw-redirect" title="Greek historiography">ancient Greek historiography</a>, traced their origins to <a href="/wiki/Argos" title="Argos">Argos</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Peloponnese_(region)" title="Peloponnese (region)">Peloponnese</a>, hence the name <i>Argeads</i> or <i>Argives</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Initially the rulers of the homonymous tribe,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> by the time of Philip II they had expanded their reign further, to include under the rule of <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(ancient_kingdom)" title="Macedonia (ancient kingdom)">Macedonia</a> all <a href="/wiki/Upper_Macedonia" title="Upper Macedonia">Upper Macedonian</a> states. The family's most celebrated members were <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Macedon" title="Philip II of Macedon">Philip II of Macedon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a>, under whose leadership the kingdom of Macedonia gradually gained predominance throughout Greece, defeated the <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">Achaemenid Empire</a> and expanded as far as <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. The mythical founder of the Argead dynasty is King <a href="/wiki/Caranus_of_Macedon" title="Caranus of Macedon">Caranus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The words "Argead" and "Argive" derive (via <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>Argīvus</i><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>) from the <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> Ἀργεῖος (<i>Argeios</i>), "of or from <a href="/wiki/Argos" title="Argos">Argos</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> which is first attested in <a href="/wiki/Homer" title="Homer">Homer</a>, where it was also used as a collective designation for the Greeks ("Ἀργείων Δαναῶν", <i>Argive <a href="/wiki/Danaans" class="mw-redirect" title="Danaans">Danaans</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The Argead dynasty claimed descent from the <a href="/wiki/Temenids" class="mw-redirect" title="Temenids">Temenids</a> of Argos, in the <a href="/wiki/Peloponnese" title="Peloponnese">Peloponnese</a>, whose legendary ancestor was <a href="/wiki/Temenus" title="Temenus">Temenus</a>, the great-great-grandson of <a href="/wiki/Heracles" title="Heracles">Heracles</a>.
</p><p>In the excavations of the royal Palace at <a href="/wiki/Vergina" title="Vergina">Aegae</a> Manolis Andronikos discovered in the "tholos" room (according to some scholars "tholos" was the throne room) an inscription relating to that belief.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> This is testified by <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a>, in <i><a href="/wiki/Histories_(Herodotus)" title="Histories (Herodotus)">The Histories</a></i>, where he mentions that three brothers of the lineage of Temenus, <a href="/wiki/Gauanes" title="Gauanes">Gauanes</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aeropus_(brother_of_Perdiccas_I)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aeropus (brother of Perdiccas I) (page does not exist)">Aeropus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Perdiccas_I_of_Macedon" title="Perdiccas I of Macedon">Perdiccas</a>, fled from Argos to the <a href="/wiki/Illyrians" title="Illyrians">Illyrians</a> and then to <a href="/wiki/Upper_Macedonia" title="Upper Macedonia">Upper Macedonia</a>, to a town called <a href="/wiki/Lebaea" title="Lebaea">Lebaea</a>, where they served the king. The latter asked them to leave his territory, believing in an omen that something great would happen to Perdiccas. The boys went to another part of <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(region)" title="Macedonia (region)">Macedonia</a>, near the garden of <a href="/wiki/Midas" title="Midas">Midas</a>, above which mount Bermio stands. There they made their abode and slowly formed their own kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Herodotus also relates the incident of the participation of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_I_of_Macedon" title="Alexander I of Macedon">Alexander I of Macedon</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Olympic_Games" title="Ancient Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a> in 504 or 500 BC where the participation of the Macedonian king was contested by participants on the grounds that he was not Greek. The <a href="/wiki/Hellanodikai" title="Hellanodikai">Hellanodikai</a>, however, after examining his Argead claim confirmed that the Macedonian kings were Greeks and allowed him to participate.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
