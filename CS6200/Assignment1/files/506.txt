<p><b>Storge</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/ɔːr/: &#39;ar&#39; in &#39;war&#39;">ɔːr</span><span title="/ɡ/: &#39;g&#39; in &#39;guy&#39;">ɡ</span><span title="/i/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;happy&#39;">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>,<sup id="cite_ref-collins_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-collins-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> word στοργή <i>storgē</i><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>) or <b>familial love</b> refers to natural or instinctual affection,<sup id="cite_ref-collins_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-collins-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Strong_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> such as the love of a parent towards offspring and vice versa.
</p><p>In social psychology, another term for love between good friends is <i><a href="/wiki/Philia" title="Philia">philia</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Strong_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extensiveness">Extensiveness</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Storge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Extensiveness">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Storge is a wide-ranging force which can apply between family members, friends, pets and owners, companions or colleagues; it can also blend with and help underpin other types of tie such as <a href="/wiki/Passionate_love" class="mw-redirect" title="Passionate love">passionate love</a> or <a href="/wiki/Friendship" title="Friendship">friendship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Thus storge may be used as a general term to describe the love between exceptional friends, and the desire for them to care compassionately for one another.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Storge_love">Storge love</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Storge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Storge love">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sometimes the term is used to refer to the love between married partners who are committed and plan to have a long relationship together, particularly as a fundamental relational foundation after initial <a href="/wiki/Infatuation" title="Infatuation">infatuation</a> (limerence).
</p><p>Another interpretation for storge is to be used to describe a sexual relationship between two people that gradually grew out of a friendship<sup id="cite_ref-Strong_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>—storgic lovers sometimes cannot pinpoint the moment that friendship turned to love.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Storgic lovers are friends first, and the friendship, and the storge, can endure even beyond the breakup of the sexual relationship.<sup id="cite_ref-Strong_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> They want their significant others to also be their best friends, and will choose their mates based on similar goals and interests—<a href="/wiki/Homogamy_(sociology)" title="Homogamy (sociology)">homogamy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Storgic lovers place much importance on commitment, and find that their motivation to avoid committing <a href="/wiki/Infidelity" title="Infidelity">infidelity</a> is to preserve the trust between the two partners.  Children and marriage are seen as legitimate long-term aims for their bond,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Passion_(emotion)" title="Passion (emotion)">passionate</a> <a href="/wiki/Human_sexuality" title="Human sexuality">sexual</a> intensity is of lesser importance than in other love styles.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Advantages">Advantages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Storge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Advantages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Advantages of storgic love may be the level of how one loves their family and understands each other. In addition, two people who are deeply devoted to one another can feel the intimacy that they share.
</p><p>The main disadvantages of storgic love may be the large time investment and the loss of that investment if the friendship ends<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Other sources claim that since other forms of love can evolve into Storge, Storge can evolve into other forms of love. Also the description of Storge in terms of investment, demanding a return on investment as integral part of evaluation, is entirely subjective and opinionable rather than factful. (July 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>.
</p>
