<p><b>Edwards</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Patronymic_surname" title="Patronymic surname">patronymic surname</a>, which arose separately in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>. It means "son of <a href="/wiki/Edward" title="Edward">Edward</a>". Edwards is the 14th most common surname in Wales and 21st most common in England.<sup id="cite_ref-fb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Within the United States, it was ranked as the 49th-most common surname as surveyed in 1990,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> falling to 51st in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-fb_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
