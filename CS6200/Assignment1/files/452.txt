<p>A <b>sister</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Woman" title="Woman">woman</a> or <a href="/wiki/Girl" title="Girl">girl</a> who shares one or more parents with another.<sup id="cite_ref-Oxford_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oxford-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The male counterpart is a <a href="/wiki/Brother" title="Brother">brother</a>. Although the term typically refers to a <a href="/wiki/Family" title="Family">familial</a> relationship, it is sometimes used endearingly to refer to non-familial relationships.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> A full sister is a <a href="/wiki/First_degree_relative" class="mw-redirect" title="First degree relative">first degree relative</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sister&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Little_Julia_tending_the_baby_at_home.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Little_Julia_tending_the_baby_at_home.jpg/220px-Little_Julia_tending_the_baby_at_home.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Little_Julia_tending_the_baby_at_home.jpg/330px-Little_Julia_tending_the_baby_at_home.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Little_Julia_tending_the_baby_at_home.jpg/440px-Little_Julia_tending_the_baby_at_home.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="732" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Little_Julia_tending_the_baby_at_home.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Two child sisters, circa 1911.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Spencer-sisters.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Spencer-sisters.jpg/220px-Spencer-sisters.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="309" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Spencer-sisters.jpg/330px-Spencer-sisters.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Spencer-sisters.jpg/440px-Spencer-sisters.jpg 2x" data-file-width="854" data-file-height="1199" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Spencer-sisters.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Three sisters, circa 1902.</div></div></div>
<p>The English word <i>sister</i> comes from <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a> systir which itself derives from Proto-Germanic *swestēr,  both of which have the same meaning, i.e. sister. Some studies have found that sisters display more traits indicating jealousy around their siblings than their male counterparts, <a href="/wiki/Brother" title="Brother">brothers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In some cultures, sisters are afforded a role of being under the protection by male siblings, especially older brothers from issues ranging from bullies or sexual advances by womanizers.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In some quarters the term <i>sister</i> has gradually broadened its colloquial meaning to include individuals stipulating kinship.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In response, in order to avoid equivocation, some publishers prefer the usage of <i>female sibling</i> over <i>sister</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Males with a twin sister sometimes view her as their female alter ego, or what they would have been like, if they had two X chromosomes.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A study in Perth Australia found that girls having only youngers brothers resulted in a <i>chastity effect</i>, losing their virginity on average more than a year later than average. This has been hypothesized as being attributed to the pheromones in their brothers' sweat and household-related errands.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sororal_relationships">Sororal relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sister&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sororal relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Various studies have shown that an older sister is likely to give a varied gender role to their younger siblings as well as being more likely to develop a close bond with their younger siblings.<sup id="cite_ref-smith8888_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smith8888-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Older sisters are more likely to play with their younger siblings.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Younger siblings display a more needy behavior when in close proximity to their older sister<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and are more likely to be tolerant of an older sister's bad behavior.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Boys with only an older sister are more likely to display stereotypically male behavior, and such masculine boys increased their masculine behavior with the more sisters they have.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The reverse is true for young boys with several sisters, as they tend to be feminine, however, they outgrow this by the time they approach <a href="/wiki/Pubescence" class="mw-redirect" title="Pubescence">pubescence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Boys with older sisters were less likely to be delinquent or have <a href="/wiki/Emotional_and_behavioral_disorders" title="Emotional and behavioral disorders">emotional and behavioral disorders</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> A younger sister is less likely to be scolded by older siblings than a younger brother.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The most common recreational activity between older brother/younger sister pairs is art drawing.<sup id="cite_ref-smith8888_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smith8888-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Some studies also found a correlation between having an older sister and constructive discussions about safe sexual practices.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Some studies have shown that men without sisters are more likely to be ineffectual at courtship and romantic relationships.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
