<p><b>County Galway</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɡ/: &#39;g&#39; in &#39;guy&#39;">ɡ</span><span title="/ɔː/: &#39;au&#39; in &#39;fraud&#39;">ɔː</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="&#39;w&#39; in &#39;wind&#39;">w</span><span title="/eɪ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;face&#39;">eɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">GAWL</span>-way</i></a>; <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <i lang="ga">Contae na Gaillimhe</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Ireland" title="Counties of Ireland">county</a> in <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>. It is located in the <a href="/wiki/West_Region,_Ireland" title="West Region, Ireland">West of Ireland</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Ireland" title="Provinces of Ireland">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Connacht" title="Connacht">Connacht</a>.
</p><p>There are several <a href="/wiki/Gaeltacht" title="Gaeltacht">Irish-speaking areas</a> in the west of the county. The traditional county includes, and is named for, the city of <a href="/wiki/Galway" title="Galway">Galway</a>, but the city and county now have separate local authorities: <a href="/wiki/Galway_City_Council" title="Galway City Council">Galway City Council</a> administers the urban area, while the rest of the county is <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland" title="Local government in the Republic of Ireland">administered</a> by <a href="/wiki/Galway_County_Council" title="Galway County Council">Galway County Council</a>.
</p><p>The population of the county was 258,058 at the 2016 census.<sup id="cite_ref-cso2016City_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cso2016City-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cso2016County_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cso2016County-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Galway_1,_Ireland.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Galway_1%2C_Ireland.jpg/220px-Galway_1%2C_Ireland.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="133" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Galway_1%2C_Ireland.jpg/330px-Galway_1%2C_Ireland.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Galway_1%2C_Ireland.jpg/440px-Galway_1%2C_Ireland.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1026" data-file-height="621" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Galway_1,_Ireland.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Dunguaire_Castle" title="Dunguaire Castle">Dunguaire Castle</a>, built c. 1520</div></div></div>
<p>The first inhabitants in the Galway area arrived over <a href="/wiki/5th_millennium_BC" title="5th millennium BC">7000 years ago</a>. Shell <a href="/wiki/Midden" title="Midden">middens</a> indicate the existence of people as early as 5000 BC.
</p><p>The county originally comprised several kingdoms and territories which predate the formation of the county. These kingdoms included <a href="/wiki/Aidhne" title="Aidhne">Aidhne</a>, <a href="/wiki/U%C3%AD_Maine" title="Uí Maine">Uí Maine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maigh_Se%C3%B3la" title="Maigh Seóla">Maigh Seóla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conmha%C3%ADcne_Mara" title="Conmhaícne Mara">Conmhaícne Mara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soghain" title="Soghain">Soghain</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1enmaige" class="mw-redirect" title="Máenmaige">Máenmaige</a>. County Galway became an official entity around 1569 AD.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The region known as <a href="/wiki/Connemara" title="Connemara">Connemara</a> retains a distinct identity within the county, though its boundaries are unclear, and so it may account for as much as one third, or as little as 20%, of the county.
</p><p>The county includes a number of inhabited islands, such as the Oileáin Árann (<a href="/wiki/Aran_Islands" title="Aran Islands">Aran Islands</a>) and Inis Bó Fine (<a href="/wiki/Inishbofin,_County_Galway" title="Inishbofin, County Galway">Inishbofin</a>).
</p><p>With the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> many <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monasteries</a> were built in the county. <a href="/wiki/Monasteries" class="mw-redirect" title="Monasteries">Monasteries</a> kept written records of events in the area and of its people. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Irish_language">Irish language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=County_Galway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Irish language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nearly 20% of the population of County Galway live in the Gaeltacht (Irish-speaking districts). County Galway is home to the largest <a href="/wiki/Gaeltacht" title="Gaeltacht">Gaeltacht</a> Irish-speaking region in Ireland. There are over 48,000 people living within this region, which extends from Galway city westwards through <a href="/wiki/Connemara" title="Connemara">Connemara</a>. The region consists of the following Irish-speaking areas; <a href="/wiki/Galway_City_Gaeltacht" title="Galway City Gaeltacht">Galway City Gaeltacht</a> (parts of the city), <a href="/wiki/Gaeltacht_Cois_Fharraige" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaeltacht Cois Fharraige">Gaeltacht Cois Fharraige</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conamara_Theas" title="Conamara Theas">Conamara Theas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aran_Islands" title="Aran Islands">Aran Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joyce_Country" title="Joyce Country">Duiche Sheoigheach</a> (a part of the northern Galway region known as "Joyce Country" and <a href="/wiki/Maam_Valley" title="Maam Valley">Maam Valley</a>).
</p><p>All schools within the Gaeltacht use the Irish language for classroom instruction. There is also a third-level constituent college of <a href="/wiki/NUIG" class="mw-redirect" title="NUIG">NUIG</a> called <a href="/wiki/Acadamh_na_hOllscola%C3%ADochta_Gaeilge" title="Acadamh na hOllscolaíochta Gaeilge">Acadamh na hOllscolaíochta Gaeilge</a> in <a href="/wiki/Carraroe" title="Carraroe">Carraroe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carna,_County_Galway" title="Carna, County Galway">Carna</a>. <a href="/wiki/Clifden" title="Clifden">Clifden</a> is the largest town in the region. Galway City is also home to Ireland's only Irish-language theatre, <a href="/wiki/Taibhdhearc_na_Gaillimhe" title="Taibhdhearc na Gaillimhe">Taibhdhearc na Gaillimhe</a>. There is a strong Irish-language media presence in this area too, which boasts the radio station <a href="/wiki/Raidi%C3%B3_na_Gaeltachta" class="mw-redirect" title="Raidió na Gaeltachta">Raidió na Gaeltachta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Foinse" title="Foinse">Foinse</a> newspaper in <a href="/wiki/Carraroe" title="Carraroe">Carraroe</a> and national TV station <a href="/wiki/TG4" title="TG4">TG4</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baile_na_hAbhann" class="mw-redirect" title="Baile na hAbhann">Baile na hAbhann</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Aran_Islands" title="Aran Islands">Aran Islands</a> are also part of the Galway Gaeltacht.
</p><p>According to Census 2016, there were 84,249 people in County Galway who could speak Irish.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> According to Census 2011, the Galway city and county Gaeltacht has a population of 48,907, of which 30,978 say they can speak Irish, 23,788 can be classed as native Irish speakers while 7,190 speak Irish daily only within the classroom.  There are 3,006 attending the ten <a href="/wiki/Gaelscoil" title="Gaelscoil">Gaelscoil</a> (Irish language primary schools) and three <a href="/wiki/Gaelchol%C3%A1iste" title="Gaelcholáiste">Gaelcholáiste</a> (Irish language secondary schools) outside the Galway Gaeltacht.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> According to the Irish Census 2016 there are 9,445 people in the county who identify themselves as being daily Irish speakers outside the education system.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government_and_politics">Local government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=County_Galway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Local government and politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Prior to the enactment of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_2001" title="Local Government Act 2001">Local Government Act 2001</a>, the county was a unified whole for administrative purposes, despite the presence of two <a href="/wiki/Local_authority" class="mw-redirect" title="Local authority">local authorities</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Since that time, the administrative re-organisation has reduced the geographical extent of the county by the extent of the area under the jurisdiction of <a href="/wiki/Galway_City_Council" title="Galway City Council">Galway City Council</a>. Today, the geographic extent of the county is limited to the area under the jurisdiction of <a href="/wiki/Galway_County_Council" title="Galway County Council">Galway County Council</a>. Each <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland" title="Local government in the Republic of Ireland">local authority</a> ranks equally as first level <a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_administrative_unit&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Local administrative unit (page does not exist)">local administrative units</a> of the <a href="/wiki/NUTS_3_statistical_regions_of_the_Republic_of_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="NUTS 3 statistical regions of the Republic of Ireland">NUTS 3</a> <a href="/wiki/West_Region,_Ireland" title="West Region, Ireland">West Region</a> for <a href="/wiki/Eurostat" title="Eurostat">Eurostat</a> purposes. There are 34 <a href="/wiki/NUTS_of_the_Republic_of_Ireland#Local_administrative_units" class="mw-redirect" title="NUTS of the Republic of Ireland">LAU 1</a> entities in the Republic of Ireland. The remit of Galway County Council includes some suburbs of the city not within the remit of Galway City Council. Both local authorities are responsible for certain local <a href="/wiki/Public_services" class="mw-redirect" title="Public services">services</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">sanitation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Planning" title="Planning">planning</a> and <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_development" title="Real estate development">development</a>, <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">libraries</a>, the collection of <a href="/wiki/Automobile" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile">motor</a> <a href="/wiki/Tax" title="Tax">taxation</a>, local <a href="/wiki/Road" title="Road">roads</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_housing" class="mw-redirect" title="Social housing">social housing</a>.
</p><p>The county is part of the <a href="/wiki/Midlands%E2%80%93North-West_(European_Parliament_constituency)" title="Midlands–North-West (European Parliament constituency)">Midlands–North-West</a> constituency for the purposes of <a href="/wiki/Elections_in_the_European_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Elections in the European Union">European elections</a>. For elections to <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1il_%C3%89ireann" title="Dáil Éireann">Dáil Éireann</a>, the county is part of three constituencies: <a href="/wiki/Galway_East_(D%C3%A1il_constituency)" title="Galway East (Dáil constituency)">Galway East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galway_West_(D%C3%A1il_constituency)" title="Galway West (Dáil constituency)">Galway West</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roscommon%E2%80%93Galway_(D%C3%A1il_constituency)" title="Roscommon–Galway (Dáil constituency)">Roscommon–Galway</a>. Together they return 11 deputies (<a href="/wiki/Teachta_D%C3%A1la" title="Teachta Dála">TDs</a>) to the Dáil.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=County_Galway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dun_Aonghasa.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Dun_Aonghasa.JPG/220px-Dun_Aonghasa.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Dun_Aonghasa.JPG/330px-Dun_Aonghasa.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Dun_Aonghasa.JPG/440px-Dun_Aonghasa.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1818" data-file-height="1228" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dun_Aonghasa.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The prehistoric hill fort of Dún Aonghasa, Inishmore Island</div></div></div>
<p>County Galway is home to Na Beanna Beola (<a href="/wiki/Twelve_Bens" title="Twelve Bens">Twelve Bens</a>) mountain range, Na Sléibhte Mhám Toirc (the Maum Turk mountains), and the low mountains of Sliabh Echtghe (Slieve Aughty). The highest point in the county is one of the Twelve Bens, Benbaun, at 729m.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lakes">Lakes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=County_Galway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lakes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>County Galway is partly home to a number of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland's</a> largest lakes including <a href="/wiki/Lough_Corrib" title="Lough Corrib">Lough Corrib</a> (the largest lake in the Republic of Ireland), <a href="/wiki/Lough_Derg_(Shannon)" title="Lough Derg (Shannon)">Lough Derg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lough_Mask" title="Lough Mask">Lough Mask</a>. The county is also home to a large number of smaller lakes, many of which are in the <a href="/wiki/Connemara" title="Connemara">Connemara</a> region. These include <a href="/wiki/Lough_Anaserd" title="Lough Anaserd">Lough Anaserd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ardderry_Lough" title="Ardderry Lough">Ardderry Lough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aughrusbeg_Lough" title="Aughrusbeg Lough">Aughrusbeg Lough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ballycuirke_Lough" title="Ballycuirke Lough">Ballycuirke Lough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ballynahinch_Lake" title="Ballynahinch Lake">Ballynahinch Lake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lough_Bofin_(Galway)" title="Lough Bofin (Galway)">Lough Bofin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lough_Cutra" title="Lough Cutra">Lough Cutra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derryclare_Lough" title="Derryclare Lough">Derryclare Lough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lough_Fee" title="Lough Fee">Lough Fee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glendollagh_Lough" title="Glendollagh Lough">Glendollagh Lough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lough_Glenicmurrin" class="mw-redirect" title="Lough Glenicmurrin">Lough Glenicmurrin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lough_Inagh" title="Lough Inagh">Lough Inagh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kylemore_Lough" title="Kylemore Lough">Kylemore Lough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lettercraffroe_Lough" title="Lettercraffroe Lough">Lettercraffroe Lough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maumeen_Lough" title="Maumeen Lough">Maumeen Lough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lough_Nafooey" class="mw-redirect" title="Lough Nafooey">Lough Nafooey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lough_Rea" title="Lough Rea">Lough Rea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ross_Lake_(Ireland)" title="Ross Lake (Ireland)">Ross Lake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lough_Shindilla" title="Lough Shindilla">Lough Shindilla</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=County_Galway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The location of County Galway, situated on the west coast of Ireland, allows it to be directly influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>. Temperature extremes are rare and short lived, though inland areas, particularly east of the Corrib, can boast some of the highest recorded temperatures of the summer in the island of Ireland (sometimes exceeding 30&#160;°C); though these temperatures only occur when land warmed east winds sweep the area; the opposite effect can occur in the winter. Overall, however, Galway is influenced mainly by Atlantic airstreams which bring ample rainfall in between the fleeting sunshine. Rainfall occurs in every month of the year, though the late autumn and winter months can be particularly wet as Atlantic cyclonic activity increases and passes over and around the area, and which is why Galway tends to bear the brunt of severe windstorms that can occur between August and March. The county on average receives about 1300mm of rainfall annually, though some areas along the west coast of the county can receive up to 1900mm and beyond. <a href="/wiki/Extreme_weather" title="Extreme weather">Extreme weather</a> such as blizzards, thunderstorms, flash flooding and hail, though rare, can and do occur, particularly when <a href="/wiki/Air_mass" title="Air mass">air masses</a> of continental origin are undercut by more humid and unstable Atlantic flows.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=County_Galway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>One of the least densely populated counties, County Galway harbors a variety of wildlife. The region's biodiversity is best represented by <a href="/wiki/Connemara_National_Park" title="Connemara National Park">Connemara National Park</a>, situated in the west of the county.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=County_Galway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to numbers published by Galway Chamber of Commerce in early 2019, there were then 196 <a href="/wiki/Information_and_communications_technology" title="Information and communications technology">information and communications technology (ICT)</a> organisations in Galway, including IBM, SAP, Oracle and Cisco.<sup id="cite_ref-chambereconomy_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chambereconomy-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> There is a number of medical device companies in the area, including Medtronic (with approximately 1,800 employees) and Boston Scientific (2,800 employees).<sup id="cite_ref-chambereconomy_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chambereconomy-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Irish fast food chain <a href="/wiki/Supermac%27s" title="Supermac&#39;s">Supermac's</a>, which also operates Irish <a href="/wiki/Papa_John%27s_Pizza" title="Papa John&#39;s Pizza">Papa John's Pizza</a> restaurants, has its head office in the Ballybrit Business Park in <a href="/wiki/Ballybrit" class="mw-redirect" title="Ballybrit">Ballybrit</a>, County Galway.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=County_Galway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Sport_in_Galway" title="Sport in Galway">Sport in Galway</a></div>
<p>Gaelic games are the most popular sport in the county. Galway had traditional regions in which <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_football" title="Gaelic football">Gaelic football</a> or hurling is played. For example, in south and eastern County Galway, in places such as <a href="/wiki/Portumna" title="Portumna">Portumna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gort" title="Gort">Gort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clarinbridge" title="Clarinbridge">Clarinbridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Athenry" title="Athenry">Athenry</a>, hurling is the dominant sport with successful teams at county and national level. Most of the rest of the county is considered to be footballing territory, with most of the county players being from <a href="/wiki/Tuam" title="Tuam">Tuam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oughterard" title="Oughterard">Oughterard</a> or parts of <a href="/wiki/Galway" title="Galway">Galway city</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Galway_United_FC" class="mw-redirect" title="Galway United FC">Galway United FC</a> compete in the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Ireland" title="League of Ireland">SSE Aitricity League of Ireland</a> and plays home games in <a href="/wiki/Eamonn_Deacy_Park" title="Eamonn Deacy Park">Eamonn Deacy Park</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Connacht_Rugby" title="Connacht Rugby">Connacht Rugby</a> competes in the <a href="/wiki/Pro14" title="Pro14">Pro14</a> is based in <a href="/wiki/Galway_city" class="mw-redirect" title="Galway city">Galway city</a>. The two main amateur rugby clubs in the county are <a href="/wiki/Galway_Corinthians_RFC" title="Galway Corinthians RFC">Galway Corinthians RFC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Galwegians_RFC" title="Galwegians RFC">Galwegians RFC</a> which compete in the <a href="/wiki/All-Ireland_League_(rugby_union)" title="All-Ireland League (rugby union)">All-Ireland League</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sport_of_athletics" title="Sport of athletics">Athletics</a> is also a very popular sport in Galway, a few clubs being; Galway City Harriers, <a href="/wiki/Craughwell" title="Craughwell">Craughwell</a> Athletic Club, <a href="/wiki/Athenry" title="Athenry">Athenry</a> A.C, <a href="/wiki/Tuam" title="Tuam">Tuam</a> A.C, <a href="/wiki/Loughrea" title="Loughrea">Loughrea</a> A.C and many others.
</p>
