<p><b>Rodríguez</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[roˈðɾiɣeθ]</a></span>, <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[roˈðɾiɣes]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> (meaning <i>Son of <a href="/wiki/Rodrigo" title="Rodrigo">Rodrigo</a></i>; archaic: <i>Rodericksson</i>) and a common <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. 
</p><p>Its Portuguese equivalent is <a href="/wiki/Rodrigues_(surname)" title="Rodrigues (surname)">Rodrigues</a>.
</p><p>The "ez" signifies "son of". The name Rodrigo is the Spanish form of <a href="/wiki/Roderick" title="Roderick">Roderick</a>, meaning "famous power", from the Germanic elements "hrod" (fame) and "ric" (power). It was the name of <a href="/wiki/Roderic" title="Roderic">Roderic</a>, the last <a href="/wiki/Visigothic" class="mw-redirect" title="Visigothic">Visigothic</a> King before the Muslim conquest, and the subject of many legends. The surname Rodríguez could have originated in the 9th century when <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> names originated.
</p><p>In Belgium the House of <a href="/wiki/Lopez-Maria_Rodriguez_d%27Evora_y_Vega,_1st_Marquess_of_Rode" title="Lopez-Maria Rodriguez d&#39;Evora y Vega, 1st Marquess of Rode">Rodriguez d'Evora y Vega</a> was for generations Great Breadmaster of Flanders, see: <a href="/wiki/Marquess_of_Rode" title="Marquess of Rode">Marquess of Rode</a>.
</p>
