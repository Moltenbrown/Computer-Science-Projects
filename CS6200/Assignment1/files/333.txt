	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Moore (surname)</h1>

<p><b>Moore</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;m&#39; in &#39;my&#39;">m</span><span title="/ʊər/: &#39;our&#39; in &#39;tour&#39;">ʊər</span></span>/</a></span></span> is a popular English-language surname. It is the 34th most common surname in Australia, 32nd most common in England,<sup id="cite_ref-fb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and was the 16th most common surname in the United States in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It can have several meanings and derivations, as it appeared as a surname long before written language had developed in most of the population, resulting in a variety of spellings.
</p><p>Variations of the name can appear as <i>Moore</i>, <i>More</i> or <i>Moor</i>; as well as the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a> originations <i><a href="/wiki/Muir_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Muir (disambiguation)">Muir</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mure_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Mure (disambiguation)">Mure</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%B3r_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Mór (disambiguation)">Mor/Mór</a></i>; the <a href="/wiki/Manx_Gaelic" class="mw-redirect" title="Manx Gaelic">Manx Gaelic</a> origination <i>Moar</i>; the <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a> originations <i>O'More</i> and <i>Ó Mórdha</i>; and the later Irish variants <i>O'Moore</i> and <i>de Mora</i>.
</p><p>The similarly pronounced surname <a href="/wiki/Mohr_(surname)" title="Mohr (surname)">Mohr</a> is of <a href="/wiki/Germanic_peoples" title="Germanic peoples">Germanic</a> lineage and is not related to the Gaelic/English variations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meanings_and_origins">Meanings and origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moore_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meanings and origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>From <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a> <i>mor</i> meaning "open land" or "bog" and given to persons dwelling near a <a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moor</a> or <a href="/wiki/Heath_(habitat)" class="mw-redirect" title="Heath (habitat)">heath</a>.</li>
<li>The Old Irish Moores are <i>O'Mordha</i>, from the Irish Gaelic word <i>mordha</i>, meaning "stately and noble". The Anglo-Norman Moores (established in Ireland's province of <a href="/wiki/Munster" title="Munster">Munster</a> soon after the Anglo/Norman invasion) are called <i>de Mora</i> in Irish, a phonetic rendering of the English name which is derived from the word "moor", or "heathy mountain".</li>
<li>Alternatively of Gaelic/<a href="/wiki/Manx_language" title="Manx language">Manx</a> origin <i>Moar</i>, the name for a collector of manorial rents on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>.</li>
<li>The spelling Moore was sometimes used to indicate a son of someone called <i>More</i> - this being one use where spelling is significant.</li>
<li>Possibly derived from <i><a href="/wiki/Maurus_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Maurus (disambiguation)">Maurus</a></i>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">first name</a> which meant "dark skinned" in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>, and related to the <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>More</i> meaning "<a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moor</a>" like <a href="/wiki/Berber_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Berber people">Berber</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Colloquial" class="mw-redirect" title="Colloquial">colloquial</a> <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nickname</a> for a person of dark complexion, often describing someone of <a href="/wiki/North_African" class="mw-redirect" title="North African">North African</a> descent.</li>
<li>Possibly originated from early references to persons who worked with <a href="/wiki/Boats" class="mw-redirect" title="Boats">boats</a> at a wharf or <a href="/wiki/Mooring_(watercraft)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mooring (watercraft)">Moorage</a>.</li>
<li>The De La Mare surname from French Normandy was progressively anglicized in England as "de la Mare" (<a href="/wiki/Walter_de_la_Mare" title="Walter de la Mare">Walter de la Mare</a>), "De La More", "More", and "Moore" after its bearers accompanied and assisted <a href="/wiki/William_the_Conqueror" title="William the Conqueror">William the Conqueror</a> in his conquest of England, eventually settling in Benenden, Kent County for several centuries, before many moved to America, including Reverend John Moore of Newtown, Long Island. The De La Mare's of Normandy trace their heritage to <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">Vikings</a> and the coastal city of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B8re_og_Romsdal" title="Møre og Romsdal">Møre og Romsdal</a> in Norway. The Møre surname is a place name derived from the Old Norse "Moerr", and the Norwegian word "Marr", meaning ocean, sea, or coastal district. <a href="/wiki/Rollo" title="Rollo">Rollo</a>, the famous Viking and founder of the <a href="/wiki/Dukes_of_Normandy" class="mw-redirect" title="Dukes of Normandy">Dukes of Normandy</a>, which includes the present day <a href="/wiki/British_Royal_Family" class="mw-redirect" title="British Royal Family">British Royal Family</a>, may have been a member of this family, if his father - as some historians have it - was <a href="/wiki/Rognvald_Eysteinsson" title="Rognvald Eysteinsson">Rognvald Eysteinsson</a>, Earl of Møre, Norway.</li></ul>
