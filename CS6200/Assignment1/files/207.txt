<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_and_variants">Origins and variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macdonald&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins and variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The surname is an <a href="/wiki/Anglicised" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicised">Anglicised</a> form of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irish_Gaelic" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Gaelic">Irish Gaelic</a> <i><b>MacDhòmhnaill</b></i> or <i><b>Dòmhnallach</b></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-m1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-m1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The name is a <a href="/wiki/Patronym" class="mw-redirect" title="Patronym">patronym</a> meaning "son of Dòmhnall". The personal name <i>Dòmhnall</i> is composed of the elements <i>domno</i> "world" and <i>val</i> "might", "rule".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Alex_Woolf" title="Alex Woolf">Alex Woolf</a>, the Gaelic personal name is probably a borrowing from the <a href="/wiki/British_language_(Celtic)" class="mw-redirect" title="British language (Celtic)">British Celtic</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Dyfnwal_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Dyfnwal (disambiguation)">Dyfnwal</a></i>.
</p><p>In the context of <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> <a href="/wiki/Clans" class="mw-redirect" title="Clans">clans</a>, the various forms of the name refer to one of the largest clans, <a href="/wiki/Clan_Donald" title="Clan Donald">Clan Donald</a>.
</p>
