	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Clarke</h1>

<p>
<b>Clarke</b> is an Anglo-Irish surname which means "clerk". The surname is of English and Irish origin but the original word comes from Latin for clericus. There are some surname variants, including the  <a href="/wiki/Clerk" title="Clerk">Clerk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clark" title="Clark">Clark</a> which predates Clarke by over 700 years.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Clarke_(given_name)" title="Clarke (given name)">Clarke</a> is also uncommonly chosen as a given name.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Irish_surname_origin">Irish surname origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clarke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Irish surname origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Clarke is a popular surname in Ireland. The Irish version of the surname is believed to have come from <a href="/wiki/County_Galway" title="County Galway">County Galway</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Antrim" title="County Antrim">County Antrim</a> and spread to <a href="/wiki/County_Donegal" title="County Donegal">County Donegal</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Dublin" title="County Dublin">County Dublin</a>.  The name is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Gaelic" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Gaelic">Irish Gaelic</a> <a href="/wiki/Sept_(social)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sept (social)">sept</a> <i><a href="/wiki/%C3%93_Cl%C3%A9irigh" title="Ó Cléirigh">Ó Cléirigh</a></i>, meaning "clerk".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="English_surname_origin">English surname origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clarke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: English surname origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Clark" title="Clark">Clark</a></div>
<p>Clarke as well as Clark is also a widespread surname in England. The English version comes from Anglo-Saxon origin and was used in the Middle Ages for the name of a scribe or secretary. The word "clerc" came from the pre 7th century Olde English  "Cler(e)c" (meaning priest) originally denoted to a member of a religious order only, but later grew rapidly outside the religious order. In the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> virtually the only people who could read and write were members of the religious order, making the word heavily associated with literacy. Thus the surname became a popular term for a literate man, particularly for the professional secretary and the scholar.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The English surnames "Clarke" and "Clark" are both spelling variations of the surname "Clerk".
‘Nobby’ is the nickname most commonly used in English for those with the surname Clark or Clarke.
</p>
