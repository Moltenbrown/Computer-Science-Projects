	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Powell</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Welsh</a> origin. It is a patronymic form of the Welsh name <i>Hywel</i> (later <a href="/wiki/Anglicized" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicized">anglicized</a> as <i>Howell</i>), with the prefix <i>ap</i> meaning "son of", together forming <i>ap Hywel</i>, or "son of Hywel". It is an uncommon name among those of Welsh ancestry. It originates in a dynasty of kings in Wales, and Brittany in the 9th and 10th century, and three Welsh royal houses of that time onwards. The <a href="/wiki/House_of_Tudor" title="House of Tudor">House of Tudor</a> one of the Royal houses of England, also descended from them. See also: <a href="/wiki/Howell_(surname)" class="mw-redirect" title="Howell (surname)">Howell (surname)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Welsh_surnames" title="Welsh surnames">Welsh surnames</a>.
</p>
