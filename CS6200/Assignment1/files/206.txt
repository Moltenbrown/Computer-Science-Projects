	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Douglas (surname)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For the Scottish family of Douglas see <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Douglas" title="Earl of Douglas">Earl of Douglas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Angus" title="Earl of Angus">Earl of Angus</a> or <a href="/wiki/Clan_Douglas" title="Clan Douglas">House of Douglas</a></div>
<table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Douglas</caption><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background:#ADD8E6">Origin</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Meaning</th><td>Black Stream</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Region of origin</th><td><a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a></td></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;border-top: #a2a9b1 1px solid;">Frequency Comparisons:<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Douglas</b> (occasionally spelled <i><a href="/wiki/Douglass_(surname)" title="Douglass (surname)">Douglass</a></i>) is a common surname of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> origin, thought to derive from the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish Gaelic language">Scottish Gaelic</a> <i>dubh glas</i>, meaning "black stream". There are numerous places in Scotland from which the surname is derived. The surname has developed into the <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Douglas_(given_name)" title="Douglas (given name)">Douglas</a></i>. <i>Douglas</i> is a habitational name, which could be derived from any of the many places so-named. While there are numerous places with this name in Scotland, it is thought, in most cases, to refer to <a href="/wiki/Douglas,_South_Lanarkshire" title="Douglas, South Lanarkshire">Douglas, South Lanarkshire</a>, the location of <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Castle" title="Douglas Castle">Douglas Castle</a>, the chief stronghold of the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Douglas" title="Earl of Douglas">Lords of Douglas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-a-last1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-a-last1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a> form of the given name is <i>Dùbhghlas</i>; the <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish language</a> form it is <i>Dúghlas</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and <i>Dubhghlas</i>, which are pronounced <small>Irish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Irish" title="Help:IPA/Irish">[duːɣləs]</a></span>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> According to George Fraser Black, in southern <a href="/wiki/Argyllshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Argyllshire">Argyllshire</a> the surname is an <a href="/wiki/Anglicised" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicised">Anglicised</a> form of the surnames <i>MacLucas</i>, <i>MacLugash</i> (which are derived from the Gaelic <i>Mac Lùcais</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Black_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Black-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Counts_Douglas_in_continental_Europe">Counts Douglas in continental Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_(surname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Counts Douglas in continental Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Field Marshal <a href="/wiki/Robert_Douglas,_Count_of_Skenninge" title="Robert Douglas, Count of Skenninge">Robert Douglas</a> was firstly created baron, and then count, in Sweden. His main fief was the town of <a href="/wiki/Sk%C3%A4nninge" title="Skänninge">Skänninge</a>, and his wife brought in the estate where they had the manor of <a href="/wiki/Stj%C3%A4rnorp_Castle" title="Stjärnorp Castle">Stjernorp</a> erected. His descendants generally continued to reside in Sweden, some offshoots to Russia, Germany etc. The head of the house received in 1848 the title of Count (count of the entail of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BChlhausen" title="Mühlhausen">Mühlhausen</a>) also in peerage of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Baden" title="Grand Duchy of Baden">Grand Duchy of Baden</a>.
The main lineage did not produce long-lasting branches (except the Russian branch, a few generations), until the <i><a href="/wiki/Riksmarskalk" class="mw-redirect" title="Riksmarskalk">riksmarskalk</a></i> of Sweden (High Marshal), <a href="/wiki/Ludvig_Douglas" title="Ludvig Douglas">Count Ludvig Douglas</a>, Minister of Foreign Affairs, in the late 1800s, had several sons and yet more grandsons. Branches starting from his sons and so:
</p>
<ul><li>von Douglas-Langenstein, descendants of count Robert, eldest son of the High Marshal Ludvig. They hold the castle of <a href="/wiki/Langenstein_Castle" title="Langenstein Castle">Langenstein</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>, Germany</li>
<li>von Reischach-Douglas, a cadet grandson of said count Robert</li>
<li>Douglas-Gerstorp, the branch (settled to Sweden) descending from the youngest son of said Robert</li>
<li>Douglas-Stjernorp, the branch descending from the Swedish general <a href="/wiki/Archibald_Douglas_(1883%E2%80%931960)" title="Archibald Douglas (1883–1960)">Archibald Douglas</a>, second son of the High Marshal Ludvig. In this branch, there's the castle of Stjärnorp, reacquired to the family in c. 1875
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Rosita_Spencer-Churchill,_Duchess_of_Marlborough" title="Rosita Spencer-Churchill, Duchess of Marlborough">Countess Dagmar Rosita Astrid Libertas Douglas-Stjernorp</a> (born 1943), British artist and former wife the 11th Duke of Marlborough.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Princess_Elisabeth,_Duchess_in_Bavaria" title="Princess Elisabeth, Duchess in Bavaria">Princess Elisabeth, Duchess in Bavaria</a> (born 1940), heir presumptive to both the former Bavarian Royal House and the Jacobite Succession</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gustaf_Douglas" title="Gustaf Douglas">Count Gustaf Archibald Siegwart Douglas</a> (born 1938), a Swedish businessman and politician</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Archibald_Douglas_(1883%E2%80%931960)" title="Archibald Douglas (1883–1960)">Count Vilhelm Archibald Douglas</a> (1883–1960), a Swedish nobleman and soldier</li></ul></li>
<li>Douglas-Kolfall, the branch descending from Oscar, youngest son of the High Marshal Ludvig.</li></ul>
