	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Grant (name)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Grant_(surname)&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Grant (surname)">Grant (surname)</a>)</span></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Grant</b> can be both a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>. The name is of English and Scottish origin, and there are several possible origins for the name.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>One possible origin of the name is from a <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nickname</a> derived from the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Norman_language" title="Anglo-Norman language">Anglo-Norman</a> <i>graund</i>, <i>graunt</i> ("tall", "large").<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This in turn was derived from the <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>grand</i>, <i>grant</i>, which was originally derived from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>grandis</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This nickname was likely given to a person of remarkable size or merely to distinguish two persons bearing the same name.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Another possible origin of the surname is from a medieval <a href="/wiki/Personal_name" title="Personal name">personal name</a> ultimately derived from the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <a href="/wiki/Byname" class="mw-redirect" title="Byname">byname</a> <i>Granta</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> which might have meant "snarler".<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Grantham-name-meaning_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Grantham-name-meaning-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The surname may also be an alternate spelling of the German <i>Grandt</i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_(surname)" title="Grand (surname)">Grand</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-Grant-name-meaning-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
