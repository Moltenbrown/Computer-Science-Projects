<p>The <b>Community of Portuguese Language Countries</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i><b>Comunidade dos Países de Língua Portuguesa</b></i>; abbreviated as the <b>CPLP</b>), also known as the <b>Lusophone Commonwealth</b> (<i>Comunidade Lusófona</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/International_organization" title="International organization">international organization</a> and political association of <a href="/wiki/Lusophone" title="Lusophone">Lusophone</a> nations across four <a href="/wiki/Continents" class="mw-redirect" title="Continents">continents</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> is an official language. The CPLP operates as a privileged, multilateral forum for the mutual cooperation of the governments, economies, <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organization" title="Non-governmental organization">non-governmental organizations</a>, and peoples of the <i><a href="/wiki/Lusophone" title="Lusophone">Lusofonia</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID46_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID46-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The CPLP consists of <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries#members" title="Member states of the Community of Portuguese Language Countries">9 member states</a> and <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries#Observers" title="Member states of the Community of Portuguese Language Countries">19 associate observers</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.
</p><p>The history of the CPLP began when it was founded in 1996, in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Verde" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabo Verde">Cabo Verde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guinea_Bissau" class="mw-redirect" title="Guinea Bissau">Guinea Bissau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_e_Pr%C3%ADncipe" class="mw-redirect" title="São Tomé e Príncipe">São Tomé e Príncipe</a>, nearly two decades after the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Decolonization" title="Decolonization">decolonization</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Empire" title="Portuguese Empire">Portuguese Empire</a>. Following the independence of <a href="/wiki/Timor-Leste" class="mw-redirect" title="Timor-Leste">Timor-Leste</a> in 2002 and the application by <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a> in 2014, both of those countries became members of the CPLP. <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Special_Administrative_Region_(China)" class="mw-redirect" title="Special Administrative Region (China)">Special Administrative Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a> (an <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_community_(Spain)" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous community (Spain)">Autonomous Community</a> of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>) are both formally interested in full membership and <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries#Officially_interested_countries_and_territories" title="Member states of the Community of Portuguese Language Countries">another 17 countries across the world</a> are formally interested in associate observer status.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:312px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Palacio_Conde_de_Penafiel_(nova_sede_da_CPLP).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Palacio_Conde_de_Penafiel_%28nova_sede_da_CPLP%29.jpg/310px-Palacio_Conde_de_Penafiel_%28nova_sede_da_CPLP%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="310" height="194" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Palacio_Conde_de_Penafiel_%28nova_sede_da_CPLP%29.jpg/465px-Palacio_Conde_de_Penafiel_%28nova_sede_da_CPLP%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Palacio_Conde_de_Penafiel_%28nova_sede_da_CPLP%29.jpg/620px-Palacio_Conde_de_Penafiel_%28nova_sede_da_CPLP%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3859" data-file-height="2410" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Palacio_Conde_de_Penafiel_(nova_sede_da_CPLP).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The seat of the <a href="/wiki/Executive_Secretary_of_the_Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Executive Secretary of the Community of Portuguese Language Countries">Executive Secreteriat of the CPLP</a> is at <a href="/wiki/Penafiel_Palace" title="Penafiel Palace">Penafiel Palace</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The idea of a type of international community or political union of <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese language</a> countries had been proposed and studies numerous times in history. However, the idea for what would become the CPLP came about in 1983, during a <a href="/wiki/State_visit" title="State visit">state visit</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Verde" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabo Verde">Cabo Verde</a> by <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Gama" title="Jaime Gama">Jaime Gama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_(Portugal)" title="Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Portugal)">Foreign Minister of Portugal</a>, when he first proposed a biennial summit of heads of state and government of <a href="/wiki/Lusophone" title="Lusophone">Lusophone</a> countries of the world and the idea of regular meetings between ministerial counterparts of the member states.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Community of Portuguese Language Countries was officially founded on 17 July 1996 at the <a href="/wiki/1st_CPLP_Summit" title="1st CPLP Summit">1st CPLP Heads of State &amp; Government Summit</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>.
</p><p>In 2005, during a meeting in <a href="/wiki/Luanda" title="Luanda">Luanda</a>, the ministers of culture of the member states declared the 5 May as the <a href="/wiki/Lusophone" title="Lusophone">Lusophone</a> Culture Day (<i>Dia da Cultura Lusófona</i> in Portuguese).<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_Newscultura_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_Newscultura-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Through successive enlargements, the Union has grown from the seven founding states—<a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe" title="São Tomé and Príncipe">São Tomé and Príncipe</a>—to the current nine, with the self-determination of <a href="/wiki/Timor-Leste" class="mw-redirect" title="Timor-Leste">Timor-Leste</a> in 2002<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsTimor_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsTimor-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and the accession of <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a> in 2014<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID3294_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID3294-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/10th_CPLP_Summit" title="10th CPLP Summit">10th CPLP Summit</a> in Dili, Timor-Leste with the issuance of the Dili Declaration.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The community has grown beyond its mission in fostering cultural ties between the Portuguese language countries into facilitating trade and political cooperation between the <a href="/wiki/Lusophone" title="Lusophone">Lusophone</a> countries of the world, with the CPLP is the fourth largest producer of oil in the world and its citizens totalling more than 270 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, in <a href="/wiki/Brasilia" class="mw-redirect" title="Brasilia">Brasilia</a>, the nine member states agreed to enlarge cooperation in matters of the seas, tourism, economy and a more ambitious defense and cooperation mechanisms. More rights to the observer states was also approved, which Argentina planned to join.
</p>

<p>The CPLP's guidelines and priorities are established by a biannual Conference of Heads of State and Government (known as the <a href="/wiki/CPLP_Summit" class="mw-redirect" title="CPLP Summit">CPLP Summits</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID3667_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID3667-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and the Organization's plan of action is approved by the Council of Foreign Ministers,<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID91_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID91-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> which meets every year. Special summits can requested at anytime by 2/3 of the member states, usually for the purpose of pressing matters or incidents at the moment. There are also monthly meetings of the Permanent Steering Committee that follow specific initiatives and projects.
</p><p>The headquarters of the CPLP is in <a href="/wiki/Penafiel_Palace" title="Penafiel Palace">Penafiel Palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, Portugal, but the organization maintains dedicated bureaus in all of the foreign ministries of the CPLP member states.
</p><p>The CPLP is financed by its member states.<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID46_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID46-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission">Mission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CPLP is a multilateral forum created to deepen cultural, economic, and political cooperation among the <a href="/wiki/Lusophone" title="Lusophone">Lusophone</a> (Portuguese-speaking) nations of the world.<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID46_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID46-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The prime objectives of the CPLP are:<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID46_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID46-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Promotion and dissemination of the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese language</a></li>
<li>Political and diplomatic cooperation between the <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Member states of the Community of Portuguese Language Countries">member states of the CPLP</a></li>
<li>Cooperation in all areas, including education, health, science and technology, defense, agriculture, public administration, communications, justice, public safety, culture, sports and media</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_Secretary">Executive Secretary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Executive Secretary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Executive_Secretary_of_the_Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Executive Secretary of the Community of Portuguese Language Countries">Executive Secretary of the Community of Portuguese Language Countries</a></div>
<p>The Executive Secretary of the CPLP (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">Secretário Executivo da CPLP</i>) is the executive head and highest representative of the CPLP. The Executive Secretary is charged with leading the Executive Secretariat (<i>Secretariado Executivo</i>), the CPLP's executive branch responsible for creating and implementing the CPLP's agenda of projects and initiatives.<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID116_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID116-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The Executive Secretary, who must be a high-ranking <a href="/wiki/Diplomat" title="Diplomat">diplomat</a> or <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a> from one of the member states, is elected for a mandate of two years at the biennial CPLP Summit, and can be reelected once to a second term.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The Executive Secretariat is headquartered at <a href="/wiki/Penafiel_Palace" title="Penafiel Palace">Penafiel Palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Defense_component">Defense component</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Defense component">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, CPLP revised its cooperation protocol in defense, affirming the organization in the promotion of peace and security.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 2017 Exercício Felino <a href="/wiki/Military_exercise" title="Military exercise">Military exercise</a> taking place in <a href="/wiki/Academia_Militar_das_Agulhas_Negras" title="Academia Militar das Agulhas Negras">Academia Militar das Agulhas Negras</a>, <a href="/wiki/Resende,_Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Resende, Rio de Janeiro">Resende</a>, in the state of <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro_(state)" title="Rio de Janeiro (state)">Rio de Janeiro</a>, Brazil, aims for the increased interoperability of the armed forces of Angola, Brazil, Capo Verde, Equatorial Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Mozambique, Portugal, São Tomé and Príncipe, and Timor-Leste. The first phase of the exercise, known as <i>Carta</i> (chart) took place in Cape Verde in 2016, in which the operation was planned and executed using computer networks as a war game. The Exercício Felino was established in the year 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>There are nine full member states of the CPLP. Seven were founding members of the CPLP: <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Verde" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabo Verde">Cabo Verde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guinea_Bissau" class="mw-redirect" title="Guinea Bissau">Guinea Bissau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_e_Pr%C3%ADncipe" class="mw-redirect" title="São Tomé e Príncipe">São Tomé e Príncipe</a>; <a href="/wiki/Timor_Leste" class="mw-redirect" title="Timor Leste">Timor Leste</a> joined in 2002, after achieving independence and <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a> joined in 2014.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Macau_candidacy">Macau candidacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Macau candidacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a> was the last <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_colony" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese colony">Portuguese colony</a> to be decolonized, and returned to <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> in 1999. It still retains traces of Lusophone culture and Portuguese is one of the official languages of the territory. Despite that, the majority of the population in Macau do not speak and understand Portuguese; rather, Cantonese is the main language. In 2006, during the II Ministerial meeting between China and Portuguese Speaking Countries, the CPLP Executive Secretary and Deputy ambassador Tadeu Soares invited the Chief Executive of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Macau" title="Government of Macau">Government of the Macau Special Administrative Region</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Ho" title="Edmund Ho">Edmund Ho</a>, to request the Associate Observer status for <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The Government of Macau has not yet formalized this request.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Equatorial_Guinea_accession">Equatorial Guinea accession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Equatorial Guinea accession">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>When the CPLP was formed, <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a> asked for observer status. Equatorial Guinea (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">Guiné Equatorial</i>) was a Portuguese colony from the 15th to 18th centuries and has some territories where <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Creole" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese Creole">Portuguese-based creole languages</a> are spoken and cultural connections with <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe" title="São Tomé and Príncipe">São Tomé and Príncipe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> are felt. In the 21st century, the country has cooperated with Portuguese-speaking African countries and Brazil at an educational level.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> At the CPLP summit of July 2004, in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe" title="São Tomé and Príncipe">São Tomé and Príncipe</a>, the member states agreed to change the statutes of the community to accept states as associate observers. Equatorial Guinea then engaged in discussion for full membership.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In June 2010, Equatorial Guinea asked to be admitted as full member. At its eighth summit in <a href="/wiki/Luanda" title="Luanda">Luanda</a> in July 2010, the CPLP decided to open formal negotiations with Equatorial Guinea about full membership in the CPLP.<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID1635_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID1635-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> At its 10th summit in <a href="/wiki/Dili" title="Dili">Dili</a> in July 2014, <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a> was admitted as CPLP member.<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID3294_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID3294-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Associate_observers">Associate observers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Associate observers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:I_F%C3%B3rum_da_Sociedade_Civil_da_Comunidade_dos_Pa%C3%ADses_de_L%C3%ADngua_Portuguesa_(6214161921)_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/I_F%C3%B3rum_da_Sociedade_Civil_da_Comunidade_dos_Pa%C3%ADses_de_L%C3%ADngua_Portuguesa_%286214161921%29_%28cropped%29.jpg/260px-I_F%C3%B3rum_da_Sociedade_Civil_da_Comunidade_dos_Pa%C3%ADses_de_L%C3%ADngua_Portuguesa_%286214161921%29_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="260" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/I_F%C3%B3rum_da_Sociedade_Civil_da_Comunidade_dos_Pa%C3%ADses_de_L%C3%ADngua_Portuguesa_%286214161921%29_%28cropped%29.jpg/390px-I_F%C3%B3rum_da_Sociedade_Civil_da_Comunidade_dos_Pa%C3%ADses_de_L%C3%ADngua_Portuguesa_%286214161921%29_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/I_F%C3%B3rum_da_Sociedade_Civil_da_Comunidade_dos_Pa%C3%ADses_de_L%C3%ADngua_Portuguesa_%286214161921%29_%28cropped%29.jpg/520px-I_F%C3%B3rum_da_Sociedade_Civil_da_Comunidade_dos_Pa%C3%ADses_de_L%C3%ADngua_Portuguesa_%286214161921%29_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1805" data-file-height="915" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:I_F%C3%B3rum_da_Sociedade_Civil_da_Comunidade_dos_Pa%C3%ADses_de_L%C3%ADngua_Portuguesa_(6214161921)_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>I Forum of <a href="/wiki/Civil_society" title="Civil society">Civil Society</a> of the CPLP; 2010.</div></div></div>
<p>In July 2006, during the <a href="/wiki/Bissau" title="Bissau">Bissau</a> summit, <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a> were admitted as Associate Observers<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID50_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID50-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> along with 17 International associations and organizations considered as Consultative Observers. On 23 July 2014, <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a> was admitted as a CPLP member.<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID3294_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID3294-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>, which was discovered by Portuguese explorers and maintains strong connections with <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> also obtained associate observer status in 2006.  In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>, with historical connections to Portuguese colonisation in <a href="/wiki/Casamance" title="Casamance">Casamance</a>, was admitted as Associate Observer.<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID50_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID50-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2014, during the <a href="/wiki/Dili" title="Dili">Dili</a> summit, the Heads of State and Government approved a resolution that grants <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> the status of Associate Observers.<sup id="cite_ref-CPLP_NewsID3294_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPLP_NewsID3294-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Japan has had historical contacts with the Portuguese language in the 16th and 17th century, and today has connections to the Lusophone world through <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Brazilians" title="Japanese Brazilians">Japanese Brazilians</a> in Brazil and Japan. Namibia has had extensive contact to the Lusophone world due to its location just south of Angola.
</p><p>Three European nations, the Czech Republic, Hungary, and Slovakia, were admitted as observers along with Uruguay at the 2016 summit. Uruguay has historical ties to Brazil and Portugal and has some speakers of <a href="/wiki/Portunol" class="mw-redirect" title="Portunol">Portunol</a>, a Spanish-Portuguese mixture. In January 2018 and prior to the 2018 summit, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> requested the observer status as effort for the consolidation of bilateral relations with all of the Portuguese-speaking countries. In its request, the Italian government referred that due to the increasing number of associated observers in the community, CPLP is becoming a forum for countries in various geographical regions. Earlier in January, Andorra also formalized its candidacy for the same status.<sup id="cite_ref-Italia_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Italia-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Italy shares a legacy of Ancient Rome and Italy is the non-lusuophone nation with the most number of university chairs in the Portuguese language. Because of immigration and close geographic proximity, Portuguese is one of the most spoken languages in Andorra.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 2018 summit with all the heads of state present, with the exception of East Timor due to national policies issues, several observers joined the organization: <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">Andorra</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, Argentina, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, Italy and the <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_Ibero-American_States" title="Organization of Ibero-American States">Organization of Ibero-American States</a> joined as observers.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Uruguay, an observer nation since 2016,  admitted in early 2018 a candidacy to become full member of the CPLP.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope">Scope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Scope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jer%C3%B3nimos_April_2014-2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Jer%C3%B3nimos_April_2014-2.jpg/260px-Jer%C3%B3nimos_April_2014-2.jpg" decoding="async" width="260" height="186" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Jer%C3%B3nimos_April_2014-2.jpg/390px-Jer%C3%B3nimos_April_2014-2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Jer%C3%B3nimos_April_2014-2.jpg/520px-Jer%C3%B3nimos_April_2014-2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6334" data-file-height="4531" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jer%C3%B3nimos_April_2014-2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The tomb of <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_de_Cam%C3%B5es" title="Luís de Camões">Luís de Camões</a> is a revered space in the <a href="/wiki/Lusofonia" class="mw-redirect" title="Lusofonia">Lusofonia</a>. Lusophone heads of state pay respects here on visits to Portugal.</div></div></div>
<p>The Portuguese-speaking countries are home to 267 million people located across the globe but having a common language, a shared history, and some cultural similarities. The CPLP nations have a combined area of about 10,742,000 square kilometres (4,148,000&#160;sq&#160;mi), which is more than twice as large as the European Union 4,475,757 square kilometres (1,728,099&#160;sq&#160;mi), but with a little more than half of the population.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_cooperation">Political cooperation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Political cooperation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since its formation, the CPLP has helped to solve problems in São Tomé and Príncipe and in <a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a>, because of <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d&#39;état">coups d'état</a> in those countries. The CPLP helped these two countries to take economic reforms (in the case of São Tomé) and democratic ones (in the case of Guinea-Bissau).
</p><p>In the early 21st century, the leaders of the CPLP believed that peace in <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>, as well as East Timor's independence, favored the development of the CPLP and a strengthening of multilateral cooperation.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Language">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Minist%C3%A9rio_da_Cultura_-_VII_Reuni%C3%A3o_de_Ministros_da_Cultura_da_CPLP_(1).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Minist%C3%A9rio_da_Cultura_-_VII_Reuni%C3%A3o_de_Ministros_da_Cultura_da_CPLP_%281%29.jpg/260px-Minist%C3%A9rio_da_Cultura_-_VII_Reuni%C3%A3o_de_Ministros_da_Cultura_da_CPLP_%281%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="260" height="173" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Minist%C3%A9rio_da_Cultura_-_VII_Reuni%C3%A3o_de_Ministros_da_Cultura_da_CPLP_%281%29.jpg/390px-Minist%C3%A9rio_da_Cultura_-_VII_Reuni%C3%A3o_de_Ministros_da_Cultura_da_CPLP_%281%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Minist%C3%A9rio_da_Cultura_-_VII_Reuni%C3%A3o_de_Ministros_da_Cultura_da_CPLP_%281%29.jpg/520px-Minist%C3%A9rio_da_Cultura_-_VII_Reuni%C3%A3o_de_Ministros_da_Cultura_da_CPLP_%281%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2362" data-file-height="1575" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Minist%C3%A9rio_da_Cultura_-_VII_Reuni%C3%A3o_de_Ministros_da_Cultura_da_CPLP_(1).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The 7th Meeting of Ministers of Culture at <a href="/wiki/Sintra_National_Palace" title="Sintra National Palace">Sintra Palace</a>; <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Riviera" title="Portuguese Riviera">Portuguese Riviera</a>, 2010.</div></div></div>
<p>Since many children in rural areas of Lusophone Africa and East Timor are out-of-school youth, the education officials in these regions seek help from Portugal and Brazil to increase the education to spread Portuguese fluency (like establishing <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Cam%C3%B5es" title="Instituto Camões">Instituto Camões</a> language center branches in main cities and rural towns), as Portuguese is becoming one of the main languages in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a>, where it is also taught in <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.
</p><p>Angola has not yet signed the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Language_Orthographic_Agreement_of_1990" title="Portuguese Language Orthographic Agreement of 1990">most recent accord on the orthography of the Portuguese language</a>, and has asked other <a href="/wiki/Portuguese-speaking_African_countries" title="Portuguese-speaking African countries">PALOP</a> countries to support it in discussions on various points of that accord with Portugal.<sup id="cite_ref-Angop_2012-01-28_22-41_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Angop_2012-01-28_22-41-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In many developing Portuguese-speaking nations, Portuguese is the language of government and commerce which means that Portuguese-speaking people from African nations can work and communicate with others in different parts of the world, especially in Portugal and Brazil, where the economies are stronger. Many leaders of Portuguese-speaking nations in Africa are fearful that language standards do not meet the fluency required and are therefore making it compulsory in schools so that a higher degree of fluency is achieved and young Africans will be able to speak a world language that will help them later in life.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lusophone_Citizenship">Lusophone Citizenship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Lusophone Citizenship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Easing citizens' cross-border movement between the member states was proposed at the 2017 CPLP Summit.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> This proposal by Portugal and Cape Verde to Brazil was thought by some to conflict with Europe's <a href="/wiki/Schengen_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Schengen area">Schengen area</a>. However, this free movement is based on a different model: as residence permits, associated with the recognition of <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">academic degrees</a> and professional qualification, and maintenance of social rights including pension systems. It would henceforth establish the Lusophone citizenship, the <i>cidadania lusófona</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The <b>Conference of Heads of State and Government of the CPLP</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">Conferência de Chefes de Estado e de Governo da Comunidade dos Países de Língua Portuguesa</i>; CCEG), commonly known as the <b>CPLP Summit</b> (<i>Cimeira da CPLP</i>) is a biennial meeting of <a href="/wiki/Heads_of_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Heads of state">heads of state</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heads_of_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Heads of government">heads of government</a> of the member states of the CPLP. It is considered one of the fundamental pillars of the CPLP's structure.
</p>
<dl><dt>Objectives</dt></dl>
<p>The mission of the CPLP Summit is to:<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Define and focus the CPLP's strategy and general policy</li>
<li>Adopt and implement the agenda agreed upon by member states</li>
<li>To create institutions of the CPLP or delegate powers to the CPLP Council of Ministers</li>
<li>Elect a rotating presidency of the CPLP</li>
<li>Elect the <a href="/wiki/Executive_Secretary_of_the_Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Executive Secretary of the Community of Portuguese Language Countries">CPLP Executive Secretary</a></li></ul>
