	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Colonial empire</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>A <b>colonial empire</b> is a collective of territories (often called <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colonies</a>), either contiguous with the imperial center or located overseas, <a href="/wiki/Plantation_(settlement_or_colony)" title="Plantation (settlement or colony)">settled</a> by the population of a certain <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">state</a> and governed by that state.
</p><p>Before the expansion of early modern European powers, other empires had conquered and colonized territory, such as the Romans in Iberia, or the Chinese in what is now south China. Modern colonial empires first emerged with a race of exploration between the then most advanced <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> maritime powers, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, during the 15th century.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The initial impulse behind these dispersed maritime empires and those that followed was <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a>, driven by the new ideas and the <a href="/wiki/Capitalism" title="Capitalism">capitalism</a> that grew out of the European <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a>. Agreements were also made to divide the world up between them in <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Alc%C3%A1%C3%A7ovas" title="Treaty of Alcáçovas">1479</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inter_caetera" title="Inter caetera">1493</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Tordesillas" title="Treaty of Tordesillas">1494</a>. European <a href="/wiki/Imperialism" title="Imperialism">imperialism</a> was born out of competition between European Christians and <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> Muslims, the latter of which rose up quickly in the 14th century and forced the Spanish and Portuguese to seek new trade routes to <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">India</a>, and to a lesser extent, China.
</p><p>Although <a href="/wiki/Colonies_in_antiquity" title="Colonies in antiquity">colonies existed</a> in <a href="/wiki/Classical_antiquity" title="Classical antiquity">classical antiquity</a>, especially amongst the <a href="/wiki/Phoenicians" class="mw-redirect" title="Phoenicians">Phoenicians</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greeks" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient Greeks">Ancient Greeks</a> who settled many islands and coasts of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, these colonies were politically independent from the <a href="/wiki/City-state" title="City-state">city-states</a> they originated from, and thus did not constitute a colonial empire.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="European_colonial_empires">European colonial empires</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colonial_empire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: European colonial empires">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Portugal began establishing the first global trade network and one of the first <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Empire" title="Portuguese Empire">empires</a> (Together with the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Henry_the_Navigator" class="mw-redirect" title="Henry the Navigator">Henry the Navigator</a>. The empire spread throughout a vast number of territories distributed across the globe (especially at one time in the 16th century) that are now parts of <a href="/wiki/Evolution_of_the_Portuguese_Empire#Countries_with_territories_part_of_the_Portuguese_Empire" title="Evolution of the Portuguese Empire">60 different sovereign states</a>. Portugal would eventually control <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, territories such as what is now <a href="/wiki/Colonia_del_Sacramento" title="Colonia del Sacramento">Uruguay</a> and some fishing ports in north, in the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>; <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Angola" title="Portuguese Angola">Angola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Mozambique" title="Portuguese Mozambique">Mozambique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Guinea" title="Portuguese Guinea">Portuguese Guinea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe" title="Portuguese São Tomé and Príncipe">São Tomé and Príncipe</a> (among other territories and bases) in the <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Subsaharan</a> <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>; cities, forts or territories in all the <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> Subcontinents, as <a href="/wiki/Muscat,_Oman" class="mw-redirect" title="Muscat, Oman">Muscat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ormus" title="Ormus">Ormus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a> (amongst other bases) in the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>; <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bombay" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombay">Bombay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daman_and_Diu" title="Daman and Diu">Daman and Diu</a> (amongst other coastal cities) in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_India" title="Portuguese India">India</a>; <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Ceylon" title="Portuguese Ceylon">Portuguese Ceylon</a>; <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Malacca" title="Portuguese Malacca">Malacca</a>, bases in Southeast Asia and <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Makassar" title="Makassar">Makassar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solor" title="Solor">Solor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Banda_Islands" title="Banda Islands">Banda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ambon_Island" title="Ambon Island">Ambon</a> and others in the <a href="/wiki/Moluccas" class="mw-redirect" title="Moluccas">Moluccas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Timor" title="Portuguese Timor">Portuguese Timor</a>; and the granted <i>entrepôt</i>-base of <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Macau" title="Portuguese Macau">Macau</a> and the entrepôt-enclave of <a href="/wiki/Dejima" title="Dejima">Dejima</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a>) in <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a>, amongst other smaller or short-lived possessions. 
</p>
<div class="center"><div class="thumb tnone"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:752px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Colonisation2.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Colonisation2.gif/750px-Colonisation2.gif" decoding="async" width="750" height="329" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Colonisation2.gif/1125px-Colonisation2.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/89/Colonisation2.gif 2x" data-file-width="1425" data-file-height="625" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Colonisation2.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><div class="center" style="width:auto; margin-left:auto; margin-right:auto;">The territorial evolution of modern colonial empires and some of their successor states (such as USSR, Turkey)</div></div></div></div></div>
<p>During its <i><a href="/wiki/Siglo_de_Oro" class="mw-redirect" title="Siglo de Oro">Siglo de Oro</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> had possession of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, most of <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, parts of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, parts of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, and many colonies in the Americas, Africa, and Asia. With the conquest of inland <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> (Cortez), <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> (Pizarro), and the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> in the 16th century, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> established overseas dominions on a scale that had never been approached by its predecessors (the Mongol Empire had been larger but was restricted to Eurasia), and with the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Union" title="Iberian Union">Iberian Union</a> (1580), reached the widest scale in history until then in world distribution. Possessions in Europe, Africa, the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, the Americas, the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, and East Asia qualified the Spanish Empire as attaining a global presence.
</p><p>From 1580 to 1640 the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Empire" title="Portuguese Empire">Portuguese Empire</a> and the Spanish Empire were conjoined in a <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a> of its <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Philippine Dynasty">Habsburg monarchs</a> during the period of the <i>Iberian Union</i>, but beneath the highest level of government, their separate administrations were maintained.
</p><p>Subsequent colonial empires included the <a href="/wiki/List_of_French_possessions_and_colonies" title="List of French possessions and colonies">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_overseas_possessions" title="English overseas possessions">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Empire" title="Dutch Empire">Dutch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Japanese Empire">Japanese</a> empires. By the mid-17th century, the <a href="/wiki/Tsardom_of_Russia" title="Tsardom of Russia">Tsardom of Russia</a>, continued later as the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, became the largest contiguous state in the world, and the modern <a href="/wiki/Russian_Federation" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian Federation">Russian Federation</a> continues to be so to this day. Russia today has nine <a href="/wiki/Time_zones" class="mw-redirect" title="Time zones">time zones</a>, stretching across about half of the world's longitude.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>, consolidated during the period of British maritime hegemony in the 19th century, became the largest empire in history by virtue of the improved transportation technologies of the time. At its height, the British Empire covered a quarter of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>'s land area and comprised a quarter of its population. During the <a href="/wiki/New_Imperialism" title="New Imperialism">New Imperialism</a>, Italy and Germany also built their <a href="/wiki/Scramble_for_Africa" title="Scramble for Africa">colonial empires in Africa</a>.
</p><p>It is worth noting that there were also non-European empires in this period, most notably the Mughals in India the Qing Empire of China, which conquered a huge area of East and Inner Asia. The British replaced the Mughals in India, and after the <a href="/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion" title="Boxer Rebellion">Boxer Rebellion</a> in 1901, <a href="/wiki/History_of_China#Imperial_China" title="History of China">Imperial China</a> made concessions to the <a href="/wiki/Eight-Nation_Alliance" title="Eight-Nation Alliance">Eight-Nation Alliance</a> (all the <a href="/wiki/Great_Powers" class="mw-redirect" title="Great Powers">Great Powers</a> of the time). By the end of the 20th century most of the previous colonial empires had been <a href="/wiki/Decolonization" title="Decolonization">decolonized</a>, though the modern nation states of Russia and China inherited much of the territory of the Romanov and Qing empires, respectively.
</p>
