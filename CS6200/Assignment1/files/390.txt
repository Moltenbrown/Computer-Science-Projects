<p><b>Xavier X. Sala i Martín</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (also <i>Sala-i-Martin</i> in English) is an American and Spanish economist, who is a professor of economics at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>. Sala i Martin is one of the leading economists in the field of economic growth.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Cabrera_de_Mar" title="Cabrera de Mar">Cabrera de Mar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, Sala i Martin earned a degree in economics from the <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_University_of_Barcelona" title="Autonomous University of Barcelona">Autonomous University of Barcelona</a> in 1985. He completed his <a href="/wiki/Doctorate" title="Doctorate">PhD</a> in Economics from <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to working at Columbia, he has been a professor at <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Universitat_Pompeu_Fabra" class="mw-redirect" title="Universitat Pompeu Fabra">Universitat Pompeu Fabra</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Barcelona_Graduate_School_of_Economics" title="Barcelona Graduate School of Economics">Barcelona Graduate School of Economics</a>, where he usually visits for a term, every summer.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_work">Professional work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xavier_Sala-i-Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sala i Martin is one of the leading economists in the field of economic growth<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and is consistently ranked among the most-cited economists in the world for works produced in the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His works include the topics of <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a>, development in Africa, monetary economics, social security, health and economics, convergence, and <a href="/wiki/Classical_liberal" class="mw-redirect" title="Classical liberal">classical liberal</a> thinking, with his book <i>Liberal economics for non-economists</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The "liberal" in the title should be understood in the classic liberal/<a href="/wiki/Libertarianism" title="Libertarianism">libertarian</a> sense.
</p><p>He has constructed an estimate of the world distribution of income,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> which he has then used to estimate poverty rates and measures of inequality. The conclusions of this study offered a new point of view for two reasons. Firstly, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> used to believe that although poverty rates were falling, the total number of poor people was increasing. He claimed that both were falling. Secondly, the United Nations and the World Bank believe that individual income inequalities were on the rise. He claimed that they were not.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sala i Martin is the author of the economic growth textbook <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.antonibosch.com/libro/apuntes-de-crecimiento-economico-2-ed"><i>Apuntes de Crecimiento Economico</i></a> (in Spanish) and the co-author (with <a href="/wiki/Robert_Barro" title="Robert Barro">Robert Barro</a>) of the textbook <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/0262025531">Economic Growth</a></i> (original in English; translated into French, German, Spanish, Chinese, and Japanese).
</p><p>Sala i Martin is, along with <a href="/wiki/Elsa_V._Artadi" class="mw-redirect" title="Elsa V. Artadi">Elsa V. Artadi</a>, the author of the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www3.weforum.org/docs/WEF_GCR_Report_2011-12.pdf">Global Competitiveness Index</a>, used since 2004<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> by the <a href="/wiki/Global_Competitiveness_Report" title="Global Competitiveness Report">Global Competitiveness Report</a> published by the <a href="/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a>, an index that ranks 142 countries by their level of economic competitiveness.
</p><p>He often collaborates with Catalan media to support the independence of Catalonia from Spain. In 2014 he had a public confrontation with Jose Manuel Durão Barroso, the President of the European Commission, reproaching him the lack of support towards a democratic resolution of the conflict between Spain and Catalonia<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_activities">Other activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xavier_Sala-i-Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Other activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was a board member at <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> and treasurer of the club between 2004 and 2010. He was the president of the club during the electoral process of 2006.
</p><p>He is the founder of <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.umbele.org">Umbele: A Future for Africa</a>, a nonprofit organization that promotes economic development in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.
</p><p>He is a columnist for the Spanish newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/La_Vanguardia" title="La Vanguardia">La Vanguardia</a></i>. He makes weekly appearances in the Catalan radio network <a href="/wiki/RAC_1" title="RAC 1">RAC 1</a> and in the television show <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.tv3.cat/videos/3674010/Lestiu-en-clau-economica-amb-Xavier-Sala-i-Martin-Part-1">Divendres</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/TV3_(Catalonia)" title="TV3 (Catalonia)">TV3</a>. He also contributes to <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He supports <a href="/wiki/Catalan_independence" class="mw-redirect" title="Catalan independence">Catalan independence</a> and gives conferences around Catalonia, in name of the pro-independence association that he and other university teachers created for that purpose (Col·lectiu Wilson)<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prizes">Prizes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xavier_Sala-i-Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Prizes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He has been recognized with a Distinguished Teacher in Graduate Economics award three times at Columbia and Yale, with the 2004 King Juan Carlos I Prize (a biannual prize given to the best economist in Spain and Latin America), and the 2006 Lenfest Prize awarded to the best teacher at Columbia University.
</p><p>On 15 January 2017, Martin was listed by UK-based company Richtopia at number 14 in the list of 100 Most Influential Economists.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
