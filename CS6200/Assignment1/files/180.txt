<p><b>Davies</b> is a patronymic Welsh <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>. It may be a corruption of Dyfed, itself a corruption of Dési, colonists from south-east Ireland who occupied the old tribal area of the <a href="/wiki/Demetae" title="Demetae">Demetae</a> in south-west Wales in the late third century AD, establishing a dynasty which lasted five centuries<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>.  Dyfed is recorded as a surname as late as the 12th century for e.g. Gwynfard Dyfed, born in 1175. 'Dafydd' appears as a given name in the 13th Century, e.g. Dafydd ap Gruffydd (1238–1283), Prince of Wales, and Dafydd ab Edmwnd (fl. c. 1450–97), Welsh poet. The given name 'Dafydd' is generally translated into English as 'David'. Alternatively it may derive from <i><a href="/wiki/David_(name)" title="David (name)">David</a></i> (a <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a> name meaning "beloved"),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> the name of <a href="/wiki/Saint_David" title="Saint David">Wales's patron saint</a>. In Wales Davies is standardly pronounced <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="/eɪ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;face&#39;">eɪ</span><span title="&#39;v&#39; in &#39;vie&#39;">v</span><span title="/ɪ/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;kit&#39;">ɪ</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">DAY</span>-vis</i></a>, that is, identically to <a href="/wiki/Davis_(surname)" title="Davis (surname)">Davis</a>. This pronunciation is also used by many outside the United Kingdom, where it competes with the <a href="/wiki/Spelling_pronunciation" title="Spelling pronunciation">spelling pronunciation</a> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="/eɪ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;face&#39;">eɪ</span><span title="&#39;v&#39; in &#39;vie&#39;">v</span><span title="/iː/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">iː</span><span title="&#39;z&#39; in &#39;zoom&#39;">z</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">DAY</span>-veez</i></a>, which is particularly common in the US.
</p><p>Davies is the 2nd most common surname in Wales and 8th most common in England of Welsh descendants,<sup id="cite_ref-fb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> where it is particularly widespread in the southwest, especially <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>. It is not common in the United States where the spelling <i>Davis</i> is preferred.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
